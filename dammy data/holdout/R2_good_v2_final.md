# OatVita Relaunch Proposal (final v2) — HOLDOUT, do not ingest

- Requirement: R2 / Client: Hoshino Beverage Co.
- Author: Hana Yamamoto (Planner) / Revised: Ayaka Sato (Executive Strategy Director)
- Date: 2025-10-02
- Status: presented to client — SELECTED

## 1. The problem we are solving

OatVita's trial is healthy; its repeat is broken. Repeat purchase runs at 28% against
a 41% category average, while trial sits at par. The client's own shopper data plus
our taste-perception scan (n=600) point to one driver: 57% of lapsed buyers describe
the texture as "watery / thin" — a *product-experience expectation gap*, not an
awareness or values gap. Sustainability messaging cannot fix a texture memory.

**Problem statement: OatVita doesn't need more people to try it — it needs the people
who tried it to expect the right thing and come back.**

## 2. Target: one segment, deliberately narrow

**Primary: lapsed triers — women 30–44 who bought OatVita 1–2 times in the last 12
months and defected back to dairy yogurt** (est. 1.4M households, identifiable via
retailer loyalty-card segments).

**Who we are NOT targeting (and why):**
- Vegans/committed plant-based buyers (already 68% aware; buying on values, not taste —
  incremental spend converts nobody new)
- Seniors / cholesterol segment (medical-adjacent claims trigger a 3-month legal review;
  timeline kills it)

## 3. Strategy

Re-set the texture expectation before asking for re-trial: **"Thicker than you
remember. We reformulated."** Lead with the reformulation fact (new packaging = new
recipe cue), then a low-friction re-trial mechanic.

## 4. Executions

- Retail-media targeting of lapsed-buyer loyalty segments: "It's not the OatVita you
  remember" + ¥50 re-trial coupon
- 15s proof video: spoon-lift viscosity shot side-by-side, old vs new recipe
- In-store: shelf-edge "reformulated" flags + sampling in top-200 velocity stores
- Brand film demoted: 6s bumper only, weeks 5+, after re-trial mechanic is live

## 5. Media plan: sequenced, gated

1. Weeks 1–4: lapsed-trier retail media + sampling only (fix the memory first)
2. Weeks 5–12: expand to adjacent dairy-yogurt heavy buyers
3. Broad awareness only if repeat rate clears 35% in the loyalty-card cohort

## 6. KPIs

- Repeat purchase in targeted cohort: 28% → 38% by month 4
- Re-trial redemptions: 220k coupons
- Category share: 1.2% → 2.4% by month 6 (client ask: 3% — we state 2.4% as the
  evidence-backed commitment and 3% as stretch)

## 7. Sources

- Retailer loyalty-card panel, Aug 2025 (repeat/trial split, lapsed-buyer sizing)
- Taste-perception scan n=600, Sep 2025 (texture verbatims)
- Plant-Based Dairy Category Report 2025 (share, awareness by segment)
