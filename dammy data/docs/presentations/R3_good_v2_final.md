# Glide Subscription Growth Proposal (final v2)

- Requirement: R3 / Client: Sakura Mobility Inc.
- Author: Riku Tanaka (Planner) / Revised: Ayaka Sato (Executive Strategy Director)
- Date: 2025-09-09
- Status: presented to client — SELECTED

## 1. The problem we are solving

Glide does not need more people to know it exists — 82,000 registered users already do.
Only 9% convert to the ¥4,980 plan, and churn concentrates in month 2: cohort data shows
a 31% month-2 drop, with cancellation verbatims dominated by rainy-day frustration
(58% mention weather). Acquiring new registrations while the conversion funnel leaks
is buying water for a cracked bucket.

**Problem statement: Glide has a 9% paid-conversion and month-2 rainy-day churn problem,
not an awareness problem.**

## 2. Target: one segment, deliberately narrow

**Primary: registered-but-unconverted users whose commute is ≤6km** (est. 34,000 of the
82,000 registered base; port-density mapping shows 6km is the radius where Glide beats
the train door-to-door). They have already raised their hand — they need a reason to pay.

**Who we are NOT targeting (and why):**
- Tourists / leisure riders (day-pass economics, never sustain a ¥4,980 subscription)
- Car commuters (parking habits and trip length structurally misfit 340-port coverage)
- Cold audiences with no registration (CPA 4–6x higher than converting the warm base)

## 3. Strategy

Sell the math, then remove the rain excuse. Message: **"¥4,980/month. Less than 25 days
of train fare — and the platform crowd is someone else's problem."** Pair with an honest
rainy-day playbook (poncho kit, port-side shelter map, pause-your-plan feature) so
month 2 stops killing month 3.

## 4. Executions

- CRM + retargeting to the 34,000 warm users: personalized fare-vs-subscription calculator
- "Rain-Ready" content series: real riders' wet-season routines, pause-plan explainer
- Port-radius OOH only within 800m of the 340 Tokyo ports — advertise where riding is possible
- CEO's brand movie retained — scoped to a 30s cut, produced in phase 3 only after the
  conversion gate is passed, positioned as a retention/pride asset rather than the lead
- All creative carries the legally required helmet-recommendation message (lockup supplied)

## 5. Media plan: sequenced, gated

1. Weeks 1–3: fix the leak — rainy-day experience messaging + pause-plan CRM to month-1 cohort
2. Weeks 4–9: conversion layer — fare-calculator retargeting, first-month ¥2,490 offer
3. Brand film + broad reach only if paid conversion exceeds 14% by week 6

## 6. KPIs

- Paid subscribers: 18,000 → 30,000 by end of November
- Registered-to-paid conversion: 9% → 16%
- Month-2 churn: 31% → 18%
