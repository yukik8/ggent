# KURO Latte Launch Proposal (final v2)

- Requirement: R1 / Client: Hoshino Beverage Co.
- Author: Riku Tanaka (Planner) / Revised: Ayaka Sato (Executive Strategy Director)
- Date: 2025-08-25
- Status: presented to client — SELECTED

## 1. The problem we are solving

Hoshino is asking a ¥248 question: why would anyone pay 1.8x the standard canned-coffee
price at the same shelf? The RTD coffee category grew only 1.9% YoY, but the premium
segment (¥200+) grew 14.3% and now holds 11% of category value. Trial of premium cans
is driven by *justification*, not desire: shoppers need a reason the price is fair.

**Problem statement: KURO doesn't have an awareness problem — it has a price-justification
problem at the shelf.**

## 2. Target: one segment, deliberately narrow

**Primary: urban office workers 25–34 who already buy ¥500+ coffee-shop lattes 3+ times
a week** (est. 2.1M people in Tokyo/Osaka/Nagoya). For them ¥248 is not an expensive
can — it is a 50% cheaper latte. The price objection inverts into the value proposition.

**Who we are NOT targeting (and why):**
- Standard canned-coffee loyalists (price-anchored at ¥140; converting them needs a
  price war the client explicitly refused)
- Students / "treat" buyers (low frequency; cannot carry a convenience-store velocity target)

## 3. Strategy

Reframe the reference price: position KURO against the ¥500 café latte, not the ¥140 can.
Message: **"Your café latte. ¥248. Same beans, no queue."**

## 4. Executions

- Comparison-led OOH within 50m of convenience stores in office districts (Marunouchi,
  Shinagawa, Umeda) — the decision happens at the shelf, so we advertise at the shelf
- 15s digital video: side-by-side brew transparency (bean origin, extraction) vs café latte
- Barista collaboration retained from draft — but scoped as *credibility evidence*
  (roast supervision), not celebrity reach
- "KURO Gallery" pop-up cut: ¥12M for reach that does not touch the target's daily path

## 5. Media plan: sequenced, gated

1. Weeks 1–2: proof assets only (brew transparency video, price-comparison OOH)
2. Weeks 3–8: conversion layer — convenience-store app coupons (first-can ¥50 off)
3. Broad awareness (TV) only if week-4 sell-through exceeds 1.8 cans/store/day

## 6. KPIs

- Sell-through: 2.2 cans/store/day by week 8 (category premium benchmark: 1.6)
- Aided recall in primary segment: 30% by end of December
- Repeat purchase within 4 weeks: 25%

## 7. Sources

- RTD Coffee Market Tracker 2025 (category & premium segment growth)
- Convenience Store Shopper Panel, Jul 2025 (price-anchoring behavior)
- Client clinic survey, Jun 2025 (price objection verbatims)
