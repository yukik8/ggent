# Email, CRM & Loyalty Marketing Benchmark 2026

**Source:** Meridian & Craft research desk analysis of the Kestrel Martech Benchmark Exchange, supplemented with anonymized send-level data licensed from the Lumenpost ESP consortium.

**Methodology:** Aggregated performance data from 4.1 billion US commercial email and SMS sends across 1,850 mid-market and enterprise brands, paired with a marketer survey of 640 CRM and lifecycle leads, fielded September through December 2025.

## The Retention Channel Landscape

Owned channels quietly became the profit engine of US consumer marketing while paid media costs climbed. The benchmark's clearest finding is a widening performance gap between automated lifecycle programs and traditional batch sending. Lifecycle-triggered emails average a 38% open rate, versus 21% for standard campaign sends. Brands in the top performance quartile now route more than half of their total email volume through triggered flows, up from roughly a third two years ago.

## Triggered Flows and Revenue Recovery

The revenue case for automation is no longer theoretical. Cart-abandonment and lapsed-buyer flows recover 5.9% of at-risk revenue on average. Recovery rates cluster tightly across verticals, with apparel and beauty brands landing slightly above the mean and grocery and household categories slightly below. The survey found that timing discipline matters more than creative polish, flows firing within the first hour of abandonment outperformed next-day sends by a wide margin.

## Loyalty Program Economics

Mature loyalty programs concentrate an outsized share of revenue in a small membership base. Loyalty members generate 42% of revenue at brands with mature programs while representing 18% of customers. Program maturity is the operative word, brands in their first year of a points program saw nothing close to that concentration. Marketers surveyed cited tier-based perks and early product access as the mechanics most correlated with sustained member spend.

## SMS as the Urgency Channel

SMS has settled into a distinct role rather than cannibalizing email. SMS opt-in subscribers convert at 2.4x the rate of email-only subscribers on time-limited offers. The channel's edge is concentrated in flash promotions, restock alerts, and appointment-style reminders, evergreen content sent by SMS performed no better than email and burned opt-outs faster. Brands that capped SMS frequency at four to six sends per month retained subscribers at nearly double the rate of heavier senders.

## Budget Allocation and ROI

The spend math increasingly favors the installed base. Median ROI for retention marketing is $4.60 per dollar spent, versus $2.20 for prospecting. Despite that gap, surveyed marketers still allocate the majority of budget to acquisition, a mismatch most attribute to growth targets set on new-customer counts rather than contribution margin. CRM leads who report ROI to finance in margin terms were markedly more likely to have won retention budget increases for 2026.

## Outlook

Expect triggered-flow coverage and loyalty tiering to become table stakes through 2026, with the competitive frontier shifting to cross-channel orchestration between email, SMS, and app push. Brands that treat retention as a measured profit center rather than a support function will keep compounding the ROI gap over acquisition-first rivals.
