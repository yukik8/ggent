# Social Media Advertising Benchmark Report 2025

**Source:** Meridian & Craft research desk compilation of the Wexford Digital paid-social benchmark pool, covering US campaigns across all major verticals.

**Methodology:** Aggregated performance telemetry from 3,900 US paid-social campaigns run between January and December 2024, normalized by vertical, objective, and audience size.

## Platform Cost Landscape

Short-form video placements remain the workhorse of paid social, and costs have stabilized after two volatile years. Instagram Reels average CPM: $9.40; TikTok: $7.80; YouTube Shorts: $6.90 (US, all verticals). The spread narrows for older audiences, where Reels' premium is justified by stronger reach, and widens for under-25 targeting, where TikTok delivers both lower cost and higher engagement density. Static feed and stories placements sit above all three on an effective-attention basis despite comparable sticker prices.

## Format Performance

The format decision matters more than the platform decision. Short-form video ads average 2.4x the completion rate of static feed ads, and completion correlates strongly with downstream recall in the pool's brand-lift subset. Videos that establish the premise inside the first two seconds account for most of the outperformance, hook quality explains more variance in completion than production budget does.

## The Creator Advantage

Who posts the ad changes its economics. Creator-partnership posts deliver 31% lower cost-per-engagement than brand-owned posts running identical offers. The advantage is largest for creators with high niche credibility rather than raw follower count, and it persists even when the creator content is boosted as paid media rather than running organically. Brand-owned posts close some of the gap when they adopt creator-style native formats, but never all of it.

## Click-Through Reality

Benchmark expectations should stay sober. The average click-through rate for paid social across verticals is 1.2%; top-quartile campaigns reach 2.6%. The top quartile is not distinguished by bigger budgets. It is distinguished by tighter audience definition, evidence-led creative, and offer-message match. Campaigns pairing a specific claim with visible proof outperformed generic brand messaging at every spend level in the pool.

## Frequency Discipline

More exposure is not better past a clear threshold. Frequency above 4 exposures per week correlates with a 18% drop in engagement rate, and the decay steepens beyond six exposures. Frequency caps at three to four per week preserved engagement while sacrificing little reach in the pool's controlled tests, making capping one of the cheapest optimizations available.

## Outlook

Expect short-form video CPMs to drift up modestly in 2025, creator partnerships to keep their cost advantage, and frequency discipline to separate efficient plans from wasteful ones.
