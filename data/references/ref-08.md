# US Retail Media Networks Study 2025

**Source:** Meridian & Craft research desk analysis of the Calder Commerce Media Index, with campaign-level performance data contributed by 85 CPG advertisers.

**Methodology:** Ad-spend aggregation across the 12 largest US retail media networks, paired with matched-store sales-lift measurement on 640 grocery campaigns run during 2024.

## The Fastest-Growing Channel in Paid Media

Retail media has moved from experiment to pillar. US retail media ad spend reached $54B in 2024, growing 21% YoY, the fastest-growing paid channel, outpacing paid social, search, and connected TV. The growth is structural rather than cyclical, retailers are monetizing first-party purchase data at exactly the moment third-party targeting signals are degrading elsewhere, and brand budgets are following the measurability.

## Grocery Is the Proving Ground

For food and beverage brands specifically, the returns are unusually legible. Grocery retail media campaigns average ROAS of 3.8x for snack and beverage brands, measured on matched-store incrementality rather than platform-reported attribution. Snack brands running retail media alongside physical in-store activity (sampling, end-caps) saw returns at the top of the distribution, suggesting the channel amplifies rather than replaces shopper-marketing fundamentals.

## On-Site Placement Effects

The workhorse format delivers dependable volume lift. On-site sponsored product placements drive 15% average lift in units per store per week for the featured SKU during the flight, with the effect strongest for new items and seasonal packs that lack organic search history on the retailer's site. Lift decays after the flight ends but leaves a residual baseline gain of a few points where the product earned repeat buyers during the campaign.

## The Price of Precision

Retail media is not cheap on a raw impression basis. Retail media CPMs average $12.60 for grocery, versus $9.40 for Instagram Reels, a premium of roughly a third. Advertisers in the index overwhelmingly judged the premium worthwhile because the impressions land at the point of purchase against verified category buyers, and because closed-loop sales measurement removes the attribution guesswork that inflates social media's apparent efficiency.

## Budget Intentions

The channel's momentum shows no sign of stalling. 68% of CPG brands plan to increase retail media budgets in 2026, with holiday and seasonal windows cited as the first flights to be upweighted. Most of the incremental budget is being reallocated from linear TV and untargeted digital display rather than from social or search.

## Outlook

Expect continued 20%-range growth, deeper integration between on-site placements and in-store activation, and rising pressure on brands to treat retail media as a planned pillar rather than a trade-spend afterthought.
