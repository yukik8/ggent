# Gen-Z Consumer Pulse Survey 2025 (n=4,000, US 18-27)

**Source:** Meridian & Craft research desk fielding of the annual Gen-Z Consumer Pulse, conducted with panel partner Novara Research.

**Methodology:** Online survey of 4,000 US respondents aged 18-27, census-balanced on region, gender, and household income, fielded November 2024, with a qualitative follow-up of 60 in-depth interviews.

## Discovery Runs Through Short-Form Video

For this cohort, brand discovery has consolidated onto one format. 71% of Gen-Z respondents discover new brands primarily through short-form video, dwarfing search (11%), static social (9%), and every traditional channel combined. In the interview follow-ups, respondents described scrolling as their default idle behavior, meaning brands are discovered in a low-intent, high-volume context where a distinctive first two seconds decides everything.

## The Evidence Requirement

Gen-Z's relationship with advertising claims is best described as forensic. 58% say they distrust ads that make claims without visible evidence or numbers. Vague superlatives ("game-changing," "best-in-class") actively harm credibility with this group, while specific figures, before-and-after demonstrations, and named third-party sources raise it. Several interviewees reported screenshotting claims to verify them later, and treating unverifiable claims as a reason to exclude a brand entirely.

## Small Creators, Big Influence

Influence in this cohort does not scale with follower count. 64% have purchased a product recommended by a niche creator with under 100K followers, and respondents consistently rated small-creator recommendations as more trustworthy than celebrity or mega-influencer endorsements. Perceived independence is the mechanism, the smaller the creator, the less the recommendation reads as bought.

## Messaging That Opens the Door

First-touch messaging strategy matters enormously. Only 22% respond to discount-led messaging as a first touch; 49% respond to problem-solution storytelling. Discounts are not useless, they perform well as a closing device once interest exists, but leading with price signals low confidence in the product and attracts one-time deal hunters rather than repeat buyers. Story-first, discount-later sequencing outperformed the reverse order in every product category tested.

## The Verification Habit

Purchases above a modest threshold trigger active research. Gen-Z respondents check an average of 2.7 sources before a first purchase over $50, typically a mix of creator reviews, review-site scores, and comment sections under the brand's own posts. Brands with a thin or inconsistent footprint across those checkpoints lose buyers at the verification stage without ever knowing it.

## Outlook

Expect the evidence requirement to intensify. As AI-generated content floods feeds, this cohort's default skepticism is rising, and verifiable, numeric, creator-corroborated claims will increasingly be the price of entry.
