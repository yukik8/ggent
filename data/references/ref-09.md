# US Premium & Fresh Pet Food Stat Pack 2025

**Source:** Meridian & Craft research desk syndication of the Barlow Pet Economy annual tracker, supplemented with cold-chain retail data licensed from ChillShelf Metrics.

**Methodology:** Retail scanner data from 9,800 US grocery and pet specialty outlets combined with a longitudinal purchase panel of 18,500 pet-owning households, fielded January through December 2024.

## Category Overview

Pet food continues to outpace most of US grocery, and the premium end is doing the heavy lifting. US pet food retail sales reached $58.1B in 2024, with fresh/refrigerated the fastest-growing segment at 17% YoY. The broader category grew in the low single digits, so nearly all incremental dollars are flowing into premium and fresh formats. Legacy kibble lines held volume but ceded dollar share as households traded up.

## The Ingredient-First Buyer

The humanization of pet food is no longer a niche behavior, it is the default posture of younger owners. 63% of dog owners under 40 rank ingredient quality above price when choosing a food brand. These buyers read ingredient panels the way they read their own groceries, and panel diaries show them cross-shopping fresh pet food against their own meal-kit subscriptions rather than against bagged kibble.

## Subscription Economics and Churn

The direct-to-consumer fresh model wins trial easily but struggles to keep it. The average fresh pet food subscriber churns after 4.2 months; top-quartile brands retain subscribers past 9 months. The gap between the average and the top quartile is the single largest economic lever in the segment, since acquisition costs for fresh subscribers run well above the category norm. Brands that survive the churn cliff typically do so with flexible portioning and pause-friendly plans rather than discounting.

## Discovery Runs Through Social Video

Traditional pet aisle marketing is losing its grip on the under-40 cohort. 57% of pet owners under 40 discover new pet brands through social video featuring real pets. Authenticity is the operative word, panel respondents consistently rated owner-shot footage of actual dogs eating the product as more persuasive than polished brand creative, and creator posts outperformed brand-account posts on recalled brand names.

## The Fridge as a Marketing Channel

Physical placement still matters, and in fresh pet food it matters in a very specific way. In-fridge grocery placement lifts brand consideration by 22% among shoppers who already buy premium dog food. Sitting next to human fresh food borrows credibility that no package claim can manufacture. Retailers that installed branded pet fridges near the produce or prepared-foods sections saw the strongest effect, while placements buried in the pet aisle underperformed.

## Outlook

Expect fresh and refrigerated formats to keep compounding through 2025 as cold-chain capacity expands in mainstream grocery. The winners will be brands that pair social-video discovery with fridge presence in-store, and that engineer their subscription experience to push retention past the 4.2-month average toward the 9-month benchmark set by the top quartile.
