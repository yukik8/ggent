# US Fast-Casual Dining Stat Pack 2025

**Source:** Meridian & Craft research desk syndication of the Corner Booth Foodservice Monitor, supplemented with transaction data licensed from PlateCount Analytics.

**Methodology:** POS transaction data from 9,400 US fast-casual locations combined with a quarterly diner survey of 18,500 adults, fielded January through December 2024.

## Category Overview

Fast casual continues to be the growth engine of US dining out, pulling share from both quick service and sit-down formats. US fast-casual sales reached $209B in 2024, growing 8.2% YoY while full-service restaurants grew 2.4%. Traffic gains were broad-based across dayparts, though lunch remains the anchor, accounting for just under half of transactions in the panel. Growth is no longer limited to coastal metros, with the fastest unit expansion recorded in suburban trade areas of the Southeast and Mountain West.

## Digital Discovery Comes First

For younger diners, the first visit now starts on a screen. 74% of diners aged 18-34 check a chain's app or social feed before first visiting a new location. In the survey, a stale or sparse feed was cited as a reason to skip a first visit almost as often as negative reviews. Menu photography and store-specific content, hours, opening announcements, local events, outperformed generic brand creative in driving stated visit intent.

## Loyalty Economics

The loyalty math in fast casual is unusually stark. Loyalty-app members visit fast-casual chains 2.3x more often than non-members and spend 31% more per order. Enrollment velocity in the first 90 days after a location opens is the strongest single predictor of that store's year-one sales trajectory in the panel. Chains that pushed enrollment at the register and tied a first-visit reward to sign-up filled their member base fastest.

## The Digital Order Mix

Off-premise and app-driven ordering has become structural rather than cyclical. Digital orders average 38% of fast-casual revenue; top-quartile chains exceed 55%. High digital mix correlates with larger average checks, driven by attachment of sides and beverages that in-line ordering tends to miss. Kitchens designed around a dedicated digital make-line handled peak-hour digital volume with materially fewer order errors than retrofit layouts.

## Winning New Markets

National media alone does not open a market. Local-store marketing tied to a new-market opening lifts first-quarter traffic by 26% versus national-only support. The most effective openings in the panel combined a community pre-opening event, partnerships with nearby employers and schools, and geo-targeted app-download offers in a three-mile radius. Openings supported only by national campaigns took roughly two additional quarters to reach the same traffic level.

## Outlook

Expect fast casual to keep outgrowing the broader restaurant industry through 2025, with digital mix and loyalty penetration widening the gap between top-quartile operators and the rest. New-market entrants should budget for local activation as a core line item, not an afterthought, and treat app enrollment in the first 90 days as the leading indicator to manage.
