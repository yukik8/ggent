# Urban Micromobility Stat Pack 2025

**Source:** Meridian & Craft research desk summary of the Beaumont Mobility Monitor annual report, with city-level infrastructure data from the Urban Transit Futures Lab.

**Methodology:** Manufacturer and dealer sales reporting across 46 US markets, combined with a commuter survey of 5,200 urban workers aged 18-55 fielded in fall 2024.

## Market Momentum

The US e-bike market has moved decisively past the early-adopter phase. US e-bike unit sales hit 1.4M in 2024, a 12% YoY increase, making e-bikes the fastest-growing personal vehicle category in the country for the third straight year. Growth is strongest not in the largest coastal metros but in mid-size cities, where commute distances and parking costs make the value case most legible.

## The Price Barrier

The single biggest obstacle to broader adoption is sticker shock. 68% of urban commuters aged 25-40 say upfront price is the main barrier to buying an e-bike, far ahead of concerns about weather (14%) or theft (11%). Notably, willingness to pay on a monthly basis is much healthier, the same respondents cluster around an acceptable range of $75-$100 per month when the cost is framed as replacing car expenses rather than buying a bicycle.

## Fit With Real Commutes

The use case is well matched to the product's capabilities. Average commute distance in mid-size US cities is 7.8 miles, within a single e-bike battery charge. Range anxiety, a dominant theme in early e-bike research, has faded as a stated concern among commuters who have test-ridden a modern model, though it remains a barrier among those who have never ridden one.

## The Rise of Subscription Models

Payment innovation is reshaping how the category is bought. Subscription and financing models grew to 21% of e-bike transactions in 2024, from 9% in 2022. Battery-swap and battery-as-a-service offerings are the fastest-growing sub-segment, since they address both the upfront price barrier and long-term battery degradation worries in a single proposition. The Monitor projects subscription-linked transactions could approach a third of the market by 2027 if current growth holds.

## Infrastructure as a Demand Multiplier

Adoption is heavily conditioned by the built environment. Cities with protected bike lanes show 2.3x higher e-bike adoption than comparable cities without. For marketers, this makes market selection as important as message, the same campaign delivers materially different conversion economics depending on lane infrastructure, and city-level targeting outperforms national buys.

## Outlook

Expect the category's center of gravity to keep shifting toward mid-size cities, monthly payment framing, and infrastructure-selected markets through 2025 and 2026.
