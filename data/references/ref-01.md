# US Salty Snacks Category Stat Pack 2025

**Source:** Meridian & Craft research desk syndication of the Hargrove Consumer Insights annual category tracker, supplemented with grocery panel data licensed from ShelfScan Analytics.

**Methodology:** Retail scanner data from 14,200 US grocery outlets combined with a longitudinal purchase panel of 22,000 households, fielded January through December 2024.

## Category Overview

The salty snacks category remains one of the most resilient corners of US grocery, but the ground underneath mid-market brands is shifting. The US salty snacks retail market valued at $34.2B in 2024, growing 3.1% YoY. That headline growth masks a redistribution of share rather than a rising tide for all players. Growth is concentrated at two poles, the top national brands with heavy media support, and value-oriented private label lines.

## Private Label Pressure

The squeeze on the middle is quantifiable. Private-label share of salty snacks rose from 18% to 23% between 2021 and 2024, a five-point swing that came almost entirely at the expense of mid-market branded players rather than the top-3 nationals. Retailer store brands have closed the perceived quality gap, and in blind taste panels conducted alongside the tracker, 41% of respondents could not distinguish a leading private-label crisp from a mid-market branded equivalent.

## Brand Salience Among Younger Buyers

Awareness is the mid-market's weakest link. Only 27% of snack buyers aged 18-34 can name a preferred mid-market crisp brand unprompted. For the top-3 national brands, unaided recall in the same cohort exceeds 80%. Younger buyers describe the mid-market shelf as "a blur of bags," and flavor innovation alone has not been enough to create durable memory structures.

## Loyalty and Repeat Behavior

The loyalty picture reinforces the salience problem. Repeat purchase rate for the average mid-market snack brand is 31%, versus 52% for the top-3 national brands. Panel data shows mid-market brands win plenty of first purchases, often on promotion or novelty flavors, but fail to convert trial into habit. Households that repurchase a mid-market crisp brand at least three times in six months represent under 9% of category buyers.

## What Moves the Needle

Physical trial remains the most reliable conversion lever the tracker measured. In-store sampling lifts trial conversion by 19% on average in grocery channels, outperforming coupon drops and end-cap displays on a cost-per-converted-buyer basis. Sampling paired with a distinctive flavor claim performed best, particularly for spicy and bold-flavor sub-lines, where trial-to-repeat rates ran roughly 6 points above the category norm.

## Outlook

Expect continued private-label encroachment through 2025 as grocers expand premium store-brand tiers. Mid-market brands that survive will be those that build unaided recall in the 18-34 cohort and design launch plans around physical trial rather than shelf presence alone.
