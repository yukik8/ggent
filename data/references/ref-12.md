# US Online Upskilling Stat Pack 2026

**Source:** Meridian & Craft research desk syndication of the Fairbanks Workforce Learning Monitor, supplemented with enrollment funnel data licensed from CourseGraph Analytics.

**Methodology:** Survey of 18,500 US adults aged 22-55 who purchased or considered an online learning product in the prior 12 months, combined with platform-side enrollment and completion data from 31 providers, fielded February through November 2025.

## Market Overview

Consumer spending on online upskilling keeps climbing even as sentiment about the products themselves cools. The US consumer upskilling market reached $13.7B in 2025, growing 11% YoY. Growth is broad across categories, with data, AI, and healthcare-adjacent credentials leading, but the monitor finds that spend is increasingly concentrated among repeat buyers purchasing their second or third program rather than first-time learners entering the market.

## The Guidance Gap

The single biggest friction point in the funnel is not price, it is direction. 66% of career changers aged 25-40 say they cannot identify which skills would actually raise their salary. Respondents in this group browse an average of 11 course pages before abandoning a purchase decision, and open-ended responses describe the catalog experience as "a wall of certificates with no map." Providers that offer a structured skills-assessment step before checkout see meaningfully lower cart abandonment than catalog-first competitors.

## Completion and Format

Format is the strongest predictor of whether a learner finishes what they buy. Completion rates average 12% for self-paced online courses versus 68% for cohort-based programs. The gap holds across price tiers and subject areas. Cohort learners cite scheduled sessions and peer accountability as the reasons they stayed, while self-paced dropouts most often stall within the first two weeks. Notably, learners who complete any program are about three times as likely to buy another within a year.

## What Converts in Acquisition

Outcome specificity beats aspiration in paid media. Ads featuring a specific salary-outcome statistic drive 2.1x higher free-trial starts than generic skill-building ads. The monitor's creative testing panel found that concrete dollar figures and named job titles outperform phrases like "future-proof your career" at every funnel stage measured. The effect is strongest among the 25-40 career-changer segment, precisely the group reporting the most confusion about which skills pay.

## The Employer Funding Leak

A large pool of funding sits untouched. Only 38% of employees eligible for employer tuition reimbursement used any of it in 2025. Non-users most commonly cite unclear approval processes and uncertainty about which programs qualify. Providers that handle reimbursement paperwork on the learner's behalf report noticeably higher enrollment from corporate-eligible buyers, suggesting the leak is an administrative problem more than a demand problem.

## Outlook

Expect the guidance gap to become the main competitive battleground through 2026. Providers that pair cohort-based delivery with concrete salary-outcome messaging, and that make employer reimbursement effortless to tap, are positioned to capture share from catalog-style incumbents whose self-paced libraries continue to bleed completions.
