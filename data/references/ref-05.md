# Regional US Tourism Stat Pack 2025

**Source:** Meridian & Craft research desk summary of the Palefield Travel Insights regional destinations report, with visitor-spend data from the State Tourism Data Cooperative.

**Methodology:** Lodging and attraction data from 118 small US regional destinations, combined with a traveler survey of 7,400 US adults who took at least one leisure trip in 2024.

## The Drive-Market Core

Small regional destinations live and die by travelers within tank-of-gas range. Drive-market weekend trips make up 61% of visits to small US regional destinations, and that share has grown modestly since 2022 as travelers substitute shorter, more frequent getaways for single long-haul vacations. The practical planning window is short, most drive-market weekend trips are booked less than three weeks out, which rewards always-on presence over long-lead campaign bursts.

## The Shoulder-Season Gap

The economic problem for regional destination marketers is concentrated in two windows. Shoulder-season (Apr-May, Sep-Oct) occupancy for regional destinations averages 46%, versus 78% peak-season. That 32-point gap represents the largest recoverable revenue opportunity most regional boards have, since the lodging, dining, and activity infrastructure is already built and staffed. Destinations that have narrowed the gap did so with event anchors and weather-resilient itinerary content rather than discounting.

## Short-Form Video as the Decision Engine

For younger travelers, the trip decision increasingly starts on a phone. 54% of travelers under 35 pick regional trips based on short-form video seen in the prior 30 days. The survey found the influence is strongest when the video shows a specific, replicable itinerary, a named trail, a named restaurant, a named viewpoint, rather than generic scenery montages. Creator-posted content outperformed destination-brand-posted content on trip intent by a meaningful margin.

## What a Visitor Is Worth

The revenue math per converted visitor is healthy. The average regional weekend visitor spends $412 per trip across lodging, food, and activities, with roughly half going to lodging. Visitors who arrive with a pre-planned itinerary sourced from social content spend about 15% more than spontaneous visitors, largely on ticketed activities.

## Always-On Beats Bursts

Publishing cadence turns out to be a loyalty lever, not just an acquisition one. Destinations running always-on social content see 27% higher repeat visitation than campaign-only destinations. Continuous content keeps the destination in the consideration set during the short booking windows that drive-market travelers actually use, whereas burst campaigns evaporate between flights.

## Outlook

Expect shoulder-season recovery, creator-led short-form video, and always-on publishing to remain the three highest-leverage plays for regional destination marketing through 2025 and 2026.
