# Clean Beauty & Skincare Stat Pack 2025

**Source:** Meridian & Craft research desk compilation drawing on the Lumen Beauty Index annual report and a proprietary DTC benchmarking pool operated by Cartwright Metrics.

**Methodology:** Sales data aggregated from 340 US beauty brands (DTC and retail), paired with a consumer survey of 6,500 US skincare buyers fielded in Q4 2024.

## Segment Growth

Clean beauty continues to outgrow the broader prestige market by a wide margin. The US clean-beauty segment reached $11.6B in 2024, up 8.4% YoY while total prestige skincare grew only 2.9%. The gap has widened for three consecutive years, and clean-positioned launches now account for a majority of new SKU introductions tracked by the index. The label "clean" itself is becoming table stakes, which is pushing differentiation toward proof, clinical framing, ingredient sourcing stories, and visible transparency.

## The Transparency Generation

Younger buyers are driving the shift. 62% of skincare buyers under 30 say ingredient transparency is a top-3 purchase driver, ranking it above price and above brand familiarity in the survey's forced-choice module. This cohort routinely cross-references ingredient lists against third-party databases before purchase, and 37% report having abandoned a cart specifically because an ingredient claim could not be verified on the product page.

## The Retention Problem

Acquisition is not where clean-beauty DTC brands are bleeding. Retention is. The average DTC skincare brand loses 44% of first-time buyers before a second order. Exit surveying within the benchmarking pool points to unremarkable reorder experiences, out-of-stock hero SKUs, and weak post-purchase communication as the leading causes, rather than product dissatisfaction. Brands with structured replenishment programs cut that first-order loss materially.

## Where Discovery Happens

Channel dynamics are moving against the pure-DTC model. Specialty retail (e.g. beauty chains) accounts for 38% of clean-beauty discovery, versus 24% for brand-owned sites. Shoppers treat the specialty shelf as a curation layer, being stocked there functions as a credibility signal that no amount of brand-owned content fully replicates. Social discovery fills most of the remaining share, led by short-form video and creator reviews.

## The Moisturizer Anchor

Among skincare categories, moisturizer is the strongest loyalty anchor. Moisturizer is the highest-frequency skincare repurchase category at 3.4 units per buyer per year, ahead of cleanser and well ahead of serums. Brands built around a moisturizer franchise therefore have an unusually strong structural base for subscription and replenishment economics, provided they survive the first-to-second-order gap.

## Outlook

Expect clean beauty's premium over the broader market to persist through 2025, with winners defined less by claims and more by verifiable transparency, specialty-retail presence, and disciplined retention mechanics around high-frequency categories like moisturizer.
