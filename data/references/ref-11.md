# US Connected Fitness Stat Pack 2026

**Source:** Meridian & Craft research desk syndication of the Pinewood Home Fitness annual tracker, supplemented with device telemetry benchmarks licensed from CadenceIQ Analytics.

**Methodology:** Point-of-sale data from 6,400 US specialty and big-box fitness retailers combined with anonymized usage telemetry from 1.4M connected devices and a survey panel of 18,500 households, fielded January through December 2025.

## Category Overview

After two bruising post-pandemic years of discounting and inventory gluts, the connected fitness category has found its footing. US connected fitness equipment sales returned to growth in 2025, up 6% YoY to $4.8B. The recovery is uneven, though. Premium bikes remain below their 2021 peak, while lower-ticket strength and rowing hardware carried most of the rebound, and the average selling price across the category fell 8% as brands repositioned toward accessible entry points.

## The Hesitation Problem

The category's biggest obstacle is psychological, not financial. 71% of connected-fitness intenders cite fear of unused equipment as their top purchase hesitation. That fear outranks price, space constraints, and subscription fatigue in the survey panel, and it is strongest among first-time buyers who have never owned home equipment. Intenders repeatedly referenced a "clothes rack" image of abandoned treadmills, a cultural meme that brands have largely failed to neutralize with warranty or buy-back messaging alone.

## The First-Month Habit Window

Telemetry data makes the retention story unusually clear. Members completing 8 or more workouts in their first month show 81% 12-month retention, versus 34% for those completing fewer. The first thirty days function as a habit gate, and almost nothing a brand does after day 30 moves the retention curve as much as what happens before it. Onboarding programs that schedule the first three workouts at purchase, rather than leaving activation to the member, pushed a meaningfully larger share of new members over the 8-workout threshold.

## Trial as the Conversion Unlock

Trial mechanics dominate the conversion data. Free in-home trial programs convert at 46%, versus 11% for standard e-commerce checkout. The gap holds even after adjusting for the self-selection of trial requesters, and returned units from trial programs ran under a fifth of what operators had budgeted for. Showroom test rides sit between the two figures but require footprint costs that in-home programs avoid.

## Modality Shift

Not all hardware is recovering equally. Rowing is the fastest-growing connected-fitness modality at 19% YoY unit growth. Rowing's appeal in the panel skews toward buyers over 40 seeking low-impact full-body work, and it benefits from a smaller storage footprint than treadmills. Bikes and treadmills grew in the low single digits, while connected strength systems posted solid but slower gains off a larger base.

## Outlook

Expect the category to keep growing modestly through 2026, with share flowing toward brands that treat the purchase-hesitation problem and the first-month habit window as one connected funnel. Winners will pair in-home trial offers with structured onboarding that gets new members past eight workouts before the box has finished flattening in the recycling bin.
