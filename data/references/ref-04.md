# Mid-Market B2B SaaS Buying Stat Pack 2025

**Source:** Meridian & Craft research desk digest of the Corestone B2B Buying Behavior Study and paid-media benchmarks from the Fenwick Digital Advertising Pool.

**Methodology:** Interviews and buying-journey reconstructions with 1,850 finance decision-makers at US companies of 50-500 employees, plus campaign telemetry from 210 B2B SaaS advertisers, collected across 2024.

## How Shortlists Get Built

The mid-market finance software buying journey now starts long before a vendor knows it exists. 73% of mid-market finance software evaluations now start with a shortlist built from peer review sites. Buyers arrive at first contact with two to four vendors already ranked, and vendors absent from the review-site consideration set rarely recover. Analyst reports and peer referrals fill out the picture, but review platforms are the dominant gate.

## Deal Velocity

Sales cycles in this segment are long enough that demand programs must be planned in quarters, not weeks. The average AP automation deal cycle for 50-500 employee firms is 94 days from first qualified conversation to signature. Cycles stretch past 120 days when procurement involves a parent company or when the buyer is replacing an incumbent tool rather than automating for the first time.

## The Pain Is Real and Measurable

The underlying problem AP automation solves remains largely unsolved in the segment. Finance teams report 62% of invoice processing time is still manual at companies without AP automation, concentrated in data entry, approval chasing, and exception handling at month-end. Controllers in the interview pool described month-end close as the single most resented block of work on their calendar, which makes time-reclaimed framing unusually potent in messaging.

## Media Cost Reality

Reaching this audience is expensive and getting more so. The LinkedIn B2B benchmark CPM for finance decision-makers is $58, versus $31 for general B2B targeting, an 87% premium that has held steady for two years. The premium punishes broad awareness plays and rewards tightly sequenced programs where expensive impressions are spent only on accounts showing intent signals.

## What Converts

Proof beats promises at the conversion layer. Case-study-led landing pages convert demo requests at 4.7%, versus 2.1% for feature-led pages, a gap that widens further when the case study names a company in the visitor's own industry. Pages leading with named customers, quantified time savings, and implementation timelines outperformed every feature-forward variant tested in the pool.

## Outlook

Expect review-site presence, patience-adjusted pipeline math built around the 94-day cycle, and evidence-led conversion assets to define winning mid-market finance SaaS programs through 2025.
