# Persona: Derek, the Dog-Dad Optimizer

**Client:** Pawstead Fresh (c-06, pet care)

## Snapshot
- **Name:** Derek Osei
- **Age:** 34
- **Occupation:** Senior product analyst at a Chicago fintech, hybrid schedule (2 days in office)
- **Location:** Logan Square, Chicago, walks his dog past a Mariano's and a Jewel-Osco daily

## Who he is
Derek adopted Biscuit, a 45-pound lab-terrier mix, from a shelter three years ago, and the dog is unambiguously the center of his household. He reads pet food labels the way he reads earnings reports, cross-checking ingredient panels and googling anything he can't pronounce. He already pays for premium kibble and toppers, so the fresh-food pitch makes sense to him on paper, quality first, price second. But he lives in a one-bedroom apartment with one fridge, splits it with a partner who meal-preps, and tracks his spending in a budgeting app. Any subscription that hogs shelf space or creeps past what feels justifiable gets an unsentimental cancel.

## Media diet
- Instagram Reels and YouTube daily, follows two vet creators and a handful of accounts starring real rescue dogs
- Lurks r/dogs and r/DogFood before any food switch, screenshots comment threads for later
- Listens to personal-finance and product podcasts on his commute
- Skips anything that feels like a brand ad, but will watch a real owner's "one month feeding fresh" video to the end

## Buying triggers
- A vet or vet-tech creator explaining the formulation on camera, credentials visible, no script energy
- Seeing the brand in the grocery fridge aisle after seeing it on social, the physical fridge presence makes it feel like real food instead of a startup
- A clear per-day cost he can rationalize, around $3 a day reads as "one less coffee," while a big monthly number reads as a bill
- A trial or starter box that lets him test whether Biscuit actually finishes the bowl before committing

## Frustrations
- Fresh food packs that colonize half the fridge, his partner has vetoed subscriptions over shelf space before
- Vague "human-grade" and "vet-approved" claims with no named vet or ingredient math behind them
- Subscriptions that make pausing or resizing a plan harder than canceling, he has rage-quit services over dark patterns
- Price increases that arrive as a quiet email in month four, right when the novelty has worn off

## What staying subscribed looks like
Derek is exactly the guy who churns around month four, not because Biscuit stopped loving the food but because the fridge filled up or the card statement pinched. What keeps him past that cliff is flexibility, a plan he can downshift to half-fresh half-kibble, delivery he can nudge by a week from his phone, and the sense that the brand knows Biscuit specifically. Once he crosses six months, he becomes the friend who evangelizes it at the dog park.

## Quote
"I'll pay more for something I can verify. What I won't do is fight my own fridge for a subscription that treats a pause request like a hostage negotiation."
