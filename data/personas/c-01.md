# Persona: Maya, the Snack-Drawer Curator

**Client:** Sunbrook Foods (c-01, food & beverage)

## Snapshot
- **Name:** Maya Kowalski
- **Age:** 27
- **Occupation:** Marketing coordinator at a Columbus logistics firm, hybrid schedule (3 days in office)
- **Location:** Columbus, Ohio metro, shops at Kroger and Meijer on weekends

## Who she is
Maya is the person friends text when they need a snack recommendation. Her desk drawer and kitchen cabinet hold a rotating cast of six or seven snacks at any time, and she treats the chip aisle like a discovery channel. She grazes between meals rather than eating big lunches, so a bag of crisps has to earn its place by being interesting, not just filling. She buys party snacks for game nights and holiday gatherings too, so multipacks matter to her a few times a year.

## Media diet
- Instagram Reels and TikTok daily, 60-90 minutes total, follows snack-review and "grocery haul" creators
- Skims r/snacks and r/Costco threads when a new flavor gets buzz
- Notices end-cap displays and sampling stations in-store, ignores TV and radio ads almost entirely
- Trusts niche creators with small followings more than brand accounts

## Buying triggers
- A flavor that sounds genuinely new (spicy, bold, limited batch) at a price she can gamble on, around $4 feels like a fair "trial fee"
- Seeing the same product twice, once on short-form video and once on shelf, within the same week
- In-store sampling, she almost always buys after tasting
- A sale on multipacks before hosting

## Frustrations
- Mid-market brands that taste identical to the store brand sitting next to them for $1 less
- "New flavor" launches that are just the old flavor with different bag art
- Ads that claim "bold" or "crafted" with nothing to back it up, she distrusts unproven claims
- Finding a flavor she loves, then never seeing it restocked

## What repurchase looks like
Maya tries roughly two new snacks a month but only about one in five makes it back into the drawer. The ones that stay are distinct enough that private label cannot imitate them, and she will name-drop them unprompted to friends.

## Quote
"I'll try anything once, but if it tastes like the Kroger version, why would I pay extra for the logo?"
