# Persona: Jordan, the Ingredient-Label Reader

**Client:** Velura Skin (c-02, cosmetics)

## Snapshot
- **Name:** Jordan Nguyen
- **Age:** 24
- **Occupation:** Junior UX designer at a Chicago health-tech startup
- **Location:** Chicago, rents with one roommate, disposable income modest but skincare is a protected budget line

## Who they are
Jordan treats skincare like a research project. Their camera roll has a folder of screenshotted ingredient lists, and before any purchase over $25 they check at least two or three sources, a creator review, an ingredient-checker app, and Reddit. They have sensitive, easily irritated skin, so fragrance-free is non-negotiable and "clean" claims get verified, not taken on faith. A moisturizer is their highest-frequency repurchase, three or four jars a year, which makes it the product they scrutinize hardest.

## Media diet
- TikTok and Instagram Reels are the primary discovery channels, follows dermatologists and niche skincare creators under 100K followers
- Uses INCIDecoder-style apps to decode labels in-aisle
- Reads r/SkincareAddiction before committing to anything new
- Increasingly browses specialty beauty retail stores in person to swatch and read packaging, discovery is shifting from brand sites to shelves
- Skips or mutes polished brand ads, especially ones making claims without visible evidence or numbers

## Buying triggers
- A creator they already trust showing real before-and-after results over weeks, not a single glowy clip
- A short, pronounceable ingredient list with the actives clearly explained
- Seeing the brand in a specialty retail chain, which reads as legitimacy
- A relaunch with a transparent "here is exactly what changed in the formula and why" story

## Frustrations
- Vague marketing words, "clean," "natural," "dermatologist-loved," with no specifics
- Brands that reformulate quietly and break a routine that was working
- A mediocre reorder experience, late shipping, a subscription that is hard to cancel, one bad touch and they quietly leave
- Discount-led emails as the first message after purchase, it feels like the brand thinks price is all they care about

## Quote
"Show me the ingredient list and the receipts. If I have to dig for what's actually in it, I'm already out."
