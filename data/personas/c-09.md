# Persona: Alicia, the Stuck Marketer

**Client:** Skillharbor (c-09, consumer edtech)

## Snapshot
- **Name:** Alicia Moreno
- **Age:** 31
- **Occupation:** Marketing manager at a regional insurance company, six years in, two title bumps but only one real raise
- **Location:** Charlotte, North Carolina suburbs, fully remote two days a week

## Who she is
Alicia is good at her job and quietly convinced it no longer matters. Her salary has crept up 3% a year while friends who jumped into analytics or product roles leapfrogged her, and she keeps a browser tab of job postings open the way other people keep a group chat open. The problem is not motivation, it is diagnosis. Every posting asks for a different stack of skills, SQL here, Tableau there, "growth analytics" somewhere else, and she genuinely cannot tell which one would move her paycheck versus which one is resume decoration. She has started three self-paced courses in two years and finished none of them, which she reads as a personal failing rather than a format problem.

## Media diet
- LinkedIn daily, half networking, half salary-envy doomscrolling, saves career-pivot posts she never reopens
- Reddit threads like r/careerguidance and r/marketing when deciding whether a skill is "worth it"
- Podcasts on her commute, career and money shows, skips ads unless a specific number catches her ear
- YouTube reviews and "is this bootcamp a scam" videos before spending anything over $100
- Ignores banner ads, but screenshots ads that name a concrete salary outcome

## Buying triggers
- A specific, sourced number, "graduates report a $14K average raise" stops her scroll where "future-proof your career" does not
- Proof that other people actually finish, a live cohort with a start date and classmates feels bindable in a way self-paced never did
- A clear skill-to-salary map for her exact situation, marketer wanting more money, not a generic catalog
- Discovering her employer's tuition reimbursement would cover part of it, money she knows she is leaving on the table
- A free trial or intro workshop that lets her test the teaching quality before the $2,400 conversation with her partner

## Frustrations
- Course catalogs with 400 options and zero guidance on which one changes her income
- "Learn in-demand skills" ads that never say which skills, for whom, or what they pay
- Her graveyard of three abandoned self-paced courses, she blames herself but resents the format
- Bootcamp testimonials that feel cherry-picked, one anonymous success story with no numbers reads as marketing, not evidence
- HR burying the tuition reimbursement policy three clicks deep, she only learned it existed from a coworker

## What enrollment looks like
Alicia researches for four to six weeks before paying anything, reading reviews, lurking in alumni threads, and pricing the decision against a vacation she would skip. She starts on the $199/month all-access tier as a low-risk test, but the moment she commits to a cohort with a start date and a Slack full of classmates, she becomes the persona Skillharbor keeps, she shows up, finishes, and posts her new title on LinkedIn with the program tagged.

## Quote
"I don't need another course. I need someone to tell me which skill is actually worth $15K more a year, and then make me finish it."
