# Persona: Tasha, the Lunch-Break Regular

**Client:** Blue Fork Kitchen (c-07, fast-casual dining)

## Snapshot
- **Name:** Tasha Reynolds
- **Age:** 26
- **Occupation:** Paralegal at a mid-size firm in uptown Charlotte, in office five days a week
- **Location:** Charlotte, North Carolina, works two blocks from a Blue Fork Kitchen and within a five-minute walk of a dozen lunch options

## Who she is
Tasha has exactly 45 minutes for lunch and treats them like a budget. She keeps a rotation of three go-to spots within walking distance of the office, and a place earns rotation status by being fast, consistent, and under $14 after tax. She orders ahead on apps almost every time, walking into a line without a mobile order feels like a failure of planning to her. She is the unofficial lunch coordinator for her pod of coworkers, which means when she switches a spot into or out of her rotation, three or four other orders tend to move with her. A bowl that looks good enough to post to her group chat is a real factor, half her rotation started because a coworker sent a photo.

## Media diet
- Instagram Stories and TikTok during her commute and lunch line, follows a couple of Charlotte food accounts and "what I eat in a workweek" creators
- Checks a restaurant's app, Instagram grid, and Google reviews before trying any new spot, and skips places with a dead social feed
- Reads the app push notifications she gets, but only acts on ones with a concrete deal or a new menu item
- Ignores billboards and radio, notices sidewalk signage and lunch-rush sampling tables

## Buying triggers
- A new bowl or limited seasonal ingredient that photographs well, bright colors and visible texture matter more than the menu description
- Loyalty points that are close to a reward, she will pick the spot where she is 1-2 visits from a free item
- Order-ahead that actually works, if the app says 12:15 and the bowl is on the shelf at 12:15, that spot moves up the rotation
- A coworker's photo or a local creator's post about a specific menu item she can order by name

## Frustrations
- Crossing the $14 line, a bowl that creeps to $16 with protein and tax quietly drops out of her rotation
- Apps that make her rebuild her usual order from scratch every time
- The 12:30 shelf pile-up, hunting for her name among thirty bags while walk-ins get their food first
- Portion roulette, when the same $13 bowl is generous on Tuesday and half-empty on Thursday she stops trusting the whole menu

## What rotation status looks like
Tasha eats out for lunch four days a week and gives a new spot two tries before deciding. Once a place makes her three-spot rotation she visits weekly and orders through the app nearly every time, but rotation status is rented, not owned. One bad wait or a price hike past her ceiling and the slot goes to whichever competitor has been showing up in her feed that month.

## Quote
"I don't need it to be exciting every day, I need it to be $13, ready when the app says it's ready, and good enough that I'm not embarrassed when the whole pod copies my order."
