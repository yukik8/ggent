# Persona: Sam, the Weekend Escape Planner

**Client:** Cascade Valley Tourism Board (c-05, regional tourism, public sector)

## Snapshot
- **Name:** Sam Whitaker
- **Age:** 29
- **Occupation:** High school teacher, predictable schedule, paid time compressed into weekends and school breaks
- **Location:** A metro area about 3 hours' drive from Cascade Valley, no car payment on a reliable hatchback

## Who they are
Sam is the trip-planner of their friend group and relationship. They take four to six weekend getaways a year, almost always drive-distance, and the decision usually starts with a 20-second video, a foggy trail, a farm-stand breakfast, a small-town festival, saved to a "go here" folder on their phone. From save to booking is fast, typically inside 30 days, because weekends fill up and Sam books before the impulse fades. Budget per trip lands around $350-450 for two people covering lodging, food, and one paid activity, so a $49 day pass or a $400 package sits exactly in their comfort zone. They actively prefer shoulder-season trips, fewer crowds, cheaper rooms, better photos.

## Media diet
- TikTok and Instagram Reels are the discovery engine, follows regional travel creators and "hidden gems within 3 hours of [city]" accounts
- Google Maps saved lists and reviews for the practical layer
- One or two travel newsletters, skimmed
- Rarely watches long-form travel content, never reads brochures, ignores banner ads

## Buying triggers
- Short-form video showing a specific, bookable experience, a trail plus a named cafe plus a place to stay, not generic scenery
- A clear itinerary they can copy, "your 36 hours in Cascade Valley"
- Off-peak pricing framed as the smart insider move
- A friend tagging them in a video, social planning starts in comments and DMs

## Frustrations
- Destination content that is all drone shots and no logistics, where do I park, what is open in October
- Finding out the featured spot is closed in shoulder season after arriving
- Clunky booking, if it takes more than a few taps they drift
- Tourism accounts that post only during peak campaigns then go silent

## Quote
"If I see it on my feed and can book it before Sunday night, we're going. If I have to research for an hour, we're not."
