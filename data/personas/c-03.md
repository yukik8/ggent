# Persona: Alex, the Car-Tired Commuter

**Client:** Northtrail Mobility (c-03, mobility)

## Snapshot
- **Name:** Alex Romero
- **Age:** 32
- **Occupation:** Insurance claims analyst, downtown office, in-person four days a week
- **Location:** Madison, Wisconsin, a mid-size city with a growing network of protected bike lanes

## Who he is
Alex lives 7.5 miles from his office, right in the range where driving feels wasteful and a regular bike feels sweaty. His household runs two cars, and the second one exists almost entirely for his commute, insurance, parking, gas, and a payment that together run well over $400 a month. He has test-ridden e-bikes twice and loved them both times. What stops him is the sticker, $1,899 upfront feels like a luxury purchase, even though he can do the math showing it beats the car. He is also quietly worried about battery degradation and being stranded with a dead pack in February.

## Media diet
- YouTube reviews and comparison videos, watches full 15-minute e-bike teardowns
- Reddit, r/ebikes and his city subreddit, where bike-lane debates and commuter testimonials run daily
- Instagram Reels casually, notices commuter-lifestyle content but does not seek it out
- Local news app for bike infrastructure announcements
- Podcasts on his current drive, ironically about personal finance

## Buying triggers
- A monthly number he can compare directly against his second-car cost, $89/month reads as "cheaper than parking alone"
- Battery-swap or service guarantees that remove range and degradation anxiety
- A new protected lane opening along his actual route
- A coworker or neighbor commuting by e-bike through a full winter, social proof from someone with his exact life

## Frustrations
- Upfront pricing that treats an e-bike like a gadget instead of a car replacement
- Spec-sheet marketing, watts and torque mean nothing to him, cost-per-month and reliability do
- Uncertainty about repairs, resale, and what the battery is worth in three years
- Feeling like e-bike brands market to cycling enthusiasts rather than to drivers who want out

## Quote
"I don't want a bike, I want to stop paying for a second car. Someone just needs to make that math easy for me."
