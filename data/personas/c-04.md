# Persona: Priya, the Drowning Controller

**Client:** Ledgerline (c-04, B2B SaaS)

## Snapshot
- **Name:** Priya Sharma
- **Age:** 41
- **Occupation:** Corporate Controller at a 200-employee regional healthcare services company
- **Location:** Charlotte, North Carolina, reports to a CFO who reports to a PE-adjacent board

## Who she is
Priya runs a five-person finance team that processes roughly 900 invoices a month, most of them still arriving as PDFs and paper. Month-end close eats ten days and her weekends. She knows AP automation exists, she has known for years, but every evaluation she starts gets buried under the next close cycle. When she does evaluate software, she is methodical, she builds a shortlist from peer review sites like G2, asks two controller friends what they use, and expects a 3-month decision process because procurement, IT security review, and her CFO all get a vote. A $24-30K annual contract is real money she has to defend line by line.

## Media diet
- LinkedIn daily, follows CFO-and-controller voices, saves posts about close-process improvements
- G2 and peer review sites are her true top of funnel, vendor websites come second
- Two finance-ops newsletters and a controllers' Slack community
- Webinars only if a peer company presents, never vendor-only pitches
- Does not watch short-form video for work, ignores display ads completely

## Buying triggers
- A case study from a company her size, in her industry, with named numbers, hours saved, error rates cut, days-to-close reduced
- A peer recommendation in her Slack community or at a conference
- A painful quarter, an audit finding, a duplicate payment, or losing an AP clerk she cannot replace
- Budget season, she buys when she can slot it into next fiscal year's plan

## Frustrations
- Feature-led pitches that list integrations instead of proving outcomes
- Demos that require three sales calls before showing the actual product
- Vendors that do not understand healthcare vendor compliance and payer complexity
- Being cold-emailed "quick question" sequences during month-end close

## Quote
"Don't tell me your features. Tell me which controller at a company like mine got her weekends back, and let me call her."
