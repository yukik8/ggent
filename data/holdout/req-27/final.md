## Opening: The Problem We Are Actually Solving

Northtrail has a price-barrier problem, not a demand problem. The category is healthy — US e-bike unit sales hit 1.4M in 2024, a 12% YoY increase (ref-03) — yet Northtrail's consumer funnel keeps stalling at checkout, because 68% of urban commuters aged 25-40 say upfront price is the main barrier to buying an e-bike (ref-03). One buyer can remove that barrier for hundreds of riders at once: the employer. The market is already moving this direction — subscription and financing models grew to 21% of e-bike transactions in 2024, from 9% in 2022 (ref-03) — and the employer-subsidized $75/month per employee rate takes the sticker shock off the individual entirely.

The Corporate Commuter-Benefit Program is our answer to that specific problem. It converts Northtrail's hardest sale (a four-figure consumer purchase) into its easiest one (a benefits line item), packaged as a turnkey benefit: subsidized subscription, unlimited battery-swap access, an HR fleet dashboard, full-service coverage, and turnkey rollout.

| Component | What It Includes |
|---|---|
| Subsidized subscription | $75/month per employee, employer co-funded |
| Battery-swap access | Unlimited swaps at all Northtrail network stations |
| Fleet dashboard | HR admin portal for enrollment, usage, and billing |
| Full-service coverage | Maintenance, theft insurance, and seasonal tune-ups |
| Turnkey rollout | On-site launch day, sizing/fitting, and safety session |

The geography is chosen by evidence, not preference: cities with protected bike lanes show 2.3x higher e-bike adoption than comparable cities without (ref-03), and the average commute distance in mid-size US cities is 7.8 miles, within a single e-bike battery charge (ref-03) — meaning the product fits the daily reality of the workforces we are pitching. Every tactic below is selected to move one number: signed corporate partnerships that route around the upfront-price barrier.

## Target Audience

Primary: HR and benefits leads at 200-2,000 employee companies in bike-lane cities — the buyers who can neutralize the 68% price-barrier objection (ref-03) with a single contract. These are peer-validated, evidence-driven purchasers: 73% of mid-market finance software evaluations now start with a shortlist built from peer review sites (ref-04), and mid-market benefits buying follows the same peer-first pattern, which is why this plan manufactures referenceable proof (Fleet Week reports, partner case studies) rather than relying on claims.

| Segment | Role | What They Care About |
|---|---|---|
| Primary | HR / Total Rewards leads | Utilization, employee satisfaction, easy admin |
| Influencer | Finance / procurement | Cost per employee, contract flexibility |
| Champion | Sustainability officers | Emissions reporting, ESG storytelling |

Employees themselves are a secondary audience — internal enthusiasm makes the HR buying decision much easier, so the campaign includes materials employers can share internally.

## The Big Idea

**"Commute Forward"** positions the Northtrail program as the one benefit employees can literally see arriving every morning. Because the core problem is that the individual purchase dies on upfront price, the centerpiece activation attacks it head-on: **Fleet Week**, a two-week on-site trial where Northtrail parks a ten-bike demo fleet at a prospect company, lets employees ride real commutes — commutes that average 7.8 miles in mid-size US cities, within a single e-bike battery charge (ref-03) — and hands HR a utilization report at the end. The employee never faces a sticker, HR gets utilization data instead of a vendor promise, and the buying committee gets the evidence it needs to sign.

## Channel & Media Plan

Each channel below maps directly to the price-barrier-and-proof problem named above.

### 1. LinkedIn Paid & Account-Based Marketing
Because the buyer is a specific title at a specific company size in specific cities, we pay the premium for precision rather than spraying general B2B inventory — the LinkedIn B2B benchmark CPM for finance decision-makers is $58, versus $31 for general B2B targeting (ref-04), and that premium is justified only because our addressable universe is narrow and named. ABM landing pages will be built around Fleet Week results and partner stories, since case-study-led landing pages convert demo requests at 4.7%, versus 2.1% for feature-led pages (ref-04) — more than double the yield from the same media spend.

### 2. HR Trade Media & Newsletter Sponsorships
Because mid-market buyers shortlist through peer and industry validation before ever meeting a vendor (73% start with peer-built shortlists in comparable mid-market categories, ref-04), we place sponsored articles and newsletter takeovers in the HR and total-rewards trade press. Thought-leadership content will lead with the commuter-cost problem and the 21%-and-rising subscription shift in the category (ref-03), positioning Northtrail as the vendor who understands the buying model, not just the bike.

### 3. Fleet Week On-Site Pilot Activations
The heart of the campaign, because it converts the named problem into evidence. Each pilot includes bike delivery, a launch-day event with fitting and safety orientation, swap-station orientation, and a closing utilization report for HR. The pilot works precisely because it removes the upfront-price objection that blocks 68% of the underlying commuter audience (ref-03) — nobody pays a sticker during Fleet Week — while generating the peer-proof artifacts the ABM and trade-media channels then amplify.

### 4. Webinars & Benefits Conference Presence
A quarterly webinar series featuring HR leaders from early partner companies, plus a demo booth with live rides at two major benefits conferences during the window. This channel exists to scale peer validation: partner HR leaders telling the story carries the credibility that a shortlist-driven, peer-first buying process (ref-04) demands, and live rides at venue turn abstract interest into Fleet Week bookings.

### 5. Email Nurture & CRM
All leads flow into a lifecycle email program that moves prospects from awareness content to Fleet Week booking, with tracks segmented by role — finance influencers see cost-per-employee content, HR sees utilization content. We build this as lifecycle-triggered rather than batch-and-blast because lifecycle-triggered emails average a 38% open rate, versus 21% for standard campaign sends (ref-16), and a multi-month B2B benefits cycle cannot afford to go dark between touches.
