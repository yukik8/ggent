## Campaign Title

**"Fridge-Worthy" — Pawstead Fresh Comes to the Fresh Aisle**

---

## Opening: The Problem We Are Actually Solving

Pawstead has a trial-and-trust problem, not a product problem. The buyers most likely to love fresh food already exist and already spend: 63% of dog owners under 40 rank ingredient quality above price when choosing a food brand (ref-09). Yet most of them have never put a fresh meal in the bowl, because as a DTC-only subscription, Pawstead reads to them as a startup bill rather than real food. And even when fresh brands win the trial, they struggle to keep it — the average fresh pet food subscriber churns after 4.2 months; top-quartile brands retain subscribers past 9 months (ref-09). Two gaps, one root cause: fresh has never lived where these shoppers already judge what counts as food.

The fridge-aisle launch is our answer to that specific problem. In-fridge grocery placement lifts brand consideration by 22% among shoppers who already buy premium dog food (ref-09) — precisely our target — and the category momentum rewards the move now: US pet food retail sales reached $58.1B in 2024, with fresh/refrigerated the fastest-growing segment at 17% YoY (ref-09). At an average feeding cost of $3.20/day, the price of a coffee, the upgrade is rationalizable at the shelf. Every tactic below is selected to close the trust gap that blocks first trial and the churn cliff that caps lifetime value, in that order.

---

## Target Audience

Primary: dog owners under 40 who already buy premium dog food but have not tried fresh — the exact segment where the 22% in-fridge consideration lift is measured (ref-09). They are quality-led, not price-led (63% rank ingredient quality above price, ref-09), and they discover brands where real dogs appear: 57% of pet owners under 40 discover new pet brands through social video featuring real pets (ref-09). Our creative therefore leads with real dogs and verifiable formulation, never with discounts.

Secondary: current Pawstead subscribers who gain in-store top-up convenience, and gift shoppers buying for the dog people in their lives.

---

## The Big Idea

**"Fridge-Worthy"** is a launch platform built around one warm, simple idea: if it is not good enough for your fridge, it is not good enough for your dog. Because the core problem is that fresh food has never felt like real food to premium-kibble buyers, the fridge itself is the strategic hero, not just a charming setting — earning a spot on the family fridge shelf is the visual proof of the category claim. Real dogs, real fridges, first-bowl moments, dogs waiting at the fridge door instead of the pantry. The mechanic dramatizes exactly the placement that moves consideration 22% among our target (ref-09).

## Channel & Media Plan

Each channel below maps directly to the trial gap or the churn cliff named above.

### 1. Paid Social — Short-Form Video (Instagram Reels & YouTube Shorts)
Because the trust gap is closed by seeing real dogs eat, we weight spend to the format where that discovery happens: 57% of pet owners under 40 discover new pet brands through social video featuring real pets (ref-09). The economics hold up — Instagram Reels average a $9.40 CPM and YouTube Shorts $6.90 (ref-06), and short-form video ads average 2.4x the completion rate of static feed ads (ref-06). We will cap exposure deliberately, since frequency above 4 exposures per week correlates with an 18% drop in engagement rate (ref-06).

### 2. Vet & Pet Creator Partnerships
A vet-formulated claim only converts skeptics when a credentialed vet makes it on camera and a real dog verifies it over weeks. Creator-partnership posts deliver 31% lower cost-per-engagement than brand-owned posts (ref-06), so vet creators walking through the formulation and dog accounts documenting the switch give us both credibility and efficiency, feeding cut-downs into the paid flight.

### 3. In-Store Fridge-Aisle Sampling & Shopper Activation
The consideration lift only cashes in if the first bowl actually happens. "First Bowl on Us" weekend activations at high-traffic launch-market groceries — branded fridge-aisle displays, take-home trial packs, staff who can walk the $3.20/day math — convert the 22% in-fridge consideration lift (ref-09) into a physical first purchase, the entry point to the retention loop.

### 4. Grocery Retail Media
Because basket-building now happens on grocers' own screens, we defend the digital shelf where the fridge-aisle shopper plans the trip. US retail media ad spend reached $54B in 2024, growing 21% YoY, the fastest-growing paid channel (ref-08), and on-site sponsored product placements drive 15% average lift in units per store per week (ref-08). At a $12.60 average grocery retail media CPM versus $9.40 for Reels (ref-08), we deploy it surgically for conversion, not reach.

### 5. Lifecycle Email & CRM
This channel exists because of the 4.2-month churn cliff (ref-09): the second and third bowls are where fresh brands die. Lifecycle-triggered emails average a 38% open rate, versus 21% for standard campaign sends (ref-16), and SMS opt-in subscribers convert at 2.4x the rate of email-only subscribers on time-limited offers (ref-16). The spend is small but the payback is the best in the plan — median ROI for retention marketing is $4.60 per dollar spent, versus $2.20 for prospecting (ref-16).

## Why Pawstead Wins

This plan does not just put fresh food in a new aisle; it attacks the two measured constraints on Pawstead's growth — a trust gap that keeps quality-led buyers from a first trial, and a 4.2-month churn cliff that caps the value of every trial won (ref-09). Social video and vet creators make fresh believable, the fridge aisle makes it feel like real food, sampling converts belief into a first bowl, and retail media plus CRM defend the purchase and the repurchase. Every dollar is answerable to the same named problem.
