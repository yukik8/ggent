# Campaign Proposal: "Charge Ahead" — Northtrail Commuter One Launch

**Client:** Northtrail Mobility
**Prepared by:** Emma Lindqvist, Junior Planner; revised by Sofia Reyes, Creative Strategy Director, Meridian & Craft
**Version:** Final
**Campaign window:** May 1, 2025 – September 30, 2025
**Total budget:** $650,000

---

## 1. Opening: Northtrail's Problem Is the Price Tag, Not the Product

Northtrail has an adoption problem, not a product problem. The category is growing, US e-bike unit sales hit 1.4M in 2024, a 12% YoY increase (ref-03), yet most of Northtrail's best prospects still are not buying. The reason is specific and measurable: 68% of urban commuters aged 25-40 say upfront price is the main barrier to buying an e-bike (ref-03). The Commuter One's $89/month subscription bundle is our answer to that exact problem, it converts a $1,899 sticker-shock decision into a monthly line item, and the market is already moving this way: subscription and financing models grew to 21% of e-bike transactions in 2024, from 9% in 2022 (ref-03). Every tactic below is selected for its ability to dismantle the upfront-price barrier, not just to generate awareness.

## 2. Target Audience

Urban commuters aged 25-40 in mid-size US cities with bike infrastructure. Two numbers make this audience unusually winnable. First, the average commute distance in mid-size US cities is 7.8 miles, within a single e-bike battery charge (ref-03), so the Commuter One fits their actual daily trip with margin to spare. Second, infrastructure is on our side: cities with protected bike lanes show 2.3x higher e-bike adoption than comparable cities without (ref-03), which is why we prioritize cities that already have the lanes rather than trying to sell the behavior change and the bike at once.

## 3. The Big Idea: "Charge Ahead"

"Charge Ahead" celebrates the feeling of gliding past traffic while everyone else is stuck. The creative centers on real commute moments, sunrise rides, the swap-and-go ritual at a battery station, arriving at work without the parking hunt. The campaign tagline is **"Swap the battery. Skip the traffic. Charge Ahead."**

The signature activation is the **Charge Ahead Demo Tour**, a traveling pop-up that sets up in five mid-size cities across the summer. Commuters test-ride the Commuter One on a short urban loop, try the battery swap themselves, and sign up for the subscription on the spot with a first-month promo. Because the core problem is upfront price, every creative asset leads with the $89/month framing, and only 22% of this cohort responds to discount-led messaging as a first touch while 49% respond to problem-solution storytelling (ref-07), so the story is "your commute, solved for $89/month," never "e-bike on sale."

## 4. Channel & Media Plan

Same channels the draft proposed, now each with a quantified reason for existing and an explicit link back to the price-barrier problem.

| Channel | Role and evidence |
|---|---|
| Short-form video (Reels, TikTok, YouTube Shorts) | Primary awareness driver. 71% of Gen-Z respondents discover new brands primarily through short-form video (ref-07), and the inventory is efficient: Instagram Reels average CPM is $9.40, TikTok $7.80, and YouTube Shorts $6.90 (ref-06). Short-form video ads average 2.4x the completion rate of static feed ads (ref-06), which matters because our core message, the $89/month barrier-remover, lives in the second half of the spot. |
| Creator partnerships | Local cycling, commuting, and lifestyle creators produce ride-along content. Creator-partnership posts deliver 31% lower cost-per-engagement than brand-owned posts (ref-06), and 64% of this audience has purchased a product recommended by a niche creator with under 100K followers (ref-07), so we brief niche local creators, not national names. Because 58% distrust ads that make claims without visible evidence or numbers (ref-07), creator briefs require on-camera price math: $89/month versus their current commute costs. |
| Charge Ahead Demo Tour (pop-ups) | Hands-on trial in five cities, prioritized by protected-lane coverage given the 2.3x adoption gap (ref-03). The test ride plus on-the-spot subscription sign-up attacks the price barrier at the moment of maximum product desire. |
| Local OOH & transit ads | Placements along commute corridors and near transit hubs, catching commuters mid-pain-point with the $89/month message. |
| Campaign landing page | Product configurator, subscription sign-up, demo tour schedule, test-ride booking. Since Gen-Z respondents check an average of 2.7 sources before a first purchase over $50 (ref-07), the page carries spec comparisons, swap-station maps, and creator reviews so the research loop closes on our property. |

## 7. KPIs

Each KPI is benchmarked, because a target without a baseline is a wish.

- Paid social click-through rate at or above the 1.2% cross-vertical average, with a stretch goal toward the 2.6% top-quartile mark (ref-06)
- Creator content cost-per-engagement at least 31% below brand-owned post benchmarks (ref-06)
- Paid frequency capped below 4 exposures per week, since frequency above that correlates with an 18% drop in engagement rate (ref-06)
- Demo tour test rides completed and on-site subscription sign-ups per city
- Subscription share of attributed conversions, tracked against the category's 21% subscription/financing transaction share (ref-03)
- Cost per acquisition trending downward through the flight

## 8. Why This Will Work

The problem is named and quantified: 68% of the target says upfront price is the main barrier (ref-03). The product answer exists: the $89/month bundle. The market is already shifting toward that answer, subscriptions grew from 9% to 21% of transactions in two years (ref-03). And the media plan reaches the audience where they actually discover brands, short-form video for 71% of them (ref-07), through the voices they actually trust, niche creators (ref-07). "Charge Ahead" does not ask commuters to fall in love with a spec sheet. It removes the one number stopping them and puts a test ride within reach.
