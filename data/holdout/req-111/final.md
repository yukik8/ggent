## Opening: The Problem We Are Actually Solving

Ledgerline's manufacturing problem is invisibility at the moment that matters. 73% of mid-market finance software evaluations now start with a shortlist built from peer review sites (ref-04), and Ledgerline currently has almost no footprint in manufacturing-relevant review categories — meaning most manufacturing deals are decided before Ledgerline is ever considered. The demand itself is real: finance teams report 62% of invoice processing time is still manual at companies without AP automation (ref-04), and manufacturers' high invoice volumes and three-way matching complexity make that burden heavier still.

Ledgerline for Manufacturing is the answer to both halves: a $30,000 vertical tier with the PO-matching and inventory-integration capabilities manufacturers actually require, launched with a program engineered first to get Ledgerline onto the shortlists where 73% of evaluations begin (ref-04). Every tactic below maps back to shortlist presence and manual-burden proof.

|---|---|
| Offer | AP Automation Suite, manufacturing tier |
| ACV | $30,000 average |

## Target Audience

Finance directors at 100-500 employee manufacturers. They build shortlists from peer review sites before any vendor contact (ref-04), evaluate slowly — the average AP automation deal cycle for 50-500 employee firms is 94 days (ref-04) — and convert on proof from companies like theirs, not on feature lists.

## The Big Idea

**"Built for the Floor and the Books"** positions Ledgerline as the AP platform that understands manufacturing. Because the core problem is absence from peer-driven shortlists, the creative's job is credibility: plant-floor imagery paired with named manufacturing outcomes, so every touchpoint reads as vertical proof rather than a generic finance tool wearing a manufacturing label.

## Channel & Media Plan

Each channel maps back to the shortlist-invisibility problem named above.

| Channel | Role |
|---|---|
| Peer review site program | Build presence in manufacturing-relevant review categories |
| LinkedIn ABM media | Targeted creative to finance directors at named manufacturers |
| Manufacturing trade media & events | Sponsored content and booth presence at two industry events |
| Case study production | Manufacturing customer stories with concrete outcomes |
| Webinars with manufacturing customers | Peer-led sessions on AP transformation |
| Landing pages & creative | Vertical landing experience with demo booking |

**Peer review site program.** Because 73% of evaluations start at review sites (ref-04), this is the program's foundation, sequenced first: seeded manufacturing customer reviews and category placement before demand spend begins.

**LinkedIn ABM media.** The LinkedIn B2B benchmark CPM for finance decision-makers is $58, versus $31 for general B2B targeting (ref-04), so we spend only against a named manufacturer account list showing fit or intent — no broad awareness at an 87% audience premium.

**Case studies, webinars, and landing pages.** Case-study-led landing pages convert demo requests at 4.7%, versus 2.1% for feature-led pages (ref-04), so manufacturing stories with named volumes and days-cut-from-close numbers anchor the vertical page, the webinars, and the trade-event follow-up alike.

**Trade media & events.** Category presence where manufacturing finance leaders already gather, feeding the ABM list and the webinar series.
