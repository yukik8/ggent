## Campaign Title

**"Your Doctor. Finally." — the Clearbrook Membership Launch**

## Opening: The Problem We Are Actually Solving

Clearbrook is not launching a subscription into a satisfied market; it is launching into a mass defection from primary care. 41% of US adults under 45 have no primary-care physician, up from 33% in 2019 (ref-15). This audience is not refusing care — it is refusing the traditional way of getting it. The average wait for a new-patient primary-care appointment in mid-size metros is 26 days (ref-15), and for a cohort that books everything else instantly, that wait reads as a reason to stay unattached. Their selection behavior confirms it: 64% of patients under 40 choose a clinic based on online booking availability before considering reputation (ref-15).

Clearbrook Membership is our answer to that specific problem: a named physician, same-week appointments booked online, unlimited messaging, an annual physical, and member pricing on urgent care — all at $79/month, cancel anytime. The product removes exactly the two barriers the data names: the 26-day wait and the phone-first front door. Every tactic below is selected for its ability to convert unattached under-45 adults, not just to advertise a subscription.

## Target Audience

Adults under 45 without a primary-care physician — the 41% and growing (ref-15). They are comfortable with subscriptions everywhere else in life, and they choose care providers by testing the booking flow first (ref-15). They are also skeptical of unevidenced marketing: 58% say they distrust ads that make claims without visible evidence or numbers (ref-07), and only 22% respond to discount-led messaging as a first touch while 49% respond to problem-solution storytelling (ref-07). Our creative therefore leads with the problem they recognize — no doctor, long waits — never with a promo.

Secondary: existing Clearbrook urgent-care patients, the warmest conversion pool.

## The Big Idea

**"Your Doctor. Finally."** Because the core problem is that a third-going-on-half of this cohort has quietly given up on getting a doctor (ref-15), the campaign does not sell features; it names the moment of not having one. Creative shows real moments — the 2am symptom search, the intake form asking for a primary-care physician you don't have — resolved by one line: *You have a doctor for that now.* The $79/month price appears in every asset as the whole answer, problem-solution storytelling in exactly the structure this audience responds to (ref-07).

## Channel & Media Plan

Each channel below maps directly to the unattached-patient problem named above.

| Channel | Role |
|---|---|
| Paid social — short-form video | Launch films and member-story content; the main awareness driver |
| Paid search | Capture "doctor near me" and "primary care" demand and route it to the membership page |
| Podcast & streaming audio | Long-form explanation of how the membership works, in trusted shows |
| Out-of-home | Simple price-led boards near commuter routes and Clearbrook locations |
| Content production | Launch films, member stories, landing assets |
| Email & SMS | Convert existing urgent-care patients into members |

**Paid social — short-form video.** Because the unattached audience is not searching for a doctor, we reach them where discovery happens: 71% of Gen-Z respondents discover new brands primarily through short-form video (ref-07). The format is efficient — Instagram Reels average a $9.40 CPM, TikTok $7.80, and YouTube Shorts $6.90 (ref-06) — and short-form video ads average 2.4x the completion rate of static feed ads (ref-06). We cap frequency, since frequency above 4 exposures per week correlates with a 18% drop in engagement rate (ref-06).

**Paid search.** Because the 26-day wait (ref-15) is what searchers hit everywhere else, our search creative leads with "same-week appointment, booked online" and routes straight to the membership page. We benchmark against the 1.2% cross-vertical paid social average CTR and its 2.6% top quartile (ref-06).

**Podcast & streaming audio.** Because a new care model needs explaining, audio buys the minutes short-form cannot, walking through how $79/month replaces the wait and the phone call.

**Out-of-home.** Because booking-first, price-clear positioning is what converts this market — transparent cash-pay pricing pages increase new-patient bookings by 34% for urgent-care services (ref-15) — the boards carry the price and the promise, nothing else.

**Email & SMS.** Because past urgent-care visitors already trust Clearbrook, lifecycle sends convert them warmest: lifecycle-triggered emails average a 38% open rate, versus 21% for standard campaign sends (ref-16), and SMS opt-in subscribers convert at 2.4x the rate of email-only subscribers on time-limited offers (ref-16).

## Why This Will Work

This plan attacks a measured, growing gap — 41% of under-45 adults with no doctor, facing 26-day waits and phone-first front doors (ref-15) — with a product built to remove exactly those barriers and a campaign that names the problem before the price. Social builds recognition where discovery happens, search and audio convert consideration, and lifecycle messaging harvests the base Clearbrook already earned. Every tactic is answerable to the same named problem.
