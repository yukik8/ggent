## Campaign Title

## Opening: The Problem We Are Actually Solving

Skillharbor's prospects are not short on motivation; they are short on a map. 66% of career changers aged 25-40 say they cannot identify which skills would actually raise their salary (ref-12), and when they do buy, the format fails them: completion rates average 12% for self-paced online courses versus 68% for cohort-based programs (ref-12). The market keeps growing — US consumer upskilling reached $13.7B in 2025, growing 11% YoY (ref-12) — but that spend flows to whoever resolves the confusion, not whoever shouts about the hottest field.

The cybersecurity cohort is our answer to that specific problem: a single, named path from a stalled job to a specific high-demand role, delivered in the one format the data says people finish. At $2,600 for the cohort or via the $199/month all-access tier, every tactic below is selected to close the guidance gap, not just to ride the cyber news cycle.

---

## Target Audience

Primary: career changers aged 25-40 eyeing high-demand security roles — people in stalled analyst, IT-support, and ops jobs. They are precisely the segment reporting the guidance gap above, which is why every asset names the role, the skills, and the outcome. Secondary: existing all-access subscribers who could upgrade into the cohort.

---

## The Big Idea

"Firewall Your Future" turns career anxiety into a call to action — but because the core problem is skill-to-salary uncertainty, the platform leads with outcome specificity, not breach headlines. Ads featuring a specific salary-outcome statistic drive 2.1x higher free-trial starts than generic skill-building ads (ref-12), so every execution, from the "Could you catch the hacker?" quiz to the countdown content, pairs the cohort with a concrete role and pay story.

## Tactics

Each tactic maps back to the guidance-and-completion problem named above.

### 1. Short-Form Video Blitz (TikTok & Reels)
Because the core problem is that prospects cannot see which skill pays, our short-form flight leads with named-role, named-outcome creative rather than trend reactions. The channel economics hold: TikTok averages a $7.80 CPM and Instagram Reels $9.40 (ref-06), and short-form video ads average 2.4x the completion rate of static feed ads (ref-06).

### 2. CareerTok Creator Partnerships
Career-switch creators document their own four-week taste of the cohort — living proof that people finish this format, directly answering the 12% self-paced completion problem (ref-12). Creator-partnership posts deliver 31% lower cost-per-engagement than brand-owned posts (ref-06).

### 3. Podcast Sponsorship Flight
Host-read spots on career and security podcasts carry the same outcome-specific script, because generic "future-proof your career" copy is exactly what underperforms by half against salary-stat creative (ref-12).

### 4. Paid Search & Display
We capture high-intent queries ("cybersecurity bootcamp," "career change to cyber") with landing pages built around the skills-to-salary map, plus display retargeting on quiz takers.

### 5. Webinar Funnel
The biweekly "Is Cyber Right for You?" session is the diagnosis step the catalog experience lacks: it tells each attendee whether this path raises their salary, then converts to enrollment calls.
