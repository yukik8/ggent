## Campaign Title

## Opening: The Problem We Are Actually Solving

Pawstead's constraint is not demand, it is the churn cliff. The average fresh pet food subscriber churns after 4.2 months; top-quartile brands retain subscribers past 9 months (ref-09), and closing that gap is the single largest economic lever available to the brand. Senior-dog owners are the segment built to close it: a dog managing weight and joint health needs the plan indefinitely, and the buyer is already quality-led — 63% of dog owners under 40 rank ingredient quality above price when choosing a food brand (ref-09). The category tailwind is real too: US pet food retail sales reached $58.1B in 2024, with fresh/refrigerated the fastest-growing segment at 17% YoY (ref-09).

The Golden Years Plan — vet-formulated senior recipes with joint support, controlled calories, and softer textures at $3.40/day — is our answer to that specific problem: acquire the owner whose need does not lapse, and convert aging-dog subscribers before they churn out. Every tactic below maps back to retention economics, not to trend reach.

---

## Target Audience

Owners of dogs aged 8+ managing weight and joint health, stated up front because they define the plan. They discover brands the way the rest of the under-40 cohort does: 57% of pet owners under 40 discover new pet brands through social video featuring real pets (ref-09). They are also evidence-hungry — 58% say they distrust ads that make claims without visible evidence or numbers (ref-07) — so every asset shows a named vet, a real senior dog, and the formulation math.

---

## The Big Idea

**"More good mornings."** Every asset centers on the moment a senior dog gets up easier, walks farther, or plays again. Because the core problem is the 4.2-month churn cliff (ref-09), the creative arc runs month by month — month-one appetite, month-three mobility, month-six the dog park evangelist — making staying subscribed the story itself.

---

## Channel Plan, Mapped to the Problem

1. **Short-form social video.** Because discovery runs through real-pet video — 57% of pet owners under 40 discover new pet brands through social video featuring real pets (ref-09) — owner-shot senior-dog footage leads. The format is efficient: TikTok averages a $7.80 CPM and Instagram Reels $9.40 (ref-06), with 2.4x the completion rate of static feed ads (ref-06).
2. **Vet & pet creator partnerships.** 64% of Gen-Z respondents have purchased a product recommended by a niche creator with under 100K followers (ref-07), and creator-partnership posts deliver 31% lower cost-per-engagement than brand-owned posts (ref-06). Vet creators carry the formulation credibility this evidence-led buyer requires.
3. **Podcast sponsorships.** Host-read pet and health spots reach committed owners during research mode, reinforcing the vet-led message.
4. **Paid search & display.** Captures high-intent "senior dog food" and joint-health searches at the decision moment.
5. **CRM & email lifecycle.** Because the core problem is retention, age-triggered flows do the compounding work: lifecycle-triggered emails average a 38% open rate, versus 21% for standard campaign sends (ref-16), and the median ROI for retention marketing is $4.60 per dollar spent, versus $2.20 for prospecting (ref-16).

## Why This Will Work

This plan aims the launch at the number that decides Pawstead's economics: 4.2 months versus 9 (ref-09).
