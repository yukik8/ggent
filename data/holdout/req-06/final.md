# Campaign Proposal: "Pass the Bowl" — Sunbrook Crisps Holiday Multipack Retail Media Push

**Client:** Sunbrook Foods
**Prepared by:** Ryo Tanabe, Junior Planner — revised by Sofia Reyes, Creative Strategy Director, Meridian & Craft
**Version:** Final
**Campaign window:** October 15 – December 31, 2025
**Budget:** $290,000

---

## 1. Opening: The Problem

Sunbrook has a loyalty problem, and the holidays are where it costs the most. Repeat purchase rate for the average mid-market snack brand is 31%, versus 52% for the top-3 national brands (ref-01), and private-label share of salty snacks rose from 18% to 23% between 2021 and 2024 (ref-01) — exactly the trade-down pressure that peaks when shoppers are filling big holiday baskets on a budget. Add the fact that only 27% of snack buyers aged 18-34 can name a preferred mid-market crisp brand unprompted (ref-01), and the risk is clear: at the highest-velocity moment of the year, the party-snack purchase defaults to a national brand or the store's own label, not to Sunbrook.

The $8.99 6-count Holiday Multipack is our answer to that specific problem. A multipack is not just a festive format — it is six Sunbrook occasions in a single purchase decision, a structural way to convert one basket into repeat consumption. Every tactic below is selected for its ability to win the shelf-moment decision away from private label and turn holiday trial into repeat behavior, not just to generate seasonal buzz.

## 2. Target Audience

Because the core problem is losing the basket decision at shelf, we target grocery shoppers buying party snacks for holiday gatherings at the moment of purchase intent — in retailer apps, on retailer sites, and in the aisle — with social used to pre-load the choice before the trip. Drive-market weekend shoppers around Sunbrook's core Midwest grocery footprint remain the geographic focus, and creative leans on problem-solution storytelling rather than discounting: only 22% respond to discount-led messaging as a first touch; 49% respond to problem-solution storytelling (ref-07).

## 3. The Big Idea: "Pass the Bowl"

Every holiday gathering has a snack bowl at the center of it. "Pass the Bowl" positions the Sunbrook Holiday Multipack as the easiest way to fill that bowl — one grab off the shelf, three flavors, zero decisions. Creative across every touchpoint shows the multipack turning into the shared bowl at the heart of the party: friendsgiving tables, watch parties, cookie exchanges, New Year's countdowns.

Because the problem is a default-to-national-brand reflex, the idea's job is to give Sunbrook a memorable, ownable role in the occasion — the brand that solves the host's "what do I put out" problem — so the multipack is the pre-decided answer before the shopper ever reaches the aisle.

## 4. Channel & Media Plan

We run a retail-first plan with social support, because the basket decision we need to win happens inside the retail environment.

### 4.1 Retail Media Networks (primary channel)
Because the core problem is losing the shelf-moment decision, retail media is the highest-leverage line in this plan: grocery retail media campaigns average ROAS of 3.8x for snack and beverage brands (ref-08), and on-site sponsored product placements drive 15% average lift in units per store per week (ref-08). This is also where the market is moving — US retail media ad spend reached $54B in 2024, growing 21% YoY, the fastest-growing paid channel (ref-08), and 68% of CPG brands plan to increase retail media budgets in 2026 (ref-08). We will run search-triggered sponsored tiles on terms like "party snacks," "chips variety pack," and "holiday snacks," plus category takeover placements in peak weeks (Thanksgiving, Christmas, New Year's). Retail media CPMs average $12.60 for grocery, versus $9.40 for Instagram Reels (ref-08) — a premium we pay deliberately, because these impressions land at the exact moment the basket is being built.

### 4.2 In-Store Shopper Marketing
End-cap displays and front-of-store seasonal placements in top-performing stores, timed to the three biggest gathering moments of the season. This attacks the recall gap directly: a shopper who cannot name Sunbrook unprompted (ref-01) can still choose it when the multipack physically owns the party-snack moment at store entry.

### 4.3 Paid Social (short-form video)
Instagram Reels and TikTok ads showing quick, satisfying "fill the bowl" moments, geo-targeted to the drive markets around Sunbrook's core grocery footprint. Short-form video ads average 2.4x the completion rate of static feed ads (ref-06), and Reels inventory is efficient at a $9.40 average CPM (ref-06), making this the lowest-cost way to pre-load the "Pass the Bowl" association before the store trip. We cap delivery deliberately: frequency above 4 exposures per week correlates with a 18% drop in engagement rate (ref-06).

### 4.4 Creator Partnerships
A small roster of Midwest food and entertaining creators producing hosting-hack content featuring the multipack. This is an efficiency play as much as an authenticity play: creator-partnership posts deliver 31% lower cost-per-engagement than brand-owned posts (ref-06), and 64% have purchased a product recommended by a niche creator with under 100K followers (ref-07) — the same shoppers whose repeat behavior we need to build.

## 7. KPIs

Each KPI maps to the problem stated in Section 1.

- **Repeat behavior:** multipack repeat purchase rate among campaign-exposed households, benchmarked against the 31% mid-market average (ref-01)
- **Efficiency:** retail media ROAS, benchmarked against the 3.8x grocery snack average (ref-08)
- **Velocity:** unit sales lift per store per week in participating stores, benchmarked against the 15% average lift for sponsored placements (ref-08)
- **Pre-store demand:** sponsored placement click-through and add-to-cart rates; social completion and engagement rates
- **Presence:** share of seasonal snack display versus last year

## 8. Why This Will Work

Sunbrook's problem this season is not awareness of a new product — it is a 31% repeat rate against a 52% national-brand benchmark (ref-01) and a private label that has taken five share points since 2021 (ref-01), both of which bite hardest in high-volume holiday baskets. This plan meets that problem where it lives: at the shelf, with the fastest-growing and highest-ROAS channel for snack brands (ref-08), backed by festive in-store presence and efficient short-form video that pre-decides the choice. "Pass the Bowl" turns one holiday purchase into six Sunbrook occasions, and that is how a mid-market brand starts closing a loyalty gap.
