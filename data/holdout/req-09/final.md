# Campaign Proposal: "The New Standard of Care for AP"

**Client:** Ledgerline
**Product:** Ledgerline AP Automation Suite — Healthcare Tier
**Prepared by:** Emma Lindqvist, Junior Planner — revised by Sofia Reyes, Creative Strategy Director, Meridian & Craft
**Budget:** $460,000
**Timeline:** March 1, 2026 – August 31, 2026
**Version:** Final (submitted)

---

## 1. Opening: The Problem We Are Solving

Ledgerline has an awareness problem in healthcare, not a product problem. The company is entering a vertical where it has no installed base, no healthcare case studies, and no presence on the peer review sites where 73% of mid-market finance software evaluations now start with a shortlist built from peer review sites (ref-04). Meanwhile, the buyers we need are living the exact pain Ledgerline removes: finance teams report 62% of invoice processing time is still manual at companies without AP automation (ref-04). And the sales motion is slow by nature — the average AP automation deal cycle for 50-500 employee firms is 94 days (ref-04), which means a six-month campaign gets each prospect through roughly one full buying cycle, with no room to waste on unqualified reach.

So the problem this campaign must solve is specific: **make Ledgerline shortlistable in healthcare within one buying cycle.** The Healthcare Tier — with its vendor-compliance tracking, cost-center routing, multi-entity approvals, and audit-ready trails at a $30,000 average annual contract value — is our answer to that problem. Every tactic below is selected and sequenced for one job: getting Ledgerline onto healthcare finance shortlists and converting that presence into pipeline before the window closes.

## 2. Target Audience

Because the core problem is shortlist entry within a 94-day cycle (ref-04), we concentrate spend on the people who build the shortlist: controllers, finance directors, and VPs of finance at 100–500 employee US healthcare providers — physician groups, outpatient networks, behavioral health organizations, and regional clinic chains. Influencers (office managers, revenue-cycle staff, CFOs) are reached through nurture only, not paid media. We are deliberately narrow: LinkedIn B2B benchmark CPM for finance decision-makers is $58, versus $31 for general B2B targeting (ref-04), so every impression bought at the premium rate must land on someone who can put Ledgerline on a shortlist.

## 3. Big Idea: "The New Standard of Care"

Healthcare organizations hold themselves to a standard of care for patients. Our campaign extends that language to the back office: **Ledgerline is the new standard of care for accounts payable.** The creative system pairs clinical visual language (charts, vitals, checklists) with finance imagery — manual AP work is the condition, Ledgerline is the treatment protocol. Crucially, the creative dramatizes the named problem itself: the "AP Health Check" diagnoses the prospect's share of manual processing time against the 62% benchmark (ref-04), turning our diagnosis of the market into a diagnosis of their own team.

The idea flexes across every channel: hero video ("Diagnose your AP process"), the AP Health Check self-assessment, case-study content ("Recovery stories"), and webinar programming ("Grand rounds for finance teams").

## 4. Channel & Media Plan — Each Tactic Mapped to the Problem

1. **Peer review site presence** — Because 73% of mid-market finance software evaluations now start with a shortlist built from peer review sites (ref-04), this is the single highest-leverage fix for our shortlist problem. Sponsored placement and enhanced profiles go live in month one, before paid media drives buyers there to check us.
2. **LinkedIn advertising** — Sponsored content and conversation ads against healthcare finance titles only. At a $58 CPM for finance decision-makers versus $31 for general B2B targeting (ref-04), broad targeting would burn the budget; tight title targeting makes the premium worth paying.
3. **Content & case studies** — Healthcare-specific case studies, the AP Health Check, and vertical landing pages. We build the landing experience case-study-first because case-study-led landing pages convert demo requests at 4.7%, versus 2.1% for feature-led pages (ref-04) — more than double the conversion from the same media spend.
4. **Webinar series** — Monthly "Grand Rounds for Finance" webinars with customer speakers and healthcare finance voices. Because the average deal cycle runs 94 days (ref-04), webinars give mid-cycle prospects a reason to re-engage instead of going dark between demo and decision.
5. **Industry conference presence** — Booth and speaking slots at two healthcare finance conferences, timed to hit prospects in the consideration phase of the 94-day cycle (ref-04) opened by our April media launch.
6. **Email nurture** — A six-touch nurture program pacing content across the full 94-day cycle (ref-04), each touch anchored to the manual-processing problem rather than feature announcements.

## 8. Why We Will Win

The Healthcare Tier is the answer, but the problem leads, and that is why this campaign will convert one full buying cycle into a healthcare beachhead for Ledgerline.
