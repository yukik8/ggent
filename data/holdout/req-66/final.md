## Campaign Title

**"The Receipts" — Skillharbor Salary-Outcomes Campaign**

## Opening: The Problem We Are Actually Solving

Skillharbor's growth ceiling is not awareness; it is disbelief. The prospects this campaign targets have been burned before — by their own abandoned courses and by an industry that promises transformation and delivers a login. Their skepticism is measurable: 58% say they distrust ads that make claims without visible evidence or numbers (ref-07), and only 22% respond to discount-led messaging as a first touch, while 49% respond to problem-solution storytelling (ref-07). Meanwhile the underlying confusion persists — 66% of career changers aged 25-40 say they cannot identify which skills would actually raise their salary (ref-12). This audience is not asking "what will I learn?" It is asking "prove it pays."

"The Receipts" is our answer to that specific problem. Skillharbor has years of documented graduate outcomes — salary increases, title changes, career pivots — and the evidence says leading with them is not just honest, it is the highest-performing move available: ads featuring a specific salary-outcome statistic drive 2.1x higher free-trial starts than generic skill-building ads (ref-12). The $199/month all-access subscription stays the offer; proof becomes the pitch. Every tactic below exists to convert disbelief, not to generate applause.

## Target Audience

Prospective learners who are skeptical that online courses pay off. Many have tried a course before, or watched a colleague pay for one and get nothing from it. They research carefully before spending — and because 58% of them distrust unevidenced claims (ref-07), every touchpoint in this plan carries a documented number rather than an adjective.

## The Big Idea

**"The Receipts" — don't take our word for it, take theirs.**

Every asset is structured as a receipt: name, program, before-salary, after-salary. Because the core problem is disbelief, the format is the strategy — a receipt is the visual grammar of proof, and it makes the campaign's evidence discipline impossible to miss. No aspirational language, no stock imagery of laptops on beaches, just documented outcomes in a consistent format.

## Channel & Media Plan

Each channel below maps directly to the disbelief problem named above.

| Channel | Role |
|---|---|
| Paid social — short-form video | Receipt-format alumni videos; primary reach and traffic driver |
| YouTube pre-roll | Longer alumni stories with outcome figures; builds credibility with research-minded prospects |
| Alumni testimonial production | A library of documented outcome stories that feeds every other channel |
| Landing page + salary-outcomes report | A downloadable outcomes report and program-level results hub as the campaign's proof center |
| Retargeting & email nurture | Sequential outcome stories for visitors who did not convert on the first touch |
| Creator partnerships | Career creators review the outcomes report and pressure-test the numbers on their own channels |

Because the core problem is disbelief, short-form video leads with the receipt itself — the salary-outcome statistic that drives 2.1x higher free-trial starts (ref-12) — on efficient inventory (Instagram Reels average a $9.40 CPM, TikTok $7.80, YouTube Shorts $6.90 (ref-06)), with short-form video ads averaging 2.4x the completion rate of static feed ads (ref-06). Creator reviews exist because skeptics trust third-party verification over brand claims, and the economics cooperate: creator-partnership posts deliver 31% lower cost-per-engagement than brand-owned posts (ref-06). The landing page and outcomes report reward the audience's research habit rather than fighting it. Retargeting and email work the long consideration window with sequenced proof — lifecycle-triggered emails average a 38% open rate, versus 21% for standard campaign sends (ref-16) — and we hold paid frequency in check, since frequency above 4 exposures per week correlates with an 18% drop in engagement rate (ref-06).

## Why This Will Work

This plan starts from the measured reason skeptics do not convert — 58% distrust claims without visible evidence (ref-07) — and makes evidence the entire creative system. The receipt format is distinctive, repeatable, and honest; the outcomes report rewards the audience's research instinct; and every channel carries the one message format proven to move this segment, the specific salary outcome (ref-12). We are not asking skeptics to believe. We are handing them the receipts.
