## Campaign Title

## Opening: The Problem We Are Actually Solving

Blue Fork has a frequency problem, not an affection problem. The guests this brief targets already like the food — they simply visit once or twice a month, and at a $12.90 average check, every visit that doesn't happen is revenue that walks two doors down. The size of that gap is measurable: loyalty-app members visit fast-casual chains 2.3x more often than non-members and spend 31% more per order (ref-10), and at brands with mature programs, loyalty members generate 42% of revenue while representing 18% of customers (ref-16). Blue Fork's current program captures almost none of that economics. The math also says this is the right money to spend: median ROI for retention marketing is $4.60 per dollar spent, versus $2.20 for prospecting (ref-16).

The relaunched Blue Fork Rewards app is our answer to that specific problem. Its features — one point per dollar, the "My Usual" one-tap reorder, the visual bowl-streak tracker, and surprise "Fork Drops" — are not app decoration; each one removes a reason to skip a visit or adds a reason to make one. Every tactic below is selected for its ability to convert the once-a-month guest into a weekly regular, not merely to drive downloads.

---

## Target Audience

Our primary target is Blue Fork's existing guests who currently visit once or twice per month — busy professionals grabbing lunch near the office, students, and families who treat Blue Fork as a reliable option. They are already digitally native diners in a category where digital orders average 38% of fast-casual revenue and top-quartile chains exceed 55% (ref-10), so the ordering behavior we need already exists; it is simply not yet attached to a reward loop. Many of them influence where coworkers and friends eat, so each converted regular carries others with them.

The opportunity is precise: these guests already like Blue Fork, they just don't think of it often enough — and the 2.3x visit-frequency gap between members and non-members (ref-10) is the exact distance this campaign is built to close.

---

## The Big Idea

**"Every Bowl Counts"** turns loyalty from a punch card into a story. Every visit fills the guest's bowl-streak tracker, every streak unlocks something tasty, and every "Fork Drop" makes opening the app feel like a small gift. Because the core problem is frequency, the campaign deliberately celebrates the ritual of the regular — the Tuesday bowl, the payday treat, the post-gym pickup — making the repeat visit itself the hero behavior, rather than the one-off promotion. It's warm, it's personal, and it makes frequency feel fun instead of transactional.

## Channel & Media Plan

Each channel below maps directly to the frequency problem named above.

### 1. Lifecycle CRM — Email, Push & SMS
Because the core problem is guests forgetting to come back, triggered lifecycle messaging is the workhorse of this plan, and the benchmarks justify the weight: lifecycle-triggered emails average a 38% open rate, versus 21% for standard campaign sends (ref-16). The program includes a warm migration series for existing members, streak reminders, points-balance nudges when guests are close to a reward, and win-back flows for lapsed visitors — where cart-abandonment and lapsed-buyer flows recover 5.9% of at-risk revenue on average (ref-16). For time-limited offers we lean on SMS, since SMS opt-in subscribers convert at 2.4x the rate of email-only subscribers on time-limited offers (ref-16).

### 2. Paid Social — Short-Form Video (Instagram Reels & TikTok)
Because lapsed and infrequent guests won't open an email from a brand they've drifted from, paid social re-enters their feed where they already are. We'll run geo-targeted Reels and TikTok flights around all 85 locations showing the app's most delightful moments — the streak tracker filling, a Fork Drop landing, the "My Usual" button in action. The format is efficient for this: Instagram Reels average a $9.40 CPM and TikTok $7.80 (ref-06), and short-form video ads average 2.4x the completion rate of static feed ads (ref-06).

### 3. Local Creator Partnerships
Because a loyalty habit is social proof made routine, we want real people modeling the ritual. Local food and lifestyle creators in Blue Fork's core Southeastern markets will share their own bowl rituals and streaks — content that is both more trusted and cheaper to work: creator-partnership posts deliver 31% lower cost-per-engagement than brand-owned posts (ref-06). Their assets also feed the paid flight.

### 4. In-Store Merchandising & Team Activation
Because the enrollment moment happens at the counter, not in the feed, the relaunch has to live in the restaurants. We'll deck out all 85 locations with counter cards, bag stuffers, and pickup-shelf signage, and equip team members with a simple script and incentive program for inviting every guest to join. Every register sign-up moves a guest from the non-member side of the 2.3x frequency gap (ref-10) to the member side, which is the entire point of the program.
