## Campaign Title

---

## Opening: The Problem We Are Actually Solving

Kinetiq's real competitor this fall is not another rower; it is the buyer's fear of their own follow-through. 71% of connected-fitness intenders cite fear of unused equipment as their top purchase hesitation (ref-11) — the "clothes rack" nightmare outranks price, space, and subscription fatigue. And the timing is unforgiving: the category has finally turned, with US connected fitness equipment sales returned to growth in 2025, up 6% YoY to $4.8B (ref-11), and rowing is the fastest-growing connected-fitness modality at 19% YoY unit growth (ref-11). The growth is there for the taking, but none of it reaches a buyer who pictures a $1,795 machine collecting laundry by March.

Row One is our answer to that specific fear, and its 30-day in-home trial is the mechanism: free in-home trial programs convert at 46%, versus 11% for standard e-commerce checkout (ref-11). The hardware makes the answer credible.

| Attribute | Detail |
|---|---|
| Hardware price | $1,795, or $63/month financing |
| Membership | $39/month, unlimited household profiles |
| Trial offer | 30-day in-home trial, free return pickup |
| Storage | Upright, approx. 2 sq ft footprint |
| Content | Daily live classes + 500-title on-demand library |

Every tactic below is selected to do one of two things: move a hesitant intender into a trial, or move a trialist past the first-month habit gate — because members completing 8 or more workouts in their first month show 81% 12-month retention, versus 34% for those completing fewer (ref-11).

---

## Target Audience

Primary: home-fitness intenders aged 28-45 who want to work out at home but hesitate because they worry the machine will go unused — the exact segment where the 71% unused-equipment fear is measured (ref-11). Many have quit a gym before, they research carefully, and they respond to honest reviews more than hype. Secondary: former connected-fitness owners considering a second try, and gift purchasers in the holiday window.

---

## The Big Idea

**"The 30-Day Proof."** Because the core problem is fear of unused equipment, we do not ask this audience to believe our marketing; we ask them to test it in their own living room. Every asset in the campaign makes the same offer: row for 30 days where you actually live, and if it is not working, we pick it up for free. Creative focuses on real weekday routines — the 6 a.m. row before the kids wake up, the lunch-break session in the basement — rather than transformation stories, because the buyer's question is not "will this change me?" but "will I still be using it in February?"

## Channel & Media Plan

Each channel below maps directly to the hesitation-and-habit problem named above.

| Channel | Role |
|---|---|
| Paid social — short-form video | Primary reach and trial-signup driver on Reels, TikTok, and Shorts |
| Creator partnerships | Review-style, "one month in" content from everyday-credible creators |
| Lifecycle email & SMS | Trial nurture from signup through day 30, plus holiday and financing reminders |
| Landing experience | Dedicated trial page with clear terms, financing math, and FAQ |

### 1. Paid Social — Short-Form Video
Because the core problem is hesitation rather than awareness, paid social's job is to put the trial offer — not the hardware — in front of intenders repeatedly and efficiently. The inventory is priced for it: Instagram Reels average a $9.40 CPM, TikTok $7.80, and YouTube Shorts $6.90 (ref-06), and short-form video ads average 2.4x the completion rate of static feed ads (ref-06). We will cap delivery deliberately, since frequency above 4 exposures per week correlates with an 18% drop in engagement rate (ref-06).

### 2. Creator Partnerships
A buyer afraid of quitting does not trust a brand's promise; they trust someone like them who did not quit. We contract creators who feel like our buyer — busy parents, desk workers, ex-gym members — to document their first 30 days on Row One honestly. The economics also favor it: creator-partnership posts deliver 31% lower cost-per-engagement than brand-owned posts (ref-06), and the review cutdowns feed the paid flight.

### 3. Lifecycle Email & SMS
Because the trial only converts if the habit forms, trial signups enter a structured nurture targeting the 8-workout first-month threshold that separates 81% 12-month retention from 34% (ref-11): setup help, first-week class picks, and check-ins through day 30, then conversion messaging around the $63/month financing option. The mechanism is proven — lifecycle-triggered emails average a 38% open rate, versus 21% for standard campaign sends (ref-16), and SMS opt-in subscribers convert at 2.4x the rate of email-only subscribers on time-limited offers (ref-16), which is exactly what a day-30 trial deadline is.

### 4. Landing Experience
A single trial-focused landing page carries the offer terms, the financing calculator, and the returns FAQ, keeping the path from ad to trial request as short as possible — the page's only job is converting fear into a trial request.

## KPIs

- **Trial requests** generated over the flight, by channel and cost per request
- **Trial-to-purchase conversion**, benchmarked against the 46% in-home trial conversion average versus 11% for standard e-commerce checkout (ref-11)
- **First-month activation** — share of trialists completing 8 or more workouts in their first month, the gate to 81% 12-month retention (ref-11)
- **Paid social CTR** at or above the 1.2% cross-vertical average, targeting the 2.6% top-quartile benchmark (ref-06)
- **Membership attach rate** at purchase, and return-logistics volume kept within plan

## Why Kinetiq Wins

This plan does not launch a rower; it neutralizes the one fear that keeps 71% of intenders (ref-11) from buying any rower. The trial converts the hesitant, the nurture builds the habit that keeps the machine out of the returns truck, and every dollar is benchmarked against category conversion math across the biggest buying window of the year.
