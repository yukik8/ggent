## 1. Opening: The Problem We Are Actually Solving

Velura has a retention problem disguised as a portfolio gap. The average DTC skincare brand loses 44% of first-time buyers before a second order (ref-02), and a one-jar relationship gives Velura only one product's worth of reasons to come back. The buyers Velura keeps are exceptionally valuable — moisturizer is the highest-frequency skincare repurchase category at 3.4 units per buyer per year (ref-02) — but every one of those repurchase moments is currently a single point of failure. At the same time, the brand's next customers are no longer walking in through the website: specialty retail (e.g. beauty chains) accounts for 38% of clean-beauty discovery, versus 24% for brand-owned sites (ref-02), which is precisely the channel Velura is expanding into without a launch-ready hero.

Daily Defense — a fragrance-free daily moisturizer with built-in broad-spectrum SPF at $36 per 50ml — is our answer to that specific problem. It converts a one-jar relationship into a two-jar morning ritual, gives the retail expansion a shelf story, and enters a category with room to grow: the US clean-beauty segment reached $11.6B in 2024, up 8.4% YoY while total prestige skincare grew only 2.9% (ref-02). Every tactic below is selected to deepen the buyer relationship and win the specialty shelf, not just to announce a new SKU.

Product highlights at a glance:

| Attribute | Daily Defense |
|---|---|
| Format | 50ml jar, matches core Velura franchise |
| Protection | Broad-spectrum SPF, daily-wear level |
| Texture | Lightweight cream, no white cast, no sunscreen smell |
| Ingredient list | Short, pronounceable, fragrance-free |
| Price | $36 per 50ml |
| Routine fit | Replaces the morning moisturizer step, adds nothing |

## 2. Target Audience

| Segment | Who they are | What we want them to do |
|---|---|---|
| Existing Velura buyers | Loyal core-moisturizer customers, already trust the formula | Add Daily Defense as their morning jar |
| SPF-skeptical skincare users under 35 | Ingredient-label readers who research before buying and avoid traditional sunscreen textures | Trial Daily Defense as their first daily SPF |

Both segments demand receipts before they buy. 62% of skincare buyers under 30 say ingredient transparency is a top-3 purchase driver (ref-02), and 58% of Gen-Z respondents say they distrust ads that make claims without visible evidence or numbers (ref-07). That is why every asset in this plan is a demonstration or an ingredient breakdown, never a mood film. Discovery behavior points the same direction: 71% of Gen-Z respondents discover new brands primarily through short-form video (ref-07), which dictates where the media weight goes.

---

## 3. The Big Idea

**"The Invisible Step."** Daily Defense is SPF that disappears into the routine you already have. The creative platform dramatizes the moment of *not noticing*: no cast, no smell, no extra step, no excuses. Every asset is built around a simple side-by-side ritual demo — same two-minute morning routine, one jar swapped — with the payoff line "You just wore SPF. Did you notice?" Because the core problem is a one-jar relationship, the swap mechanic matters strategically, not just creatively: it frames Daily Defense as a replacement inside an existing habit rather than a new habit to build, which is exactly how you earn the second jar from a buyer base that already repurchases moisturizer 3.4 times per year (ref-02).

## 4. Channel & Media Plan

Each channel below maps directly to the retention-and-shelf problem named above.

| # | Channel | Role in the plan |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Paid social — short-form video | Awareness and demo delivery |
| 2 | Creator & derm partnerships | Credibility and proof |
| 3 | CRM — email & SMS lifecycle | Converting the existing buyer base |
| 4 | Specialty retail activation | Physical discovery and trial |

### 4.1 Paid Social — Short-Form Video (Instagram Reels & TikTok)
Because the skeptical under-35 segment discovers brands in-feed — 71% of Gen-Z respondents discover new brands primarily through short-form video (ref-07) — the swap-one-jar demo and white-cast stress tests run as a sustained Reels and TikTok flight. The format is efficient and attention-holding for demonstration content: Instagram Reels average a $9.40 CPM and TikTok $7.80 (ref-06), and short-form video ads average 2.4x the completion rate of static feed ads (ref-06). We will cap delivery deliberately, since frequency above 4 exposures per week correlates with an 18% drop in engagement rate (ref-06).

### 4.2 Creator & Dermatologist Partnerships
Proof has to come from voices this audience already trusts. 64% of Gen-Z respondents have purchased a product recommended by a niche creator with under 100K followers (ref-07), so we prioritize niche skincare creators and dermatologists running on-camera ingredient decodes and multi-week wear tests over celebrity reach plays. The economics reinforce the choice: creator-partnership posts deliver 31% lower cost-per-engagement than brand-owned posts (ref-06), and the resulting library feeds the paid flight.

### 4.3 CRM — Email & SMS Lifecycle
Because the named problem is losing buyers between orders, the buyer file is where this launch pays back fastest. Lifecycle-triggered emails average a 38% open rate, versus 21% for standard campaign sends (ref-16), so the announcement, routine-upgrade, and win-back sequences are all trigger-built rather than batch-sent. SMS opt-in subscribers convert at 2.4x the rate of email-only subscribers on time-limited offers (ref-16), which is why the launch-week early-access window runs SMS-first. The channel's efficiency is structural: median ROI for retention marketing is $4.60 per dollar spent, versus $2.20 for prospecting (ref-16), and lapsed-buyer flows recover 5.9% of at-risk revenue on average (ref-16).

### 4.4 Specialty Retail Activation
Because 38% of clean-beauty discovery now happens in specialty retail versus 24% on brand-owned sites (ref-02), the shelf is not a supporting channel — it is where Velura meets most of its next customers for the first time. "Invisible Step" endcaps, tester units, and a mini-size sampling program at the chain's highest-traffic doors let skeptics swatch the no-cast texture and read the label in hand, converting the brand's retail expansion from a distribution move into an acquisition engine.

"The Invisible Step" positions Velura to own the morning routine, not just one jar in it.
