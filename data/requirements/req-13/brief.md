# Client Request — Northtrail Cargo E-Bike Launch

**To:** Meridian & Craft
**From:** Marcus Webb, Chief Marketing Officer, Northtrail Mobility
**Date received:** 2026-04-03
**Subject:** Launch campaign — Haulmate cargo e-bike for urban families

---

Team,

Third launch together, biggest budget yet. Here's the brief.

## What we're launching

The Haulmate — our first cargo e-bike. Extended rear deck rated for two child seats, low step-through frame, hydraulic disc brakes, integrated lighting, and our swappable battery system with an extended-range option. Three colorways, full accessory ecosystem. Pricing: $2,799 upfront or $119/month on the bundle plan.

The engineering team has outdone themselves and the early press rides have been glowing. This is the best-built cargo bike in the category, full stop.

## What we want

A launch campaign that puts the Haulmate on the map with urban families. We want the product seen — the deck, the battery swap, the ride feel — and we want the brand moment to be big: hero content, creators, events, whatever mix you think wins. Make families fall in love with this bike.

## Audience

Urban parents aged 30-45 replacing second-car trips, in our existing mid-size-city markets.

One observation from the spring test-ride clinics that I keep chewing on: the parent on the bike was always sold within ten minutes. It was the partner standing next to them who stalled the sale — the question was never about the bike, it was "what does this actually save us versus keeping the old second car?" We never had a good answer on hand. Probably a sales-enablement thing, but I wanted you to have the color.

## Budget and timeline

- **Budget:** $560,000 all-in.
- **Timeline:** In market 2026-05-15 through 2026-09-30, closing on the back-to-school window.

## Success measure

Board-level target: **1,500 Haulmate pre-orders and bundle sign-ups attributed to the campaign by September 30.** Brand lift in launch cities is the supporting measure.

## Deliverables

1. Launch strategy and creative platform
2. Channel plan and flighting
3. Budget breakdown against the $560K
4. KPI and measurement plan

## Process

Given the spend, our board has asked for a formal competitive review — four agencies, including two that specialize in mobility and outdoor. Proposals due April 24; we decide by the end of May. Your track record with us on Commuter One and the battery-swap push obviously counts, but this one will be judged on the deck.

Bring the good stuff.
