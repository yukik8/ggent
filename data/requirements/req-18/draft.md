## Campaign Title

**"Pack the Week" — the Sunbrook Variety-Pack Repositioning**

---

## Opening: The Product

The Sunbrook 8-count variety pack is getting a full repositioning. The refreshed pack contains eight single-serve bags across four Sunbrook flavors — two Original, two Sea Salt & Vinegar, two Honey BBQ, and two of a rotating "Flavor of the Season" slot — in a redesigned carry-handle carton priced at $7.99. The rotating slot is the key product feature: it means the pack is never exactly the same purchase twice, and it gives households a built-in reason to check what is inside this month.

The carton itself has been redesigned around how families actually store snacks. It sits flat in a pantry, the perforated front panel folds down into a grab-and-go dispenser, and each single-serve bag now carries a small "day dot" so kids can pick their bag for the day. These are practical, well-thought-out features, and the campaign's job is to make sure weekly shoppers see them.

## Target Audience

Our primary audience is household shoppers aged 30-49 who buy snack multipacks on a weekly basis. These shoppers plan their grocery trips, shop from lists, and value products that make the week run smoother. Many of them are parents packing lunches, and many buy the same multipack week after week mostly out of habit. They are reachable on social media, they read retailer emails and apps, and they respond well to offers tied to their regular store.

A secondary audience is households that currently only buy variety packs for parties or holidays. The repositioning gives us a chance to move them from occasional to weekly purchase.

---

## The Big Idea

**"Pack the Week"** repositions the Sunbrook variety pack from a party-occasion purchase to a standing item on the weekly grocery list. The creative frames the pack as the week's snack plan solved in one grab: eight bags, five school days, two weekend outings, one decision. All assets — social video, retail media placements, in-store displays, and email — repeat one visual device: a weekly calendar filled in with Sunbrook bags. It is a simple, repeatable idea that fits how our audience already thinks about groceries.

## Channel & Media Plan

| # | Channel | Role in plan |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Paid social (Facebook + Instagram Reels) | Awareness of the repositioned pack |
| 2 | Grocery retail media | Conversion at the digital shelf |
| 3 | In-store shopper marketing & sampling | Trial and visibility in aisle |
| 4 | CRM — email & SMS via retailer programs | Repeat purchase and habit building |

### 1. Paid Social — Facebook + Instagram Reels
Social media is a very effective way to reach busy household shoppers, and short video performs well for food content. We will run "Pack the Week" videos showing the Sunday grocery haul, the pantry dispenser moment, and kids picking their day-dot bags. Social carries the awareness load in the first half of the campaign.

### 2. Grocery Retail Media
Retail media is growing quickly and is a natural fit for a grocery product. We will run sponsored product placements and category banners on our retail partners' networks so the pack shows up when shoppers search for snacks or build their weekly online order. Many weekly shoppers now build baskets online even when they pick up in store, so this keeps us present at the decision moment.

### 3. In-Store Shopper Marketing & Sampling
We will place end-cap displays in the multipack aisle using the weekly-calendar visual, plus weekend sampling of the rotating seasonal flavor at high-traffic stores. Sampling is a reliable driver of trial for food products, and the seasonal slot gives shoppers a fresh reason to stop.

### 4. CRM — Email & SMS Through Retailer Loyalty Programs
Email remains a cost-effective channel, and loyalty program members are valuable customers. We will run lifecycle offers through retailer loyalty programs: a first-purchase offer, a second-purchase follow-up two weeks later, and a lapsed-buyer offer for households that skip three weeks. This is how we turn trial into the weekly habit the repositioning is aiming for.
