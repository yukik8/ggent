## Campaign Title

**"The Clinic Down the Hall" — Clearbrook Employer Partnerships**

## Opening

Clearbrook's employer on-site clinic offering — a staffed clinic room at the workplace, custom-contracted per employer — is a genuinely differentiated product in a benefits market crowded with vague wellness promises. This campaign brings it to regional employers with a modern B2B playbook: LinkedIn video, the HR podcast circuit, a webinar and content series, conference presence with a live demo build, and account-based display sequencing. Each element is chosen because it is currently working for the best B2B teams, and because the offering is concrete enough to earn attention in all of them.

## Tactic Stack

### 1. LinkedIn Video & Lead Gen
Short video is the strongest-performing format on LinkedIn today. We produce a series of walk-through films of an operating on-site clinic, CFO-angle explainers on absenteeism costs, and quick myth-busting clips, backed by lead-gen forms and retargeting.

### 2. Executive Thought-Leadership Podcast Tour
We book Clearbrook's chief medical officer onto the HR and benefits podcast circuit — the shows benefits leaders listen to on commutes — with a repeatable narrative about the future of workplace care.

### 3. Webinar & Content Series
A monthly webinar series ("What On-Site Care Actually Costs," "The Post-Renewal Playbook") plus downloadable ROI one-pagers and case studies, gated for pipeline capture and remixed into shareable clips.

### 4. HR Trade Events & Conference Presence
A booth-plus-speaking strategy at the two biggest regional HR and benefits conferences in the window, anchored by a live "clinic room" demo build attendees can walk through — concrete, memorable, and photographed constantly.

### 5. ABM Display & Retargeting
Account-based display against a curated list of regional employers, sequenced to warm accounts before outbound and event invitations land.

## Target Audience

HR leaders at 500+ employee regional employers — benefits directors, CHROs, and the CFOs who sign off. They are drowning in benefits-vendor pitches, skeptical of healthcare promises, and moved by peer proof and hard cost numbers. Benefits brokers and consultants form a deliberate secondary audience, since they shape a large share of employer decisions; industry surveys suggest a majority of mid-size employers lean on a broker or consultant when adding a new health benefit.

## The Big Idea

**"The Clinic Down the Hall."** Health benefits are abstract until the clinic is physically in the building. Every asset makes the offering concrete: real square footage, real staffing, real employees skipping a half-day off for a ten-minute visit. In a category full of vague wellness promises, concreteness is the differentiator — and it is a claim only a provider that actually builds and staffs clinics can make.

## Why This Will Work

The offering is concrete, the audience is reachable, and the playbook is current. LinkedIn video, podcast circuits, walk-through demos, and ABM sequencing give Clearbrook surround-sound presence with the people who decide on benefits, and the product itself is interesting enough to carry every format. "The Clinic Down the Hall" turns a complicated benefits sale into something an HR leader can literally picture — and then walk through at a conference.
