# Blue Fork Kitchen Summer Limited-Time Bowl Promotion — Proposal

**Prepared by:** Emma Lindqvist, Junior Planner, Meridian & Craft
**Reviewed by:** Daniel Okafor, Senior Planner
**Client:** Blue Fork Kitchen
**Campaign window:** June 1 – August 15, 2026
**Budget:** $210,000
**Version:** Final v2

---

## Campaign Title

**"Golden Hour" — the Blue Fork Summer Bowl**

---

## Opening: The Most Beautiful Bowl of the Year

Introducing the Golden Hour Bowl, the most photogenic item to ever come off a Blue Fork make-line. Charred sweet corn, blistered peaches, honey-lime chicken (or crispy chickpeas), whipped feta, and a chili-honey drizzle over golden turmeric rice — summer in a bowl, priced at **$13.90** for a strictly limited run from June 1 to August 15. When it's gone, it's gone.

Limited-time menu items reliably create excitement, and this one has all the ingredients: seasonal produce at its peak, colors that perform on camera, and a name that taps into the golden-hour moment people already love to post. Industry tracking regularly credits well-executed limited-time offers with meaningful traffic lifts during their run. Summer is when guests want something bright and fresh, and the Golden Hour Bowl gives loyal regulars and lapsed guests alike a genuine reason to come back through the door. This is Blue Fork at its sunniest, and the campaign around it should feel as warm as the bowl itself.

---

## Target Audience

Our primary audience is Blue Fork's existing guests — regulars who already have the app and a favorite order, and who respond to something new to try. Our second audience is lapsed visitors: people who used to come in but have drifted away over the past six months or more. A strictly seasonal bowl is exactly the kind of low-pressure nudge that reminds them why they liked Blue Fork in the first place. Both groups are active on social, comfortable ordering ahead, and responsive to limited-time news from brands they already know.

---

## The Big Idea

**"Golden Hour"** celebrates the fleeting, glowing, best part of the day — and of the menu. The bowl is only here for the summer, just as golden hour is only here for a moment, and the campaign leans into that urgency: catch it while it glows. Creative shows the bowl in warm evening light, ingredients tumbling in slow motion, and real guests catching their own golden hour on patios and park benches, bowl in hand. Every asset ends on the same line: *Here for a golden moment. Gone August 15.*

---

## Channel & Media Plan

### 1. Paid Social — Short-Form Video (Instagram Reels & TikTok)
Beautiful food deserves beautiful video. We will run geo-targeted Reels and TikTok flights around all 85 locations with sun-drenched hero footage, make-line ASMR of the bowl coming together, and countdown creative as August 15 approaches. Food content thrives in short-form video, and this bowl was practically designed for the format.

### 2. CRM — App Push, Email & In-App Features
Our warmest audience already carries Blue Fork in their pocket. Members get first taste: an early-access day before the public launch, a "Golden Hour is here" announcement flow, bonus points on their first Golden Hour Bowl, and a "last call" series in the final two weeks. Lapsed guests receive a dedicated win-back message — the bowl is the natural excuse to say "come see what's new."

### 3. Local Creator Partnerships
We will invite food and lifestyle creators across Blue Fork's markets to share their golden hour — a favorite patio, a post-beach stop, the bowl in evening light. Creator content brings a personal warmth that brand ads cannot quite match, and their footage feeds the paid flight.

### 4. In-Store Merchandising
The restaurants themselves go golden. Window clings, counter cards, and pickup-shelf signage in warm sunset tones announce the bowl, and team members wear "Ask me about Golden Hour" pins during launch weeks. The in-store moment is where curiosity becomes a first taste.
