# Client Request — Battery-Swap Membership Standalone Push

**To:** Meridian & Craft
**From:** Marcus Webb, Chief Marketing Officer, Northtrail Mobility
**Date received:** 2025-12-16
**Subject:** Campaign brief — battery-swap membership as a standalone product, spring 2026

---

Meridian & Craft,

After what you did with the Commuter One launch, this one's coming straight to you. Shorter brief, smaller budget, but strategically it's a pet project of mine, so read closely.

## The setup

We're breaking the battery-swap subscription out as a **standalone membership: $39/month**, unlimited swaps at any Northtrail station, works with every Northtrail bike sold since 2023. Swap takes under sixty seconds. The station network has doubled in eighteen months and it is, frankly, the coolest thing we've built — and almost nobody knows it exists as something you can just buy.

## What we want

Make the swap network famous. We want a campaign that shows off the swap moment — that sixty-second ritual — everywhere our riders and future riders look. Video, creators, out-of-home near the bike lanes, the whole kit. This is an awareness play for the membership as a product in its own right.

For your back pocket, two things I keep chewing on: when shoppers walk away from us, the reason we hear over and over is the upfront sticker price of the bike itself. And once someone does buy a bike, we essentially never hear from their wallet again — one purchase, then silence for years. Maybe the membership speaks to some of that. Anyway — the brief is the swap network and the $39 price point, front and center.

## Target

Two groups:

- **Existing Northtrail e-bike owners** — they bought outright and charge at home today; the membership is a natural upgrade
- **Price-sensitive commuters aged 25-40** — e-bike-curious but haven't committed; a monthly membership is a much softer front door

## Budget & timeline

- **Budget:** $350,000
- **Flight:** 2026-02-01 to 2026-05-31 — live before spring riding season kicks in

## Success metric

**3,000 membership sign-ups during the flight**, with at least a quarter coming from the existing owner base. Sign-ups are the whole ballgame here — this isn't a branding exercise.

## Deliverables

- Creative platform for the membership
- Channel plan and budget allocation
- An owner-base activation component (we'll open up CRM/email access)
- Flight plan and measurement framework

## Process

Sole-source, no review — you earned that last summer. But my CEO will read the proposal personally, so make the logic airtight. Can you turn it around by mid-January?

Marcus
