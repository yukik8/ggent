# Stackwise Tax-Season Savings Push — DRAFT

**Prepared by:** Emma Lindqvist, Junior Planner, Meridian & Craft
**Client:** Stackwise
**Campaign window:** January 15 – April 15, 2027
**Budget:** $230,000

---

## Campaign Title

**"Refund, Meet Future You" — the Stackwise Tax-Season Push**

---

## Opening: Stackwise's Biggest Moment of the Year

Tax season is the one time of year when millions of young people suddenly have a meaningful chunk of money land in their account — and Stackwise has the absolutely perfect product for that moment. The app's beautiful savings buckets, effortless auto-save rules, and satisfying goal-tracking screens are tailor-made for turning a refund into something real: an emergency fund, a moving fund, a first-ever "I'm actually okay" cushion. The free tier makes starting frictionless, and the $8/month premium tier is right there for anyone who wants more buckets for their newfound momentum.

This campaign puts Stackwise directly in the path of the refund, with warm, optimistic creative that makes saving your refund feel like the most exciting thing you could possibly do with it.

---

## Target Audience

Tax refund recipients aged 22-35. These are young earners who file early, get a refund, and genuinely want to do something smart with it this year. Lots of them are on TikTok and Instagram daily, they respond to content that feels honest and encouraging, and refund season is one of the few times money feels exciting to them instead of stressful.

---

## The Big Idea

**"Refund, Meet Future You."**

Instead of lecturing people about not blowing their refund, we celebrate the moment. The creative introduces "Future You" — the version of yourself three months from now who is so, so glad you stacked even part of that refund. It's charming, it's positive, and it completely sidesteps the guilt-trip tone that makes young people tune out finance brands.

---

## Channel Plan

### 1. Paid Social — Short-Form Video
A gorgeous flight of TikTok and Reels spots built around the "Future You" character and real refund-to-bucket moments in the app. Short-form video is hugely effective with this audience and perfect for the warm, funny tone we want. This is the campaign's main engine and runs the entire window.

### 2. Paid Search — Tax-Season Terms
When refund season starts, search interest around "what to do with tax refund" and related terms spikes wonderfully. A focused search flight captures that high-intent moment and routes it to a dedicated refund landing page.

### 3. Creator Partnerships
A handful of relatable creators share their own "Future You" refund plans — splitting their actual refunds into buckets on camera. Creator content makes the behavior feel normal and doable, not preachy, and gives us authentic assets to amplify.

### 4. Email & Push to Existing Users
Existing free users get a lovely little refund-season moment of their own: a "give your refund a home" flow with a pre-built Refund Bucket, plus a gentle premium nudge for users whose refund plans need more buckets.

## Why This Will Work

Refund season is a once-a-year emotional high point around money, and Stackwise is the perfect place for that feeling to land. The "Future You" idea is warm, shareable, and completely free of the finger-wagging tone this audience hates. With social, search, creators, and lifecycle all pointed at one glowing moment, this campaign can make stacking your refund feel like the obvious move of spring 2027.
