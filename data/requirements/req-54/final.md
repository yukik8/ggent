## Campaign Title

**"Blue Fork to Your Door" — the Delivery Growth Push**

---

## Opening: Introducing the Blue Fork Delivery Program

Blue Fork Kitchen is expanding its delivery business across all 85 locations, and the product is well suited to the channel: bowls travel well, the packaging keeps hot and cold components separated until the guest mixes them, and the build-your-own menu works cleanly inside the major delivery apps. The average delivery basket lands at **$15.50**, above the in-store average check, because delivery orders more often include sides, drinks, and multi-person carts.

Delivery is a meaningful and growing part of fast-casual revenue — industry reporting puts digital ordering at roughly a third of the category's sales — and winter is its strongest season. Blue Fork is currently under-indexed on the major platforms relative to its store footprint, with inconsistent menu presentation and little sponsored visibility. This campaign fixes that: a coordinated push across the delivery platforms, paid social, and Blue Fork's own channels to grow delivery order volume and make "Blue Fork to Your Door" a habit for delivery-first diners.

---

## Target Audience

| Segment | Description | Priority |
|---|---|---|
| Delivery-first diners 18-34 | Young professionals and students who default to delivery apps for weeknight meals | Primary |
| Existing Blue Fork guests | In-store and app customers who have not yet ordered Blue Fork delivery | Secondary |
| Office group orders | Teams placing shared delivery carts on weekdays | Secondary |

Our primary audience is delivery-first diners aged 18-34. They browse delivery apps the way earlier generations browsed food courts, they are strongly influenced by app placement and ratings, and they order several times per week. Reaching them requires visibility inside the platforms themselves, reinforced by reminders in their social feeds.

---

## The Big Idea

**"Blue Fork to Your Door"** makes the delivery bowl the hero. The creative platform shows the honest moment delivery is for — the couch, the desk, the group order at 12:40 — and the Blue Fork bowl arriving intact, fresh, and exactly as built. Every asset ends with the same instruction: find Blue Fork on your delivery app. Simple, consistent, and repeated across every channel in the plan.

## Channel & Media Plan

| # | Channel | Role in Plan |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Delivery-platform sponsored listings | Win visibility and conversion inside the apps where orders happen |
| 2 | Paid social — short-form video (Reels, TikTok) | Build delivery association and drive app-to-app clicks |
| 3 | CRM — push, email & in-app | Convert existing guests into first delivery orders |
| 4 | Launch promotions & delivery offers | Reduce first-order friction with time-limited offers |

### 1. Delivery-Platform Sponsored Listings
Sponsored placement inside the major delivery platforms during peak dinner and lunch windows, focused on the metros with the densest store coverage. Placement inside the app is the single most direct lever on delivery volume, and consistent menu photography and ratings hygiene support conversion once the listing is seen.

### 2. Paid Social — Short-Form Video
Geo-targeted Reels and TikTok flights showing the delivery experience: the seal on the bag, the components staying separate, the mix moment on the couch. Short-form video performs well for food and keeps Blue Fork present in the feeds our audience scrolls while deciding what to order.

### 3. CRM — Push, Email & In-App
Blue Fork's existing guests are the easiest delivery converts. Push and email flows introduce delivery availability, a "first delivery on us" fee offer, and reorder reminders after each delivery. In-app banners route loyalty members to their preferred platform.

### 4. Launch Promotions & Delivery Offers
Time-limited offers break down the first-order barrier: free-delivery windows in launch weeks, a bundled group-order deal for office carts, and platform-coordinated promotions during the flight's two heavy-up periods.
