# Velura Fragrance-Free Deodorant Launch Teaser — DRAFT

**Prepared by:** Ryo Tanabe, Junior Planner, Meridian & Craft
**Client:** Velura Skin
**Campaign window:** August 1 – October 31, 2027
**Budget:** $170,000

---

## Campaign Title

**"The Quiet One" — Velura Deodorant Teaser Program**

---

## Opening

Introducing the teaser program for Velura's first deodorant: an $18 fragrance-free stick built on the brand's barrier-first formulation approach. This campaign runs ahead of the retail launch and has one job — build a waitlist and a base of aware, warmed-up buyers before the product ships. The teaser positions the stick as the deodorant with nothing to hide: no fragrance, no masking, no noise.

---

## Target Audience

| Segment | Description |
|---|---|
| Primary | Clean-beauty buyers under 35 skeptical of natural deodorants |
| Secondary | Existing Velura face-care customers |

Many in this audience have tried a natural deodorant before and been disappointed, so skepticism is the starting condition. Familiarity with the Velura name should help open the conversation.

---

## The Big Idea

**"The Quiet One"** leans into restraint. While the category shouts scents and slogans, Velura's stick says almost nothing — matte packaging, plain typography, a simple promise of skin-first formulation. The teaser builds curiosity around what a deodorant looks like when it has nothing to cover up.

---

## Channel & Media Plan

| Channel | Role |
|---|---|
| Paid social — short-form video | Teaser films and "why fragrance-free" explainers on Reels and TikTok |
| Creator partnerships | Skincare creators tease early access and share first impressions |
| Email teaser & waitlist | Existing customers get the reveal first and claim waitlist spots |

Social builds intrigue, creators lend credibility with a skeptical audience, and the waitlist converts attention into a launch-day buyer file.

## KPIs

- Waitlist signups ahead of retail launch
- Engagement rates on teaser and reveal content
- Email open and click rates on the reveal sequence
- Share of existing customers joining the waitlist

---

## Why This Will Work

The deodorant category is loud, and quiet stands out. The teaser structure gives the launch a running start, the waitlist gives the client a measurable asset before a single stick ships, and the brand's fragrance-free credibility carries naturally into a category where fragrance is the problem.
