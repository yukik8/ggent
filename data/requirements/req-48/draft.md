## Campaign Title

**"Unwrap the Wag" — the Pawstead Holiday Gift Box**

---

## Opening: The Product

The Pawstead Holiday Gift Box is a $39 limited-edition gift set built around Pawstead's vet-formulated refrigerated meals. The box contains a curated selection of the following components:

| Component | Description |
|---|---|
| Fresh meal sampler | Four single-serve refrigerated meal packs across Pawstead's top recipes |
| Holiday topper | A seasonal turkey-and-cranberry meal topper, exclusive to the box |
| Keepsake item | A branded bandana with a holiday pattern |
| Gift card insert | A discount card the recipient can redeem toward a full subscription |

The box ships in festive cold-pack packaging that photographs well and looks great under a tree or on a counter. It is also designed to fit into the grocery fridge sets Pawstead has been rolling into, so a version of the box can sit alongside the regular meal packs during the holiday window.

The gift box does two jobs at once. First, it is a giftable product at an accessible price point for the holiday season. Second, every box is a sampling vehicle, because the discount insert gives the recipient a natural next step into the subscription. Pet products are a very popular gift category, and fresh dog food is one of the fastest-growing parts of the pet space, so the timing for a giftable format is strong.

---

## Target Audience

Our primary audience is **gift-giving pet parents**, people buying for their own dog as a holiday treat, and people buying for the dog owners in their lives (friends, family members, coworkers with office-famous dogs).

| Segment | Description | Role in campaign |
|---|---|---|
| Self-gifting dog parents | Owners who treat the dog as family and include it in holiday rituals | Core buyer, most likely to convert to subscription |
| Friends & family of dog owners | Gift shoppers who know the recipient loves their dog | Volume driver during peak gifting weeks |
| Existing Pawstead subscribers | Current customers who may buy boxes as gifts for others | Low-cost early sales via CRM |

Many dog owners buy their pets holiday gifts, and social content about dogs performs very well during the season, so this audience is naturally receptive to a giftable fresh-food product.

---

## The Big Idea

**"Unwrap the Wag"** positions the gift box as the one holiday present the dog actually wants. Creative centers on the unboxing moment, the dog reacting to the box being opened, filmed by real owners. It is a simple, endlessly repeatable format that creators and customers can recreate, and it puts the product's freshness and premium feel on camera without needing a hard sell. Every asset ends on the same line: *This year, unwrap the wag.*

## Channel & Media Plan

| # | Channel | Role |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Paid social (Instagram Reels, TikTok) | Primary awareness and conversion driver with unboxing-reaction video |
| 2 | Creator partnerships | Pet and rescue-dog creators seeding authentic unboxing content |
| 3 | Email & CRM to existing subscribers | Early access window and gift-a-box push to the current base |
| 4 | Grocery fridge-aisle POS | Holiday shelf-talkers and a gift-box fridge presence in partner stores |
| 5 | Digital gift guides | Placements in online holiday gift guides for pets and lifestyle |

### 1. Paid Social
Short-form video is very effective for pet content, and dogs reliably stop the scroll. We will run a conversion-focused flight of Reels and TikTok ads built from unboxing-reaction footage, retargeting site visitors as the shipping cutoff approaches.

### 2. Creator Partnerships
We will gift boxes to a roster of pet creators, prioritizing vet-tech creators and rescue-dog accounts whose audiences trust them. Their unboxing videos become the backbone of our paid creative.

### 3. Email & CRM
Existing subscribers already love the product, so a subscriber-exclusive early access window should generate strong early sales and word of mouth at essentially no media cost.

### 4. Grocery Fridge-Aisle POS
Holiday shelf-talkers and gift-box facings in Pawstead's grocery fridge sets catch shoppers who saw the campaign on social. Seeing the box in a real fridge aisle makes it feel like real food, not just a startup promotion.

### 5. Digital Gift Guides
Pet gift guides get a lot of traffic in November and December, and inclusion adds credibility for gift shoppers who don't know the brand yet.
