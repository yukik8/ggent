## Campaign Title

**"The First 30 Club"**

---

## Opening: The Problem We Are Actually Solving

Kinetiq does not have a motivation problem; it has an activation cliff. Members completing 8 or more workouts in their first month show 81% 12-month retention, versus 34% for those completing fewer (ref-11). That single threshold — eight workouts in thirty days — is worth more to lifetime membership revenue than anything Kinetiq can do in month two onward, and right now crossing it is left entirely to the member. The economics of fixing it are lopsided in our favor: median ROI for retention marketing is $4.60 per dollar spent, versus $2.20 for prospecting (ref-16).

The First 30 Club is our answer to that cliff. It takes the $39/month membership's most dangerous month and gives it structure: a streak tracker, milestone badges, a live class series, swag drops, creator-hosted livestreams, and a community challenge, all engineered around one number — eight workouts before day 30. Every tactic below is judged by whether it moves a new member toward that threshold.

---

## Target Audience

New Kinetiq members in their first month of ownership — the household that just unboxed a rower and is deciding, in real time, whether it becomes a habit or furniture. They skew 28-45, many are first-time connected-fitness owners, and their motivation is highest in week one and most fragile in weeks two and three. This is the cohort standing on the 81%-versus-34% retention line (ref-11), which is why the program spends nothing on anyone else.

---

## The Big Idea

Because the core problem is the eight-workout threshold, your first month on the rower shouldn't feel like homework, it should feel like joining something. The First 30 Club gives every new member a visible 30-day journey with moments of celebration built in. Day 1 gets a welcome video and a starter class plan. Day 10 unlocks a badge. Day 20 unlocks a swag drop entry. Day 30 makes you a Club alum with a shareable milestone card — and note that the milestone map (badges at 4, 8, 12, and 20 sessions) deliberately front-loads celebration around the 8-workout retention gate (ref-11).

## Tactics

Each tactic below maps back to the activation cliff.

### 1. In-App Streak Challenge & Badges
Because the core problem is workout count in month one, the app track makes the count visible and rewarding: streak counter, workout milestones at 4, 8, 12, and 20 sessions, and a screenshot-worthy finisher badge. The 8-session badge is the strategic center of the entire program (ref-11).

### 2. Live "First 30" Class Series
Beginner-friendly live classes programmed specifically for the first month — short sessions, form-first coaching, and shout-outs for members hitting milestones — because a confused or intimidated week one is how members end up under eight workouts. Replays join the on-demand library.

### 3. Lifecycle Email & Push
A day-by-day message arc: welcome, first-class nudge, week-two slump rescue, milestone celebrations, and a day-30 congratulations that rolls into month-two programming. This is the highest-leverage channel we own for this job: lifecycle-triggered emails average a 38% open rate, versus 21% for standard campaign sends (ref-16).

### 4. SMS Nudges
Short, timely texts for scheduled-workout reminders and milestone moments. SMS earns its budget line on conversion behavior: SMS opt-in subscribers convert at 2.4x the rate of email-only subscribers on time-limited offers (ref-16), and a tonight-at-7 workout reminder is precisely a time-limited call to action.

### 5. Creator-Hosted Onboarding Livestreams
Monthly livestreams where a fitness creator and a Kinetiq coach row the beginner series together and answer new-member questions live. Because the fragile weeks are two and three, the livestream calendar is timed to catch each cohort's slump window and make it social instead of solitary.

### 6. Milestone Rewards
Physical swag drops (water bottle at 8 workouts, hoodie lottery for finishers) plus partner perks. The 8-workout reward placement is not decoration — it pays out exactly at the retention threshold (ref-11), and unboxing moments feed the community challenge hashtag.
