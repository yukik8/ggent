## Campaign Title

**"Clean, In Person"**

---

## Opening: The Problem We Are Solving

Velura has a discovery problem, not a product problem. Specialty retail (e.g. beauty chains) accounts for 38% of clean-beauty discovery, versus 24% for brand-owned sites (ref-02) — which means the single largest place new clean-beauty customers are found is a place Velura, as a DTC-only brand, has never existed. Worse, the shoppers Velura is about to meet there are skeptical by default: 58% say they distrust ads that make claims without visible evidence or numbers (ref-07), and Gen-Z respondents check an average of 2.7 sources before a first purchase over $50 (ref-07) — a threshold Velura's $28-$46 hero SKUs sit right around.

This launch is our answer to that specific problem: make Velura's first shelf appearance the moment skeptical in-store discoverers decide it is trustworthy. Every tactic below is selected for its ability to convert in-aisle discovery into a verified, evidence-backed first purchase — not just to announce that Velura has arrived. The category rewards getting this right: the US clean-beauty segment reached $11.6B in 2024, up 8.4% YoY while total prestige skincare grew only 2.9% (ref-02).

---

## Target Audience

In-store beauty shoppers aged 20-35 discovering clean-beauty brands — specifically the ingredient-verifiers among them. 62% of skincare buyers under 30 say ingredient transparency is a top-3 purchase driver (ref-02), and this campaign is built for exactly that shopper: the one standing in the aisle, phone in hand, checking her 2.7 sources (ref-07) before she puts a $32 jar in her basket. We win her by making the shelf itself the most credible source she checks.

---

## The Big Idea: "The Open Shelf"

Velura's whole brand is transparency, so our shelf presence should be too. **The Open Shelf** turns the Velura in-store display into a giant, readable ingredient label. Every fixture, endcap, and shelf-talker literally shows what is inside the jar, with nothing hidden and nothing abbreviated. Testers sit in an open tray shaped like the label itself, inviting shoppers to try before they buy.

Because the core problem is skeptical in-store discovery, the Open Shelf is not decoration — it is the evidence. It puts the ingredient proof at the exact moment and place of discovery, answering the 58% who distrust unevidenced claims (ref-07) before they even reach for their phone. The same visual system extends to social content, creator videos, and out-of-home near stores, so a shopper who sees Velura online will immediately recognize the display in the aisle.

---

## Channel & Media Plan

Each channel below maps directly to the discovery-and-trust problem named above.

1. **In-store activation & sampling** — Branded Open Shelf fixtures, endcaps, and tester bars in launch-wave stores, supported by trained brand ambassadors on peak weekend traffic days. Because discovery happens on this shelf (38% of clean-beauty discovery, ref-02), this is the primary conversion surface, not a support tactic.

2. **Short-form video / paid social** — Reels, TikTok, and Shorts content showing the Open Shelf, ingredient close-ups, and "first time seeing Velura in real life" moments. 71% of Gen-Z respondents discover new brands primarily through short-form video (ref-07), short-form video ads average 2.4x the completion rate of static feed ads (ref-06), and Instagram Reels inventory is efficient at a $9.40 average CPM (ref-06) — making this the cheapest way to pre-seed shelf recognition before a shopper ever enters the store.

3. **Creator partnerships** — A roster of skincare creators visits stores, films honest first-impression reviews, and posts shelf-location content. We deliberately weight toward niche creators: 64% of Gen-Z consumers have purchased a product recommended by a niche creator with under 100K followers (ref-07), and creator-partnership posts deliver 31% lower cost-per-engagement than brand-owned posts (ref-06). These reviews become source #2 and #3 in the shopper's pre-purchase check.

4. **Retail launch events** — Opening-week events in flagship locations with skin consultations, mini-facials, and gift-with-purchase. Because the problem is trust at first encounter, events use problem-solution storytelling rather than discounts as the lead message — only 22% of Gen-Z respond to discount-led messaging as a first touch, while 49% respond to problem-solution storytelling (ref-07).

5. **Out-of-home near stores** — Transit and street-level placements within walking distance of launch stores, using the Open Shelf visual language to drive foot traffic to the shelf where discovery actually converts.

## KPIs

- Sell-through in launch-wave stores, benchmarked weekly against the retailer's clean-beauty set average
- Trial-to-purchase conversion at sampled stores versus unsampled control stores
- Short-form video click-through rate targeting the 2.6% top-quartile benchmark, versus the 1.2% cross-vertical average (ref-06)
- Cost-per-engagement on creator posts held at least 31% below brand-owned post benchmarks (ref-06)
- Aided and unaided awareness lift among beauty-chain shoppers aged 20-35, measured pre/post
- Retailer commitment to expanded shelf placement after the launch window
