## Campaign Title

**"$149. That's It." — Clearbrook Transparent Pricing**

## Opening: The Problem We Are Actually Solving

For cash-pay and high-deductible patients, the barrier to care is not the symptom — it is the unknown bill. These patients functionally pay out of pocket, so every clinic visit is a financial gamble, and the rational response is to delay care or avoid it entirely. That avoidance is Clearbrook's addressable market, and the fix is measured: transparent cash-pay pricing pages increase new-patient bookings by 34% for urgent-care services (ref-15). The pool is also large and self-renewing — 41% of US adults under 45 have no primary-care physician, up from 33% in 2019 (ref-15), which means episodic urgent-care need is exactly where these patients meet the system.

Clearbrook's $149 flat visit is our answer to that specific problem: one printed number a stretched household can weigh against toughing it out. This campaign puts the number everywhere the fear of the bill lives. **$149. That's It.**

## Target Audience

Cash-pay and high-deductible patients in the Clearbrook footprint — people for whom an unknown bill is scarier than the symptom, and for whom a printed price is the single most persuasive thing we can show. They research before they go: this cohort also selects providers digitally, where 64% of patients under 40 choose a clinic based on online booking availability before considering reputation (ref-15), so every price message lands one tap from the booking flow. They are evidence-hungry by disposition — 58% say they distrust ads that make claims without visible evidence or numbers (ref-07) — which is why a verifiable flat price outperforms any slogan we could write.

## The Big Idea

The price is the campaign. Because the core problem is bill uncertainty, we do not decorate the message with care-quality claims; we repeat the most refreshing number in healthcare until it is the first thing anyone in the footprint thinks when they think urgent care. The culture is already primed — bill-shock stories are a running genre across social platforms — and Clearbrook is the only player in the footprint with a number simple enough to put on a billboard.

## Tactic Stack

Each tactic below maps directly to the bill-uncertainty problem named above.

### 1. Short-Form Video & Social
Because bill anxiety is narrated in short-form video, that is where we answer it. We flip the bill-reveal format: Clearbrook patients reveal a bill with no surprise — receipt-cam close-ups, "guess what this visit cost" hooks, a reactive calendar that answers each week's billing story with the flat price. The channel economics hold: Instagram Reels average a $9.40 CPM and TikTok $7.80 (ref-06), short-form video ads average 2.4x the completion rate of static feed ads (ref-06), and we cap exposures because frequency above 4 exposures per week correlates with a 18% drop in engagement rate (ref-06).

### 2. Out-of-Home Price Billboards
Because the problem is an unknown number, the board is a known one: "$149. That's It." near highways, competitor clinics, and ER exits, with a QR code dropping straight into booking. This is the 34% transparent-pricing conversion lever (ref-15) rendered at highway scale, and boards minimal enough to be photographed and posted extend the buy organically.

### 3. Paid Search & Geofenced Display
Because price-anxious patients literally search the problem — "urgent care cost," "how much is an urgent care visit" — always-on search meets the question with the answer, and geofenced display around competitors and ERs serves the comparison at the moment of decision. We benchmark against the 1.2% cross-vertical paid social average CTR and its 2.6% top quartile (ref-06).

### 4. Creator Partnerships
Because a price claim is most credible from someone auditing it, personal-finance and "adulting" creators run real cost comparisons — ER vs. urgent care vs. waiting it out — featuring the flat price. The audience already buys this way: 64% of Gen-Z respondents have purchased a product recommended by a niche creator with under 100K followers (ref-07), and creator-partnership posts deliver 31% lower cost-per-engagement than brand-owned posts (ref-06).

## Why This Will Work

This plan attacks the measured reason price-sensitive patients stay home — bill uncertainty — with the one asset competitors cannot copy overnight: a flat, printable price, deployed against a proven 34% booking lever (ref-15).
