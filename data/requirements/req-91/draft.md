## Campaign Title

**"$149. That's It." — Clearbrook Transparent Pricing**

## Opening

Price-transparency content is having a massive moment right now. "What I paid for my ER visit" storytimes are pulling huge numbers on TikTok, medical-bill reaction videos are a whole genre, and Reddit threads about surprise billing go viral weekly. Clearbrook's $149 flat urgent-care visit is tailor-made for this wave, and this campaign rides it with a full stack of the formats that are working right now.

We lead with the number. Everywhere. **$149. That's It.** on billboards, in Reels hooks, in creator scripts, on the landing page. The number is the brand.

## Tactic Stack

### 1. Short-Form Video & Social
Bill-reveal content is the hottest format in health right now, so we flip it: instead of a horror story, Clearbrook patients reveal a bill with no surprise. Receipt-cam close-ups, "guess what this visit cost" hooks, duet-bait formats. We also jump on trending audio as it rotates and keep a reactive content calendar so we can ride whatever billing story is blowing up that week.

### 2. Out-of-Home Price Billboards
Minimal boards, giant number. "$149. That's It." near highways, urgent-care competitors, and hospital ER exits. QR code drops straight into the booking flow. OOH photos of the boards themselves become social content — boards designed to be posted.

### 3. Paid Search & Geofenced Display
Always-on coverage of "urgent care cost," "how much is an urgent care visit," and symptom searches, plus geofenced display around competitor clinics and ERs serving price-comparison messaging in the moment.

### 4. Creator Partnerships
Personal-finance and "adulting" creators are the perfect messengers — they already make cost-breakdown content. We brief a roster of them to run real cost comparisons (ER vs. urgent care vs. toughing it out) featuring the flat price. Micro-creators keep it authentic and give us a stream of clips to boost.

## Target Audience

Cash-pay and high-deductible patients in the Clearbrook footprint. These are people who functionally pay out of pocket for care, so an unknown bill is scarier than the symptom, and a printed price is the single most persuasive thing we can show them. They skew younger, mobile-first, and financially stretched, and they research costs before they go anywhere.

## The Big Idea

The price is the campaign. No taglines about care quality, no stock photos of stethoscopes — just the most refreshing number in healthcare, repeated until it is the first thing anyone in the footprint thinks when they think urgent care.
