## Campaign Title

**"Below the Chin" — Velura Body Care, Fragrance-Free from Head to Toe**

---

## Opening: The Problem We Are Actually Solving

Velura has two measured problems this line must solve at once. First, retention: the average DTC skincare brand loses 44% of first-time buyers before a second order (ref-02), and a brand anchored to one face franchise gives lapsing buyers nothing else to stay for. Second, discovery: specialty retail (e.g. beauty chains) accounts for 38% of clean-beauty discovery, versus 24% for brand-owned sites (ref-02), which means a line that does not perform in-aisle does not get discovered at all.

Velura Body Care — three fragrance-free SKUs at $26-$34 — is our answer to both. For existing customers, it multiplies the reasons to reorder; for new shoppers, it gives the specialty shelf a second Velura facing. The market rewards the move: the US clean-beauty segment reached $11.6B in 2024, up 8.4% YoY while total prestige skincare grew only 2.9% (ref-02). Every tactic below maps back to retention or shelf discovery, the two named problems.

---

## Target Audience

| Segment | Description |
|---|---|
| Primary | Sensitive-skin shoppers aged 20-40 extending face routines to body |
| Secondary | New-to-brand sensitive-skin shoppers who start with body care |

This audience buys on verification: 62% of skincare buyers under 30 say ingredient transparency is a top-3 purchase driver (ref-02), and 58% say they distrust ads that make claims without visible evidence or numbers (ref-07). The creative shows full ingredient lists on screen, every time.

---

## The Big Idea

**"Below the Chin"** points at the double standard: you check every ingredient on your face cream, then use whatever on the rest. Because the core problem is giving buyers more reasons to stay, the routine-extension frame matters strategically — it positions body care as the next step of an existing Velura habit, not a new decision.

---

## Channel & Media Plan

| Channel | Role |
|---|---|
| Paid social — short-form video | Routine-extension content and ingredient-transparency explainers on Reels and TikTok |
| Creator partnerships | Sensitive-skin and eczema-adjacent creators trial the line over several weeks |
| Specialty retail launch activation | Launch displays, testers, and staff training at retail partners |
| CRM & email | Existing face-line customers get first access and bundle offers |

Because discovery lives in-feed — 71% of Gen-Z respondents discover new brands primarily through short-form video (ref-07) — paid social carries the double-standard idea at a $9.40 average Reels CPM and $7.80 on TikTok (ref-06). Creators supply the proof this audience demands: 64% have purchased a product recommended by a niche creator with under 100K followers (ref-07), at creator-partnership economics of 31% lower cost-per-engagement than brand-owned posts (ref-06). Because 38% of clean-beauty discovery happens at specialty retail (ref-02), the launch activation earns the shelf, not just the feed. And CRM attacks the retention problem directly: lifecycle-triggered emails average a 38% open rate, versus 21% for standard campaign sends (ref-16), with retention marketing returning a median $4.60 per dollar spent, versus $2.20 for prospecting (ref-16).
