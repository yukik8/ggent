## Campaign Title

**"Feed the Whole Pack"**

---

## Opening: The Problem We Are Actually Solving

Pawstead's core economic problem is that subscribers leave before the subscription pays back: the average fresh pet food subscriber churns after 4.2 months; top-quartile brands retain subscribers past 9 months (ref-09). Multi-dog households currently mixing food brands are the highest-leverage fix available — a $190/month two-dog bundle deepens the household's commitment, consolidates the fridge footprint that drives cancellations, and doubles the cost of switching away. The buyer is already predisposed: 63% of dog owners under 40 rank ingredient quality above price when choosing a food brand (ref-09), and the segment is growing underneath us — US pet food retail sales reached $58.1B in 2024, with fresh/refrigerated the fastest-growing segment at 17% YoY (ref-09).

The Multi-Dog Household Bundle is our answer to that specific problem: individually portioned meals per dog, one combined delivery, one fridge footprint, and savings versus two separate plans. Every tactic below maps back to retention and household expansion, not just reach.

---

## Target Audience

Two-or-more-dog households currently mixing food brands. They discover brands where the rest of the under-40 cohort does — 57% of pet owners under 40 discover new pet brands through social video featuring real pets (ref-09) — and they are skeptical of unsupported claims: 58% say they distrust ads that make claims without visible evidence or numbers (ref-07). Our creative therefore shows real packs, real fridges, and the real per-month math.

---

## The Big Idea

**"Feed the Whole Pack"** celebrates multi-dog life — synchronized dinner dances, double zoomies — landing one promise: one plan, every bowl, no chaos. Because the core problem is the 4.2-month churn cliff (ref-09), the creative dwells on the exact friction that triggers cancellation — the crowded fridge, the mismatched deliveries — and shows the bundle removing it.

---

## Channel & Media Plan

### 1. Paid Social — Short-Form Video
Because the core problem is winning committed households and 57% of pet owners under 40 discover new pet brands through social video featuring real pets (ref-09), pack-life video leads the plan. Instagram Reels average a $9.40 CPM and TikTok $7.80 (ref-06), and short-form video ads average 2.4x the completion rate of static feed ads (ref-06).

### 2. Creator Partnerships
Multi-dog creator households document switching their whole pack. Creator-partnership posts deliver 31% lower cost-per-engagement than brand-owned posts (ref-06), and 64% of Gen-Z respondents have purchased a product recommended by a niche creator with under 100K followers (ref-07) — niche multi-dog accounts over mega-pet influencers.

### 3. CRM & Email to Existing Subscribers
The cheapest bundle household is one we already bill. Lifecycle-triggered emails average a 38% open rate, versus 21% for standard campaign sends (ref-16), and the median ROI for retention marketing is $4.60 per dollar spent, versus $2.20 for prospecting (ref-16) — so triggered "bring the second dog" flows to single-dog subscribers do the heaviest lifting per dollar.

### 4. Paid Search & Display
Captures high-intent multi-dog feeding and subscription-comparison searches at the decision moment.

## Why This Will Work

This plan attacks the number that governs Pawstead's economics — churn at 4.2 months versus 9 for the top quartile (ref-09) — by converting the households with the most bowls to fill and the most to lose by leaving. Real-pack video earns the trust, lifecycle CRM converts it at $4.60 per dollar (ref-16), and the bundle locks in the whole pack.
