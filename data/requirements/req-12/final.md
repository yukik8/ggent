# Campaign Proposal: "Velura Refill Ritual" — Loyalty & Replenishment Program

**Client:** Velura Skin
**Product:** Velura loyalty and replenishment program for moisturizer buyers
**Prepared by:** Ryo Tanabe, Junior Planner | Revised by Daniel Okafor, Senior Planner
**Budget:** $180,000
**Timeline:** May 1, 2026 – September 30, 2026

---

## 1. Opening: Introducing the Refill Ritual

Velura Skin has built something special with its fragrance-free moisturizer franchise, and the new subscription refill offer is the natural next chapter. At $29 per refill (versus $32 for a one-off jar), customers get their favorite moisturizer delivered on their own schedule, backed by the same clean-beauty promise that made them try Velura in the first place. The subscription includes flexible delivery windows, easy pause-and-skip controls, and a members-only feel that turns a repurchase into a ritual.

This is a strong product offer. The $3 savings is meaningful, the refill mechanic is convenient, and the clean-ingredient story remains best in class. Our campaign, "Velura Refill Ritual," will bring this offer to Velura's existing buyers and make subscribing feel like the obvious, rewarding choice. Skincare fans love a good routine, and a subscription is the routine that ships itself. As a general industry rule of thumb, retaining an existing customer costs a fraction of acquiring a new one, which makes this program an efficient use of budget.

## 2. Target Audience

Our primary audience is existing Velura first-time buyers at risk of lapsing before their second purchase. Secondarily, we will reach anyone in Velura's customer file who has purchased the moisturizer at least once, plus lookalike skincare enthusiasts with similar behavior. Young consumers respond well to brands that reward loyalty, and this generation of skincare buyers appreciates subscription convenience. Many first-time buyers simply forget to reorder, so a well-timed nudge should convert a lot of them.

## 3. The Big Idea: "Your Skin Doesn't Take Months Off"

Skincare works when it is consistent, and running out of moisturizer breaks the streak. The Refill Ritual campaign positions the subscription as the way to never break your streak. Creative will show the small daily moment of applying Velura, framed as a ritual worth protecting, with the refill arriving just before the jar runs low. The tone is warm, minimal, and a little indulgent, consistent with Velura's existing brand world.

Supporting hooks:
- "Ritual, uninterrupted." — hero line for CRM and social
- Milestone rewards at the 2nd, 4th, and 6th refill (samples, early access, member pricing on new SKUs)
- A refer-a-friend layer to capture word of mouth, which remains powerful in beauty

## 4. Channel & Media Plan

We will focus on channels where we can speak directly to people who already know Velura:

1. **Email / CRM lifecycle program** — a 5-touch flow triggered by first purchase, timed around the typical usage window of a 50ml jar. Email is a strong, cost-efficient retention channel.
2. **Paid social retargeting (Instagram & TikTok)** — short-form video retargeting existing buyers and site visitors with the Refill Ritual story. Social performs well for beauty audiences, and short-form video is particularly engaging.
3. **SMS reminders** — opt-in "jar running low?" nudges with one-tap subscribe.
4. **Package inserts & unboxing refresh** — every one-off jar shipped during the campaign carries a Refill Ritual insert with a QR code and the $29 offer.
5. **Loyalty rewards layer** — milestone gifts and member perks funded by the campaign so staying subscribed feels generous.

## 7. KPIs

- **Primary:** subscription sign-ups from the existing buyer file
- Second-purchase rate among first-time buyers, measured against the pre-campaign baseline
- Subscriber retention at 60 and 90 days
- Email click-through rate across the lifecycle flow
- Retargeting click-through rate and cost per sign-up
- Incremental repeat revenue during the campaign window

We are confident the Refill Ritual will meaningfully grow Velura's subscriber base. The offer is strong, the audience already loves the product, and the creative gives them a reason to commit. Loyalty programs are a proven driver of long-term value in beauty, and this one has the right ingredients.
