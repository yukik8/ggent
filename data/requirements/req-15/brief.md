# Client Request — Fall Foliage Rail-and-Trail Campaign

**To:** Meridian & Craft

**Date received:** 2026-07-01
**Subject:** RFP — 2026 fall foliage rail-and-trail visitor campaign

Folks,

Fall planning time. This one is the board's pet project for the year, so read carefully.

## The situation

We've partnered with the heritage railway and the trails coalition to launch a Rail-and-Trail Day Pass: $49 per person for a full day of scenic rail travel plus curated trail access, timed to peak leaf season. It comes with a foliage map, trailhead shuttle access, and partner discounts at cafes and outfitters along the route. Two years of negotiation went into this and the product is genuinely lovely — the rail line runs straight through the best color in the valley.

## What we want

A campaign that makes the Rail-and-Trail Pass the fall trip everyone's seeing this year. We want the valley's foliage all over the feeds of young travelers — beautiful film, creators, the works — and we want that attention converting into pass sales. Awareness first, bookings behind it.

## Audience

Travelers under 35 who find their trips through short-form travel video. Couples and small friend groups within driving distance, mostly.

Between us: the reason the board pushed so hard for this pass is our lodging partners. Even at peak color, October weekday occupancy across the valley runs under 50%, and the innkeepers have been loud about it at every meeting. The pass was supposed to give people a reason to stay over midweek, though you wouldn't know it from how we've talked about it publicly. Anyway — context.

## Budget and timeline

- **Budget:** $330,000 total (public funds, full line-item accounting).
- **Timeline:** In market 2026-08-15 through 2026-11-15.

## Success measure

Board target: **12,000 Rail-and-Trail Day Passes sold at $49 per person during the campaign window** — roughly $588K in pass revenue, which is what makes the railway partnership pencil out for year two.

## Deliverables

1. Campaign strategy and creative concept
2. Media and creator plan with audience rationale
3. Itemized budget (procurement rules apply)
4. Measurement plan

## Process

Open RFP per public-sector rules — three agencies responding, you included. Same selection committee as the spring festival review, and I'll be honest, after this spring they are in a numbers mood. Evidence for every claim. Submissions due July 24; decision by late August.

Give them something they can't score down.
