## Campaign Title

**"While You Sleep" — the Overnight Repair Mask Launch**

---

## Opening: The Problem We Are Actually Solving

Velura's problem is not acquiring first-time buyers; it is keeping them. The average DTC skincare brand loses 44% of first-time buyers before a second order (ref-02), and a single-hero-product brand feels that loss hardest — one jar, one purchase, no reason to come back until it runs out. The Overnight Repair Mask is our answer to that specific problem: a second evening-routine step, at $42 per 75ml jar, that turns a one-product customer into a two-product routine.

The structural logic favors us. Moisturizer is the highest-frequency skincare repurchase category at 3.4 units per buyer per year (ref-02), so pairing the mask to the Barrier Repair Moisturizer attaches the new product to the brand's strongest repurchase engine. And the segment can absorb a premium launch: the US clean-beauty segment reached $11.6B in 2024, up 8.4% YoY while total prestige skincare grew only 2.9% (ref-02). Every tactic below is selected for its ability to deepen routines and close the second-order gap, not just spike launch-week sales.

---

## Target Audience

Existing Velura buyers and routine-builders under 35. This audience verifies before it buys — 62% of skincare buyers under 30 say ingredient transparency is a top-3 purchase driver (ref-02) — and discovers in-feed: 71% of Gen-Z respondents discover new brands primarily through short-form video (ref-07). They are also allergic to unproven claims: 58% say they distrust ads that make claims without visible evidence or numbers (ref-07). The creative therefore shows the formula, the routine, and the results over time.

---

## The Big Idea

**"While You Sleep"** romanticizes the eight hours skincare marketing forgets — bedside tables, moonlit bathrooms, the click of the jar closing. Because the core problem is second-order churn, the nightly ritual is the strategy: a product used every single evening keeps Velura physically present between orders, exactly where the 44% first-order loss (ref-02) happens.

---

## Channel & Media Plan

### 1. Paid Social — Short-Form Video
Because routine-builders discover here — 71% discover new brands primarily through short-form video (ref-07) — nighttime-routine content on Reels and TikTok carries launch awareness, at an efficient $9.40 average CPM on Reels and $7.80 on TikTok (ref-06), with short-form video averaging 2.4x the completion rate of static feed ads (ref-06).

### 2. Creator Partnerships
Because this audience trusts evidence over polish, dermatologists and niche skincare creators document real results over weeks. 64% of Gen-Z respondents have purchased a product recommended by a niche creator with under 100K followers (ref-07), and creator-partnership posts deliver 31% lower cost-per-engagement than brand-owned posts (ref-06).

### 3. CRM & Email to Existing Buyers
This is the channel aimed squarely at the named problem. Lifecycle-triggered emails average a 38% open rate, versus 21% for standard campaign sends (ref-16), and the median ROI for retention marketing is $4.60 per dollar spent, versus $2.20 for prospecting (ref-16) — early access, moisturizer-pairing guides, and replenishment-friendly flows convert first-order buyers into two-product routines.

### 4. Specialty Retail Activation
Because specialty retail (e.g. beauty chains) accounts for 38% of clean-beauty discovery, versus 24% for brand-owned sites (ref-02), shelf displays, swatch-friendly testers, and staff education kits make the mask discoverable where the category is actually found.
