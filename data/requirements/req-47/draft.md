## Campaign Title

**"Fresh on Every Walk" — the Three-Metro Out-of-Home Test**

---

## Opening: Pawstead Steps Outside

Introducing Pawstead Fresh to the streets! Pawstead's vet-formulated refrigerated meals are the most exciting thing happening in the dog food fridge right now — real ingredients you can actually see, recipes developed by veterinarians, and gorgeous clear-tray packaging that looks more like a fresh meal kit than a bag of kibble. Until now, Pawstead has told that story almost entirely through screens. This spring, we take it outside, into the neighborhoods, parks, and transit corridors where dog people actually live their lives.

"Fresh on Every Walk" is a bold, colorful out-of-home awareness test across three fantastic dog cities — Chicago, Denver, and Austin. These are vibrant, walkable, dog-obsessed metros where you can't go two blocks without meeting a golden retriever, and they give us the perfect proving ground for whether big, beautiful outdoor creative can make Pawstead a household name beyond the Instagram feed. At an average plan of $120/month, Pawstead is a premium choice, and premium brands deserve a real-world presence that feels as fresh as the food itself.

---

## The Big Idea

Every dog walk is a media moment. Dog owners are out on the sidewalk two or three times a day, rain or shine, and right now those walks are a totally untapped canvas for Pawstead. **"Fresh on Every Walk"** turns the daily walk into the campaign: our creative lives exactly where the walk happens — on the corner, at the park gate, along the transit line — and it always stars real, delightfully expressive dogs mid-walk, mid-zoomies, or mid-dinner, paired with punchy one-liners like *"This good boy eats better than you do"* and *"Fresh food. Fresher walks."*

The magic of the idea is repetition with warmth. A dog owner who passes our park-gate panel every single morning starts to feel like Pawstead is part of the neighborhood, and that neighborly familiarity is exactly what a fridge-aisle brand needs. Out-of-home is such a powerful trust-builder — when people see a brand out in the real world, it just feels bigger and more established.

---

## Target Audience

Our primary audience is urban dog owners in Chicago, Denver, and Austin. These are devoted, modern pet parents who treat their dogs like family members, care deeply about ingredient quality, and are already spending real money on premium food and treats. They walk their dogs daily through dense, walkable neighborhoods, they notice their surroundings, and they talk to each other constantly — at the dog park, on the sidewalk, in neighborhood group chats. Word of mouth travels incredibly fast in dog communities.

Many of these owners have seen fresh dog food advertised online but haven't taken the leap yet. Meeting them repeatedly in the physical world, in their own neighborhood, is a wonderful way to move Pawstead from "that brand I keep scrolling past" to "that brand that's everywhere."

## Channel & Media Plan

### 1. Transit & Large-Format Billboards
Big, unmissable statements in each metro: bulletins along key commuter corridors, transit shelters near dog-dense neighborhoods, and interior cards on high-ridership lines. Transit media is great for building broad awareness quickly, and our vivid dog photography will absolutely shine at this scale.

### 2. Neighborhood & Dog-Park Placements
The heart of the campaign: street-furniture panels, park-gate signage, and hand-painted murals placed within walking distance of the most popular dog parks and dog-friendly blocks in each city. This is where frequency lives — the same owners pass these spots every single day, often with their dogs in tow.

### 3. "Fresh Stop" Street-Team Pop-Ups
On spring weekends, branded Fresh Stop carts appear at high-traffic parks with water bowls, treats, meal samples to take home, and friendly brand ambassadors who can talk through the vet-formulated recipes. Nothing beats a real conversation while the dog is happily crunching a sample.

### 4. Geo-Targeted Social Amplification
A supporting layer of geo-targeted Reels and local social content in the three metros, featuring our outdoor creative and Fresh Stop moments, so the campaign follows people from the sidewalk back to their phones and we capture the buzz the outdoor work creates.
