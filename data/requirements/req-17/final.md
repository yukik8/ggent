## Campaign Title

**"Crunch Time" — the Sunbrook Campus Takeover**

---

## Opening: The Problem We Are Actually Solving

Sunbrook has a pipeline problem: the next generation of snack buyers is forming lifelong brand habits in college towns where Sunbrook is barely present. Only 27% of snack buyers aged 18-34 can name a preferred mid-market crisp brand unprompted (ref-01), and the youngest end of that cohort — students aged 18-24 — is choosing between national brands and store brands every week while private-label share of salty snacks rose from 18% to 23% between 2021 and 2024 (ref-01). The loyalty stakes are measurable: repeat purchase rate for the average mid-market snack brand is 31%, versus 52% for the top-3 national brands (ref-01). If Sunbrook is not the crisp students reach for during their college years, it will spend the next decade paying to win back buyers it could have kept from the start.

The campus-town launch is our answer to that specific problem. Distribution into campus-adjacent grocery, corner marts, and delivery apps across ten Midwest campus towns — with the $3.99 bag for solo study sessions and the $6.49 share size built for dorm hangouts — inserts Sunbrook into the exact occasions where snack habits form. Every tactic below is selected for its ability to build durable brand preference in this cohort, not just a semester of trial.

---

## Target Audience

Primary: college students aged 18-24 in ten Midwest campus towns — the entry point of the 18-34 segment where the 27% unaided-recall gap is measured (ref-01). This audience discovers brands where they scroll: 71% of Gen-Z respondents discover new brands primarily through short-form video (ref-07). They are also skeptical of empty brand talk — 58% say they distrust ads that make claims without visible evidence or numbers, and only 22% respond to discount-led messaging as a first touch while 49% respond to problem-solution storytelling (ref-07). Our creative therefore leads with the shared crunch-moment story, and offers arrive only after the ritual is established.

Secondary: campus-town residents and staff who shop the same stores, plus parents assembling care packages during exam season.

---

## The Big Idea

**"Crunch Time"** turns Sunbrook into the official fuel of the campus grind. Every semester has its crunch moments — midterms, finals week, the big game, the group project due at 9 a.m. — and Sunbrook shows up for all of them. Because the core problem is habit formation, the ritual mechanic matters strategically, not just creatively: tying the share size to recurring semester moments creates repeat purchase occasions on a calendar the audience already lives by, attacking the 31%-versus-52% repeat gap that separates mid-market brands from national brands (ref-01). You order the share size, you pass the bag, you get through it together.

## Channel & Media Plan

Each channel below maps directly to the habit-formation problem named above.

### 1. Paid Social — Short-Form Video (TikTok, Reels, Shorts)
Because the core problem is that students are forming snack habits without Sunbrook in the consideration set, we put the heaviest weight where this cohort discovers brands: 71% of Gen-Z respondents discover new brands primarily through short-form video (ref-07). The mix is also efficient — TikTok averages a $7.80 CPM, YouTube Shorts $6.90, and Instagram Reels $9.40 (ref-06) — and the format holds attention, with short-form video ads averaging 2.4x the completion rate of static feed ads (ref-06). We will cap delivery per student deliberately, since frequency above 4 exposures per week correlates with a 18% drop in engagement rate (ref-06).

### 2. Campus Micro-Creator Partnerships
Student creators make Crunch Time feel like campus culture rather than an ad campaign — and the evidence says small is better: 64% of Gen-Z respondents have purchased a product recommended by a niche creator with under 100K followers (ref-07). The economics reinforce the choice, as creator-partnership posts deliver 31% lower cost-per-engagement than brand-owned posts (ref-06). We recruit per-campus rosters of meme accounts, vloggers, and club-sports personalities whose content feeds the paid flight.

### 3. Delivery-App Placements & Late-Night Bundles
Because habit formation happens at the point of order, we defend the digital shelf students actually use at midnight. Retail media is the proven conversion layer for the category — grocery retail media campaigns average ROAS of 3.8x for snack and beverage brands, and on-site sponsored product placements drive 15% average lift in units per store per week (ref-08). The "Crunch Time Bundle" (share size + drink pairing) surfaces in evening ordering windows, converting late-night cravings into Sunbrook orders.

### 4. Campus Sampling & Event Activations
A habit starts with a first taste, and in-store sampling lifts trial conversion by 19% on average in grocery channels (ref-01). Crunch Time sampling stations at campus-adjacent grocery stores, student unions, basketball watch parties, and finals-week "fuel stations" in library lobbies put the product in hands at the exact moments the campaign celebrates — then the share size closes the loop on the next store run.

### 5. Crunch Time Club — SMS & Email
Because the repeat-purchase gap is the problem, we need an owned channel that re-triggers the ritual each crunch moment. SMS is the right lever for a deal-drop audience: SMS opt-in subscribers convert at 2.4x the rate of email-only subscribers on time-limited offers (ref-16), and lifecycle-triggered emails average a 38% open rate, versus 21% for standard campaign sends (ref-16). Club sends are timed to midterms, game weekends, and finals — the calendar the habit lives on.

## Why Sunbrook Wins

This plan does not just put crisps in college towns; it attacks the measured cause of Sunbrook's long-term squeeze — a recall gap forming at the exact age when snack loyalties lock in — with a ritual engineered for repeat purchase. Short-form video and student creators build the name, sampling converts it at first taste, delivery placements own the late-night order, and the Club re-triggers the habit every crunch moment, each tactic answerable to the same named problem.
