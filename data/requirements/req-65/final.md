## Campaign Title

## Opening: The Problem We Are Actually Solving

Skillharbor's prospects do not have a motivation problem; they have a diagnosis problem and a completion problem. 66% of career changers aged 25-40 say they cannot identify which skills would actually raise their salary (ref-12) — they are stuck staring at a wall of certificates with no map. And when they do buy, the format fails them: completion rates average 12% for self-paced online courses versus 68% for cohort-based programs (ref-12). The result is a target audience full of capable professionals with a graveyard of abandoned courses and a deep skepticism that the next one will be different.

The Data-Analytics Career-Change Cohort is our answer to both halves of that problem. A live, twelve-week, instructor-led program covering SQL, Tableau, dashboarding, and stakeholder storytelling — with weekly sessions, a Slack community, portfolio projects, and a career-services sprint — attacks the completion problem with the exact format that finishes at 68% (ref-12), while a single, focused analytics track answers the "which skill actually pays" question directly. The market rewards getting this right: the US consumer upskilling market reached $13.7B in 2025, growing 11% YoY (ref-12). It is available as a $2,400 cohort program or through the $199/month all-access subscription. Every tactic below is selected for its ability to convert skeptical, stuck professionals, not just reach them.

---

## Target Audience

Career changers aged 25-40 in stagnant roles — the segment where the guidance gap is measured. These professionals are active on LinkedIn, listen to career and money podcasts on their commutes, and spend evenings in short-form video feeds. Critically, they are evidence-hungry: ads featuring a specific salary-outcome statistic drive 2.1x higher free-trial starts than generic skill-building ads (ref-12), so every asset in this campaign leads with a concrete outcome, never with "future-proof your career."

## The Big Idea

**"Level Up"** frames the cohort as the moment your career finally starts moving again. Because the core problem is diagnosis and completion, the creative does more than inspire — it names the exact skills taught, shows the salary outcomes they map to, and makes the cohort's built-in accountability visible (a start date, live classmates, a finish line). Every asset ends on the same invitation: your cohort starts soon, claim your seat.

## Channel & Media Plan

Each channel below maps directly to the diagnosis-and-completion problem named above.

### 1. Paid Social — Short-Form Video (Reels, TikTok, Shorts)
Because the core problem is that prospects cannot tell which skills pay, our video creative answers it on screen with specific outcomes — the format that drives 2.1x higher free-trial starts (ref-12). The inventory is efficient: Instagram Reels average a $9.40 CPM, TikTok $7.80, and YouTube Shorts $6.90 (ref-06), and short-form video ads average 2.4x the completion rate of static feed ads (ref-06). We cap frequency deliberately, since frequency above 4 exposures per week correlates with an 18% drop in engagement rate (ref-06).

### 2. LinkedIn Paid + Paid Search
LinkedIn is where this audience already confronts its stagnation, so sponsored content and lead-gen placements meet the problem in context. Paid search captures the high-intent tail of prospects actively researching analytics courses.

### 3. Podcast Sponsorships
Host-read spots on career and personal-finance podcasts reach commuters at the moment they think about money and progress. Scripts carry the same salary-outcome specificity as the paid social flight, because that is what stops this audience mid-scroll (ref-12).

### 4. Creator Partnerships
Career-pivot creators who genuinely made the leap into data roles give the salary-outcome claim a face and a receipt. Creator-partnership posts also deliver 31% lower cost-per-engagement than brand-owned posts (ref-06), so this roster feeds the paid flight efficiently.

### 5. Free Intro Workshop Funnel
Because the audience carries a graveyard of unfinished self-paced courses, a live "First Dashboard in 60 Minutes" workshop lets them feel the cohort format — the one that completes at 68% versus 12% for self-paced (ref-12) — before the $2,400 decision. Attendees flow into an email nurture sequence leading up to enrollment deadlines.
