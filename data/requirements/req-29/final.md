## Campaign Title

**"Second Wind" — the Northtrail Trade-In & Upgrade Launch**

## Opening

The Northtrail Trade-In & Upgrade Program makes upgrading an e-bike as simple as riding one. Riders bring in any e-bike — including competitor models — and receive up to $600 in instant trade-in credit toward a new Northtrail commuter model. The process is straightforward: ride in on the old bike, get a fast in-store appraisal, and ride out on a new one the same day. Trade-in credit stacks with Northtrail's battery-swap subscription, so upgraders start with a fresh battery experience from day one.

The program removes the friction that normally stops an upgrade. There is no listing an old bike online, no negotiating with strangers, and no guessing what a three-year-old battery is worth. Northtrail handles the appraisal, refurbishes what can be refurbished, and responsibly recycles the rest, which adds a credible sustainability story to a strong value story. E-bike technology has advanced considerably in range, motor quality, and connectivity over the past few years, and riders on older models are the ones with the most to gain. "Second Wind" invites them back into the category's best experience.

## Target Audience

Our primary target is owners of e-bikes three or more years old in Northtrail's core mid-size city markets. Industry reporting suggests most e-bike batteries lose a noticeable share of their usable range within three to five years of regular commuting, so many of these riders are living with shrinking range and aging tech. They tend to be practical and value-conscious, they respond to a clear deal, and many already recognize the Northtrail brand from their own cities. Most assume upgrading is expensive and complicated — an assumption this program directly removes.

Secondary audiences include lapsed riders whose e-bike is sitting unused in the garage, and early Northtrail owners ready for their next model.

## The Big Idea

**"Second Wind"** turns the aging e-bike from a quiet liability into an asset. The creative centers on the moment a rider learns their old bike is worth real money — and that their next bike is closer than they thought. The hero visual is the side-by-side: the faded, well-worn commuter next to the new Northtrail, with the line "Your old ride just earned you $600 toward this one." Every channel drives to a single action: book a trade-in appraisal.

## Channel & Media Plan

### 1. Email & CRM to the Owner Base
Northtrail's owner list is the campaign's most valuable asset, and email is an effective channel for reaching existing riders. We will build a lifecycle series that identifies owners of older models, presents their estimated trade-in value, and invites them to book an appraisal. Lapsed riders receive a dedicated reactivation flow.

### 2. Paid Social — Short-Form Video
Short-form video suits before-and-after storytelling, and it is where riders discover and share bike content. We will run Reels and TikTok flights featuring real upgraders, appraisal-day moments, and "old bike in, new bike out" transitions that make the program feel like a movement rather than a promotion.

### 3. YouTube & Online Video
E-bike owners research thoroughly, and long-form review content plays a large role in their decisions. We will run pre-roll and partner with respected e-bike review channels to walk through the trade-in process candidly, including what different bikes appraise for. Transparency here builds the trust the program depends on.

### 4. In-Store Trade-In Weekends
Live appraisals make the offer tangible. "Second Wind Weekends" at Northtrail retail locations will combine on-the-spot appraisals, test rides on the new lineup, and same-day upgrade incentives. These events create urgency and supply our social channels with a steady stream of authentic content.

## Why Northtrail Wins

The Trade-In & Upgrade Program is a strong offer wrapped in a story riders want to share. Spring is when riders recommit to their bikes — or decide it is time for a new one — and "Second Wind" meets them in that moment with real money on the table and no hassle. This campaign will make upgrading feel as easy as riding, and position Northtrail as the obvious next bike for riders across its core markets this spring.
