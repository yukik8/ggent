## Opening: The Problem We Are Actually Solving

Stackwise's monetization problem is concentration: revenue depends on converting a small paying minority from a large free base, and today the upgrade ask is scattered across generic prompts instead of aimed at the users most likely to say yes. The economics of focusing on existing users are well established — the median ROI for retention marketing is $4.60 per dollar spent, versus $2.20 for prospecting (ref-16) — and the pattern holds across categories, where loyalty members generate 42% of revenue at brands with mature programs while representing 18% of customers (ref-16). Stackwise's equivalent of that high-value minority is the free user who has stayed past 60 days, a rare and valuable cohort in a category where the average consumer fintech app loses 73% of new users within 30 days of download (ref-13).

"Level Up Your Stack" is our answer to that specific problem. Premium — unlimited custom buckets, advanced auto-save rules, shared buckets, priority support at $8/month or $79/year — is positioned as the graduation step for users who have already proven the habit, and every tactic below is aimed at the 60-day-plus cohort where conversion probability is highest.

## Premium Feature Story

| Free tier | Premium ($8/mo or $79/yr) |
|---|---|
| 3 savings buckets | Unlimited custom buckets |
| Standard auto-save rule | Advanced rules (round-ups, percentage splits, payday triggers) |
| Individual buckets only | Shared buckets with partner or roommate |
| Standard support | Priority support |

Because the core problem is converting proven users rather than acquiring new ones, the pitch is timed to demonstrated engagement — the 60-day mark — not to download.

## Target Audience

Free users active for 60 or more days: regular openers with at least one funded bucket, many already at the three-bucket ceiling. One trust rule carries over from everything we know about this audience — 61% of savers under 30 say they distrust finance apps that lead with investment returns instead of safety (ref-13) — so premium is sold on control and room to grow, never on yield.

## The Big Idea

**"You built the habit. Now build the system."**

We frame premium as graduating within the app, not paying for it. Creative celebrates what the user has already done (their streaks, buckets, totals) and presents premium as the next step. The evidence-first styling is deliberate: 58% of Gen-Z respondents say they distrust ads that make claims without visible evidence or numbers (ref-07), so every upgrade message leads with the user's own numbers.

## Channel Plan

| Channel | Role |
|---|---|
| Lifecycle email & push flights | The workhorse. A staged upgrade journey triggered at day 60+, personalized with the user's own stats |
| In-app upgrade placements | Contextual prompts at natural moments (hitting the bucket limit, completing a goal, payday) |
| Paid social retargeting | Reels and TikTok retargeting of active users with premium feature spotlights |
| Annual-plan promo | A limited spring offer making the $79/year plan the highlighted choice |

Because the core problem is conversion within an existing base, the weight sits on owned lifecycle channels, where triggered messaging measurably outperforms: lifecycle-triggered emails average a 38% open rate, versus 21% for standard campaign sends (ref-16). Paid retargeting adds frequency for non-openers, held deliberately below saturation since frequency above 4 exposures per week correlates with an 18% drop in engagement rate (ref-06). The annual promo creates the conversion moment, and time-limited offers convert best where opt-in is deepest — SMS opt-in subscribers convert at 2.4x the rate of email-only subscribers on time-limited offers (ref-16), so the promo flight prioritizes push-and-SMS-opted users first.

## Why This Will Work

The cheapest premium subscriber Stackwise will ever acquire is already inside the app. This program concentrates spend on the proven 60-day cohort, asks at the moments the product itself creates, and gives the flight one urgent conversion window — with every channel choice justified by the retention-economics numbers rather than habit.
