# Stackwise Premium-Tier Upgrade Program — DRAFT

**Prepared by:** Ryo Tanabe, Junior Planner, Meridian & Craft
**Client:** Stackwise
**Campaign window:** March 1 – June 30, 2026
**Budget:** $280,000

---

## Campaign Title

**"Level Up Your Stack" — the Premium Upgrade Program**

---

## Opening

Stackwise Premium is a strong product with a clear feature story. For $8/month or $79/year, premium users get unlimited custom savings buckets, advanced auto-save rules, shared buckets with a partner or roommate, and priority support. The free tier is generous, which is exactly why the upgrade story works: users who have already built a savings habit on the free tier have concrete, personal reasons to want more room to grow.

This program targets the users most ready to hear that story — free users who have been active for 60 or more days — and puts the premium feature set in front of them through a coordinated set of owned and paid touchpoints.

## Premium Feature Story

| Free tier | Premium ($8/mo or $79/yr) |
|---|---|
| 3 savings buckets | Unlimited custom buckets |
| Standard auto-save rule | Advanced rules (round-ups, percentage splits, payday triggers) |
| Individual buckets only | Shared buckets with partner or roommate |
| Standard support | Priority support |

The upgrade pitch is simple: you have outgrown the starter setup. Industry experience suggests that established users respond far better to upgrade messaging than new users, so timing the pitch to the 60-day mark is the core of the plan.

## Target Audience

Free users active for 60 or more days. These users open the app regularly, have at least one funded bucket, and have demonstrated the habit that premium features extend. Many of them are already hitting the three-bucket ceiling, which makes the unlimited-buckets feature the natural lead message.

## The Big Idea

**"You built the habit. Now build the system."**

We frame premium not as paying for an app but as graduating within it. Creative celebrates what the user has already done on the free tier (their streaks, their buckets, their totals) and presents premium as the obvious next step for someone taking their money seriously.

## Channel Plan

| Channel | Role |
|---|---|
| Lifecycle email & push flights | The workhorse. A staged upgrade journey triggered at day 60+, personalized with the user's own stats |
| In-app upgrade placements | Contextual prompts at natural moments (hitting the bucket limit, completing a goal, payday) |
| Paid social retargeting | Reels and TikTok retargeting of active users with premium feature spotlights |
| Annual-plan promo | A limited spring offer making the $79/year plan the highlighted choice |

Email and in-app messaging are highly effective channels for upgrade programs because the audience already knows and uses the product. Paid retargeting adds frequency for users who don't open messages, and the annual-plan promo gives the program a conversion moment with urgency.

## KPIs

| KPI | Goal |
|---|---|
| Free-to-premium conversion rate (60+ day cohort) | Meaningful lift over baseline |
| Annual-plan share of new premium sign-ups | Majority share during promo window |
| Upgrade journey email engagement | Above program norms |
| Premium churn at 90 days | At or below current premium baseline |

## Why This Will Work

The audience is already sold on Stackwise; they just haven't been asked to upgrade at the right moment with the right story. By concentrating spend on owned channels where the audience already pays attention, timing the pitch to demonstrated engagement, and giving the flight a clear annual-plan conversion moment, this program turns the free tier's generosity into a reliable premium revenue engine.
