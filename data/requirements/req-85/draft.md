## Campaign Title

**"Skip the Aisles" — the Hartline Pickup Push**

---

## Opening: Convenience Commerce Is Eating Retail

Convenience commerce is reshaping how everyone shops, and hardware is next. Same-day expectations are the new normal, curbside behaviors from the grocery world are spilling into every category, and retail apps are training shoppers to expect their order waiting for them. Hartline's free two-hour pickup is a genuinely competitive offer, and "Skip the Aisles" surrounds it with a full-funnel tactic stack: short-form video, geo-targeted search, curbside signage, app push, and a launch promo, all driving one behavior — order ahead, pick up in two hours, get back to the project.

The mechanics are simple. Customers order on the Hartline site or app, get a text when the order is staged (within two hours, guaranteed), and grab it from the dedicated pickup counter or curbside spot without walking a single aisle.

---

## The Tactic Stack

### 1. Short-Form Video
Punchy Reels and TikToks dramatize the two-hour promise: the clock starts, the project keeps moving, the order is waiting. Short-form is the discovery engine for every audience right now.

### 2. Geo-Targeted Search & Display
Search terms like "hardware store near me" and "same day pickup" get owned in every store's radius, with display retargeting for site visitors who browsed but didn't order.

### 3. In-Store & Curbside Signage
Dedicated pickup counters, curbside spots, and directional signage make the service visible to every walk-in customer — the easiest audience to convert is the one already shopping.

### 4. Email & App Push
The full customer list gets a pickup-launch series, and app users get push notifications when their store stages orders in under an hour.

### 5. Launch Promo
A "first pickup free coffee" partnership with a regional coffee chain gives the first order a little ceremony and a reason to try the behavior now.

---

## Target Audience

Convenience-driven DIY shoppers in Hartline's trade areas — busy homeowners and weekend project people who know what they need and don't want to hunt for it. Mid-project shoppers are a key moment: when the project is half-done and a part is missing, two-hour pickup beats a wandering store trip every time. A secondary audience is night-shift and off-hours workers who order after hours and collect on the way home.

---

## Why This Works Now

Pickup behaviors are fully normalized by grocery and big-box training, two-hour staging is a real differentiator at regional scale, and every completed pickup is a fast, positive brand experience. The tactic stack covers discovery (video), intent (search), habit (app push), and trial (promo), so the behavior gets built from every direction at once.

## Why This Will Work

Every trend in retail convenience points the same direction, and Hartline already has the hard part built: real stores, close to customers, with a two-hour promise big e-commerce can't match locally. "Skip the Aisles" stacks the right tactics on top of that advantage and turns Hartline's 40 stores into the fastest way to keep a project moving.
