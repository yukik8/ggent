## Campaign Title

**"Skip the Aisles" — the Hartline Pickup Push**

---

## Opening

Hartline's free two-hour pickup is a genuinely competitive offer, and this campaign is built to make it a habit. Customers order on the Hartline site or app, get a text when the order is staged — within two hours, guaranteed — and grab it from a dedicated pickup counter or curbside spot without walking a single aisle. Convenience expectations set by grocery and big-box retail have reached the hardware category, and industry reporting suggests a majority of shoppers now expect a same-day pickup option from the retailers they use most.

"Skip the Aisles" surrounds the two-hour promise with a coordinated plan — short-form video, geo-targeted search, curbside signage, email and app push, and a launch promo — all driving one behavior: order ahead, pick up in two hours, get back to the project.

---

## Target Audience

Convenience-driven DIY shoppers in Hartline's trade areas — busy homeowners and weekend project people who know what they need and don't want to hunt for it. The mid-project moment is the sharpest use case: when the project is half-done and a part is missing, two-hour pickup beats a wandering store trip every time. A secondary audience is night-shift and off-hours workers who order after hours and collect on the way home.

---

## The Plan

### 1. Short-Form Video
Punchy Reels and TikToks dramatize the two-hour promise: the clock starts, the project keeps moving, the order is waiting at the counter. This carries awareness for the campaign and gives the service a memorable, repeatable visual.

### 2. Geo-Targeted Search & Display
We own search terms like "hardware store near me" and "same day pickup" in every store's radius, with display retargeting for site visitors who browsed but didn't order. This captures shoppers at the exact moment of need.

### 3. In-Store & Curbside Signage
Dedicated pickup counters, curbside spots, and directional signage make the service visible to every walk-in customer — the easiest audience to convert is the one already shopping.

### 4. Email & App Push
The full customer list receives a pickup-launch series, and app users get push notifications highlighting their store's staging speed. Post-purchase emails invite every in-store buyer to try pickup for their next trip.

### 5. Launch Promo
A "first pickup, free coffee" partnership with a regional coffee chain gives the first order a little ceremony and a reason to try the behavior now rather than someday.

## KPIs

- Pickup order volume and share of total transactions
- First-time pickup customers acquired during the flight
- Two-hour staging SLA hit rate
- Repeat pickup orders within 60 days of first order
- Search impression share on "near me" and pickup terms

---

## Why This Will Work

Hartline already has the hard part built: real stores, close to customers, with a two-hour promise that big e-commerce cannot match locally. This plan makes that promise visible everywhere the customer is — the feed, the search bar, the parking lot, the inbox — and gives the first order a reason to happen this week. Each completed pickup is a fast, positive brand experience that earns the next one, and by the end of August the two-hour pickup should be a habit, not a novelty.
