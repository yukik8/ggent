## Campaign Title

**"Row Together" — the Kinetiq Corporate Wellness Program**

## Opening: The Problem We Are Actually Solving

The HR buyer's nightmare is the same as the consumer's: a wellness benefit nobody uses. 71% of connected-fitness intenders cite fear of unused equipment as their top purchase hesitation (ref-11), and a benefits lead multiplies that fear by a thousand employees and a budget line she must defend at renewal. The stakes are measurable — members completing 8 or more workouts in their first month show 81% 12-month retention, versus 34% for those completing fewer (ref-11) — so a corporate program lives or dies on first-month activation, not on signup counts. Row Together is our answer to that specific problem: a $30/month per-employee subsidized membership sold not as a perk but as an engagement guarantee, with activation mechanics and utilization reporting built in. Every tactic below is selected to prove usage to the HR buyer before, during, and after the sale.

## Target Audience

HR and benefits leads at 500+ employee remote-heavy companies. They are not fitness buyers; they are utilization buyers who must justify renewal with engagement data. Secondary: internal wellness champions who carry the program inside each company.

## The Big Idea

**"Row Together"** — team leaderboards, department challenges, and a shared company distance goal. Because the core problem is proving the benefit gets used, the gamified layer is the product's evidence engine: it manufactures the first-month workout volume that decides 12-month retention (ref-11) and generates the utilization reporting HR needs at renewal.

## The Tactics

Each tactic maps back to the utilization-proof problem named above.

### 1. LinkedIn Thought-Leadership Flight
Because HR buyers must defend spend with evidence, the flight leads with retention and activation data, not lifestyle imagery — the sponsored content argues the 8-workout activation case (ref-11) directly to benefits titles.

### 2. HR-Benefits Platform Co-Marketing
Joint webinars, marketplace listings, and co-branded content with Kinetiq's HR-benefits platform partner put Row Together in front of buyers at enrollment-planning time, when budget decisions are actually made.

### 3. Company Challenge Toolkit
Department leaderboards and the "Row the Distance" company goal drive employees past the first-month activation gate that separates 81% retention from 34% (ref-11).

### 4. Pilot Demo Kits
Because trial is the strongest conversion mechanic in this category — free in-home trial programs convert at 46%, versus 11% for standard e-commerce checkout (ref-11) — a 60-day loaner pilot lets HR test real engagement before committing.

### 5. Webinar & Virtual Event Series
Monthly "State of Remote Wellness" webinars nurture the pipeline between flights with pilot data as the draw.

### 6. Case-Study Content Engine
Every pilot becomes utilization proof: engagement stats, testimonials, and HR quotes packaged for sales follow-up. Nurture runs on lifecycle-triggered email flows, which average a 38% open rate versus 21% for standard campaign sends (ref-16).
