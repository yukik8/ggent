# Hartline Tool-Rental Awareness Campaign — DRAFT

**Prepared by:** Emma Lindqvist, Junior Planner, Meridian & Craft
**Client:** Hartline Home & Hardware
**Campaign window:** February 1 – May 31, 2027
**Budget:** $210,000

---

## Campaign Title

**"Rent the Big Stuff"**

---

## Opening: The Best-Kept Secret in the Store

Introducing Hartline's amazing tool-rental counter — honestly one of the best-kept secrets in the entire store. Tucked at the back of every Hartline location is a fantastic fleet of professional-grade equipment: powerful tile saws, heavy-duty floor sanders, post-hole diggers, pressure washers, carpet cleaners, and so much more, all available from just $19 a day. It is an incredible resource, lovingly maintained, and most shoppers walk right past it without ever knowing it exists.

"Rent the Big Stuff" changes that. This campaign shines a big, bright spotlight on the rental counter and tells every occasional DIYer in the trade area the wonderful news: you don't have to buy a $600 tile saw to redo one bathroom floor. You can rent it, nail the project, and hand it back.

---

## Target Audience

Occasional DIYers who take on a real project once or twice a year and are unwilling to buy specialty tools they will use once. They are practical, budget-aware, and often surprised and delighted to learn rental is even an option.

---

## The Big Idea

**"Rent the Big Stuff"** celebrates the joy of having the right tool for one glorious weekend. The creative shows real projects transformed by serious equipment — the floor sander that saved the hardwood, the pressure washer that resurrected the deck — always ending on the price: from $19 a day.

---

## Channel Plan

### 1. Paid Social & Video
Beautiful, satisfying before-and-after videos across Reels, TikTok, and YouTube — sanded floors, gleaming decks, perfect tile lines — each one revealing that the hero tool was rented. Social video is wonderfully effective for this kind of transformation content.

### 2. Content Production
A lovely set of short films and photography covering the rental fleet's greatest hits, plus quick explainers on how renting works (reserve, pick up, return — it really is that easy).

### 3. Local Out-of-Home
Playful billboards near stores: "That tile saw? Rented." / "Own the weekend, not the sander." These build broad awareness that the rental counter exists.

### 4. In-Store Signage & Rental Counter Displays
Wayfinding from the front door to the rental counter, project-pairing signage in relevant aisles ("Redoing floors? Rent the sander →"), and a gorgeous refreshed counter presentation.

### 5. Email & CRM
Rental spotlights to the customer list, timed to seasonal projects — deck season, garden season, spring refresh — with simple reservation links.
