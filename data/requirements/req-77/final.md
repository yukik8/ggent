## Campaign Title

**"Say It In The Stars" — the Stackwise Ratings & Reviews Program**

---

## Opening

Stackwise is an app people genuinely enjoy, and the app stores should reflect it. The product is full of natural high points — the auto-save animation, the weekly "your stack grew" recap, the confetti when a goal gets hit — and each one is an opportunity to invite a happy user to share that experience publicly. This program builds a carefully timed system that turns Stackwise's best moments into a steady stream of strong ratings and detailed, specific reviews.

A stronger store presence supports everything else Stackwise does: better conversion on install-page visits, better keyword rankings, and ready-made social proof for every skeptical friend who receives a download link. Industry studies suggest a one-star improvement in average rating can lift install conversion by double digits, which makes this a high-leverage program for the budget.

---

## Target Audience

Satisfied active users — people who open Stackwise regularly, fund buckets, hit goals, and stay. They already like the app; most have simply never been asked at the right moment. We will also address lapsed reviewers, whose older reviews reference features that have since improved, with a "look how far we've come" update prompt.

---

## Program Plan

### 1. Smart In-App Review Prompts
The heart of the program. We map Stackwise's genuine delight moments — goal completed, first $100 saved, 30-day streak, positive weekly recap — and trigger the native review prompt only at those peaks. Prompt logic, cooldowns, and eligibility rules are built and tested in the first phase, so the ask is well timed rather than intrusive.

### 2. Feedback Fork
Before any store prompt, a lightweight "How's your stack going?" check-in routes unhappy users to support and happy users onward to the store. Genuine problems get solved privately instead of aired publicly, which serves both the score and the user.

### 3. Lifecycle Email & Push Moments
A small set of milestone celebrations ("You just hit your first $500!") carry a soft review invitation. These are celebration messages first and asks second, keeping the program's tone generous.

### 4. Surprise & Delight
For the most engaged users, small unexpected extras — early feature access, a custom bucket icon pack, a milestone badge — build the goodwill that naturally spills into reviews. Nothing transactional and nothing that reads as buying reviews.

### 5. Review Response & Store Presence Refresh
We stand up a review-response operation (every review receives a human, on-brand reply within 48 hours) and refresh the store listings — new screenshots highlighting the features reviewers praise most, updated copy, and a seasonal preview video.

## Why This Will Work

Stackwise already earns its users' goodwill — this program gives that goodwill a public destination. Well-timed prompts catch users at their best moments, the feedback fork protects the score while genuinely helping frustrated users, and the response operation shows every store visitor a brand that listens. It is a low-budget, high-craft program whose returns compound across every future install, and it can be live within thirty days of signature using systems our team already operates for Stackwise's lifecycle messaging.
