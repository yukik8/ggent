## Campaign Title

**"Say It In The Stars" — the Stackwise Ratings & Reviews Program**

---

## Opening

Stackwise is an app people genuinely love, and it's time the app stores showed it. The product is packed with delightful moments — the satisfying auto-save animation, the weekly "your stack grew" recap, the confetti when a goal gets hit — and every one of those moments is a chance to ask a happy user to share the love. This program builds a beautiful, thoughtfully timed system that turns Stackwise's happiest moments into a steady stream of five-star ratings and glowing, detailed reviews.

A stronger store presence lifts everything else Stackwise does: better conversion on every install page visit, better keyword rankings, and social proof for every skeptical friend who gets sent a download link. It's the highest-leverage $150,000 in the plan book.

---

## Target Audience

Satisfied active users — people who open Stackwise regularly, have funded buckets, hit goals, and stick around. They already love the app; most have simply never been asked to say so at the right moment. We'll also pay gentle attention to lapsed reviewers (users whose old reviews reference features that have since improved) with a warm "look how far we've come" update prompt.

---

## Program Plan

### 1. Smart In-App Review Prompts
The heart of the program. We map Stackwise's genuine delight moments — goal completed, first $100 saved, 30-day streak, weekly recap with a positive number — and trigger the native review prompt only at those peaks. No interruptions, no begging, just a perfectly timed ask when the user is already smiling. Prompt logic, cooldowns, and eligibility rules all get built and tested in this phase of work.

### 2. Feedback Fork
Before any store prompt, a lightweight "How's your stack going?" check-in routes unhappy users to support and happy users onward to the store. This keeps genuine problems out of public reviews and gets them solved, which is better for everyone.

### 3. Lifecycle Email & Push Moments
A small set of wonderfully warm lifecycle touches celebrate user milestones ("You just hit your first $500!") and include a soft review invitation. These are celebration messages first, asks second.

### 4. Surprise & Delight
For our most engaged users, small unexpected delights — early access to a new feature, a custom bucket icon pack, a milestone badge — create the kind of goodwill that naturally spills into reviews. Nothing transactional, nothing that reads as buying reviews; just a brand being lovely to its best users.

### 5. Review Response & Store Presence Refresh
We stand up a review-response operation (every review gets a human, on-brand reply within 48 hours) and refresh the store listings themselves — new screenshots highlighting the features reviewers praise most, updated copy, seasonal preview video.

## Why This Will Work

Stackwise already earns the love — this program simply gives that love somewhere public to go. Perfectly timed prompts catch users at their happiest, the feedback fork protects the score while actually helping frustrated users, and the response operation shows every store visitor a brand that listens. It's a low-budget, high-craft program with compounding returns on every future install.
