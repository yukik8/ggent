# Stackwise Payroll-Link Activation Campaign — DRAFT

**Prepared by:** Marcus Bell, Junior Planner, Meridian & Craft
**Client:** Stackwise
**Campaign window:** May 1 – September 30, 2025
**Budget:** $400,000

---

## Campaign Title

**"Set It On Payday" — the Payroll-Link Activation Push**

---

## Opening

Stackwise's payroll-link feature is genuinely one of the slickest pieces of the product. Connect your direct deposit once and the app does the rest — auto-splitting a slice of every paycheck into savings buckets, showing a clean money timeline, and sending a weekly "your stack grew" moment. It takes under five minutes to set up, works with the major payroll providers, and pairs perfectly with the free tier and the $8/month premium upgrade. This campaign is all about getting that feature in front of new users in a way that feels current, native, and impossible to scroll past.

The plan below stacks the formats that are winning on social right now — payday POV videos, duet chains, streak mechanics, and creator "money resets" — into a five-month activation flight.

---

## Tactic 1: Payday POV on TikTok & Reels

Payday content is having a moment. "Payday routine" and "money reset" videos are pulling huge organic numbers right now, and the aesthetic (cash-stuffing energy, but digital) maps perfectly onto Stackwise's auto-split animation. We produce a run of payday POV spots — the paycheck lands, the app quietly moves the money, the user doesn't even look up from their coffee — and flight them as paid on TikTok and Reels through the whole window. Every spot ends on the same beat: "Set it on payday."

## Tactic 2: Creator Duet Chains

We recruit finance-adjacent and lifestyle creators to kick off duet/stitch chains: "show me your paycheck split." Creators show their (real or stylized) Stackwise split screens, then invite followers to duet with their own. Duet chains are the most reliably viral participation format on TikTok right now and give us a wall of user-generated proof that real people actually link their pay.

## Tactic 3: The 7-Day Link Streak

Inside the app, we run a streak mechanic for a user's first week: open the app each day, complete one tiny setup step per day (name a bucket, set a split percentage, link payroll), and unlock a "Day One Done Right" badge plus a premium trial week. Streaks are the retention mechanic every top app is leaning on, and this gives the campaign a product-side hook to point all the media at.

## Tactic 4: Push & Email Nudge Sequence

New users get a tuned sequence of pushes and emails across their first seven days, each one framed around payday rather than the app ("Your next paycheck could split itself"). We keep it light — a few well-timed nudges, not spam — and sync send timing to common payday dates (1st, 15th, Fridays).

## Tactic 5: Payday Sound & Hashtag Play

We commission a short, satisfying "payday sound" (cha-ching, but softer) for TikTok, seed it through the duet creators, and run the #SetItOnPayday hashtag across everything. If the sound catches even modestly, it becomes free reach for the whole flight.

---

## Target Audience

The people we're aiming all of this at are new Stackwise users in their first week after download — mostly younger earners getting their first steady paychecks, on their phones constantly, fluent in short-form video, and skeptical of anything that smells like a finance-bro pitch. They've downloaded the app, which means they're curious, and the first week is our window to turn that curiosity into a linked paycheck.

## Why This Works

Payday is already a cultural moment on social — we're not inventing a behavior, we're branding one. By stacking the hottest participation formats (duets, streaks, sounds) on top of a product feature that genuinely delivers, "Set It On Payday" gives Stackwise a launch-summer flight that feels native to the feed and points every view at one action: link the paycheck.
