## Campaign Title

**"Set It On Payday" — the Payroll-Link Activation Push**

---

## Opening: The Problem We Are Actually Solving

Stackwise does not have a download problem; it has a first-week disappearing act. The average consumer fintech app loses 73% of new users within 30 days of download (ref-13), and the users who slip away are exactly the ones Stackwise just paid to acquire. The single strongest lever against that loss is already in the product: users who link a payroll deposit in their first week retain at 3.2x the rate of those who do not (ref-13). Yet payroll linking is treated as a feature to discover rather than the one action the entire first week should be engineered around.

"Set It On Payday" is our answer to that specific problem. The payroll-link feature — a five-minute setup that auto-splits every paycheck into savings buckets — becomes the sole conversion event of the campaign, and every tactic below exists to move one number: the share of new users who link their pay in week one. This is not an awareness flight; it is a retention intervention dressed as one.

---

## Target Audience

New Stackwise users in their first week after download: young earners on their first steady paychecks, fluent in short-form video, and deeply skeptical of finance marketing. That skepticism is measurable — 61% of savers under 30 say they distrust finance apps that lead with investment returns instead of safety (ref-13), and 58% of Gen-Z respondents say they distrust ads that make claims without visible evidence or numbers (ref-07). So the campaign never talks about returns or wealth; it shows the paycheck quietly splitting itself, with real, unglamorous numbers on screen.

---

## Tactics

Because the core problem is first-week payroll-link completion, every tactic maps back to it.

### Tactic 1: Payday POV on TikTok & Reels
Because the core problem lives in a user's first seven days, we reach them where they already are: 71% of Gen-Z respondents discover new brands primarily through short-form video (ref-07). The economics reinforce the choice — short-form video app-install campaigns average a $4.10 CPI for finance apps, versus $6.80 on search (ref-13), and short-form video ads average 2.4x the completion rate of static feed ads (ref-06). The payday POV spots dramatize the payroll link itself, ending on "Set it on payday."

### Tactic 2: Creator Duet Chains
Duet chains turn the link action into visible social proof, which matters because 64% of Gen-Z respondents have purchased a product recommended by a niche creator with under 100K followers (ref-07). We prioritize those niche creators deliberately — creator-partnership posts also deliver 31% lower cost-per-engagement than brand-owned posts (ref-06) — and every duet shows a real split screen, answering this cohort's demand for visible evidence.

### Tactic 3: The 7-Day Link Streak
Because the retention data says the payroll link must happen in week one, not eventually, the streak compresses setup into seven daily micro-steps culminating in the link, rewarded with the "Day One Done Right" badge and a premium trial week. The streak is the product-side machine that converts the attention the media buys.

### Tactic 4: Push & Email Nudge Sequence
Lifecycle messaging carries the week-one deadline directly to each user: lifecycle-triggered emails average a 38% open rate, versus 21% for standard campaign sends (ref-16). Sends are timed to common paydays, framed around the paycheck rather than the app, and deliberately sparse — this audience mutes and deletes apps that spam.

### Tactic 5: Payday Sound & Hashtag Play
The commissioned payday sound and #SetItOnPayday hashtag extend the flight's reach organically. We hold paid frequency in check, since frequency above 4 exposures per week correlates with an 18% drop in engagement rate (ref-06).

## KPIs

- **Week-one payroll-link rate** among new users — the campaign's primary number, chosen because week-one linkers retain at 3.2x the rate of non-linkers (ref-13)
- **Day-30 retention** of campaign-period cohorts, measured against the category's 73% thirty-day loss (ref-13)
- **CPI from short-form video** at or below the $4.10 finance-app benchmark (ref-13)
- **Paid social CTR** at or above the 1.2% cross-vertical average, targeting the 2.6% top quartile (ref-06)
- **Streak completion and premium trial starts** from the 7-day flow

## Why This Works

The category's biggest unclaimed retention lever is the payroll link, and Stackwise already owns the feature. This plan points every dollar, every duet, and every push notification at that single week-one action, so the media spend compounds into retained users rather than evaporating with the 73% who normally vanish (ref-13). Payday is already a cultural moment on social; we attach Stackwise's stickiest behavior to it.
