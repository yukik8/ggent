## Campaign Title

## Opening: The Problem We Are Actually Solving

Clearbrook's pediatric opportunity exists because the traditional front door to care is broken for young families. 41% of US adults under 45 have no primary-care physician, up from 33% in 2019 (ref-15), and their children's coverage is often just as patchy — when a fever spikes on Saturday night, there is no established pediatrician to call. Even families who try to get one hit the bottleneck: the average wait for a new-patient primary-care appointment in mid-size metros is 26 days (ref-15). And these parents choose care the way they choose everything else, from their phones: 64% of patients under 40 choose a clinic based on online booking availability before considering reputation (ref-15).

Clearbrook Pediatric Urgent Care is our answer to that specific problem: $149 flat-fee visits and $59 pediatric telehealth, bookable online, with evening and weekend hours. The flat price is itself a growth lever — transparent cash-pay pricing pages increase new-patient bookings by 34% for urgent-care services (ref-15). Every tactic below is selected to intercept a no-pediatrician parent at the moment of need and convert the first visit into a returning family.

## Target Audience

Parents under 45 without an established pediatrician — high-deductible households who fear the unknown bill more than the symptom, decide from a Google listing at 9pm, and bail at any phone-only step. The booking test comes before reputation for this cohort (64%, ref-15), so every asset ends on the booking screen.

## Tactics

Because the core problem is a missing front door to care, every tactic is a door.

1. **Short-form video (Reels, TikTok, Shorts).** The Saturday-night fever, the playground wrist, the 9pm rash photo — each 20-second scenario ends on the booking screen and the flat price. Short-form video ads average 2.4x the completion rate of static feed ads (ref-06).
2. **Local search & maps.** Because 64% of under-40 patients screen clinics by online booking first (ref-15), the Google Business listings carry book-now links, current hours, and fresh reviews — this is the highest-intent channel in the plan.
3. **Parent-creator partnerships.** A walkthrough visit from a trusted local parent answers the "what's it actually like" question, at favorable economics: creator-partnership posts deliver 31% lower cost-per-engagement than brand-owned posts (ref-06).
4. **Out-of-home near schools and daycares.** Price-forward boards — "$149. Tonight. Booked from your phone." — because the flat number is the message that converts (34% booking lift from transparent pricing, ref-15).
5. **Email & SMS follow-up flows.** The first visit only pays off if the family returns: 55% of urgent-care visitors return to the same network within 18 months when they receive a follow-up message within a week (ref-15). Every visit triggers a one-tap next step.
6. **Back-to-school surge plan.** A concentrated August-September burst timed to enrollment physicals and the first fever wave of the school year.

We boost top creator posts, retarget incomplete bookings, and swap in flu-season creative.
