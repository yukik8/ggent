## Campaign Title

**"Firewall Your Future"**

---

## Opening

Introducing Skillharbor's cybersecurity career-change cohort: a live, instructor-led program with hands-on attack-and-defend labs, certification prep baked in, and a job-search sprint in the final month. At $2,600 for the full cohort (or folded into the $199/month all-access tier), it's the most complete package Skillharbor has ever shipped. Cybersecurity is everywhere right now — breach headlines, AI-powered attacks, ransomware documentaries — and CareerTok is exploding with "I switched to cyber" content. The moment is now, and Skillharbor should own it.

---

## The Big Idea

"Firewall Your Future" turns career anxiety into a call to action. We ride every wave the culture gives us: breach-news reactive content, day-in-the-life SOC analyst videos, CareerTok duets, AMA threads, and a "Could you catch the hacker?" interactive quiz that funnels into the cohort page.

---

## Tactics

### 1. Short-Form Video Blitz (TikTok & Reels)
Reactive, trend-native edits: breach-news stitches, lab-footage screen captures, countdown-to-cohort-start content. Volume matters — we ship multiple posts weekly and boost the winners.

### 2. CareerTok Creator Partnerships
Career-switch creators document their own four-week taste of the cohort in real time. Authentic transformation arcs are the top-performing format in this space right now.

### 3. Podcast Sponsorship Flight
Host-read spots on career, tech, and true-crime-adjacent security podcasts. Security stories are having a mainstream moment and the audiences skew professional.

### 4. Paid Search & Display
Capture "cybersecurity bootcamp," "career change to cyber," and certification-related queries, plus display retargeting on quiz takers.

### 5. Webinar Funnel
A free "Is Cyber Right for You?" live session every two weeks, hosted by cohort instructors, converting attendees to enrollment calls.

---

## Target Audience

Primary: career changers aged 25-40 eyeing high-demand security roles — people in stalled analyst, IT-support, and ops jobs who keep hearing security pays. Secondary: existing all-access subscribers who could upgrade into the cohort.
