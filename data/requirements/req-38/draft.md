# Cascade Valley Summer Family Adventure Pass Campaign — DRAFT

**Prepared by:** Emma Lindqvist, Junior Planner, Meridian & Craft
**Client:** Cascade Valley Tourism Board
**Campaign window:** June 1 – August 31, 2026
**Budget:** $300,000

---

## Campaign Title

**"Summer Unlocked: One Pass, a Whole Valley of Adventures"**

---

## Opening: The Best Summer Product the Valley Has Ever Offered

Introducing the Cascade Valley Summer Family Adventure Pass — a wonderful, wallet-friendly, all-in-one ticket to the most fun a family can have within a tank of gas. For just $129, one pass covers the whole family all summer long: the riverside splash park, the treetop rope course, the historic narrow-gauge railway ride, ranger-led junior explorer walks, the Saturday farm market kids' zone, canoe rentals on Miller Lake, and a punch-card trail of ten family-friendly hikes with collectible badges at every trailhead. It is genuinely an amazing amount of adventure packed into one simple purchase.

What makes the pass so exciting is how easy it makes everything. Parents don't have to research ten different attractions, compare ten different prices, or buy ten different tickets. One pass, one price, endless summer memories. The pass even comes with a colorful fold-out adventure map that kids can stamp at each stop, which turns the whole valley into a giant treasure hunt. Families today are looking for exactly this kind of experience, and summer is when they are most ready to hit the road. This campaign is our chance to make the Adventure Pass THE thing families talk about at the school pickup line this June.

---

## Target Audience

Our primary audience is drive-market families with kids aged 6 to 14. These are busy parents within comfortable driving distance of Cascade Valley who want their kids off screens and outdoors, but who don't have the time or energy to plan complicated trips. They love a good deal, they love things that are easy, and they really love it when the kids come home tired and happy. Many parents in this group plan their summer activities fairly last-minute, so being visible all season matters.

Secondary audiences include grandparents hosting grandkids for summer visits, and local-ish families within an hour of the valley who might use the pass multiple times.

---

## The Big Idea

**"Summer Unlocked"** turns the Adventure Pass into a key. Every piece of creative shows a family literally unlocking something — a trailhead gate swinging open, a canoe shed rolling up, the railway conductor punching the pass like a golden ticket. The stamp-collecting adventure map becomes the campaign's engine: kids race to fill their maps, parents share the finished ones, and every stamped map is a mini-testimonial that the pass paid for itself many times over. The invitation is simple and irresistible: one pass unlocks the whole valley, so what will your family unlock first?

## Channel & Media Plan

### 1. Paid Social — Short-Form Video (Reels, TikTok, YouTube Shorts)
Short-form video is where parents actually plan things these days, and travel content performs wonderfully in the format. We will run a summer-long flight of energetic videos: kids stamping maps, splash park chaos, treetop course bravery, golden-hour lake paddles. Social video carries the awareness load for the whole campaign.

### 2. Family & Regional Travel Creator Partnerships
We will partner with family travel creators and regional "day trips near you" accounts to document real pass days. Parents trust other parents far more than they trust ads, and creator videos give us a steady supply of authentic content showing exactly what a pass day looks like, logistics and all.

### 3. Regional Paid Search & Digital Display
When parents search for things like "family day trips" and "summer activities for kids," we will be right there with the pass as the easy answer. Display retargeting keeps the pass in front of families who visited the booking page but haven't purchased yet.

### 4. Drive-Market Out-of-Home
Big, sunny, joyful billboards on the key highway corridors into the valley and in our top drive-market metros. Simple message, big visual: one pass, whole valley, $129.

### 5. Email & Landing Page
A dedicated Summer Unlocked landing page where families can buy the pass in a few taps, plus a summer email series to the tourism board's existing list with itinerary ideas ("Your first pass day, hour by hour").
