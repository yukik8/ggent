# Cascade Valley Summer Family Adventure Pass Campaign — Proposal

**Prepared by:** Emma Lindqvist, Junior Planner, Meridian & Craft
**Reviewed by:** Daniel Okafor, Senior Planner
**Client:** Cascade Valley Tourism Board
**Campaign window:** June 1 – August 31, 2026
**Budget:** $300,000
**Version:** Final v2

---

## Campaign Title

**"Summer Unlocked: One Pass, a Whole Valley of Adventures"**

## Opening

Introducing the Cascade Valley Summer Family Adventure Pass: a $129 all-in-one ticket that covers the whole family, all summer long. One pass includes the riverside splash park, the treetop rope course, the historic narrow-gauge railway, ranger-led junior explorer walks, the Saturday farm market kids' zone, canoe rentals on Miller Lake, and a punch-card trail of ten family-friendly hikes with collectible badges at every trailhead.

The pass's real strength is simplicity. Parents don't have to research ten attractions, compare ten prices, or buy ten tickets — one pass, one price, a full summer of things to do. Each pass comes with a fold-out adventure map that kids stamp at every stop, turning the valley into one large treasure hunt. This campaign makes the Adventure Pass the obvious summer purchase for families across the drive market.

## Target Audience

Our primary audience is drive-market families with kids aged 6 to 14: busy parents within comfortable driving distance of Cascade Valley who want their kids outdoors and off screens, but who lack the time to plan complicated trips. They respond to value and to ease, and industry surveys suggest that more than half of family summer outings are planned less than a month in advance — so staying visible across the full season matters.

Secondary audiences include grandparents hosting grandkids over the summer and near-local families within an hour of the valley who are likely to use the pass repeatedly.

## The Big Idea

**"Summer Unlocked"** turns the Adventure Pass into a key. Every piece of creative shows a family unlocking something: a trailhead gate swinging open, a canoe shed rolling up, the railway conductor punching the pass like a golden ticket. The stamp-collecting adventure map is the campaign's engine — kids race to fill their maps, parents share the finished ones, and every completed map doubles as a testimonial that the pass paid for itself. The invitation is simple: one pass unlocks the whole valley. What will your family unlock first?

## Channel & Media Plan

### 1. Paid Social — Short-Form Video (Reels, TikTok, YouTube Shorts)
Short-form video is where parents plan outings today, and family travel content performs strongly in the format. We will run a summer-long flight of energetic videos — kids stamping maps, splash park chaos, treetop bravery, golden-hour lake paddles. Social video carries the campaign's awareness load.

### 2. Family & Regional Travel Creator Partnerships
We will partner with family travel creators and regional day-trip accounts to document real pass days. Parents trust other parents more than they trust ads, and creator videos supply authentic content that shows exactly what a pass day looks like, logistics included.

### 3. Regional Paid Search & Digital Display
When parents search for "family day trips" or "summer activities for kids," the pass appears as the easy answer. Display retargeting keeps the offer in front of families who visited the booking page without purchasing.

### 4. Drive-Market Out-of-Home
Bright, joyful billboards on the key highway corridors into the valley and in the top drive-market metros. One message, one visual: one pass, whole valley, $129.

### 5. Email & Landing Page
A dedicated Summer Unlocked landing page where families can buy the pass in a few taps, supported by a summer email series to the tourism board's existing list with copy-ready itineraries ("Your first pass day, hour by hour").

## Closing

The Summer Family Adventure Pass is a strong product with a built-in story: one simple purchase that unlocks a whole valley of memories. "Summer Unlocked" gives families a playful reason to come, a treasure hunt that keeps them exploring, and a finished map they will want to show off. With social, creators, search, out-of-home, and email working together across the full season, we will keep the pass in front of families from the last school bell to the last weekend of August.
