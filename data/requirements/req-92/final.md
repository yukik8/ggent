## Campaign Title

**"Clearbrook Checks In" — the Follow-Up & Return-Visit Program**

---

## Opening: The Problem We Are Actually Solving

Clearbrook has a leaky-bucket problem: it wins a new urgent-care patient, delivers a good visit, and then goes silent — and silent patients do not come back. The fix is narrow, cheap, and measured: 55% of urgent-care visitors return to the same network within 18 months when they receive a follow-up message within a week (ref-15). Without that contact, most of Clearbrook's hard-won visitors become one-time transactions. The economics favor fixing retention before buying more strangers: median ROI for retention marketing is $4.60 per dollar spent, versus $2.20 for prospecting (ref-16).

Clearbrook Checks In is our answer to that specific problem: a post-visit sequence — day 3 check-in, day 10 recovery note, day 30 nudge — triggered off visit records, with one-tap next steps and a gentle path to the $79/month membership. The audience is disproportionately convertible, because so many of these visitors have no doctor to return to instead: 41% of US adults under 45 have no primary-care physician, up from 33% in 2019 (ref-15).

---

## Target Audience

Recent Clearbrook urgent-care visitors — people who already chose Clearbrook once and then mostly never heard from us again. Timing, not polish, is the active ingredient: the 55% return rate depends on the follow-up landing within a week of the visit (ref-15), so the program is built around triggers, not campaigns. Their next-step behavior is booking-led — 64% of patients under 40 choose a clinic based on online booking availability before considering reputation (ref-15) — so every message ends in exactly one tap-able action.

Secondary audience: household members of recent visitors, who often book on each other's behalf.

---

## The Big Idea

**"Clearbrook Checks In."** Healthcare usually goes silent the moment you walk out the door. Because the core problem is that silence — the difference between a 55% return rate and losing most visitors (ref-15) — the creative idea is simply to be the clinic that actually follows up, and to make the next step effortless every single time. Over the sequence, the check-ins gently introduce the membership, because the natural end state of a clinic that keeps checking in is having them as your doctor.

## Channel & Tactic Plan

Each tactic below maps directly to the post-visit silence problem named above.

### 1. Lifecycle Email & SMS Program
Because the return effect is triggered by a message within a week of the visit (ref-15), the heart of the program is a visit-triggered sequence, not a broadcast calendar. Triggered sends earn the attention broadcasts don't: lifecycle-triggered emails average a 38% open rate, versus 21% for standard campaign sends (ref-16). SMS carries the time-sensitive steps, since SMS opt-in subscribers convert at 2.4x the rate of email-only subscribers on time-limited offers (ref-16). Sequences branch by visit type for a personal feel.

### 2. Retargeting Paid Social
Because not every visitor opts into messaging, warm retargeting extends the check-in to recent visitors and their lookalikes — recovery-tip content and membership stories in the same caring voice. Short-form video ads average 2.4x the completion rate of static feed ads (ref-06), and we hold frequency down because frequency above 4 exposures per week correlates with a 18% drop in engagement rate (ref-06).

### 3. Membership Upsell Landing Experience
Because the day-30 nudge needs somewhere to land, a dedicated page makes the jump from "that clinic I went to once" to "my $79/month doctor" feel obvious. Membership is the retention end-state worth engineering toward: loyalty members generate 42% of revenue at brands with mature programs while representing 18% of customers (ref-16).

### 4. Direct Mail Touch
Because a physical recovery card with the care team's signature lands where inboxes are crowded, a single printed piece backs up the digital sequence for the visit types where it will matter most. People keep things like this on the fridge.

## Why Clearbrook Wins

This program attacks the cheapest, most measured growth lever Clearbrook has: a 55% return rate that hinges on one well-timed message (ref-15), aimed at an audience that already chose Clearbrook once and mostly has no doctor to defect to (ref-15). Triggered messaging does the heavy lifting at a 38% open rate (ref-16), retargeting catches the rest, and the membership gives the relationship somewhere to go. Every tactic is answerable to the same named problem.
