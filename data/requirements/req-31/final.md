## Opening: The Problem We Are Actually Solving

Ledgerline has a growth-mix problem, not a product problem. New-logo growth is structurally slow and expensive in this category — the average AP automation deal cycle for 50-500 employee firms is 94 days (ref-04), and every one of those deals starts from zero trust. Meanwhile, the cheapest revenue in the plan is sitting inside the installed base: median ROI for retention marketing is $4.60 per dollar spent, versus $2.20 for prospecting (ref-16). Ledgerline's existing AP customers have already cleared security review, already integrated the platform, and already seen the payoff — yet everything upstream of the invoice at those same companies still runs on email threads and spreadsheets, the same manual chaos they escaped in AP. The scale of that pain is well documented: finance teams report 62% of invoice processing time is still manual at companies without AP automation (ref-04), and their purchasing workflows today look exactly like their AP inbox did before Ledgerline.

Procurement Suite is our answer to that specific problem. The $18,000 annual add-on extends the platform upstream — purchase order creation, multi-step approval workflows, vendor catalogs with negotiated pricing, real-time budget checks before spend happens, and automatic three-way matching — with no new implementation project, because it plugs into the AP product customers already run. Every tactic below is selected for one job: converting existing trust into expansion revenue before budget season closes, not generating cold awareness.

---

## Target Audience

Primary: finance and operations leaders — controllers, VPs of finance, and ops directors — at companies already running Ledgerline AP. These buyers are peer-driven and evidence-driven by habit: 73% of mid-market finance software evaluations now start with a shortlist built from peer review sites (ref-04), which means vendor claims alone will not move them, but proof from a controller at a company like theirs will. Secondary: the CFOs who sign off on the add-on, and the AP managers who become internal champions because they see the messy purchase requests first. Because this audience distrusts feature lists and trusts outcomes, every asset in this campaign leads with a named customer result, never a feature grid.

## The Big Idea

**"Beyond the Invoice"** reframes Ledgerline from a tool that cleans up invoices into a platform that governs spend before it happens. The creative hook: every invoice has a backstory, and right now that backstory lives in email threads, Slack pings, and spreadsheets. Our hero content follows real purchase requests on their messy journey to becoming an invoice, then shows the same journey inside Procurement Suite. Because the core problem is converting existing trust into expansion, this format matters strategically, not just creatively — it dramatizes a pain the audience already lives with daily, in the voice of peers rather than the vendor, which is precisely how this audience decides. It gives us a story engine that works across every channel: podcast episodes, LinkedIn clips, webinar segments, and email narratives all pull from the same "backstory of an invoice" format.

## Channel & Media Plan

Each of the five plays below maps directly to the expansion problem named above.

### 1. Lifecycle Email & In-App Messaging
Because our buyers are already customers, lifecycle triggers beat batch-and-blast: lifecycle-triggered emails average a 38% open rate, versus 21% for standard campaign sends (ref-16). We build a segmented journey keyed to product behavior — companies with high PO-related invoice exceptions get the workflow story first — plus in-app announcements and guided tours that introduce Procurement Suite at the exact moment the pain is visible on screen.

### 2. LinkedIn — Paid + Executive Thought Leadership
Reaching finance decision-makers on LinkedIn costs real money — the B2B benchmark CPM for finance decision-makers is $58, versus $31 for general B2B targeting (ref-04) — so we spend it surgically against a matched list of existing customer accounts rather than broad prospecting, which is exactly where the $4.60-versus-$2.20 retention economics (ref-16) justify the premium. Ledgerline's CFO-facing executives post weekly about spend governance, approval bottlenecks, and month-end war stories, giving the paid flight organic voices to amplify.

### 3. "Beyond the Invoice" Video Podcast Miniseries
A six-episode video podcast featuring Ledgerline customers and finance-ops voices talking about how purchasing really works inside growing companies. This is peer proof at scale for an audience whose evaluations start with peer evidence — 73% of mid-market finance software evaluations now start with a shortlist built from peer review sites (ref-04) — and every episode feeds LinkedIn clips, newsletter features, webinar teasers, and sales enablement snippets.

### 4. Customer Webinar & Virtual Summit Series
Monthly working-session webinars where existing customers demo how they run purchasing today, capped by a half-day virtual "Spend Under Control" summit in January. The summit lands in January deliberately: with the average deal cycle at 94 days for this segment (ref-04), even a faster in-base motion needs its biggest urgency moment positioned ahead of fiscal-year budget locks in February.

### 5. ABM & CSM-Led Expansion Playbook
For the top accounts, we go high-touch: intent-based account scoring, personalized landing pages, direct-mail kits for controllers ("the last binder clip you'll ever need"), and a co-owned playbook so CSMs can trigger campaigns from inside QBRs. Every ABM landing page leads with a customer case study rather than a feature tour, because case-study-led landing pages convert demo requests at 4.7%, versus 2.1% for feature-led pages (ref-04) — more than doubling the yield on identical traffic.
