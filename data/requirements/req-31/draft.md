## Campaign Title

**"Beyond the Invoice" — the Procurement Suite Cross-Sell Launch**

---

## Opening: The Next Chapter of the Ledgerline Platform

Introducing the Ledgerline Procurement Suite, the biggest expansion of the platform since launch. Procurement Suite takes everything customers love about Ledgerline's AP automation and extends it upstream: purchase order creation, multi-step approval workflows, vendor catalogs with negotiated pricing, real-time budget checks before spend happens, and automatic three-way matching that connects POs, receipts, and invoices in one flow. It ships as an $18,000 annual add-on that plugs directly into the AP product customers already run, no new implementation project required.

This launch also lands at the perfect cultural moment for B2B marketing. Buyers are tired of cold outreach, community-led growth is exploding, dark social is where deals actually get influenced, and AI-powered personalization is finally table stakes. "Beyond the Invoice" is our chance to run a genuinely modern B2B campaign — one that layers ABM, executive thought leadership, a branded video podcast, and lifecycle automation into a surround-sound moment for the Ledgerline customer base. Finance software marketing is usually boring. This won't be.

---

## The Big Idea

**"Beyond the Invoice"** reframes Ledgerline from a tool that cleans up invoices into a platform that governs spend before it happens. The creative hook: every invoice has a backstory, and right now that backstory lives in email threads, Slack pings, and spreadsheets. Our hero content follows real purchase requests on their messy journey to becoming an invoice, then shows the same journey inside Procurement Suite. It's relatable, it's a little funny, and it gives us a story engine that works across every channel — podcast episodes, LinkedIn clips, webinar segments, and email narratives all pull from the same "backstory of an invoice" format.

---

## Channel & Media Plan

We're stacking five mutually reinforcing plays so the campaign shows up everywhere our buyers spend time.

### 1. Lifecycle Email & In-App Messaging
The customer base is already inside the product every day, so we'll build a segmented email journey plus in-app announcements and guided tours that introduce Procurement Suite in context. Email remains one of the most reliable channels in B2B, and in-app is basically free reach we'd be crazy not to use.

### 2. LinkedIn — Paid + Executive Thought Leadership
LinkedIn is where finance leaders live, and thought leadership is having a huge moment there. We'll run paid placements against our customer account list while Ledgerline's CFO-facing executives post weekly about spend governance, approval bottlenecks, and month-end war stories. Employee advocacy and founder-style posting consistently outperform brand pages right now, so we lean into personal voices.

### 3. "Beyond the Invoice" Video Podcast Miniseries
A six-episode video podcast featuring Ledgerline customers and finance-ops voices talking about how purchasing really works inside growing companies. Podcasts build trust in a way ads can't, and every episode becomes a content goldmine: LinkedIn clips, newsletter features, webinar teasers, and sales enablement snippets. Video podcasts are the fastest-growing format in B2B and this positions Ledgerline as a category voice.

### 4. Customer Webinar & Virtual Summit Series
Monthly working-session webinars where existing customers demo how they run purchasing today, capped by a half-day virtual "Spend Under Control" summit in January. Peer-led sessions convert far better than vendor pitches, and the summit gives sales a natural big moment to drive urgency around budget season.

### 5. ABM & CSM-Led Expansion Playbook
For the top accounts, we go high-touch: intent-based account scoring, personalized landing pages, direct-mail kits for controllers ("the last binder clip you'll ever need"), and a co-owned playbook so CSMs can trigger campaigns from inside QBRs. ABM works best when marketing and customer success run the same play, and this makes expansion a team sport.

## Who We're Talking To

Primary audience: finance and operations leaders — controllers, VPs of finance, and ops directors — at companies already running Ledgerline AP. These are busy, skeptical buyers who trust peers over vendors and hate being sold to during close. Secondary: CFOs who sign off on the add-on, and the AP managers who become internal champions because they see the messy purchase requests first.
