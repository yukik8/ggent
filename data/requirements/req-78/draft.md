# Stackwise Campus Ambassador Program — DRAFT

**Prepared by:** Ryo Tanabe, Junior Planner, Meridian & Craft
**Client:** Stackwise
**Campaign window:** September 1, 2026 – January 31, 2027
**Budget:** $220,000

---

## Campaign Title

**"Stack U" — the Stackwise Campus Ambassador Program**

---

## Opening

Stackwise is a natural fit for college life. The app is free to start, takes minutes to set up, and solves problems students actually have: rent splits, meal budgets, spring-break funds, and the first real paychecks from campus jobs. The $8/month premium tier adds shared buckets, which work perfectly for roommates. "Stack U" puts that product story in students' hands through the channel campuses trust most — other students.

The program recruits, trains, and equips ambassadors at 30 campuses to run events, share content, and drive downloads across the fall semester and January term.

## Program Structure

| Component | Detail |
|---|---|
| Ambassadors | 2-3 per campus, 75 total across 30 campuses |
| Compensation | Monthly stipend + performance bonuses tied to verified downloads |
| Training | Two-week onboarding, monthly content briefs, ambassador Slack |
| Events | 2 campus events per semester per campus (tabling, workshops, finals-week "budget your break" pop-ups) |
| Content | Weekly short-form posts from each ambassador on their own accounts |

## Target Audience

College students at the 30 selected campuses, with a focus on sophomores and juniors who are managing money independently for the first time — campus jobs, shared apartments, meal plans, and tight margins. Students trust peer recommendations far more than advertising, which is the core logic of the ambassador model.

## The Big Idea

**"Your campus money, sorted."**

Ambassadors position Stackwise as the app that sorts campus money problems: the rent-split bucket, the spring-break stack, the textbook fund. Every event and post is built around a specific, relatable campus money moment rather than generic finance talk.

## Channel Plan

| Channel | Role |
|---|---|
| Ambassador events | Tabling, dorm workshops, finals pop-ups — the program's physical presence |
| Ambassador social content | Weekly posts on ambassadors' own TikTok/Instagram; authentic, campus-specific |
| Geo-targeted paid social | Reels/TikTok flights fenced to the 30 campuses, amplifying the best ambassador content |
| Campus partnerships | Student org sponsorships and campus newspaper/newsletter placements |

## KPIs

| KPI | Goal |
|---|---|
| Verified downloads from ambassador links | Primary program metric |
| Events completed | 120+ across the program |
| Ambassador content volume | 75 ambassadors × weekly posts |
| Cost per verified download | Below paid-channel benchmark |
| Ambassador retention | 85%+ through the full program |

## Measurement & Operations

Every ambassador link, event QR code, and campus paid flight is tagged separately, so the program reads out at the campus level as well as in aggregate. A monthly scorecard ranks campuses by verified downloads and cost per download, and the January-term phase reallocates event and paid support toward the top third of campuses. The ambassador Slack doubles as the reporting channel: weekly photo and metrics check-ins keep the field operation honest without heavy process.

## Why This Will Work

The ambassador model puts Stackwise inside campus life instead of advertising at it. Students hear about the app from people they already follow and see it at events they already attend. With a disciplined program structure, performance-tied compensation, and paid amplification behind the best content, "Stack U" gives Stackwise a repeatable playbook for the campus segment at a modest cost per campus.
