## Opening: The Problem We Are Actually Solving

Hartline's kitchen opportunity is a confidence problem, not an awareness problem. The homeowners who most want a kitchen update are the least likely to start one: 69% of first-time homeowners say they feel intimidated starting any project beyond painting (ref-14), and a kitchen — even a no-remodel refresh — reads as exactly that kind of project. They save inspiration content endlessly and act on almost none of it, and when they do shop, the big boxes hand them an aisle instead of a plan. That hesitation is the volume Hartline's project-guidance positioning exists to capture, in a category where DIY now accounts for 52% of revenue in a $538B market (ref-14).

The Weekend Kitchen Glow-Up is our answer to that specific problem: bundles priced $149-$499 that turn "redo the kitchen" into five finishable weekend projects — cabinet painting, hardware swaps, peel-and-stick backsplash, faucet upgrades, under-cabinet lighting. Every tactic below is selected to move a saver from inspiration to a completed first project.

## Target Audience

Homeowners aged 28-50 who want a kitchen update without a remodel — led by the intimidated first-time owner, who saves constantly and acts rarely. They respond to guided projects, not discounts: project-based how-to content drives 2.7x more store visits than price-and-item advertising for regional chains (ref-14).

## The Big Idea

Because the core problem is hesitation, the before-and-after format is the strategy: every 30-second transformation is proof that someone like them finished the project. The reveal engine then turns each finished customer project into the next customer's permission slip.

## Tactics

Each tactic maps back to the confidence gap named above.

1. **Short-form video (Reels & TikTok).** Because hesitant homeowners need to see the whole project succeed before starting, we lead with complete transformations, not product shots. Short-form video ads average 2.4x the completion rate of static feed ads (ref-06).
2. **Creator partnerships.** Regional creators completing a bundle in their own kitchen make the project credible, and the economics work: creator-partnership posts deliver 31% lower cost-per-engagement than brand-owned posts (ref-06).
3. **Pinterest & Instagram inspiration ads.** We intercept the save-but-never-start behavior where it happens and route it to one bundle and one shopping list.
4. **In-store weekend workshop series.** "Glow-Up Saturdays" let a nervous homeowner practice on demo rigs before risking their own cabinets — and in-store workshop attendees spend 43% more annually than matched non-attendees (ref-14).
5. **Email & SMS.** Workshop invites and bundle countdowns, with SMS carrying the time-sensitive sends: SMS opt-in subscribers convert at 2.4x the rate of email-only subscribers on time-limited offers (ref-16).
6. **Reveal-content engine.** Customer before-and-afters close the loop, because the problem is belief, and nothing builds it like a neighbor's finished kitchen.

We will boost top creator posts, retarget workshop attendees with bundle offers, and close with a spring-finale montage of customer reveals.
