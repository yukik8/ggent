---

## Opening: The Problem We Are Actually Solving

Hartline's growth problem with new homeowners is not awareness or price — it is fear. 69% of first-time homeowners say they feel intimidated starting any project beyond painting (ref-14). These customers close on a house, paint a room, and then stall: they don't know which tools to buy, they're afraid of making it worse, and a "$4 off drill bits" flyer means nothing to someone who doesn't own a drill. The prize for solving this is large and growing — US home improvement retail reached $538B in 2025, with DIY purchases accounting for 52% of revenue (ref-14) — and it sits squarely in Hartline's service-and-guidance positioning, where big-box rivals cannot follow.

The First Fix Bundles are our answer to that specific fear. A curated line of starter project kits priced from $79 to $249 — Hang It Right, Mount a Shelf, Fix That Faucet, and First Yard Kit — packages one complete project per box: exact tools, hardware, materials, a printed step-by-step guide, a companion video, and the "Finish It Guarantee" that any Hartline associate will walk a stuck customer through the next step in store. The product removes the two decisions that paralyze this audience — which parts, and what if I fail. Every tactic below is selected for its ability to convert intimidation into a first finished project.

| Bundle | Price | Contents |
|---|---|---|
| Hang It Right | $79 | Level, stud finder, anchors, drill bits, guide |
| Mount a Shelf | $129 | Shelf hardware set, level, anchor kit, driver bits, guide |
| Fix That Faucet | $149 | Basin wrench, plumber's tape, washers, cartridge puller, guide |

## Target Audience

First-time homeowners within 12 months of purchase across Hartline's lower-Midwest trade areas — precisely the cohort where the 69% intimidation figure is measured (ref-14). They research projects heavily before buying anything, which is why guidance-led content, not price-led promotion, is the entry point to this segment.

---

## The Big Idea

**"Your First Fix"** turns the intimidating first project into a guided, guaranteed experience. Because the core problem is hesitation rather than consideration, the campaign's single action — pick your first project, grab the bundle, finish it this weekend — is engineered to produce a completed project, the moment that converts a frozen new homeowner into a repeat project customer.

## Channel Plan

Each channel maps back to the named problem: fear of starting.

| Channel | Role |
|---|---|
| How-to video series (YouTube + site) | Four full project walkthroughs matching the four bundles |
| Paid social (Reels, TikTok, YouTube) | Cut-downs of the how-to series plus bundle-focused creative |
| New-homeowner welcome program | Email and direct-mail sequence triggered by new-mover data |
| In-store "First Project" zone | Front-of-store bundle displays with associate training |
| Local community & geo-targeted digital | Neighborhood Facebook group presence and geo-targeted ads around all 40 stores |

### 1. How-To Video Series
Because the core problem is intimidation, we lead with education, not items and prices: project-based how-to content drives 2.7x more store visits than price-and-item advertising for regional chains (ref-14). Four polished walkthroughs, one per bundle, hosted by Hartline associates, become the campaign's durable confidence-building asset.

### 2. Paid Social
Short-form cut-downs carry the how-to message efficiently: Instagram Reels average a $9.40 CPM, TikTok $7.80, and YouTube Shorts $6.90 (ref-06), and short-form video ads average 2.4x the completion rate of static feed ads (ref-06). We will cap frequency deliberately, since frequency above 4 exposures per week correlates with a 18% drop in engagement rate (ref-06).

### 3. New-Homeowner Welcome Program
Because the audience is defined by a life event, lifecycle triggers outperform blasts: lifecycle-triggered emails average a 38% open rate, versus 21% for standard campaign sends (ref-16). A new-mover welcome mailer with a bundle offer is followed by a four-email onboarding sequence introducing one project at a time.

### 4. In-Store "First Project" Zone
The Finish It Guarantee only works if the store delivers it. Front-of-store displays and associates trained to ask "What are you working on?" convert guided content into guided purchases — the service experience big-box staffing models cannot match.

### 5. Local Community & Geo-Targeted Digital
New homeowners take store recommendations from neighbors. A presence in neighborhood Facebook groups plus geo-targeted ads around each of the 40 stores keeps Hartline the named answer when someone asks where to start.
