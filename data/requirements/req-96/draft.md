# Sunbrook Crisps "Game Day Batch" Tailgate Season Program — DRAFT

**Prepared by:** Ryo Tanabe, Junior Planner, Meridian & Craft
**Client:** Sunbrook Foods
**Campaign window:** August 15 – November 30, 2027
**Budget:** $360,000

---

## Campaign Title

**"Bring the Batch" — Game Day Batch Tailgate Program**

---

## Opening

Introducing Sunbrook Crisps Game Day Batch, a football-season lineup built for the watch party. Game Day Batch pairs two limited flavors (Stadium BBQ and Cheese Blitz) with a 6-count multipack format, so hosts can stock the table in one grab. Pricing is $4.29 per bag and $8.99 per multipack. The bags carry a team-colors design system that looks great stacked on a snack table, and the multipack box doubles as a serving tray.

Football season is the biggest snacking occasion of the year, and hosts are looking for easy crowd-pleasers. Game Day Batch gives Sunbrook a seasonal platform it can own every fall.

---

## Target Audience

| Segment | Description |
|---|---|
| Primary | Football watch-party hosts aged 21-44 in Midwest drive markets |
| Secondary | Guests who taste the product at a party and buy later |

Hosts shop the weekend before games and value convenience, quantity, and flavors a group will like. Social platforms are a big part of how they plan gatherings.

---

## The Big Idea

**"Bring the Batch"** makes Sunbrook the official move for whoever hosts. Every asset shows the multipack landing on the table like a play call. We give hosts simple rituals (the pre-game stack, the flavor draft) that are easy to film and repeat weekly through the season.

---

## Channel & Media Plan

| Channel | Role |
|---|---|
| Paid social — short-form video (Reels, TikTok) | Weekly game-day content flights timed to matchups; hosting hacks and flavor-draft formats |
| Creator partnerships | Food and tailgate creators run "Bring the Batch" hosting content through the season |
| Grocery retail media | Sponsored placements on grocery partner networks against snack and party searches |
| In-store sampling & displays | Weekend sampling with team-table end-caps in priority Midwest markets |

Short-form video performs well with this group, and creator content tends to feel more genuine than brand ads. Retail media keeps the multipack visible where the weekly shop actually happens, and sampling converts hosts at shelf.
