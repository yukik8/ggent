# Blue Fork Kitchen "First Fork" Breakfast Launch Proposal — DRAFT

**Prepared by:** Ryo Tanabe, Junior Planner, Meridian & Craft
**Client:** Blue Fork Kitchen
**Campaign window:** March 1 – June 30, 2026
**Budget:** $390,000

---

## Campaign Title

**"First Fork" — Blue Fork Owns the Morning**

---

## Opening: Introducing the Blue Fork Breakfast Menu

Blue Fork Kitchen is launching a full breakfast daypart across its 85 locations, and the menu is built on the same build-your-own format guests already know from lunch. The anchor product is the new breakfast bowl line: a warm grain base, choice of eggs or plant protein, roasted vegetables, and signature sauces, assembled to order in under four minutes. Paired with the new in-house drip and cold brew coffee program, the headline offer is the **$8.50 breakfast bowl and coffee bundle** — one price, one tap in the app, ready when you walk in.

The breakfast menu carries over Blue Fork's strengths: fresh ingredients, visible assembly, order-ahead through the existing app, and a dedicated morning pickup shelf. Breakfast is a growing daypart in fast-casual, and morning customers value speed and consistency, which is exactly what the Blue Fork format delivers. The "First Fork" campaign will introduce the bundle to commuters and make Blue Fork part of the weekday morning routine.

---

## Target Audience

| Segment | Description | Priority |
|---|---|---|
| Weekday commuters | Workers passing urban and suburban Blue Fork locations on their morning route, ordering ahead on mobile | Primary |
| Existing lunch regulars | Current app users and loyalty members who already trust Blue Fork at lunch | Primary |
| Remote/hybrid workers | Nearby residents looking for a morning coffee-plus-food run on office days | Secondary |

Our primary audience is weekday commuters near urban and suburban locations. Many commuters currently default to drive-thru coffee chains or skip breakfast entirely, and a lot of them already pass a Blue Fork on their way to work. Our existing lunch customers are also a natural audience, since they already have the app installed and know the brand. Social media and app notifications are effective ways to reach both groups.

---

## The Big Idea

**"First Fork"** positions Blue Fork as the first stop of the workday. The creative platform is simple: your morning has a first fork in the road — the same old coffee line, or a real breakfast that's ready when you are. Every asset shows the bundle in hand, the pickup shelf with the guest's name, and a clock: order at 7:40, pick up at 7:52. The campaign turns order-ahead breakfast into a visible morning ritual, with the $8.50 bundle as the price everyone remembers.

## Channel & Media Plan

| # | Channel | Role in Plan |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Paid social — short-form video (Reels, TikTok) | Awareness of the new daypart and bundle price |
| 2 | Loyalty app, CRM & push notifications | Convert existing lunch users into breakfast users |
| 3 | Commuter out-of-home (transit + sidewalk signage) | Reach commuters on the physical morning route |
| 4 | Local creator partnerships | Authentic morning-routine content by market |
| 5 | Morning sampling & local-store marketing | Trial at high-traffic locations during launch |

### 1. Paid Social — Short-Form Video
Short-form video performs very well for food content, and morning-routine content is a popular format. We will run Reels and TikTok flights geo-targeted around location trade areas, timed to morning hours, showing the bundle being made and picked up. Social will do the heavy lifting for daypart awareness.

### 2. Loyalty App, CRM & Push
Blue Fork already has a strong digital ordering base. We will use push notifications, lifecycle emails, and in-app banners to introduce breakfast to existing customers, including a "first breakfast on us" loyalty bonus points offer and a saved "usual morning order" feature promotion. Loyalty members are the most valuable customers, so this channel should drive efficient early volume.

### 3. Commuter Out-of-Home
Transit placements, station-adjacent posters, and sidewalk A-frames within walking distance of locations will hit commuters at the exact moment breakfast decisions happen. Creative is route-specific: "Your 7:45 just got better. $8.50 bowl + coffee, two blocks ahead."

### 4. Local Creator Partnerships
We will partner with local food and lifestyle creators in key metros to post real morning-routine content featuring the bundle and the order-ahead experience. Creator content feels more trustworthy than brand ads and gives us assets to amplify in paid.

### 5. Morning Sampling & Local-Store Marketing
During launch weeks, sampling teams at high-traffic locations will hand out mini bowls and coffee during the 7–9 AM window, with QR codes to download the app and claim the launch offer. Store teams get local marketing kits for nearby offices.
