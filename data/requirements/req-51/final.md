## Campaign Title

**"First Fork" — Blue Fork Owns the Morning**

---

## Opening: The Problem We Are Actually Solving

Blue Fork has an empty-morning problem. Eighty-five locations carry full rent, staff, and kitchens from early morning, yet earn essentially nothing before 11 AM, while thousands of commuters walk past those same doors every weekday and spend their breakfast money at drive-thru coffee chains. The category is growing fast enough to reward whoever captures those mornings — US fast-casual sales reached $209B in 2024, growing 8.2% YoY while full-service restaurants grew 2.4% (ref-10) — and Blue Fork's format is unusually suited to claim them, because the behavior breakfast demands is one the brand already runs at scale: digital orders average 38% of fast-casual revenue, and top-quartile chains exceed 55% (ref-10).

The new breakfast daypart is our answer to that specific problem. The build-your-own breakfast bowl line — warm grain base, eggs or plant protein, roasted vegetables, signature sauces, assembled in under four minutes — pairs with the in-house coffee program in the headline **$8.50 breakfast bowl and coffee bundle**: one price, one tap in the app, ready when you walk in. Every tactic below is selected for its ability to fill the empty morning hours with a repeatable commuter habit, not just to announce a menu.

## Target Audience

| Segment | Description | Priority |
|---|---|---|
| Weekday commuters | Workers passing urban and suburban Blue Fork locations on their morning route, ordering ahead on mobile | Primary |
| Existing lunch regulars | Current app users and loyalty members who already trust Blue Fork at lunch | Primary |
| Remote/hybrid workers | Nearby residents looking for a morning coffee-plus-food run on office days | Secondary |

Our primary audience is weekday commuters near urban and suburban locations, most of whom currently default to coffee chains or skip breakfast entirely. Winning them starts on their phones: 74% of diners aged 18-34 check a chain's app or social feed before first visiting a new location (ref-10), and a new daypart is, behaviorally, a first visit. Our existing lunch regulars are the second engine, and they are disproportionately valuable — loyalty-app members visit fast-casual chains 2.3x more often than non-members and spend 31% more per order (ref-10) — so converting even a fraction of them into morning users compounds quickly.

---

## The Big Idea

**"First Fork"** positions Blue Fork as the first stop of the workday. The creative platform is simple: your morning has a first fork in the road — the same old coffee line, or a real breakfast that's ready when you are. Every asset shows the bundle in hand, the pickup shelf with the guest's name, and a clock: order at 7:40, pick up at 7:52. Because the core problem is that mornings are currently spent elsewhere on autopilot, the campaign makes the order-ahead ritual itself the story, with the $8.50 bundle as the price everyone remembers.

## Channel & Media Plan

| # | Channel | Role in Plan |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Paid social — short-form video (Reels, TikTok) | Awareness of the new daypart and bundle price |
| 2 | Loyalty app, CRM & push notifications | Convert existing lunch users into breakfast users |
| 3 | Commuter out-of-home (transit + sidewalk signage) | Reach commuters on the physical morning route |
| 4 | Local creator partnerships | Authentic morning-routine content by market |
| 5 | Morning sampling & local-store marketing | Trial at high-traffic locations during launch |

### 1. Paid Social — Short-Form Video
Because the core problem is that commuters don't yet associate Blue Fork with mornings, short-form video builds that association where the audience already scrolls. We will run Reels and TikTok flights geo-targeted around location trade areas and timed to morning hours, showing the bundle being made and picked up. The format earns its lead position on the numbers: short-form video ads average 2.4x the completion rate of static feed ads (ref-06), with Instagram Reels at a $9.40 average CPM and TikTok at $7.80 (ref-06). We will cap delivery deliberately, since frequency above 4 exposures per week correlates with an 18% drop in engagement rate (ref-06).

### 2. Loyalty App, CRM & Push
Because our cheapest breakfast customer is someone who already buys lunch from us, this channel converts the installed base first. Push notifications, lifecycle emails, and in-app banners introduce breakfast with a "first breakfast on us" loyalty bonus and a saved "usual morning order" promotion — and lifecycle-triggered emails average a 38% open rate, versus 21% for standard campaign sends (ref-16), so the triggered flow does the heavy lifting. Members are worth the focus: loyalty-app members visit 2.3x more often and spend 31% more per order (ref-10).

### 3. Commuter Out-of-Home
Because the breakfast decision is made on the physical route at 7:45, not on a couch at 9 PM, transit placements, station-adjacent posters, and sidewalk A-frames within walking distance of locations intercept the exact moment of choice. Creative is route-specific: "Your 7:45 just got better. $8.50 bowl + coffee, two blocks ahead."

### 4. Local Creator Partnerships
Because a new morning habit spreads by imitation, local food and lifestyle creators in key metros will post real morning-routine content featuring the bundle and the order-ahead experience. The channel is efficient as well as credible — creator-partnership posts deliver 31% lower cost-per-engagement than brand-owned posts (ref-06) — and their assets feed the paid flight.

### 5. Morning Sampling & Local-Store Marketing
Because no ad beats a warm bowl in hand at 7:30 AM, sampling teams at high-traffic locations will hand out mini bowls and coffee during the 7–9 AM launch window, with QR codes to download the app and claim the launch offer. Store teams get local marketing kits for nearby offices, keeping each location loud in its own trade area.

## Closing

Blue Fork's morning problem is specific — paid-for kitchens earning nothing while commuters spend breakfast money next door — and "First Fork" attacks it with the assets Blue Fork already owns: an order-ahead app its guests use daily, a loyalty base that visits 2.3x more often than non-members (ref-10), and a format built for speed. The $8.50 bundle gives commuters a memorable reason to switch, and the plan reaches them in the feed, on the route, and in the app. We recommend proceeding on the March 1 timeline.
