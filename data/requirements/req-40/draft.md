# Cascade Valley RV & Campground Awareness Push Proposal — DRAFT

**Prepared by:** Marcus Bell, Junior Planner, Meridian & Craft
**Client:** Cascade Valley Tourism Board
**Campaign window:** May 1 – August 31, 2027
**Budget:** $180,000

---

## Campaign Title

**"Full Hookup Summer" — Cascade Valley's RV & Campground Season**

---

## Opening: The Valley Is Ready to Host

Cascade Valley's campgrounds are genuinely great product. The region has riverfront pull-through sites with full hookups, walk-in tent loops under old-growth canopy, and a handful of newer glamping-adjacent options, all priced between $45 and $85 a night. Several parks have upgraded bathhouses and dump stations in the last few seasons, most sites are big-rig friendly, and nearly everything is bookable online. Add trail access, small-town farm stands, and swimming holes within a short drive of every campground, and you have a destination that stacks up against anywhere in the region for a rolling-home summer.

Meanwhile, camping culture has never been bigger online. Vanlife content keeps pulling huge audiences, campground-tour videos and setup POVs are a thriving genre of their own, and RV trip-planning apps have turned route research into a social activity. Remote workers are running whole workweeks from campsites, families are treating RV trips as the affordable alternative to flying, and "campsite cooking" is basically its own content vertical now. The plan below plugs Cascade Valley into every one of those currents at once, so that wherever camping culture is happening this summer, the Valley shows up in it.

---

## The Big Idea

**"Full Hookup Summer"** reclaims a piece of RV jargon as an invitation: everything's connected here — your rig, your route, and your whole crew. The campaign packages the Valley as one big connected basecamp, with a "Valley Loop" of campgrounds you can hop between over a week, each stop paired with a signature nearby experience (the riverfront site plus the swimming hole, the canyon loop plus the farm-stand breakfast). Every piece of content ends the same way: a site number, a nightly rate, and a booking link. It's a hook that campers will get instantly and non-campers will find charming.

## Channel & Media Plan

### 1. Vanlife & RV Creator Convoys
Creator road-trip content is the engine of camping culture right now, so we'll invite waves of vanlife, RV-family, and campground-review creators to run the Valley Loop and document every hookup, setup, and sunset. We'll go broad on the roster — big rigs, converted vans, first-time renters — because volume of authentic setup content matters more than any single big name. Campground-tour walkthroughs and "is this site worth it" reviews are performing incredibly well lately, and we want a steady stream of them shot in the Valley.

### 2. Paid Short-Form Video (TikTok, Reels, Shorts)
We'll boost the best convoy content and produce our own fast-cut POV series — pulling into the site, one-tap booking screen recordings, camp-dinner ASMR, morning fog off the river. Short-form is where trip inspiration happens, and camping POV formats are consistently strong there. Retargeting will chase viewers who watched a full site tour but haven't booked, with the site number and rate right in the creative.

### 3. RV Trip-Planner Apps & Camping Podcasts
Serious RV travelers plan inside route and campground apps, so we'll take sponsored placements and featured-destination slots there, putting the Valley Loop directly into the tools people use to build their summer routes. We'll pair that with host-read spots on RV and camping podcasts, which have very loyal audiences and are a natural fit for a talk-through of the Loop itinerary.

### 4. Highway Corridor OOH & Fuel-Stop Takeovers
RVers make decisions on the road, so we'll run billboards along the main approach corridors and takeovers at major fuel and travel stops within the drive radius — pump-topper ads, window clings, and a QR code that opens live site availability. "You're 90 minutes from a riverfront full hookup" is a hard message to ignore when you're standing at a gas pump next to your rig.

### 5. Always-On Trend Content
Camping content trends move weekly, so a lightweight content studio will keep the board's channels jumping on whatever's current — campsite-cooking formats, setup speedruns, "working from the campground" day-in-the-life posts — always tagged back to a bookable Loop stop. Tourism accounts that go quiet between campaigns lose the algorithm, so this keeps the Valley in the conversation all season.

---

## Target Audience

Primary audience is RV travelers and campers within six hours' drive of Cascade Valley — rig owners planning their summer routes, families booking a week of full-hookup camping, and renters trying the RV life for the first time. They research in apps and on social, they compare campgrounds by video before committing, and a clearly presented $45–$85 nightly rate reads as excellent value to them. Many are already saving campground-tour videos for exactly this kind of trip.

Secondary audience is tent campers and glamping-curious weekenders in the same drive radius who follow camping content and will book the walk-in loops and upgraded sites.
