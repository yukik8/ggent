## Campaign Title

**"Saving for Two"**

---

## Opening: The Problem We Are Actually Solving

Stackwise's growth problem is not downloads, it is disappearing users. The average consumer fintech app loses 73% of new users within 30 days of download (ref-13), and the strongest known antidote is anchoring the app to real money flows early — users who link a payroll deposit in their first week retain at 3.2x the rate of those who do not (ref-13). The category is still expanding — 58% of US adults under 35 used a budgeting or savings app in 2025, up from 44% in 2022 (ref-13) — so the winner is whoever keeps the users everyone else churns.

Shared Savings Goals is our answer to that specific problem. A joint goal with automatic contributions from both partners gives two users at once a recurring reason to return, and the partner-invite mechanic recruits each new user's most trusted accountability system: the person they live with. Free for every user, with the $8/month premium unlocking unlimited goals, every tactic below is selected to move day-30 retention, not just installs.

---

## Target Audience

Cohabiting couples aged 24-35 merging finances for the first time — new and existing users at the exact life moment when money habits get rebuilt, and when a shared goal can anchor both partners past the 30-day cliff (ref-13).

---

## The Big Idea

**"Saving for Two"** celebrates the sweet, slightly awkward reality of merging money. Because the core problem is trust-fragile new users abandoning empty dashboards, the creative shows real couples, real goals, and real screens with modest, believable numbers — and never a whiff of yield talk, since 61% of savers under 30 say they distrust finance apps that lead with investment returns instead of safety (ref-13).

---

## Channel Plan

Each channel maps back to the 30-day retention problem named above.

### 1. Short-Form Video App-Install Campaign (TikTok & Reels)
Because the core problem is retention economics, acquisition must be cheap enough to survive the funnel: short-form video app-install campaigns average a $4.10 CPI for finance apps, versus $6.80 on search (ref-13). Couple-POV creative shows the shared goal screen filling up — the retained-user behavior, advertised.

### 2. Couple-Creator Partnerships
Creators document a real 30-day shared goal with actual numbers on screen, modeling safety-first, believable saving rather than returns talk, per the 61% distrust finding (ref-13).

### 3. Paid Social Retargeting
Follow-up flights for video viewers and lookalikes built from existing power users, keeping spend concentrated on audiences most likely to activate a goal, not just install.

### 4. In-App & Lifecycle Program
Because retention is won inside the product, announcement flows, partner-invite prompts, and milestone push moments drive the two behaviors the data rewards: an active shared goal and a funded contribution in week one — the same first-week anchoring logic behind the 3.2x payroll-link retention effect (ref-13).
