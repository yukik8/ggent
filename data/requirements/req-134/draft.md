## Campaign Title

**"Saving for Two"**

---

## Opening: A Feature Couples Will Fall in Love With

Introducing Shared Savings Goals, the most delightful feature Stackwise has ever shipped. Couples can now create joint goals — the first apartment, the big trip, the emergency cushion — with automatic contributions from both partners, a shared progress view, and gentle celebratory moments when milestones hit. It's free for every user, with the $8/month premium tier unlocking unlimited goals and custom rules.

Merging money is one of the most emotional moments in a young relationship, and this beautiful, thoughtfully designed feature makes it feel exciting instead of scary. This launch is Stackwise's chance to become the app couples start their financial life on.

---

## Target Audience

Cohabiting couples aged 24-35 merging finances for the first time. They're moving in together, splitting rent and groceries, and dreaming about shared milestones. They're social, they're on their phones constantly, and they love sharing relationship moments online.

---

## The Big Idea

**"Saving for Two"** celebrates the sweet, slightly awkward reality of merging money. Real couples, real goals, real screens: the couple watching their trip fund tick up, the roommates-turned-partners building a deposit. Warm, funny, and utterly shareable.

---

## Channel Plan

### 1. Short-Form Video App-Install Campaign (TikTok & Reels)
Charming couple-POV videos showing the shared goal screen filling up. This is where our audience lives, and video is incredibly effective for app installs.

### 2. Couple-Creator Partnerships
Beloved couple and personal-finance creators document a real 30-day shared goal, showing their actual numbers and celebrating milestones with their audiences.

### 3. Paid Social Retargeting
Follow-up flights for video viewers and site visitors, plus lookalikes built from existing power users.

### 4. In-App & Lifecycle Program
Announcement flows to the existing base, partner-invite prompts, and milestone-celebration push moments designed to be screenshotted.
