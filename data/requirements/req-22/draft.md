## Campaign Title

**"The Starting Lineup" — the Velura Foundations Men's Launch**

---

## Opening: Velura Enters the Men's Game

Introducing Velura Foundations, a three-SKU men's entry line that brings Velura's fragrance-free, clean-formula DNA to a whole new aisle. The lineup is a gel cleanser, a lightweight version of the hero moisturizer, and a daily SPF fluid, priced $24-$38 across three SKUs so guys can build a real routine without a luxury-counter commitment. The packaging is matte slate with numbered steps right on the bottle — 01, 02, 03 — so the routine explains itself.

The timing could not be better. Men's grooming content is absolutely everywhere right now. Morning-routine videos, "get ready with me" clips from male creators, barbershop content, gym-locker-room skincare talk — the whole culture has shifted, and skincare is no longer a hard sell for men, it is a trend. Skinimalism is also huge, and a numbered three-step system is basically skinimalism in a box. On top of that, clean beauty keeps growing fast, and Velura is one of the most trusted names in the space. Foundations lets Velura ride all of these waves at once and plant a flag in men's skincare before the moment passes.

---

## The Big Idea

**"The Starting Lineup"** frames the three SKUs like a roster: three players, three positions, one team. Cleanser is your opener, moisturizer is your anchor, SPF is your closer. Every asset uses the roster framing — creator videos introduce "the lineup," retail displays are built like a locker-room shelf, and the numbered bottles reinforce that there is an order and it is easy. The invitation to guys who have never done more than splash water on their face is simple: you don't need to figure skincare out, you just need a starting lineup.

## Channel & Media Plan

There is a lot happening in this space right now, and we want to be present across all of it.

### 1. Paid Social — Short-Form Video (TikTok, Instagram Reels, YouTube Shorts)
Short-form video is where grooming culture lives, and it is a very effective format for routine content. We will run a sustained flight of paid placements across all three platforms — routine demos, "01-02-03" transition edits, and cut-downs of creator content. Trends move fast here, so we will keep a rapid-response editing capability on retainer to jump on formats as they emerge.

### 2. Creator Partnerships — Grooming, Fitness & Everyday Guys
We will build a roster of male creators across grooming, fitness, and lifestyle — plus a deliberate tier of regular, non-influencer-looking guys, because authenticity is everything in this category. Creators will document their first thirty days on the lineup. Male skincare creators are having a real moment, and getting Velura into their routines early positions the brand as the default recommendation.

### 3. Specialty Retail Activation
Velura is expanding into specialty beauty retail, and Foundations should be the spearhead. We will build "Starting Lineup" end-caps and shelf displays in partner beauty chains, with tester stations and a simple three-step counter card. Retail staff get a one-page playbook so they can walk a first-timer through the routine in under a minute. In-store discovery is a big deal in beauty, and men shopping these stores are often there for someone else — the display converts them for themselves.

### 4. Lifecycle Email & SMS — The 30-Day Routine Coach
Every Foundations buyer gets enrolled in a "Routine Coach" flow: a short series of emails and optional SMS nudges over their first month, covering how much product to use, what order, what results to expect by week. Retention is where DTC brands win or lose, and a guy who makes it through his first jar is very likely to reorder. The flow also cross-sells the core Velura franchise once the habit is formed.

---

## Target Audience

Our primary target is men aged 22-38 who are new to multi-step skincare. Most of them currently use a bar of soap, maybe an aftershave balm, and whatever moisturizer a partner left in the bathroom. They are curious — the content is in their feeds already — but intimidated by ingredient jargon and ten-step routines. They respond to clear instructions, guy-coded creative, and social proof from people who look like them, not from beauty-industry polish. Many of them will happily spend on skincare once someone makes the first step obvious.

Secondary audiences include partners and family members buying a starter routine as a gift (especially in the holiday window) and existing Velura customers introducing the brand to the men in their household.
