## Campaign Title

**"Small Space, Big Pull" — the Row One Mini Launch**

## Opening: The Problem We Are Actually Solving

Kinetiq's growth ceiling is not demand, it is fear. 71% of connected-fitness intenders cite fear of unused equipment as their top purchase hesitation (ref-11), and for apartment dwellers that fear is doubled: an abandoned rower is not just wasted money, it is wasted living room. The retention data shows exactly where the fear comes true — members completing 8 or more workouts in their first month show 81% 12-month retention, versus 34% for those completing fewer (ref-11). The Row One Mini is our answer to that specific problem: a rower that folds to a 20-inch upright footprint at $1,295 or $46/month, in the category's fastest-growing modality — rowing, at 19% YoY unit growth (ref-11) — inside a market back in growth, up 6% YoY to $4.8B (ref-11). Every tactic below is selected to defuse the unused-equipment fear before purchase and drive first-month activation after it.

## Target Audience

Apartment dwellers aged 25-40 with limited floor space. They research heavily before a purchase at this price — Gen-Z respondents check an average of 2.7 sources before a first purchase over $50 (ref-07) — and they respond to proof, not promises: 58% say they distrust ads that make claims without visible evidence or numbers (ref-07). Our creative therefore shows real apartments, real folding, and real retention numbers.

## The Big Idea

**"Small Space, Big Pull."** Because the core problem is the fear of a $1,295 clothes rack, the creative does not sell fitness transformation — it shows the Mini disappearing into closets and sliding under lofted beds in real apartments, proving the machine fits the life the buyer already has.

## Channel & Media Plan

Each channel maps back to defusing hesitation and activating the first month.

| Channel | Role |
|---|---|
| Paid social — short-form video | Apartment-transformation creative on Reels and TikTok. Short-form video ads average 2.4x the completion rate of static feed ads (ref-06) at a $9.40 Reels and $7.80 TikTok CPM (ref-06); frequency stays capped, since above 4 exposures per week engagement drops 18% (ref-06) |
| YouTube creator & review partnerships | Because hesitant buyers hunt for evidence across 2.7 sources (ref-07), honest long-form reviews and small-space setup tours supply it; 64% of Gen-Z respondents have purchased on a niche creator's recommendation (ref-07) |
| Paid search & retargeting | Capture compact-equipment demand and re-engage researchers mid-consideration |
| 30-day in-home trial offer promotion | The conversion centerpiece: free in-home trial programs convert at 46%, versus 11% for standard e-commerce checkout (ref-11). Every asset routes to the trial |
| Email / CRM | Trial-requester nurture built as lifecycle-triggered flows — 38% open rate versus 21% for standard sends (ref-16) — scheduling the first three workouts to push new members past the 8-workout retention gate (ref-11) |

## Why This Will Work

The plan attacks the measured cause of lost sales — the unused-equipment fear held by 71% of intenders (ref-11) — with proof-first creative, a trial that converts at four times checkout, and onboarding engineered around the first-month retention gate. Every dollar answers the same named problem.
