# Kinetiq Row One Mini Launch — DRAFT

**Prepared by:** Ryo Tanabe, Junior Planner, Meridian & Craft
**Client:** Kinetiq Home
**Campaign window:** September 15, 2027 – January 31, 2028
**Budget:** $560,000

---

## Campaign Title

**"Small Space, Big Pull" — the Row One Mini Launch**

## Opening

Introducing the Row One Mini, Kinetiq's compact connected rower engineered for apartments. The Mini folds to a 20-inch upright footprint, rolls into a closet, and runs the full Kinetiq live and on-demand class library on a fold-flat display. It launches at $1,295 or $46/month financing, with the standard $39/month membership. The Mini brings Kinetiq's full training experience to homes that could never fit the original Row One, opening a large new segment for the brand.

## Product Summary

| Element | Detail |
|---|---|
| Product | Row One Mini compact connected rower |
| Footprint | Folds to 20-inch upright; under 8 sq ft in use |
| Price | $1,295 or $46/month financing |
| Membership | $39/month live + on-demand classes |
| Trial | 30-day in-home trial available |

## Target Audience

Apartment dwellers aged 25-40 with limited floor space. They want to work out at home but assume connected equipment is for houses with spare rooms. Many are current boutique-studio members frustrated by class costs and commutes.

## The Big Idea

**"Small Space, Big Pull."** Creative shows the Mini living in real apartments — folding out of closets, sliding from under lofted beds, setting up between a couch and a coffee table — proving a full-body workout fits anywhere.

## Channel & Media Plan

| Channel | Role |
|---|---|
| Paid social — short-form video | Apartment-transformation creative on Reels and TikTok, the core awareness engine |
| YouTube creator & review partnerships | Honest long-form reviews and small-space setup tours from fitness and apartment-living creators |
| Paid search & retargeting | Capture compact-equipment demand and re-engage site visitors |
| 30-day in-home trial offer promotion | The trial is the conversion centerpiece across all media |
| Email / CRM | Nurture flows for trial requesters and waitlist signups |

Social video reaches this audience efficiently, and reviews matter a great deal for a considered purchase like this. The in-home trial reduces perceived risk and should meaningfully lift conversion.

## KPIs

- Awareness of the Row One Mini among apartment-dwelling fitness intenders
- Trial requests and trial-to-purchase conversion
- Cost per acquisition versus the original Row One launch
- Membership attach rate on Mini purchases

## Why This Will Work

The Mini removes the biggest practical barrier to home rowing — space — at a more accessible price. With strong review coverage, apartment-native creative, and the trial lowering the stakes, the launch should establish the Mini as the default compact rower for urban customers.
