# Proposal Draft — Cascade Valley Shoulder-Season Weekend Getaway Campaign

**Client:** Cascade Valley Tourism Board
**Prepared by:** Emma Lindqvist, Junior Planner, Meridian & Craft
**Version:** Draft v1
**Campaign window:** 2025-08-15 to 2025-10-31
**Budget:** $310,000

---

## Campaign Title

**"The Golden Hours: 48 Hours in Cascade Valley"**

---

## Opening

Cascade Valley is one of the most beautiful regional destinations in the country, and this fall it will be at its absolute best. Introducing The Golden Hours, a campaign built around everything that makes the Valley special: the amber foliage along the ridge trails, the riverside farm markets, the small-town main streets strung with lights, the tasting rooms, and the cozy inns that make a weekend here feel like a week away. Our getaway packages, positioned around $400 per visitor, bundle lodging, food, and activities into one easy booking, so a visit to Cascade Valley has never been simpler or better value.

The campaign will showcase the Valley's signature experiences through beautiful short-form video, inspiring creator content, and a streamlined booking hub, bringing the magic of Cascade Valley in autumn to travelers everywhere.

## Target Audience

Our primary audience is drive-market travelers under 35 within roughly 4 hours of Cascade Valley. This is a generation that loves travel and experiences. Young people today are always looking for their next weekend escape, and they respond strongly to visual content. Social media is where they live, so that is where we will meet them. We will also pick up secondary audiences along the way, including couples, young families, and anyone in the drive market dreaming about a fall weekend, because great content travels.

## The Big Idea

**Golden hour only happens twice a day, and the Valley's golden season only happens once a year.** The Golden Hours frames the shoulder season as the Valley's most cinematic window, and packages it as a ready-made 48-hour itinerary. We will produce a series of "48 Hours in Cascade Valley" itinerary films, each following one real weekend from Friday arrival to Sunday send-off: sunrise on the ridge trail, lunch at the farm market, an afternoon paddle, dinner on a lit-up main street. Every film ends with the same simple promise: your golden weekend is one booking away.

The idea works because young travelers love authentic, itinerary-style content. It shows rather than tells, and it makes the trip feel achievable instead of aspirational.

## Channel & Media Plan

1. **Short-form video (Instagram Reels, TikTok, YouTube Shorts)** — the campaign's engine. Short vertical video is extremely effective with young audiences and delivers strong engagement, so the itinerary films will run as a sustained paid program across all three platforms throughout the window.
2. **Creator partnerships** — we will partner with regional travel and outdoors creators to produce their own 48-hour weekends in the Valley. Creator content feels more trustworthy to young consumers than brand advertising and tends to perform very well.
3. **Content production** — a two-wave shoot (early fall and peak foliage) producing the hero itinerary films plus a large library of cutdowns and platform-native edits.
4. **Booking hub and email** — a dedicated "Golden Hours" landing page with the 48-hour itineraries, package booking, and an email capture flow for trip planners who are not ready to book yet.
5. **Metro digital out-of-home** — digital boards and transit screens in the two largest drive-market metros, timed to Thursday and Friday commutes when weekend plans are being made.

## KPIs

- **Reach & awareness:** broad reach among the drive-market audience, with strong video views and completion across platforms
- **Engagement:** healthy engagement rates on hero and creator content
- **Traffic:** meaningful visits to the Golden Hours booking hub
- **Conversion:** package bookings and email captures attributable to the campaign
- **Visitation:** an uplift in shoulder-season visits versus last year
