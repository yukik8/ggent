---

## Campaign Title

**"The Great Exhale"**

---

## Opening: The Problem We Are Actually Solving

Cascade Valley's spring problem is a measured occupancy gap, not a marketing-fashion problem. Shoulder-season (Apr-May, Sep-Oct) occupancy for regional destinations averages 46%, versus 78% peak-season (ref-05) — a 32-point hole in exactly the April-May window this campaign covers, with lodging and dining infrastructure already built and staffed. The demand to fill it is close by and valuable: drive-market weekend trips make up 61% of visits to small US regional destinations (ref-05), and the average regional weekend visitor spends $412 per trip across lodging, food, and activities (ref-05).

The Spring Wellness Retreat — a weekend package around $450 per couple bundling lodging, guided forest-bathing walks, riverside yoga, spa access, and a farm-to-table dinner — is our answer to that specific gap: a reason for burned-out drive-market professionals to book the valley's emptiest weeks. Every tactic below maps back to shoulder-season bookings, not to wellness-trend reach for its own sake.

---

## Target Audience

Burned-out professionals aged 28-45 within drive-market range, named up front because they decide our success. Their trip decisions start on a phone: 54% of travelers under 35 pick regional trips based on short-form video seen in the prior 30 days (ref-05), and the older half of the cohort follows the same creators. Our content shows a specific, bookable reset — a named trail, a named spa, a $450 package — because that specificity is what converts.

---

## The Big Idea

**"The Great Exhale"** frames Cascade Valley as the place where the drive market goes to breathe out — slow pacing, natural sound, one repeated visual motif of a long exhale as the valley opens up through the windshield. Because the core problem is empty shoulder-season rooms, every asset ends on the stay itself: the package, the property, the booking link.

---

## Channel Plan, Mapped to the Problem

1. **Short-form video (TikTok, Reels).** Because the core problem is filling April-May beds and 54% of travelers under 35 pick regional trips based on short-form video seen in the prior 30 days (ref-05), this is the primary demand engine. TikTok inventory averages a $7.80 CPM and Instagram Reels $9.40 (ref-06), and short-form video ads average 2.4x the completion rate of static feed ads (ref-06).
2. **Hosted wellness creator stays.** Creator-partnership posts deliver 31% lower cost-per-engagement than brand-owned posts (ref-06); hosted stays produce the authentic, itinerary-specific footage that drives trip intent.
3. **Podcast & audio sponsorships.** Host-read spots reach the stressed 28-45 professional during commutes, reinforcing the video flight.
4. **Paid search & display.** Captures in-market "spring retreat" demand at the moment of booking.
5. **Email, CRM & landing page.** Lifecycle-triggered emails average a 38% open rate, versus 21% for standard campaign sends (ref-16), so the booking funnel uses triggered follow-ups rather than blast sends — and sustained presence matters, since destinations running always-on social content see 27% higher repeat visitation than campaign-only destinations (ref-05).

## Why This Will Work

This plan aims the wellness wave at the valley's real problem: a 46%-versus-78% occupancy gap (ref-05) in the exact weeks the campaign runs. Video and creators create the urge to exhale; search, email, and the package turn that urge into occupied rooms.
