## Campaign Title

**"Certified Good Dog" — the Pawstead Treats Holiday Gifting Push**

---

## Opening: Treats Are Having a Moment

Pawstead Treats arrive at exactly the right time. The new line takes the same vet-formulated, fresh-first philosophy behind Pawstead's refrigerated meals and packs it into a shelf-stable $14 pouch — single-protein, limited-ingredient, with the ingredient panel printed prominently on the front, because that transparency is the whole point. There are four launch flavors, the pouches look genuinely giftable, and they require no fridge space, which removes the biggest friction point of the core product.

Meanwhile, pet content dominates social feeds every fourth quarter, and the holiday pet-gifting habit keeps growing — recent industry surveys suggest roughly three in four dog owners now buy their pets a holiday gift. Dog unboxing videos, "my dog reviews his advent calendar" formats, and treat-bag ASMR all surge from October onward. Pets are family, a $14 pouch is a natural impulse-gift price, and if Pawstead shows up with the right formats at the right moments, the Treats line can ride the holiday pet-content wave from October through mid-December.

---

## The Big Idea

**"Certified Good Dog."** Every dog made the Nice List this year — obviously — and Pawstead Treats are how you make it official. Each pouch in the holiday window ships with a tear-off "Certified Good Dog" certificate (name line, paw-print stamp box, official-looking seal), and the campaign is built around owners certifying their dogs on camera. It is a ready-made UGC format: read the certificate aloud, dog gets the treat, dog loses its mind, everyone tears up a little. Creators can riff on it endlessly — graduation-ceremony parodies, certifying the neighborhood dogs, redemption arcs for dogs who ate the couch in March.

It is shareable, it is remixable, and it turns a treats pouch into a moment instead of a purchase.

## Channel & Media Plan

### 1. Paid Social — TikTok & Instagram Reels
Short-form video is where pet content lives and where it performs best, so this is our largest line. We will run certificate-ceremony hero cuts, treat-reaction compilations, crinkle-ASMR spots, and gift-guide-style carousels, refreshed frequently so the campaign stays current with whatever sound or format is trending that week. Heavy-up flights are planned around Black Friday and the December gifting window.

### 2. Dogfluencer & Vet-Creator Partnerships
We will build a roster of dog accounts plus a small group of vet and vet-tech creators to launch the certification format. Dogfluencers bring reach and meme energy; the vet creators bring credibility on the ingredient story, which matters to this label-reading buyer. Creator content consistently outperforms brand-made ads with pet audiences, and everything the roster produces feeds the paid engine.

### 3. UGC Gifting & Seeding Program
A few hundred seeding kits — pouch, oversized certificate, gold "Good Dog" sticker sheet — go to micro creators and to real customers drawn from the Pawstead community, timed so unboxing content lands in early November just as gifting content takes off. Low spend, high content volume, and it keeps the campaign feeling like it comes from actual dog owners.

### 4. Email & SMS to the Pawstead Base
Pawstead already has a subscriber list that loves the brand, and treats are the easiest add-on there is. The holiday flow includes early access for subscribers, a one-tap add-to-my-next-box offer, and a "gift a fellow dog parent" push in December. This is the warmest audience we have, and it costs almost nothing to reach.

### 5. Grocery Retail Media
Sponsored placements on retail partners' networks during peak gifting weeks ensure that shoppers who saw the certificate content mid-scroll find the pouch again at the digital shelf. Retail media closes the loop between social buzz and the actual purchase.

---

## Target Audience

Primary: existing Pawstead subscribers and past purchasers — people who already trust the brand and simply need a low-effort way to say yes to one more item. Secondary: gift-buying pet parents more broadly, the large seasonal audience shopping for their own dogs and for the dog people in their lives during the holidays. These are engaged, label-reading owners who treat their dogs like family members and spend significant time in pet content. Many are already primed by Pawstead's fresh-food positioning even if they have not yet purchased.
