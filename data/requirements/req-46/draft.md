## Campaign Title

**"Certified Good Dog" — the Pawstead Treats Holiday Gifting Push**

---

## Opening: Treats Are Having a Moment

Pawstead Treats are here, and the timing could not be better. The new line takes the same vet-formulated, fresh-first philosophy behind Pawstead's refrigerated meals and packs it into a shelf-stable $14 pouch — single-protein, limited-ingredient, with the ingredient panel printed big on the front because that's the whole point. There are four launch flavors, the pouches are genuinely giftable-looking, and they don't need fridge space, which removes the biggest friction point of the core product.

Meanwhile, pet content is absolutely everywhere right now. Dog unboxing videos, "my dog reviews his advent calendar" formats, POV meme audios, ASMR treat-bag crinkle content — this stuff dominates feeds every Q4 and it keeps getting bigger every year. Pets are family, everyone gifts their pets during the holidays now, and a $14 pouch is the perfect impulse-gift price. If we show up with the right trend formats at the right moments, Pawstead Treats can ride the entire holiday pet-content wave from October through mid-December.

---

## The Big Idea

**"Certified Good Dog."** Every dog made the Nice List this year — obviously — and Pawstead Treats are how you make it official. Each pouch in the holiday window ships with a tear-off "Certified Good Dog" certificate (name line, paw-print stamp box, official-looking seal), and we build the whole campaign around owners certifying their dogs on camera. It's a built-in UGC format: read the certificate out loud, dog gets the treat, dog loses its mind, everyone cries a little. Creators can riff on it endlessly — graduation-ceremony parodies, "certifying the neighborhood dogs," redemption arcs for dogs who ate the couch in March.

It's shareable, it's remixable, and it turns a treats pouch into a moment instead of a purchase.

---

## Channel & Media Plan

### 1. Paid Social — TikTok & Instagram Reels
Short-form video is where pet content lives and it performs incredibly well, so this is our biggest line. We'll run certificate-ceremony hero cuts, treat-reaction compilations, crinkle-ASMR spots, and gift-guide-style carousels, refreshed constantly so we're always on whatever sound or format is trending that week. Heavy-up flights around Black Friday and the December gifting window.

### 2. Dogfluencer & Vet-Creator Partnerships
We'll build a roster of dog accounts and a couple of vet or vet-tech creators to launch the certification format. Dogfluencers bring the reach and the meme energy; the vet creators bring credibility on the ingredient story, which matters a lot to this kind of buyer. Creator content consistently outperforms brand-made ads with pet audiences, and everything they make feeds the paid engine.

### 3. UGC Gifting & Seeding Program
A few hundred seeding kits — pouch, oversized certificate, gold "Good Dog" sticker sheet — sent to micro creators and to real customers pulled from the Pawstead community, timed so unboxing content starts landing in early November right as gifting content takes off. Low spend, high content volume, and it makes the campaign feel like it's coming from actual dog owners.

### 4. Email & SMS to the Pawstead Base
Pawstead already has a subscriber list that loves the brand, and treats are the easiest add-on there is. A holiday flow: early access for subscribers, an add-to-my-next-box one-tap offer, a "gift a fellow dog parent" push in December. This is the warmest audience we have and it costs almost nothing to reach them.

### 5. Grocery Retail Media
Sponsored placements on retail partners' networks during peak gifting weeks so shoppers who saw the certificate content mid-scroll find the pouch again at the digital shelf. Retail media is growing fast and it closes the loop between the social buzz and the actual purchase.

---

## Target Audience

Primary: existing Pawstead subscribers and past purchasers — people who already trust the brand and just need a low-effort way to say yes to one more item. Secondary: gift-buying pet parents more broadly, the huge seasonal audience shopping for their own dogs and for the dog people in their lives during the holidays. These are engaged, label-reading owners who treat their dogs like family members and spend a lot of time in pet content. Many of them are already primed by Pawstead's fresh-food positioning even if they haven't bought yet.
