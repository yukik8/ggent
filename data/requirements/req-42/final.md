## Campaign Title

**"The Good Start Club" — the Pawstead Puppy Starter Program**

---

## Opening: The Problem We Are Actually Solving

Pawstead has a churn problem disguised as an acquisition problem. The average fresh pet food subscriber churns after 4.2 months; top-quartile brands retain subscribers past 9 months (ref-09). Pawstead's subscribers today mostly arrive mid-dog-life, switching an adult dog off an established food — which means the habit Pawstead is fighting was formed years earlier, by someone else. The owners Pawstead never has to fight for are the ones it onboards at day zero of ownership, when every feeding habit is still being written. The category tailwind makes the window worth owning now: US pet food retail sales reached $58.1B in 2024, with fresh/refrigerated the fastest-growing segment at 17% YoY (ref-09).

The $49 Puppy Starter Kit is our answer to that specific problem. It packages Pawstead's vet-formulated refrigerated recipes into a complete first-two-weeks feeding system — puppy-sized fresh meal packs, a week-by-week portion and transition guide, a slim fridge organizer sleeve, 90-day milestone cards, and a voucher into the $120/month plan — so the first food habit a new owner forms is a Pawstead habit. The audience will pay for it: 63% of dog owners under 40 rank ingredient quality above price when choosing a food brand (ref-09). Every tactic below is selected for one job — acquiring subscribers in the first 90 days of ownership, the cohort built to outlast the 4.2-month churn cliff.

---

## Target Audience

Primary: new puppy owners in their first 90 days of ownership — the only window in a dog's life when the food decision is still open, and the window this entire program is engineered around. This audience discovers brands where real dogs are visible: 57% of pet owners under 40 discover new pet brands through social video featuring real pets (ref-09). They are also evidence-hungry and allergic to empty claims — 58% of younger respondents say they distrust ads that make claims without visible evidence or numbers, and 49% respond to problem-solution storytelling versus only 22% to discount-led messaging as a first touch (ref-07). Our creative therefore leads with the overwhelmed-first-week story and the vet formulation, never with a coupon.

| Segment | Description | Role in plan |
|---|---|---|
| First-time puppy owners | Adopted or purchased in last 90 days, researching everything | Primary conversion target |
| Experienced owners with a new puppy | Already have food habits, open to upgrading | Secondary |
| Gifters | Friends/family buying for a new puppy household | Tertiary, seasonal (holidays) |

## The Big Idea

**"The Good Start Club"** turns the starter kit into a membership, not a purchase. Buying the $49 kit enrolls the owner and their puppy in a 90-day guided program: week-by-week feeding and transition content, puppy milestone badges to share on social, and vet Q&A sessions. Because the core problem is the 4.2-month churn cliff (ref-09), the club structure is strategic, not decorative: a 90-day guided arc keeps the owner actively engaged with Pawstead through exactly the period when habits harden, so the $120/month plan activates as the continuation of a routine rather than a new bill. Every channel drives to one action: join the club with the $49 kit.

## Channel & Media Plan

Each channel below maps directly to the day-zero acquisition and churn problem named above.

| # | Channel | Role |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Paid social short-form video | Awareness & kit sales |
| 2 | Vet & real-pet creator partnerships | Trust & education |
| 3 | Grocery retail media & in-fridge POS | Point-of-purchase visibility |
| 4 | Lifecycle email & SMS onboarding | Kit-to-plan conversion |
| 5 | Shelter & adoption-day partnerships | Reaching owners at day zero |

### 1. Paid Social — Short-Form Video (Reels, TikTok, YouTube Shorts)
Because new owners find pet brands through footage of real dogs — 57% of pet owners under 40 discover new pet brands through social video featuring real pets (ref-09) — our flight is built on real puppies experiencing their first Pawstead bowl and real club-member milestones, not studio product shots. The inventory is efficient: Instagram Reels average a $9.40 CPM, TikTok $7.80, and YouTube Shorts $6.90 (ref-06). We will cap delivery deliberately, since frequency above 4 exposures per week correlates with an 18% drop in engagement rate (ref-06).

### 2. Vet & Real-Pet Creator Partnerships
The named problem is trust at a moment of overwhelm, and small credible voices beat big polished ones: 64% of younger buyers have purchased a product recommended by a niche creator with under 100K followers (ref-07). We build a roster of vet and vet-tech creators, credentials on camera, plus real rescue-puppy accounts documenting their own 90-day club journeys. The economics reinforce the choice — creator-partnership posts deliver 31% lower cost-per-engagement than brand-owned posts (ref-06) — and creator cut-downs feed the paid flight.

### 3. Grocery Retail Media & In-Fridge POS
Because Pawstead's fridge-aisle expansion is itself a credibility asset, we spend to make it visible: in-fridge grocery placement lifts brand consideration by 22% among shoppers who already buy premium dog food (ref-09). Sponsored placements on grocery retail media networks plus in-fridge materials flagging the starter kit close the loop for shoppers who met the club on social earlier in the week.

### 4. Lifecycle Email & SMS Onboarding
This channel is where the churn problem is actually won. Lifecycle-triggered emails average a 38% open rate, versus 21% for standard campaign sends (ref-16), so every kit purchase triggers a 90-day flow of transition tips, portion reminders, and milestone prompts, with the plan-activation offer timed before the kit runs out. We will push SMS opt-in hard at kit checkout, because SMS opt-in subscribers convert at 2.4x the rate of email-only subscribers on time-limited offers (ref-16). The investment logic is straightforward: median ROI for retention marketing is $4.60 per dollar spent, versus $2.20 for prospecting (ref-16).

### 5. Shelter & Adoption-Day Partnerships
No channel reaches day zero more literally. Co-branded Good Start Club vouchers in adoption packets and adoption-day booths in priority metros put the $49 kit in front of owners before any competing food habit exists — the earliest possible strike against the 4.2-month churn pattern (ref-09).
