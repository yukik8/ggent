## Campaign Title

**"Hartline Hands-On" — the Workshop Relaunch**

---

## Opening: Experiential Retail Is Having a Moment

Experiential retail is the biggest trend in the category right now, and Hartline is sitting on a ready-made experiential engine: its in-store workshop program. Live commerce is exploding, "learn with me" content is everywhere on TikTok, and retailers from grocery to beauty are turning stores into venues. Hartline Hands-On relaunches the free workshop program with a full modern content stack around it — livestreamed sessions, short-form recap videos, a UGC hashtag, local creator takeovers, and take-home project kits priced $49 to $149 so attendees walk out with everything they practiced on.

The relaunch calendar covers one flagship workshop per store per month across all 40 stores, themed to the season: fall fix-ups in September and October, holiday prep in November and December, and a "New Year, New Skills" series in January.

---

## The Tactic Stack

### 1. Workshop Livestreams
We livestream one flagship workshop per month on TikTok Live and Instagram Live. Live commerce is one of the fastest-growing formats in retail and gives people who can't attend a way to participate.

### 2. Short-Form Recap Content
Every workshop generates recap Reels and TikToks — best moments, before/afters, attendee reactions. Short-form video is where discovery happens now, so this is the campaign's awareness engine.

### 3. UGC Hashtag Challenge
Attendees post their finished projects under #HartlineHandsOn. UGC challenges are proven engagement drivers and give us a stream of authentic content to amplify.

### 4. Local Creator Takeovers
DIY and home creators from each metro co-host one workshop per quarter and bring their audiences with them. Creator-led events feel more authentic than brand-run events.

### 5. Email & CRM Push
Monthly workshop calendars go to the full Hartline list, with reminder flows for registrants and follow-up offers on the matching project kits.

### 6. In-Store Signage & Local PR
Workshop zones get permanent signage, and we pitch local news and community calendars in each of the 40 store markets.

---

## Target Audience

The core audience is DIY-curious homeowners aged 28-50 in Hartline's trade areas — people who save project ideas constantly but hesitate to start, and who would show up to practice on someone else's drywall before risking their own. A secondary audience is existing DIYers looking to level up to new skill categories.

---

## Why Workshops, Why Now

Workshops are trending across retail, they generate content, and they drive foot traffic on otherwise slow weekend mornings. Free attendance keeps the barrier low, and the $49-$149 project kits give every session a natural register moment. The combination of live events, livestreams, and short-form recaps means one workshop can reach thousands of people beyond the room.
