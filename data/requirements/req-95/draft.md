# Clearbrook Telehealth Service Awareness Push — DRAFT

**Prepared by:** Emma Lindqvist, Junior Planner, Meridian & Craft
**Client:** Clearbrook Health
**Campaign window:** March 1 – June 30, 2027
**Budget:** $220,000

---

## Campaign Title

**"Care From the Couch" — Clearbrook Telehealth**

---

## Opening: The Doctor Will See You Wherever You Are

Introducing Clearbrook Anywhere, a wonderfully convenient telehealth service that brings a real Clearbrook clinician to any screen in the footprint for a simple, refreshingly honest $59 flat visit. Video visits run seven days a week with generous evening hours, booking takes about a minute, and prescriptions land at the patient's pharmacy of choice before the kettle boils. It is fast, friendly, genuinely affordable care — no drive, no waiting room, no taking a half day off work.

Telehealth is one of the most exciting things happening in healthcare, and Clearbrook's version is a truly polished experience. Our campaign will make sure everyone in the footprint — especially people far from a clinic or squeezed for time — knows that great care is now as close as their phone.

---

## The Big Idea

**"Care From the Couch."** The creative celebrates all the places a Clearbrook visit can now happen: the couch at 9pm, the truck cab at lunch, the farmhouse kitchen an hour from the nearest clinic, the break room between shifts. Warm, funny, instantly relatable vignettes end with the same easy payoff: **Seen tonight. $59. Clearbrook Anywhere.**

---

## Target Audience

Rural and time-pressed patients across the Clearbrook footprint. For rural families, the nearest clinic can be a long drive away, and a video visit saves an entire afternoon. For busy workers and parents, evening slots mean care finally fits around life instead of interrupting it. Both groups appreciate convenience and clear pricing, and both are more comfortable on video calls than ever after years of remote work and FaceTime with family.

---

## Channel & Media Plan

### 1. Paid Social — Short-Form Video
Charming vignette content showing visits happening from couches, cabs, and kitchens. Social video is a fantastic way to make a new behavior feel normal and easy, and it gives the campaign personality.

### 2. YouTube & Streaming TV
Longer vignette cuts on YouTube and connected TV reach rural households and families during the evening hours when a telehealth visit is most tempting. Streaming lets us focus spend on the rural counties where the message matters most.

### 3. Paid Search
Always-on coverage of telehealth and symptom searches across the footprint so that anyone looking for care options finds the $59 visit right away.

### 4. Rural Radio
Local radio remains a beloved daily companion across the rural footprint, and friendly, plain-spoken spots will introduce the service and repeat the price until it sticks.
