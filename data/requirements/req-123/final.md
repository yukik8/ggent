## Campaign Title

**"Fork After Dark" — the Late-Night Pilot**

## Opening: The Problem We Are Actually Solving

Blue Fork's revenue day ends at 8 p.m. while the neighborhoods around its urban stores keep eating. Night-shift workers and students within walking distance of 20 locations currently default to pizza and drive-thrus because Blue Fork simply is not open — a frequency ceiling built into the operating hours. The daypart is also digital by nature, and that plays to a gap worth closing: digital orders average 38% of fast-casual revenue; top-quartile chains exceed 55% (ref-10). Fork After Dark is our answer to that specific problem: 20 urban stores open until 1 a.m. Thursday through Saturday, full menu plus a three-bowl express board at the standard $12.90 average check. Every tactic below is selected to convert nearby late-night demand into app-driven repeat visits, not one-off novelty traffic.

## Target Audience

Night-shift workers and students near the 20 pilot locations — hospital staff, service workers, and students keeping late hours on a repeating weekly schedule. That schedule is the strategic asset: a nurse who orders once on a Thursday shift can become an every-Thursday regular, and loyalty-app members visit fast-casual chains 2.3x more often than non-members and spend 31% more per order (ref-10). They also verify before they visit: 74% of diners aged 18-34 check a chain's app or social feed before first visiting a new location (ref-10), so the new hours must be unmistakable in both.

## The Big Idea

**"Real food doesn't sleep."** Because the core problem is that late-night demand near our stores has nowhere to land, the creative contrasts sad late-night cliches with fresh bowls in the blue-lit calm of a late-night store — and every asset carries the hours and the app as the call to action.

## Channel & Media Plan

Each channel maps back to converting nearby shift-schedule demand into repeat digital orders.

| Channel | Role |
|---|---|
| Paid social (Reels, TikTok) | Late-night creative geo-targeted around pilot stores — short-form video ads average 2.4x the completion rate of static feed ads (ref-06), and TikTok's $7.80 average CPM (ref-06) keeps tight geo-flights efficient |
| Delivery-app placements | Sponsored placement in late-night browsing windows, defending the digital shelf where the daypart's orders actually happen (ref-10) |
| Campus & hospital-area out-of-home | Posters and transit ads on actual shift-worker routes near the 20 locations |
| App push, SMS & email | Because repeat visits are the goal, announcements run as lifecycle-triggered flows — 38% open rate versus 21% for standard sends (ref-16) — and SMS opt-in subscribers convert at 2.4x the rate of email-only subscribers on time-limited offers (ref-16), ideal for tonight-only late-night pushes |

## KPIs

- **Late-night transactions per store per week**, with repeat-guest share tracked via the app
- **Digital order share** of late-night revenue against the 38% category average (ref-10)
- **SMS and push conversion** on time-limited offers against the 2.4x SMS benchmark (ref-16)
- **New loyalty enrollments** at pilot stores during the flight

## Why This Will Work

The plan attacks a named problem — real late-night demand around 20 stores with no Blue Fork open to serve it — and routes that demand through the app, where visits compound at 2.3x member frequency (ref-10). Media builds awareness on shift-worker routes, delivery placements capture the browsing window, and triggered CRM turns the first midnight bowl into a weekly habit.
