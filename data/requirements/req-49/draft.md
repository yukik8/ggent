## Campaign Title

**"The Bowl Drop" — Blue Fork Lands in Nashville & Charlotte**

---

## Opening: Blue Fork Goes Big

Blue Fork Kitchen is bringing its build-your-own grain bowls to two of the buzziest food cities in the country. With 85 locations across the Southeast, a slick digital ordering experience, and a bowl format that basically photographs itself, Blue Fork has everything it needs to make noise in Nashville and Charlotte. The bowls are fresh, customizable, and land at a $12.90 average check, which is a sweet spot for lunch right now.

And the timing could not be better. Food TikTok is absolutely on fire, drop culture has jumped from sneakers to menus, and everyone is talking about limited-edition food collabs. Fast-casual is the hottest segment in dining, and grain bowls are one of the most shared lunch formats on social. If we launch these two markets the way streetwear brands launch a release, Blue Fork can be the most talked-about lunch opening of the summer.

---

## The Big Idea

**"The Bowl Drop"** treats each city opening like a limited release. Countdown content, city-exclusive bowls, and a first-week scarcity mechanic turn a restaurant opening into an event people line up for.

The stack:

- **City-exclusive drop bowls** — a Nashville Hot Honey Bowl and a Charlotte Gold Rush Bowl, available only in their home city, only for the first eight weeks.
- **Countdown content** — teaser videos, blurred-menu reveals, and "guess the drop" polls in the weeks before each opening.
- **The First 500** — the first 500 app pre-registrants in each city get a numbered "Founding Fork" card loaded with a free bowl and permanent early access to future drops.
- **Drop-day energy** — DJ, sticker walls, and a photo-ready bowl bar at each grand opening, built to be filmed.
- **Secret menu seeding** — a hidden off-menu item that only creators and Founding Fork holders know how to order, so the internet can "discover" it.

Every tactic feeds the same flywheel: hype, drop, share, repeat.

## Channel & Media Plan

### 1. Paid Social — Short-Form Video (TikTok & Instagram Reels)
Short-form video is where food trends are born, and bowl content performs incredibly well there. We will run geo-targeted TikTok and Reels flights in both metros with countdown creative, drop-bowl reveals, and reaction cut-downs from opening week. Social video carries the hype phase and keeps the drops trending all summer.

### 2. Local Creator Partnerships
Nashville and Charlotte both have thriving local food creator scenes, and audiences trust creators way more than they trust ads. We will partner with a mixed roster of food reviewers, "what I eat in a workweek" creators, and a couple of bigger lifestyle names in each city to unbox the drop bowls, leak the secret menu item, and film drop-day lines. Their content gets amplified through the paid flight.

### 3. Loyalty App Launch & Lifecycle
The Blue Fork app is the engine behind the First 500 mechanic. We will push app pre-registration hard in the hype phase, then use push notifications, email, and SMS to convert opening-week visitors into regulars — new-bowl alerts, points nudges, and drop announcements. Loyalty members are the most valuable customers in fast-casual, so the earlier we get people into the app, the better.

### 4. Opening-Week Activations & Local-Store Marketing
Nothing beats being there. Grand-opening events with sampling tables in nearby office districts, street teams handing out drop cards at lunch hour, sidewalk signage, and partnerships with neighboring businesses keep each new store loud in its own zip code for the first month. Sampling puts the product directly in people's hands, which is always the strongest sell.

---

## Target Audience

Our core target is fast-casual diners aged 18-34 in the Nashville and Charlotte metros — young professionals, students, and office workers who eat lunch out several times a week and discover new spots through their phones. They love customization, they care about how food looks, and they will absolutely line up for something limited. Many of them already order ahead on apps, and a lot of them decide where to eat based on what shows up in their feed. Secondary audiences include downtown office pods (one convert brings coworkers along) and weekend visitors exploring each city's food scene.
