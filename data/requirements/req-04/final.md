# Ledgerline AP Automation Suite — Demand-Generation Program

**Campaign title:** "Month-End, Handled"
**Prepared by:** Ryo Tanabe, Junior Planner · Revised by Sofia Reyes, Creative Strategy Director, Meridian & Craft
**Client:** Ledgerline
**Budget:** $540,000
**Flight:** June 1, 2025 – November 30, 2025

---

## 1. Opening

Ledgerline has a shortlist problem, not a product problem. 73% of mid-market finance software evaluations now start with a shortlist built from peer review sites (ref-04), and today Ledgerline is not reliably on that shortlist when a controller starts looking. Compounding this, the average AP automation deal cycle for 50-500 employee firms is 94 days (ref-04), which means any lead generated after roughly August cannot close inside this flight. The demand problem, stated plainly, is this: Ledgerline must enter more shortlists, earlier, and feed a 94-day pipeline from day one.

The good news is that the buyer's pain is acute and specific. Finance teams report 62% of invoice processing time is still manual at companies without AP automation (ref-04). That is the wound "Month-End, Handled" presses on. Every tactic below is selected and sequenced for one job: getting Ledgerline onto the peer-built shortlist and converting that shortlist presence into demo requests fast enough to close within the flight. The AP Automation Suite, with its automated invoice capture, configurable approval workflows, and mid-market ERP integrations at a $24,000 average annual contract, is the answer to that problem, and the campaign is built so the buyer meets the problem before the product.

## 2. Target Audience

Because the core problem is shortlist entry, we target the people who build the shortlists: finance directors and controllers at US companies with 50–500 employees. These buyers live the manual month-end firsthand, and with 62% of invoice processing time still manual at un-automated companies (ref-04), the pain narrative requires no education, only recognition. We will also reach adjacent stakeholders in the buying committee, CFOs, VPs of finance, and senior AP managers, but the media plan concentrates spend on the shortlist-builders rather than spraying across all finance titles, because reaching finance decision-makers on LinkedIn carries a $58 benchmark CPM versus $31 for general B2B targeting (ref-04), and that premium only pays back when aimed at the person who starts the evaluation.

## 3. The Big Idea — "Month-End, Handled"

Every controller knows the feeling of month-end close, the late nights, the invoice piles, the chasing of approvals. "Month-End, Handled" dramatizes the before-and-after of AP automation: the calendar flips to the last week of the month and our protagonist finance director calmly leaves the office on time, because Ledgerline is handling the invoices in the background. The idea stays exactly as conceived, because it is already a problem-solution story. What changes in this revision is the proof around it: every claim the campaign makes will carry evidence, since buyers reward that specificity, and the plan below quantifies why each channel earns its budget.

## 4. Channel & Media Plan

Same channels as originally proposed. Each is now justified against the named problem.

- **Peer review platforms (G2, Capterra, TrustRadius).** Because the core problem is that 73% of evaluations start with a peer-review shortlist (ref-04), this channel is the strategic anchor of the program, not a supporting line item. Premium profiles, category placements, and review-generation programs put Ledgerline where the shortlist is actually written.
- **LinkedIn (paid social & sponsored content).** Because finance decision-maker inventory costs $58 CPM versus $31 for general B2B (ref-04), we run tightly targeted always-on flights against controllers and finance directors only, with retargeting doing the heavy lifting, so every premium impression is aimed at a shortlist-builder.
- **Content & webinar series.** The monthly "The Modern Close" webinar series plus downloadable guides and benchmark checklists, all reframed around the 62% manual-processing pain point (ref-04) rather than product features, so gated content qualifies buyers who recognize the problem.
- **Paid search.** Always-on coverage of high-intent AP automation and invoice-processing keywords. Because the 94-day deal cycle (ref-04) means only H1-of-flight leads can close within the flight, search launches at full weight in July rather than ramping gradually.
- **Landing pages & creative production.** The campaign landing environment will be rebuilt case-study-led rather than feature-led, because case-study-led landing pages convert demo requests at 4.7%, versus 2.1% for feature-led pages (ref-04). That single structural change more than doubles the expected yield of every paid dollar arriving on the page.

## 7. KPIs

Each KPI is anchored to a benchmark so success is falsifiable.

- Demo requests and marketing-qualified leads generated (primary), with landing page conversion measured against the 4.7% case-study-led benchmark (ref-04)
- Cost per demo request and cost per MQL, monitored against the $58 finance-decision-maker CPM baseline (ref-04)
- Shortlist presence: review platform category ranking and profile traffic, the leading indicator given that 73% of evaluations start there (ref-04)
- Webinar registrations and attendance
- Pipeline velocity tracked against the 94-day average deal cycle (ref-04), and contribution to closed-won revenue
