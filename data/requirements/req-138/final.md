## Opening: The Problem We Are Actually Solving

Hartline's weatherization opportunity is blocked by hesitation, not demand. 69% of first-time homeowners say they feel intimidated starting any project beyond painting (ref-14), and weatherization — caulk guns, heat tape, crawl spaces — sits squarely on the far side of that line. These homeowners are not choosing a big-box competitor; they are choosing to do nothing, and paying for it every winter. Meanwhile the category rewards whoever solves the confidence gap: US home improvement retail reached $538B in 2025, with DIY purchases accounting for 52% of revenue (ref-14).

Winter-Ready Weekend is our answer to that specific problem. Grab-and-go kits priced $59-$179 — Draft Stopper, Window Shield, Pipe & Faucet, and Whole-House, each with a printed checklist and a good/better/best end-cap — turn an intimidating project into one box, one weekend, one finished thing. That is Hartline's project-guidance positioning against the big boxes made physical. Every tactic below is selected to convert hesitation into a completed first project, not just to move seasonal inventory.

## Target Audience

Homeowners aged 30-55 prepping houses before winter — with the intimidated first-time owner as the priority segment, because that is where the untapped volume sits (69% intimidated beyond painting, ref-14). They respond to project guidance, not price-and-item ads: project-based how-to content drives 2.7x more store visits than price-and-item advertising for regional chains (ref-14).

## The Big Idea

One weekend, one checklist, one trip. Because the core problem is confidence rather than price, the checklist is the strategy, not a merchandising flourish: it tells a nervous homeowner exactly what "done" looks like before they commit a dollar, and every asset points to it.

## Channel & Media Plan

Each channel maps back to the confidence gap named above.

| Channel | Role |
|---|---|
| Paid social & YouTube video | Seasonal how-to videos and kit walk-throughs to build awareness |
| How-to content production | A four-part "Winter-Ready in a Weekend" video and article series |
| In-store workshops | Free Saturday weatherization workshops in priority stores |
| Email & CRM | Checklist reminders and kit offers to the Hartline customer file |
| Local digital display & search | Capture "how to winterize" and local hardware searches |

**Paid social, YouTube, and the how-to series.** Because the core problem is intimidation, we lead with project walk-throughs rather than kit-price ads — project-based how-to content drives 2.7x more store visits than price-and-item advertising (ref-14).

**In-store workshops.** Workshops attack hesitation directly and pay off beyond the event: in-store workshop attendees spend 43% more annually than matched non-attendees (ref-14).

**Email & CRM.** Checklist-triggered sends beat generic blasts — lifecycle-triggered emails average a 38% open rate, versus 21% for standard campaign sends (ref-16). We also steer fulfillment toward pickup, since regional hardware chains retain 71% of customers who use in-store pickup, versus 48% of delivery-only customers (ref-14).

**Local display & search.** Captures homeowners already searching for winterizing help and routes them to the checklist page, not a product grid.
