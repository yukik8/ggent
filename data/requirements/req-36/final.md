## Campaign Title

**"Firelight Weekends" — the Cascade Valley Winter Cozy-Cabin Campaign**

---

## Opening: The Problem We Are Actually Solving

Cascade Valley has an empty-cabin problem, not an appeal problem. Off-peak occupancy for regional destinations averages 46%, versus 78% in peak season (ref-05), and the valley's cabin operators live that gap every winter: the same properties that sell out in July sit dark from mid-November through February. That is not a demand ceiling — it is a conversion failure. The travelers most likely to fill those cabins are already nearby: drive-market weekend trips make up 61% of visits to small US regional destinations (ref-05), and the average regional weekend visitor spends $412 per trip across lodging, food, and activities (ref-05). The demand exists within a tank of gas; the valley has simply never given it a winter product to book.

Firelight Weekends is our answer to that specific problem. A single bookable package — two nights in a participating cabin, firewood bundle and s'mores kit, a $30 partner dining voucher, late Sunday checkout — at around $380 per couple, priced deliberately under the $412 average regional trip spend (ref-05) so the whole weekend feels like the smart insider move rather than a splurge. Every tactic below is selected for one job: converting drive-market couples into booked winter weekends, measured in occupancy.

## Target Audience

| Segment | Description | Role in Plan |
|---|---|---|
| Primary: Couples under 40, drive-market | Live within roughly a 3-hour drive, plan short getaways together, discover trips on social feeds | Core booking audience for the package |
| Secondary: Past Cascade Valley visitors | Have visited in summer or fall, already know the destination | Warm remarketing audience via email |
| Tertiary: Friend-group planners | The person in the group chat who organizes trips | Amplification through tags and shares |

This audience decides fast and decides on their feeds: 54% of travelers under 35 pick regional trips based on short-form video seen in the prior 30 days (ref-05), which means the window between inspiration and booking is measured in weeks, not months — exactly the cadence our flight plan is built around. They also respond to substance over gimmicks: only 22% of Gen-Z respondents respond to discount-led messaging as a first touch, while 49% respond to problem-solution storytelling (ref-07). Our creative therefore sells the copyable weekend, never a coupon code.

## The Big Idea

**"Firelight Weekends"** turns Cascade Valley's quietest season into its most atmospheric one. The creative platform is built around the 36-hour copyable itinerary: every piece of content shows a real, bookable sequence — check in Friday, firelight and hot tub that night, snowy trail and bakery Saturday morning, taproom Saturday night, slow Sunday checkout. Because the core problem is empty winter cabins, the itinerary format matters strategically, not just aesthetically: it closes the gap between a saved video and a confirmed reservation inside the 30-day decision window where 54% of under-35 travelers make regional trip choices (ref-05). Every video ends with the same message: this exact weekend is one tap away at one price.

## Channel & Media Plan

Each channel below maps directly to the winter-occupancy problem named above.

| # | Channel | Purpose |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Paid social — short-form video | Awareness and direct booking traffic |
| 2 | Regional creator partnerships | Authentic itinerary content |
| 3 | Itinerary content hub & booking page | Conversion layer |
| 4 | Email & past-visitor remarketing | Warm-audience bookings |

### 1. Paid Social — Short-Form Video (Instagram Reels & TikTok)
Because winter bookings start on the feed — 54% of travelers under 35 pick regional trips based on short-form video seen in the prior 30 days (ref-05) — this is where the heaviest budget weight goes. The economics hold up: Instagram Reels average a $9.40 CPM and TikTok $7.80 (ref-06), and short-form video ads average 2.4x the completion rate of static feed ads (ref-06), which matters when the creative asset is a 36-hour itinerary that needs to be watched to the booking prompt. We will cap frequency deliberately, since frequency above 4 exposures per week correlates with an 18% drop in engagement rate (ref-06). And this flight is the start of a habit, not a one-off: destinations running always-on social content see 27% higher repeat visitation than campaign-only destinations (ref-05), so all assets are built for the Tourism Board to keep publishing after February 15.

### 2. Regional Creator Partnerships
Creator itineraries are how the package earns trust instead of buying attention. 64% of Gen-Z respondents have purchased a product recommended by a niche creator with under 100K followers (ref-07), which is precisely the profile of the regional "hidden gems within 3 hours" accounts this audience already follows — so we prioritize engaged local creators over large national accounts. The economics reinforce the choice: creator-partnership posts deliver 31% lower cost-per-engagement than brand-owned posts (ref-06), and every creator weekend produces footage that feeds the paid flight.

### 3. Itinerary Content Hub & Booking Page
Inspiration that cannot be booked in a few taps does not fill cabins. The "Your 36 Hours in Cascade Valley" hub hosts three seasonal itineraries (romantic, outdoorsy, food-first), the winter logistics this audience actually checks — parking, what's open in February — and the single-price booking flow. Because the core problem is converting saved videos into reservations before the impulse fades, every paid and creator asset links to this one page, and we instrument it end to end so occupancy lift is attributable by channel.

### 4. Email & Past-Visitor Remarketing
The cheapest winter booking is a visitor the valley has already won. Lifecycle-triggered emails average a 38% open rate, versus 21% for standard campaign sends (ref-16), so the winter series is triggered off past-visit season and booking-page behavior rather than blasted to the full list. Abandoned-booking flows earn their keep too — cart-abandonment and lapsed-buyer flows recover 5.9% of at-risk revenue on average (ref-16) — and the channel's efficiency justifies its slice of budget: median ROI for retention marketing is $4.60 per dollar spent, versus $2.20 for prospecting (ref-16).

## KPIs

- **Winter cabin occupancy** across participating properties, measured weekly against the 46% off-peak regional average (ref-05), with the peak-season 78% benchmark (ref-05) as the aspirational ceiling
- **Firelight Weekends packages booked**, attributed by channel through the instrumented hub
- **Paid social CTR** at or above the 1.2% cross-vertical average, targeting the 2.6% top-quartile benchmark (ref-06)
- **Email open rate** on the triggered winter series against the 38% lifecycle benchmark (ref-16)
- **Repeat-visit intent**, tracked via post-stay survey and list growth, building toward the 27% repeat-visitation advantage of always-on destinations (ref-05)

## Closing

This plan does not just decorate the valley's winter; it attacks the measured cause of the season's weakness — cabins sitting at off-peak occupancy while 61% of regional visits come from drive-market travelers (ref-05) who have never been handed a bookable winter product. One package, four channels, one booking page, every tactic answerable to the same named problem: fill the cabins from November to February, and give the Tourism Board a framework it can rerun and sharpen every winter.
