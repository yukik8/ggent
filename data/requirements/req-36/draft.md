## Campaign Title

**"Firelight Weekends" — the Cascade Valley Winter Cozy-Cabin Campaign**

---

## Opening: Introducing the Firelight Weekends Package

Introducing Firelight Weekends, a bookable winter cabin package that bundles the best of Cascade Valley's cozy season into one simple offer. Each package includes two nights in a participating valley cabin, a stocked firewood bundle and s'mores kit on arrival, a $30 dining voucher redeemable at partner restaurants in town, and late Sunday checkout — all for around $380 per couple. The package is built for easy booking: one landing page, one price, a few taps from social post to confirmed reservation.

The product has everything winter travelers respond to. Wood stoves and hot tubs. Snow-dusted trails ten minutes from the cabin door. A walkable main street with a bakery that opens early and a taproom that stays warm late. Winter getaways are an appealing category right now, and cozy-cabin content performs consistently well on social platforms. Firelight Weekends packages all of that into a single product the Tourism Board can promote, track, and repeat every winter.

This proposal lays out a structured plan to put the package in front of the right couples at the right moment and convert interest into booked weekends.

---

## Target Audience

| Segment | Description | Role in Plan |
|---|---|---|
| Primary: Couples under 40, drive-market | Live within roughly a 3-hour drive, plan short getaways together, discover trips on social feeds | Core booking audience for the package |
| Secondary: Past Cascade Valley visitors | Have visited in summer or fall, already know the destination | Warm remarketing audience via email |
| Tertiary: Friend-group planners | The person in the group chat who organizes trips | Amplification through tags and shares |

Many couples in this age group take multiple short trips per year and prefer drive-distance destinations over flights. They plan quickly, often booking within weeks of first seeing a destination, and social media plays a big role in how they choose where to go. Winter is also an attractive window for them because rooms are cheaper and crowds are smaller.

---

## The Big Idea

**"Firelight Weekends"** turns Cascade Valley's quietest season into its most atmospheric one. The creative platform is built around the 36-hour copyable itinerary: every piece of content shows a real, bookable sequence — check in Friday, firelight and hot tub that night, snowy trail and bakery Saturday morning, taproom Saturday night, slow Sunday checkout. No abstract drone footage, no generic scenery. Every video ends with the same message: this exact weekend is one tap away at one price.

## Channel & Media Plan

| # | Channel | Purpose |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Paid social — short-form video | Awareness and direct booking traffic |
| 2 | Regional creator partnerships | Authentic itinerary content |
| 3 | Itinerary content hub & booking page | Conversion layer |
| 4 | Email & past-visitor remarketing | Warm-audience bookings |

### 1. Paid Social — Short-Form Video (Instagram Reels & TikTok)
Short-form video is where trip inspiration happens for this audience, and it is a very effective format for travel content. We will run a sustained flight of Reels and TikTok placements built from the 36-hour itinerary content, geo-targeted to metro areas within drive distance. Cozy winter content tends to perform well in the colder months, so the timing works in our favor.

### 2. Regional Creator Partnerships
We will partner with regional travel creators — the "hidden gems within 3 hours" accounts this audience already follows — to shoot and post their own Firelight Weekends. Creator content feels more trustworthy than polished tourism ads, and it gives us a library of authentic footage to amplify through paid. We will favor creators with engaged local followings over large national accounts.

### 3. Itinerary Content Hub & Booking Page
A dedicated "Your 36 Hours in Cascade Valley" hub will host three seasonal itineraries (romantic, outdoorsy, food-first), practical logistics (parking, what's open in winter), and the single-price booking flow. Everything in paid and creator content links here. Reducing friction between inspiration and booking is essential for this audience.

### 4. Email & Past-Visitor Remarketing
The Tourism Board's list of past visitors and newsletter subscribers is a valuable asset. We will run a winter email series introducing the package, plus retargeting for site visitors who did not complete a booking. Email is a reliable, low-cost channel for audiences who already know the destination.

## Closing

Firelight Weekends gives Cascade Valley a clean, repeatable winter product with a clear price and a copyable itinerary. The plan is deliberately simple: one package, four channels, one booking page. We believe this structure will make winter a season the valley's operators can count on, and give the Tourism Board a campaign framework it can rerun and refine every year.
