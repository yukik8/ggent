**Prepared by:** Marcus Bell, Junior Planner, Meridian & Craft
**Client:** Sunbrook Foods
**Campaign window:** May 15 – August 15, 2026
**Budget:** $270,000

---

## Campaign Title

**"Sidekick Summer" — Sunbrook Crisps x Cold Creek Craft Soda**

---

## Opening: The Cookout Just Got an Official Pairing

This summer, Sunbrook Crisps teams up with Cold Creek Craft Soda, one of the Midwest's favorite regional beverage brands, for a co-branded grilling promotion. The centerpiece is the promo bundle: any bag of Sunbrook Crisps at $3.49 when purchased alongside a Cold Creek 6-pack, flagged with co-branded shelf tags, cooler clings, and a shared summer visual identity that puts both brands on the same picnic table. The bundle covers the full Sunbrook flavor range, features limited summer bag stickers with pairing suggestions (which crisp goes with which soda), and shows up everywhere the cookout run happens — the chip aisle, the beverage cooler, and the seasonal grilling end-cap.

Cookout culture is having a massive moment right now. Grilling content is everywhere on social, "cookout menu" videos rack up huge numbers every summer, and pairing content — what to eat with what — is one of the fastest-growing food formats. Two regional Midwest brands joining forces is exactly the kind of authentic, local story that people love to share. Sidekick Summer puts Sunbrook at the center of that energy.

---

## The Big Idea

**Every grill hero needs sidekicks.** The burgers and brats get the glory, but everyone knows the sides make the cookout. Sidekick Summer crowns Sunbrook Crisps and Cold Creek Soda as the official sidekicks of the Midwest cookout — the two things you grab on the same shopping trip, set out on the same table, and run out of first. The creative leans into buddy-movie energy: two iconic sidekicks, one legendary summer.

The idea flexes across everything trending right now — POV cookout videos, pairing-menu carousels, "grocery run for the cookout" hauls, duet-style creator content where one creator brings the crisps and another brings the soda, and even a co-branded AR "build your cookout table" filter if budget allows.

---

## Channel & Media Plan

### 1. Paid Social — Short-Form Video (TikTok, Reels, Shorts)
Short-form video is where cookout culture lives, and food pairing content performs incredibly well in the summer months. We run a steady flight of co-branded videos: sizzling grill footage, the crisps-and-soda handoff moment, pairing-menu formats, and cut-downs of creator content. We can also jump on trending sounds and cookout memes in real time with a rapid-response content calendar, which keeps the campaign feeling current all summer.

### 2. Creator Partnerships — Grillfluencers & Backyard Hosts
Grilling creators are one of the hottest creator categories going. We partner with Midwest grilling, BBQ, and "backyard host" creators to build Sidekick Summer menus, film cookout POVs, and run duet-style handoffs between paired creators. Creator content feels more real than polished ads, and it gives us a library of authentic material to boost through paid.

### 3. Retail Media & Co-Branded In-Store
Sponsored placements on grocery partners' retail media networks keep the bundle visible during the weekly shop, while in-store the co-branded end-caps, cooler clings, and shelf tags do the heavy lifting at the moment of decision. Shoppers who saw the social content earlier in the week will recognize the sidekicks on sight.

### 4. Sidekick Summer Cookout Tour
A weekend sampling tour hits high-traffic grocery stores and summer events across priority markets — a branded grill station serving crisp-and-soda pairings, with a photo moment and coupon handouts for the bundle. Nothing converts like tasting the pairing on a hot Saturday.

### 5. Streaming Audio & Playlists
Summer playlists are a cookout staple, so a light layer of streaming audio ads (plus a co-branded "Sidekick Summer" cookout playlist) keeps the brands in the backyard even when phones are face-down.

---

## Target Audience

The core buyer for this promotion is cookout hosts aged 25-44 in Midwest grocery drive markets — the people who own the grill, send the group chat invite, and do the big weekend shopping run. They plan gatherings around summer weekends and holidays, they buy snacks and drinks in the same trip, and they care about looking like a good host. Many of them discover food ideas on social and are drawn to fun, shareable pairings. Secondary audiences include guests who bring the sides, and younger snackers who catch the campaign on social and pick up the bundle for smaller hangouts.
