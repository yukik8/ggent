---

## Campaign Title

**"Sidekick Summer" — Sunbrook Crisps x Cold Creek Craft Soda**

---

## Opening

This summer, Sunbrook Crisps partners with Cold Creek Craft Soda, one of the Midwest's best-loved regional beverage brands, for a co-branded grilling promotion. The centerpiece is a simple bundle offer: any bag of Sunbrook Crisps at $3.49 when purchased alongside a Cold Creek 6-pack. The offer is flagged with co-branded shelf tags, cooler clings, and a shared summer visual identity that puts both brands on the same picnic table. The bundle spans the full Sunbrook flavor range, carries limited summer bag stickers with crisp-and-soda pairing suggestions, and appears at every stop on the cookout shopping run — the chip aisle, the beverage cooler, and the seasonal grilling end-cap.

Cookout culture is a genuine cultural moment. Grilling and pairing content performs strongly on social every summer, and industry surveys suggest the average American host plans more than half a dozen cookouts between Memorial Day and Labor Day. Two regional Midwest brands joining forces is an authentic, local story that travels well, and Sidekick Summer places Sunbrook at the center of it.

---

## The Big Idea

**Every grill hero needs sidekicks.** The burgers and brats get the glory, but the sides make the cookout. Sidekick Summer crowns Sunbrook Crisps and Cold Creek Soda as the official sidekicks of the Midwest cookout — the two items you grab on the same shopping trip, set out on the same table, and run out of first. The creative carries buddy-movie energy: two iconic sidekicks, one legendary summer.

The platform flexes naturally across the formats our audience already watches — POV cookout videos, pairing-menu carousels, cookout grocery hauls, and duet-style creator content in which one creator brings the crisps and the other brings the soda.

---

## Channel & Media Plan

### 1. Paid Social — Short-Form Video (TikTok, Reels, Shorts)
Short-form video is where cookout culture lives, and food pairing content performs especially well in the summer months. We will run a steady flight of co-branded videos: grill footage, the crisps-and-soda handoff moment, pairing-menu formats, and cut-downs of creator content. A rapid-response content calendar lets us pick up trending sounds and formats so the campaign stays current all summer.

### 2. Creator Partnerships — Grilling and Backyard-Host Creators
We will partner with Midwest grilling, BBQ, and backyard-host creators to build Sidekick Summer menus, film cookout POVs, and run duet-style handoffs between paired creators. Creator content reads as more genuine than polished brand advertising, and it produces a library of material we can amplify through paid.

### 3. Retail Media & Co-Branded In-Store
Sponsored placements on grocery partners' retail media networks keep the bundle visible during the weekly shop, while co-branded end-caps, cooler clings, and shelf tags carry the message at the moment of decision. Shoppers who saw the social content earlier in the week will recognize the sidekicks on sight.

### 4. Sidekick Summer Cookout Tour
A weekend sampling tour visits high-traffic grocery stores and summer events across priority markets: a branded grill station serving crisp-and-soda pairings, a photo moment, and coupon handouts for the bundle. Tasting the pairing on a summer Saturday is the shortest path to the basket.

### 5. Streaming Audio & Playlists
A light layer of streaming audio ads, plus a co-branded Sidekick Summer cookout playlist, keeps both brands present in the backyard even when phones are face-down.

---

## Target Audience

The core buyer is the cookout host aged 25-44 in Midwest grocery drive markets — the person who owns the grill, sends the group-chat invite, and does the big weekend shopping run. These hosts plan gatherings around summer weekends and holidays, buy snacks and drinks in the same trip, and take pride in hosting well. Many discover food ideas on social platforms and respond to fun, shareable pairings. Secondary audiences include guests who bring the sides and younger snackers who encounter the campaign on social and pick up the bundle for smaller get-togethers.
