## Campaign Title

**"The Pickiest Critic" — the Pawstead Fresh Feline Launch**

---

## Opening: The Problem We Are Actually Solving

Pawstead has a ceiling problem, not a product problem. The brand has built real momentum in fresh dog food, but a single-species portfolio caps growth in a market that is expanding fastest exactly where Pawstead plays: US pet food retail sales reached $58.1B in 2024, with fresh/refrigerated the fastest-growing segment at 17% YoY (ref-09). Every cat-owning household that walks past Pawstead's fridge doors — including the meaningful share of Pawstead's own dog-food subscribers who also own cats — is demand the brand currently cannot serve. And the category's second structural problem compounds the first: the average fresh pet food subscriber churns after 4.2 months, while top-quartile brands retain subscribers past 9 months (ref-09). A cat line that wins trial but loses the fourth-month subscriber does not move Pawstead's economics.

Fresh Feline is our answer to both problems. Three vet-formulated recipes — Chicken & Pumpkin, Salmon & Spinach, and Turkey & Sweet Potato — in slim, stackable single-serve trays built for a shared fridge, at an average feeding cost of $2.60/day, priced as an everyday upgrade rather than a bill. The audience is primed for a quality-led pitch: on the dog side of the same market, 63% of dog owners under 40 rank ingredient quality above price when choosing a food brand (ref-09). Every tactic below is selected for its ability to open the cat household to Pawstead and then hold the subscriber past the 4.2-month churn cliff, not merely to generate launch buzz.

---

## Target Audience

Primary: cat owners under 40 who are skeptical of dry kibble — the segment where the category ceiling breaks first. They discover brands the way this campaign is built to reach them: 57% of pet owners under 40 discover new pet brands through social video featuring real pets (ref-09). They are also evidence-sensitive, which shapes the creative mandate — 58% say they distrust ads that make claims without visible evidence or numbers (ref-07), and only 22% respond to discount-led messaging as a first touch while 49% respond to problem-solution storytelling (ref-07). That is why the campaign leads with real cats rendering real verdicts, never with a coupon or an adjective.

Secondary: existing Pawstead dog-food subscribers who also own cats (the cheapest cat households to acquire), and multi-pet grocery shoppers already in the fridge aisle.

---

## The Big Idea

**"The Pickiest Critic"** turns the biggest objection to cat food marketing into the campaign itself. Cats are the harshest food critics on the planet — they cannot be bribed and they will walk away from a bowl mid-bite. So instead of telling this evidence-demanding audience that Fresh Feline is delicious, we let the critics decide on camera: unscripted first-bite verdict videos, real cats, real kitchens, one bowl. Because the core problem is skepticism — of kibble, and of unverified pet-food claims — the verdict mechanic is strategy, not just charm: it supplies the visible evidence that 58% of this audience says advertising lacks (ref-07). If the pickiest critic approves, that is the only endorsement that matters.

## Channel & Media Plan

Each channel below maps directly to the two named problems: opening the cat household, and holding the subscriber past month four.

### 1. Paid Social — Short-Form Video (Instagram Reels & TikTok)
Because the target discovers pet brands through real-pet video — 57% of pet owners under 40 discover new pet brands through social video featuring real pets (ref-09) — we put the heaviest weight behind verdict videos, recipe close-ups, and fridge-unpacking content on Reels and TikTok. The inventory is efficient for a launch of this size: Instagram Reels average a $9.40 CPM and TikTok $7.80 (ref-06), and short-form video ads average 2.4x the completion rate of static feed ads (ref-06).

### 2. Creator Partnerships — Cat Creators & Vet-Tech Voices
The verdict format only works if it is credible, so we buy credibility two ways: beloved cat creators whose pets deliver the verdicts, and veterinary and vet-tech creators who walk through the formulation on camera with credentials visible. The economics favor this over brand-owned content — creator-partnership posts deliver 31% lower cost-per-engagement than brand-owned posts (ref-06) — and niche voices convert: 64% of Gen-Z respondents have purchased a product recommended by a niche creator with under 100K followers (ref-07).

### 3. In-Store Fridge-Aisle Activation — "The Critic's Table"
Because Pawstead's expansion thesis runs through the grocery fridge aisle, we convert social skepticism into physical trial exactly there. In-fridge grocery placement lifts brand consideration by 22% among shoppers who already buy premium dog food (ref-09) — precisely our secondary audience of multi-pet fridge-aisle shoppers. Weekend Critic's Table activations (white tablecloth, tiny cloche, chilled tray) send shoppers home with a free trial tray and a critic's scorecard, turning the campaign's verdict mechanic into a first feeding.

### 4. Lifecycle CRM — Trial-to-Subscriber Journey
This is the channel that attacks the 4.2-month churn cliff (ref-09) directly. The starter-box flow — verdict check-ins after the first feeding, half-fresh half-dry plan sizing, one-tap pause and delivery controls — runs on lifecycle triggers because lifecycle-triggered emails average a 38% open rate, versus 21% for standard campaign sends (ref-16). The retention weighting is deliberate: median ROI for retention marketing is $4.60 per dollar spent, versus $2.20 for prospecting (ref-16), and a launch judged on subscribers held rather than trials generated must fund the held half.

## KPIs

- **Aided and unaided awareness** of Fresh Feline among cat owners under 40, measured pre/post in launch markets
- **Verdict-video engagement**, benchmarked against the 2.4x completion-rate advantage short-form video holds over static formats (ref-06)
- **Creator cost-per-engagement** at or below the 31% advantage creator posts hold over brand-owned posts (ref-06)
- **Trial-tray redemption and consideration lift** in activation stores, read against the 22% consideration lift benchmark for in-fridge placement (ref-09)
- **Subscriber retention at month 4 and month 6**, tracked explicitly against the category's 4.2-month average churn point and the 9-month top-quartile retention mark (ref-09)

## Why Pawstead Wins

This plan does not just launch a cat line; it attacks the two measured constraints on Pawstead's growth — a single-species ceiling in a segment growing 17% YoY (ref-09), and a category churn cliff at 4.2 months (ref-09) — with a verdict-driven platform engineered for a skeptical audience. Cat creators build believable reach, the Critic's Table converts it in the fridge aisle Pawstead already owns, and the lifecycle program holds the subscribers the launch is ultimately judged on, each tactic answerable to the same named problems.
