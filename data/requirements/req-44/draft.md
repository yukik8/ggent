## Campaign Title

**"The Pickiest Critic" — the Pawstead Fresh Feline Launch**

---

## Opening: Fresh Food, Finally for Cats

Introducing Pawstead Fresh Feline, a gorgeous, thoughtfully crafted line of vet-formulated fresh cat food that brings everything people love about Pawstead's refrigerated dog meals to the feline side of the household. The line launches with three delicious recipes — Chicken & Pumpkin, Salmon & Spinach, and Turkey & Sweet Potato — each gently cooked, portioned into sleek single-serve peel-and-serve trays, and designed in slim, stackable fridge packs that slide neatly onto a refrigerator shelf without taking over the whole thing.

The recipes are built by Pawstead's own veterinary nutrition team, with real, recognizable ingredients you can actually see through the film — shredded chicken, flaked salmon, whole peas. No mystery pellets, no rendered meals, no long chemical names. And at an average feeding cost of $2.60/day, Fresh Feline lands at a price that feels like an easy everyday upgrade rather than a luxury splurge.

Fresh food is one of the most exciting trends in pet care right now, and cat owners have been asking for it for years. Everyone knows cats deserve better than dusty kibble, and this launch is Pawstead's chance to be the brand that finally delivers it — in the same grocery fridge aisles where the brand is already winning with dog owners.

---

## Target Audience

Our primary target is cat owners under 40 who are skeptical of dry kibble. These are curious, label-reading, deeply devoted cat people who treat their cats like family members and are increasingly uncomfortable pouring the same brown pellets into the bowl every day. Many of them already buy premium or "natural" cat food, follow cat accounts on social media, and love sharing their cats' quirks online. They're open to fresh food in theory, but they need to see proof that a real cat — a genuinely picky one — will actually eat it.

Secondary audiences include existing Pawstead dog-food subscribers who also own cats, and multi-pet grocery shoppers who already shop the fridge aisle.

---

## The Big Idea

**"The Pickiest Critic"** turns the biggest objection to cat food marketing into the campaign itself. Every cat owner knows the truth: cats are the harshest food critics on the planet. They cannot be bribed, they cannot be marketed to, and they will walk away from a bowl mid-bite without apology. So instead of telling people Fresh Feline is delicious, we let the critics decide — on camera.

The whole campaign is built around unscripted first-bite verdict videos. Real cats, real kitchens, one bowl of Fresh Feline, and a verdict: the slow blink of approval, the full-body commitment to the bowl, or (occasionally, honestly) the dramatic walk-away. If the pickiest critic approves, that's the only endorsement that matters.

## Channel & Media Plan

### 1. Paid Social — Short-Form Video (Instagram Reels & TikTok)
Short-form video is where cat content absolutely thrives, and cat owners spend a huge amount of time there. We'll run a sustained paid flight of verdict videos, recipe close-ups, and fridge-unpacking content across Reels and TikTok. Cat videos are basically the native language of the internet, so this content should travel really well organically too.

### 2. Creator Partnerships — Cat Creators & Vet-Tech Voices
We'll build a roster of beloved cat creators whose pets have real personalities, plus a smaller bench of veterinary and vet-tech creators who can walk through the formulation on camera with their credentials visible. The mix gives us both charm and credibility — the cats deliver the verdicts, the vets explain why the recipes earn them.

### 3. In-Store Fridge-Aisle Activation — "The Critic's Table"
Weekend sampling activations at high-traffic grocery partners, staged as a tiny fine-dining setup: white tablecloth, tiny cloche, a chilled single-serve tray. Shoppers take home a free trial tray plus a "critic's scorecard" for their own cat's verdict. Field teams emphasize that the trays live in the fridge aisle, right where shoppers already are.

### 4. Lifecycle CRM — Trial-to-Subscriber Journey
A starter-box program with a structured follow-up flow: verdict check-in messages after the first feeding, easy plan sizing (including a half-fresh, half-dry option), and flexible pause and delivery controls. Retention messaging keeps new subscribers engaged past the early months when novelty fades and churn risk creeps in.

## Why Pawstead Wins

Cats sell themselves — we just have to point the camera. "The Pickiest Critic" gives Pawstead an honest, endlessly repeatable content engine, a charming in-store moment, and a clear path from a free trial tray to a flexible subscription. Fresh food changed the game for dogs, and this is the campaign that brings that same energy to the other half of the pet aisle. We can't wait to see the verdicts roll in.
