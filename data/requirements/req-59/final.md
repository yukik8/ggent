## Campaign Title

**"This Year, Actually" — the Kinetiq Resolution Season**

---

## Opening: The Problem We Are Actually Solving

Kinetiq's resolution-season problem is not demand; it is disbelief. January intent arrives every year on schedule, but the buyer standing between intent and checkout is blocked by a specific fear: 71% of connected-fitness intenders cite fear of unused equipment as their top purchase hesitation (ref-11). A $200 holiday discount lowers the price of the machine, but it does not lower the price of failing at it — which is why discounting alone has never fixed January conversion in this category. Meanwhile the prize for solving it keeps growing: US connected fitness equipment sales returned to growth in 2025, up 6% YoY to $4.8B (ref-11), and rowing is the fastest-growing connected-fitness modality at 19% YoY unit growth (ref-11).

"This Year, Actually" is our answer to that disbelief. The campaign pairs the $1,795 rower and $200 holiday discount with the one proof point hesitant resolvers can act on — the 30-day in-home trial, where free in-home trial programs convert at 46%, versus 11% for standard e-commerce checkout (ref-11) — and creative built around believable follow-through rather than transformation fantasy. Every tactic below is selected to convert seasonal intent past the fear, not just to ride the seasonal wave.

---

## Target Audience

New Year fitness resolvers aged 25-45 — optimistic, busy people who genuinely want this year to be different, and the population where the 71% unused-equipment fear (ref-11) does its damage. They are planning resolutions as early as November, they respond to warmth and encouragement over drill-sergeant messaging, and they are scrolling feeds full of gift guides and new-year content all season. Secondary: holiday gift buyers shopping for a partner or parent, and lapsed shoppers who considered Kinetiq earlier in the year but did not buy. The 30-day in-home trial remains available all season as the low-pressure way in for the hesitant majority.

---

## The Big Idea

**"This Year, Actually."** Everyone has said "this year is the year" before. Because the core problem is disbelief in one's own follow-through, our campaign celebrates the version of you that means it this time — not with intimidating transformation montages, but with warm, funny, deeply relatable scenes of real people fitting real workouts into real Januaries. The line works twice: as a holiday gift message ("give them the year they keep talking about") and as a January rallying cry ("this year, actually"). The creative shows the Tuesday-night reality the buyer is actually bargaining with, because that is the reality they doubt.

## Channel & Media Plan

Each channel below maps directly to the disbelief problem named above.

### 1. Paid Social — Short-Form Video
Because the core problem is converting seasonal intent, we buy the season's most efficient attention: Instagram Reels average a $9.40 CPM, TikTok $7.80, and YouTube Shorts $6.90 (ref-06), and short-form video ads average 2.4x the completion rate of static feed ads (ref-06) — completion matters when the message is a story about follow-through, not a price flash. We hold discipline through the noisy weeks, since frequency above 4 exposures per week correlates with an 18% drop in engagement rate (ref-06).

### 2. Creator Partnerships
A resolver who doubts herself will not believe a brand's January promise, but she will believe a creator who kept the resolution last year. Creators share their own "this year, actually" stories — the resolutions they finally kept and how the rower fit into their mornings. The buy is also efficient: creator-partnership posts deliver 31% lower cost-per-engagement than brand-owned posts (ref-06).

### 3. Lifecycle Email, SMS & Retargeting
Because the warmest audience is the one that already almost bought, we re-engage Kinetiq's list, SMS base, and past site visitors for the season. The mechanics are the strongest in the plan: lifecycle-triggered emails average a 38% open rate, versus 21% for standard campaign sends (ref-16), cart-abandonment and lapsed-buyer flows recover 5.9% of at-risk revenue on average (ref-16), and SMS opt-in subscribers convert at 2.4x the rate of email-only subscribers on time-limited offers (ref-16) — and a discount deadline on December 24 is exactly such an offer.

## KPIs

- **Seasonal unit sales** across gifting and resolution windows, with trial-path conversion benchmarked against the 46% in-home trial average versus 11% standard checkout (ref-11)
- **Paid social CTR** at or above the 1.2% cross-vertical average, targeting the 2.6% top-quartile benchmark (ref-06)
- **Lifecycle re-engagement** — lapsed-buyer flow revenue recovery measured against the 5.9% at-risk benchmark (ref-16)
- **First-month activation of January buyers** — share completing 8 or more workouts, the gate to 81% versus 34% 12-month retention (ref-11), so resolution sales become retained members rather than spring returns
- **Campaign-line organic pickup** and creator content volume

## Why Kinetiq Wins

Every brand in the category will shout about January motivation; this plan is built on why January motivation fails — a measured, dominant fear of unused equipment (ref-11) — and prices, proves, and messages against it. The discount opens the door, the trial removes the risk, the creative makes follow-through believable, and the lifecycle engine converts the people who almost said yes last year.
