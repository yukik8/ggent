## Campaign Title

---

## Opening: Retention Is the New Acquisition

Introducing the Clean Bowl Club, Pawstead Fresh's first gamified loyalty layer. Gamification is absolutely everywhere right now — streaks, badges, milestone rewards — and the brands winning at retention are the ones borrowing mechanics from apps people already open every day. The Clean Bowl Club brings that energy to fresh dog food. Every delivery a subscriber keeps extends their "Clean Bowl Streak," unlocking milestone perks: a personalized bowl tag at month 4, a free topper pack at month 6, and "Founding Pack" status at month 9 with early access to new recipes.

On top of the streak layer, we are stacking the tactics that are working hardest in DTC right now: lifecycle SMS drops (SMS is having a huge moment in subscription commerce), UGC-style creator content starring real rescue dogs, a win-back flow with a "We Saved Your Bowl" comeback kit, and paid social retargeting that keeps lapsed households warm. Community-led retention, conversational CRM, pet UGC — this program pulls all the current levers at once, wrapped in one club identity that makes staying subscribed feel like membership instead of a bill.

The $110/month average plan stays exactly where it is. We are not discounting our way to loyalty — we are making the subscription feel alive.

---

## The Big Idea

**"The Clean Bowl Club"** turns the most ordinary signal in dog ownership — an empty bowl — into a streak worth keeping. Subscribers get a monthly "Clean Bowl Report" for their dog (fun stats, feeding milestones, a shareable card with their dog's photo), and each kept delivery advances their streak toward real perks. Lapsed subscribers don't get a generic discount email; they get a "Your Streak Is Waiting" comeback kit that restores their old milestone progress if they return within 60 days. It's Duolingo-style streak psychology meets the dog park flex — people love showing off their dogs, and we're giving them a branded reason to do it.

## Channel & Media Plan

### 1. Lifecycle CRM — Email & SMS Flows
Email is still one of the most effective retention channels out there, and SMS performance in subscription brands has been really impressive lately. We will build a full flow architecture: a months 3-5 "streak defense" sequence (milestone previews, flexibility nudges like pause/downshift options), a win-back sequence for lapsed subscribers, and SMS streak alerts for members who opt in. Conversational, dog-first copy throughout — every message written as if it's about the dog, not the invoice.

### 2. Clean Bowl Club Loyalty Layer
The streak program itself: milestone rewards, the monthly Clean Bowl Report, shareable streak cards, and the comeback-kit mechanic for win-back. This is the connective tissue for everything else, and loyalty programs are proven revenue drivers for subscription brands. Rewards are engineered to be Instagrammable — the month-4 bowl tag is designed to be photographed.

### 3. Creator Content — Real Dogs, Real Months
We will partner with vet-tech creators and real-dog accounts to produce "month 4 with Pawstead" style content — honest, diary-format videos showing what staying subscribed actually looks like (fridge organization hacks included, because that's a real conversation in the community). Creator content feels far more authentic than brand ads for this audience, and the diary format is trending hard in pet content right now.

### 4. Paid Social — Retention Retargeting & Win-Back
Paid social running against two custom audiences only: current subscribers in months 3-5 (streak milestone creative) and lapsed subscribers within 6 months (comeback kit creative). Short-form video performs great with pet owners, and retargeting warm audiences is far cheaper than prospecting cold ones.

---

## Target Audience

Primary: current Pawstead Fresh subscribers in months 3-5 of their subscription — the stretch where the novelty wears off, the fridge fills up, and the card statement starts to pinch. These are quality-first pet parents, mostly urban dog owners under 40, who chose fresh food deliberately and need a reason to stay past the honeymoon phase.

Secondary: lapsed subscribers who cancelled within the past 6 months. Many of them left over logistics (fridge space, delivery timing, budget season) rather than the product itself, which makes them very winnable.
