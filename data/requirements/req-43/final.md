## Opening: The Problem We Are Actually Solving

Pawstead has a month-four problem, not a product problem. The average fresh pet food subscriber churns after 4.2 months, while top-quartile brands retain subscribers past 9 months (ref-09). That gap — roughly five months of retained revenue per subscriber at Pawstead's $110/month average plan — is the single largest lever in the business right now, larger than any acquisition play. And the economics of pulling it are decisively in retention's favor: median ROI for retention marketing is $4.60 per dollar spent, versus $2.20 for prospecting (ref-16).

The category context makes the stakes higher, not lower. Fresh/refrigerated is the fastest-growing segment of a US pet food market that reached $58.1B in 2024, growing at 17% YoY (ref-09) — which means every subscriber Pawstead loses at month four is re-acquirable by a competitor riding the same wave. The Clean Bowl Club is our answer to that specific problem: a streak-based membership layer, lifecycle flows, and a win-back mechanic engineered to carry subscribers from the 4.2-month churn cliff into top-quartile territory past month 9. Every tactic below is selected for its measured ability to move retention and win-back, not for novelty.

---

## Target Audience

Primary: current Pawstead Fresh subscribers in months 3-5 — the cohort sitting directly on the 4.2-month churn cliff (ref-09). These are quality-first pet parents, mostly urban dog owners under 40, and the evidence says they did not choose Pawstead on price: 63% of dog owners under 40 rank ingredient quality above price when choosing a food brand (ref-09). They leave over logistics — fridge space, delivery rigidity, the monthly statement — not over the food. The program therefore defends the months where logistics friction peaks, with flexibility nudges rather than discounts.

Secondary: lapsed subscribers who cancelled within the past 6 months. Structured re-engagement of this group is a measured, bankable channel — cart-abandonment and lapsed-buyer flows recover 5.9% of at-risk revenue on average (ref-16) — and because they churned over logistics rather than product, a mechanic that restores their progress outperforms a coupon.

---

## The Big Idea

**"The Clean Bowl Club"** turns the most ordinary signal in dog ownership — an empty bowl — into a streak worth keeping. Because the core problem is the 4.2-month churn cliff (ref-09), the streak mechanic is strategic, not decorative: milestone rewards at months 4, 6, and 9 place a tangible perk directly on top of each statistically dangerous decision point, and the monthly shareable "Clean Bowl Report" gives subscribers a recurring, dog-first reason to feel the membership working. Lapsed subscribers get a "Your Streak Is Waiting" comeback kit that restores their milestone progress if they return within 60 days — a win-back offer built on loss aversion instead of margin erosion. The prize for getting this right is structural: loyalty members generate 42% of revenue at brands with mature programs while representing 18% of customers (ref-16).

## Channel & Media Plan

Each channel below maps directly to the month-four problem named above.

### 1. Lifecycle CRM — Email & SMS Flows
Because churn concentrates at a predictable moment, triggered messaging beats calendar messaging: lifecycle-triggered emails average a 38% open rate, versus 21% for standard campaign sends (ref-16). We build a months 3-5 "streak defense" sequence (milestone previews, pause and plan-downshift nudges timed to the cliff), a lapsed-subscriber win-back sequence benchmarked against the 5.9% at-risk revenue recovery average (ref-16), and SMS streak alerts for opted-in members — prioritized because SMS opt-in subscribers convert at 2.4x the rate of email-only subscribers on time-limited offers (ref-16). Every message is written about the dog, not the invoice.

### 2. Clean Bowl Club Loyalty Layer
The streak program is the retention asset itself: milestone rewards, the monthly Clean Bowl Report, shareable streak cards, and the comeback-kit mechanic. The investment case is the 42%-of-revenue-from-18%-of-customers economics of mature loyalty programs (ref-16) — this budget line builds the infrastructure that compounds after the campaign window closes. Rewards are engineered to be photographed, because owned social proof feeds the next channel.

### 3. Creator Content — Real Dogs, Real Months
Because our at-risk subscribers need proof that staying subscribed is normal and workable, we show them real households doing it: 57% of pet owners under 40 discover new pet brands through social video featuring real pets (ref-09), and the same format re-persuades existing ones. Vet-tech creators front the formulation story for an audience that distrusts unsupported claims — 58% of Gen-Z respondents say they distrust ads that make claims without visible evidence or numbers (ref-07) — and the diary-format "month 4 with Pawstead" videos (fridge hacks included) speak directly to the logistics objections that drive cancellation. The economics hold up too: creator-partnership posts deliver 31% lower cost-per-engagement than brand-owned posts (ref-06).

### 4. Paid Social — Retention Retargeting & Win-Back
Paid social runs against two custom audiences only: months 3-5 subscribers (streak milestone creative) and sub-6-month lapsed subscribers (comeback kit creative). Short-form video carries the weight at an efficient $9.40 average CPM on Instagram Reels (ref-06), and short-form video ads average 2.4x the completion rate of static feed ads (ref-06) — completion matters when the creative's job is re-persuasion, not reach.

## Why Pawstead Wins

This plan attacks the measured cause of Pawstead's revenue leak — a churn cliff at 4.2 months in a category where the best brands retain past 9 (ref-09) — with a membership mechanic that places rewards exactly where subscribers fall off, flows that trigger on behavior instead of a calendar, and a win-back offer built on restored progress instead of discounts. The bowl empties every day; the Clean Bowl Club makes that daily moment the reason subscribers stay.
