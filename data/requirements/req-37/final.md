## Campaign Title

**"Follow the Fork" — the Cascade Valley Culinary Trail**

---

## Opening: The Problem We Are Actually Solving

Cascade Valley has an empty-spring problem, not an awareness problem. Shoulder-season (Apr-May, Sep-Oct) occupancy for regional destinations averages 46%, versus 78% peak-season (ref-05) — and the valley's April and May weekends sit squarely in that gap while its restaurants, cideries, and farm stands carry full fixed costs. The visitors the valley does attract are worth real money: the average regional weekend visitor spends $412 per trip across lodging, food, and activities (ref-05). Every unfilled spring weekend is that spend, forgone, across every room and every table in the region.

The Culinary Trail is our answer to that specific problem. A $65 passport that strings the valley's best farm stands, cideries, smokehouse joints, bakery counters, and chef-driven small-town restaurants into one collectible, stampable route gives food-motivated travelers a concrete reason to book the April and May weekends they would otherwise skip — and a multi-stop format that spreads their $412 of trip spend across the valley's producers rather than concentrating it in one town. The audience is reachable where the plan puts its weight: drive-market weekend trips make up 61% of visits to small US regional destinations (ref-05), so we fish where the fish are, in the drive metros within three hours of the valley. Every tactic below is selected for its ability to convert spring weekends and passport sales, not just to generate buzz.

---

## Target Audience

Primary: food-motivated weekend travelers aged 25-45 living within roughly a three-hour drive of Cascade Valley — the drive-market segment that already supplies 61% of visits to destinations like ours (ref-05). These are the trip-planners of their friend groups, and their planning starts on their phones: 54% of travelers under 35 pick regional trips based on short-form video seen in the prior 30 days (ref-05). They are deliberate about paid experiences — Gen-Z respondents check an average of 2.7 sources before a first purchase over $50 (ref-07), which is exactly where the $65 passport sits — so the plan surrounds them with creator proof, UGC proof, and owned-channel proof before we ask for the sale. Many actively prefer off-peak weekends when rooms are cheaper and the trail is less crowded, which is precisely the behavior the valley needs more of.

Secondary: in-region locals collecting stamps over multiple weekends and food-scene day-trippers from the nearest metro.

---

## The Big Idea

**"Follow the Fork"** turns the valley into a game board. Every stop on the trail gets a numbered fork marker, every stamp earned is a share-worthy moment, and finishing the trail becomes a badge of honor. Because the core problem is empty shoulder-season weekends, the stamp-book mechanic matters strategically, not just creatively: a passport is unfinished until its holder comes back, so the format manufactures the repeat spring visits that a one-off event never could. The creative also leads with the traveler's problem — "your best food weekend, mapped and bookable" — rather than a discount, because only 22% respond to discount-led messaging as a first touch; 49% respond to problem-solution storytelling (ref-07).

## Channel & Media Plan

Each channel below maps directly to the empty-spring, passport-conversion problem named above.

### 1. Paid Social — Short-Form Video (TikTok & Instagram Reels)
Because spring trips are decided on the feed — 54% of travelers under 35 pick regional trips based on short-form video seen in the prior 30 days (ref-05), and 71% of Gen-Z respondents discover new brands primarily through short-form video (ref-07) — the heaviest media weight goes to geo-targeted TikTok and Reels flights in drive-market metros: fast-cut trail run-throughs, stamp-book close-ups, and "one day, six stops" itineraries. The buy is efficient at Instagram Reels' $9.40 average CPM and TikTok's $7.80 (ref-06), and we cap delivery deliberately, since frequency above 4 exposures per week correlates with a 18% drop in engagement rate (ref-06).

### 2. Food Creator Partnerships & Hosted Trail Weekends
Creators are how a $65 considered purchase clears its proof bar. 64% of Gen-Z respondents have purchased a product recommended by a niche creator with under 100K followers (ref-07), so we host regional food and travel creators for full passport weekends and let them post the experience end to end. The economics reinforce the choice: creator-partnership posts deliver 31% lower cost-per-engagement than brand-owned posts (ref-06), and their footage becomes our best-performing paid assets.

### 3. The Stamp Challenge — UGC & Always-On Social
Because the valley's problem recurs every shoulder season, the fix cannot be a burst-and-vanish campaign. Destinations running always-on social content see 27% higher repeat visitation than campaign-only destinations (ref-05), so the Stamp Challenge keeps the board's feeds active across the entire window — traveler stamp-book posts featured on the tourism board's channels, stop spotlights, producer mini-profiles, and a finisher giveaway that keeps books moving toward completion.

### 4. Email & CRM — Past-Visitor Passport Pre-Sale
The board's newsletter list and past event sign-ups are the cheapest passport sales in the plan: median ROI for retention marketing is $4.60 per dollar spent, versus $2.20 for prospecting (ref-16). We open with a pre-sale to this warm list, then run lifecycle sends through the season — new stop announcements, finisher stories, and a late-season "last chance to finish your book" push — because lifecycle-triggered emails average a 38% open rate, versus 21% for standard campaign sends (ref-16).

## Why Cascade Valley Wins

This plan does not chase a food-tourism trend; it attacks the measured cause of the valley's weakest quarter — spring weekends running at shoulder-season occupancy while peak weekends sell themselves — with a passport format engineered to create bookings, repeat visits, and spend spread across the valley's producers. Short-form video fills the top of the funnel where these trips actually start, creators supply the proof a $65 purchase demands, the Stamp Challenge keeps the valley on feeds all season, and the warm list converts first at retention-level economics. Every dollar answers to the same named problem.
