## Campaign Title

**"Follow the Fork" — the Cascade Valley Culinary Trail**

---

## Opening: A Trail Made for the Food Tourism Moment

Introducing the Cascade Valley Culinary Trail: a $65 passport that strings together the valley's best farm stands, cideries, smokehouse joints, bakery counters, and chef-driven small-town restaurants into one collectible, stampable route. Passport holders get a physical stamp book plus a digital version, tasting perks at every stop, and a finisher reward for anyone who completes all the stops in a single season.

Food tourism is absolutely everywhere right now. Culinary trails, passport programs, and "eat your way through a region" content are blowing up across social platforms, and destinations that get in early on this trend are the ones winning the conversation. People plan entire trips around a single viral sandwich these days. Cascade Valley has the goods — real producers, real kitchens, real stories — and the trail packages them into something modern travelers instinctively understand: a checklist worth driving for.

The trend layer is what makes this campaign special. We are not just promoting restaurants, we are giving the internet a format it already loves — the stamp collection, the completion streak, the "we did all twelve stops" post. This is the kind of program that generates its own content flywheel once it gets moving.

---

## The Big Idea

**"Follow the Fork"** turns the valley into a game board. Every stop on the trail gets a numbered fork marker, every stamp earned is a share-worthy moment, and finishing the trail becomes a badge of honor. We seed the format with creators, fuel it with short-form video, and let travelers' own stamp-book posts do the rest. The passport is the product, the trail is the story, and the fork is the icon that ties every touchpoint together — road signs, stickers, filters, and the stamp itself.

## Channel & Media Plan

We stack the tactics that are working in travel right now.

### 1. Paid Social — Short-Form Video (TikTok & Instagram Reels)
Short-form video is the engine of travel discovery right now, and food content is the single most reliable performer in the format. We will run geo-targeted flights of TikTok and Reels in drive-market metros, built around fast-cut trail run-throughs, stamp-book close-ups, and "one day, six stops" itineraries. Social video is where trips start these days, so this is where the bulk of our media goes.

### 2. Food Creator Partnerships & Hosted Trail Weekends
We will bring regional food and travel creators into the valley for hosted trail weekends, letting them run the passport experience end to end and post as they go. Creator content feels authentic in a way brand content never quite does, and niche food creators have incredibly loyal audiences. Their footage also becomes our best-performing paid assets.

### 3. The Stamp Challenge — UGC & Always-On Social
A standing challenge across the campaign: post your stamp book progress with the campaign tag, get featured on the tourism board's channels, and finishers get entered for a valley getaway giveaway. We keep the board's social feeds active the whole window — trend sounds, stop spotlights, producer mini-profiles — because accounts that stay active simply perform better than ones that go quiet between campaigns.

### 4. Email & CRM — Past-Visitor Passport Pre-Sale
The tourism board's newsletter list and past event sign-ups are a warm audience that already knows the valley. We will run a passport pre-sale to this list before the public push, followed by lifecycle sends through the season — new stop announcements, finisher stories, and a late-season "last chance to finish your book" push. Email is a quietly effective channel that most destinations underuse.

---

## Target Audience

Our primary audience is food-motivated weekend travelers aged 25-45 living within roughly a three-hour drive of Cascade Valley. These are the trip-planners of their friend groups — people who save food and travel videos all week and turn the best ones into weekend plans. They love a specific, bookable experience over generic scenery, and a $65 passport sits comfortably inside their typical weekend budget. Many of them actively prefer off-peak weekends when rooms are cheaper and the trail is less crowded, which fits our April start nicely.

Secondary audiences include in-region locals collecting stamps over multiple weekends and food-scene day-trippers from the nearest metro.
