## Campaign Title

**"Offsite, Elevated" — Cascade Valley for Meetings That Matter**

---

## Opening: The Cascade Valley Meetings Product

Cascade Valley now offers a coordinated portfolio of small-conference and retreat venues, packaged for the first time under a single booking framework. The venue packages start at $4,500 per event and bundle meeting space, lodging blocks, catering coordination, and access to the valley's outdoor experiences into one point of contact through the Tourism Board's group-sales desk.

The product lineup covers a range of formats:

| Package Tier | Capacity | Core Inclusions |
|---|---|---|
| Summit Room | 10–25 attendees | Boardroom-style space, AV, lodging block at partner inn |
| Lodge Retreat | 25–60 attendees | Dedicated lodge buyout option, breakout rooms, catering |
| Valley Conference | 60–120 attendees | Conference hall, multi-property lodging, shuttle coordination |

Every package includes a single-invoice billing option, a dedicated valley concierge, and add-ons such as guided hikes, tasting-room evenings, and team activities that turn a standard meeting into a genuine retreat. Corporate offsites and retreats are a growing area right now, and many organizations are looking for meeting destinations that feel different from a downtown hotel ballroom. Cascade Valley's combination of scenery, walkable small towns, and turnkey packages puts it in a strong position to capture that interest. This campaign is designed to introduce the meetings product to the professional planning community and build a pipeline of bookings through the fall, winter, and into early 2027.

---

## Target Audience

Our primary audience is event planners at regional companies and associations, the people who own the decision on where an offsite, board retreat, training session, or small annual conference gets held. This includes in-house executive assistants and office managers who plan events as part of a broader role, dedicated corporate event managers, and association staff who run recurring member meetings.

These planners are practical buyers. They care about capacity, cost certainty, AV that works, and how hard it will be to get everyone there. They also respond to destinations that make them look good to leadership and attendees. Most of them are active on LinkedIn professionally and read industry newsletters, so B2B channels will do the heavy lifting in this plan.

Secondary audience: executives and team leads who influence or veto the destination choice once a planner shortlists it.

---

## The Big Idea

**"Offsite, Elevated"** positions Cascade Valley as the upgrade path from the interchangeable airport-hotel meeting. The creative contrasts two versions of the same agenda, one in a windowless conference room, one in the valley, and lands on a simple promise: same meeting, better outcomes. The centerpiece asset is the **Planner's Valley Playbook**, a downloadable planning kit with floor plans, sample agendas, catering menus, transport logistics, and per-person cost worksheets for each package tier. The Playbook gives planners everything they need to build an internal pitch, and it doubles as our lead-capture engine across every channel.

## Channel & Media Plan

| # | Channel | Role in Plan |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | LinkedIn (paid + organic) | Primary awareness and lead generation |
| 2 | Event-industry trade media & newsletters | Credibility with professional planners |
| 3 | Planner FAM (familiarization) weekends | Convert warm leads through firsthand experience |
| 4 | Paid search & retargeting | Capture in-market demand and nurture site visitors |
| 5 | Email nurture program | Move Playbook downloads toward RFPs |

### 1. LinkedIn — Paid and Organic
LinkedIn is very effective for reaching professionals who plan events, and it lets us target by job title and company type. We will run sponsored content promoting the Playbook, short venue walkthrough videos, and lead-gen forms, supported by an organic posting cadence from the Tourism Board's page.

### 2. Trade Media & Newsletters
Placements and sponsored features in event-planning trade publications and regional business newsletters build credibility where planners already do their research. Advertorial-style features on the package tiers will carry the detail that display ads cannot.

### 3. Planner FAM Weekends
We will host two familiarization weekends, one in October and one in early December, bringing qualified planners to the valley to experience a compressed retreat firsthand. FAM programs are a proven tactic in destination sales, and firsthand experience is the strongest closer for this kind of decision.

### 4. Paid Search & Retargeting
Search campaigns capture planners actively looking for retreat and small-conference venues in the region, while retargeting keeps Cascade Valley in front of everyone who visited the meetings landing page or downloaded the Playbook.

### 5. Email Nurture
A five-touch email sequence for Playbook downloaders, moving from inspiration to logistics to a direct invitation to submit an RFP or book a site visit.
