# Client Request — Copperleaf "Cold Craft" RTD Cold Brew Grocery Launch

**To:** Meridian & Craft — New Business
**From:** Elena Vasquez, Director of Marketing, Copperleaf Coffee Roasters
**Date received:** 2026-07-01
**Subject:** RFP — Launch campaign for Cold Craft ready-to-drink cold brew, fall 2026

---

Hello,

We haven't worked together before — Meridian & Craft came recommended by two people I trust, and your regional consumer work is why you're on this list. Here's what we're trying to do.

## Who we are

Copperleaf Coffee Roasters runs 14 cafés across Ohio. In our cities, we're a bit of an institution: lines out the door on weekends, a loyalty app people actually use, baristas who know regulars by name. This September we're taking our first real step outside the café: **Cold Craft**, a canned single-origin cold brew line, launching into Midwest grocery chains.

The product is genuinely excellent — small-batch roasted, 18-hour steep, no additives, three origins at launch. Everyone who tries it at our cafés loves it. It will retail at **$4.29 per 11oz can**, against a national RTD average around $3.49, and we think the quality carries that premium.

## What we want

A launch campaign that brings the Copperleaf café experience to the grocery aisle — the craft, the roast story, the origin transparency. We want people picking up Cold Craft the way they'd order at our counter. Big, visible, exciting: social, in-store, sampling, whatever it takes to make this launch feel like Copperleaf showing up in a new place.

One thing I'll flag from our soft test this spring: in the six pilot stores near our cafés, cans moved great. In the four pilot stores in Indianapolis, where nobody knows us, shoppers picked the can up, looked at it, and put it back next to the brand they already knew. I'm sure that fixes itself once the campaign gets our story out there.

## Target

Grocery shoppers aged 21-35 who already buy premium RTD coffee. They care about quality and story, they're on their phones constantly, and they're willing to pay for something real.

## Budget & timeline

- **Budget:** $390,000 all-in (media, production, sampling, measurement)
- **Campaign window:** 2026-09-15 to 2026-12-31 — we ship to roughly 180 grocery doors across Ohio, Indiana, and Kentucky in mid-September

## Success

Our board has set the bar at an **average sell-through of 10 units per store per week across all 180 doors by December**, not just the stores in our home markets. If we only sell where we already have cafés, the grocery expansion doesn't pencil.

## Deliverables

- Launch strategy and creative platform
- Channel and media plan with budget allocation
- In-store/sampling recommendation
- Measurement framework tied to the sell-through goal

## Process

This is a competitive review — three agencies, proposals due 2026-08-07, decision by end of August. As the one shop on the list we haven't met, consider this your chance to surprise us.

Looking forward to seeing how you think.

Elena Vasquez
Director of Marketing, Copperleaf Coffee Roasters
