# Hartline Paint-Color Trend Lookbook Campaign — DRAFT

**Prepared by:** Ryo Tanabe, Junior Planner, Meridian & Craft
**Client:** Hartline Home & Hardware
**Campaign window:** February 1 – April 30, 2028
**Budget:** $150,000

---

## Campaign Title

**"The Hartline Color Book — Spring 2028"**

---

## Opening

Introducing the Hartline Color Book, a seasonal paint-color trend lookbook produced in print and digital editions. The lookbook curates eight spring palettes built from paint available in every Hartline store from $34 per gallon, with room photography, palette pairings, and a tear-out shopping card for each look. It gives Hartline a branded answer to the color-of-the-year content the national paint brands publish, localized to what is actually on Hartline shelves.

Spring is the biggest interior painting season of the year, and color inspiration content performs consistently well with homeowners. The Color Book gives Hartline an ownable seasonal asset it can refresh annually.

## Lookbook Contents

| Section | Contents |
|---|---|
| Eight trend palettes | Hero room photo, three coordinating colors, finish guidance |
| Room guides | Bedroom, kitchen, exterior front-door features |
| Shopping cards | Tear-out card per palette listing paint and supplies |
| How-much-paint chart | Simple coverage calculator by room size |

## Target Audience

Homeowners planning spring interior refreshes. They browse inspiration content through the winter and buy paint when the weather turns, and industry reporting suggests color confidence is a common sticking point before purchase.

## The Big Idea

Make choosing a color feel finished before the first brushstroke. The lookbook does the coordinating work, so the customer just picks a page and brings the card to the counter.

## KPIs

- Lookbook pickup rate in stores and digital edition views
- Engagement on palette content across social and Pinterest
- Email click-through on the palette series
- Paint department sales versus last spring

## Why This Will Work

The lookbook is a simple, well-organized asset that removes the hardest part of a paint project — choosing colors that work together. It is inexpensive to produce, easy to refresh every season, and gives every media channel one clear destination.
