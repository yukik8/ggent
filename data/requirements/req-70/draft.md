## Campaign Title

**"Season One: The Career Change Diaries"**

---

## Opening

Docu-style content is eating the feed right now. Day-in-the-life formats keep outperforming polished brand spots, mini-documentary series are the breakout format on YouTube, and "real people, real stakes" storytelling is what audiences reward with watch time. The Career Change Diaries takes that wave and gives Skillharbor its own binge-able franchise: a season of short, honest, beautifully shot alumni episodes, cut down into every format the algorithm loves.

And the timing stacks up. New-year career-reset content peaks in exactly our launch window, "quit-tok" and career-pivot storytimes keep trending across platforms, and episodic drops are how creators are building followings right now. A weekly season rhythm lets Skillharbor plug into all of those currents at once instead of betting everything on a single hero asset.

---

## The Tactics

### 1. Alumni Docu-Series ("Season One")
Eight episodes, each following one Skillharbor graduate: where they started, the messy middle, and where they are now. Shot vertical-first, docu-style, no scripts. Episodes drop weekly to build a season rhythm and a reason to follow the brand.

### 2. Paid Social Distribution
Each episode gets a paid push on Reels, TikTok, and Shorts, with hook-tested openers and episode trailers. We ride the docu-content trend with format-native cutdowns rather than ad-style edits, so the paid flight feels like content, not interruption. Best-performing hooks get re-cut mid-season, keeping the flight fresh against whatever the algorithm is favoring that week.

### 3. Podcast & YouTube Clip Cutdowns
Every episode also becomes audio-format clips and a YouTube mid-length cut, catching the audience that prefers longer storytelling. Alumni with strong audio presence get pitched to career podcasts as guests, extending each story beyond our own channels.

## Who This Reaches

Prospects in the consideration stage — people who already know Skillharbor exists and are deciding whether it is worth their money. They are researching reviews, lurking in alumni threads, and comparing options. For them, a real graduate's story carries more weight than any promise the brand could make. The series gives them the proof they are quietly looking for, one relatable episode at a time.

### 4. LinkedIn Amplification
Alumni post their own episodes to LinkedIn with first-person reflections, amplified with paid. Career-change stories are LinkedIn's native genre, and each alumni post doubles as social proof in the exact feed where consideration-stage prospects spend their research time.

### 5. Landing Hub
A "Career Change Diaries" hub collects the full season, organizes episodes by starting career and program, and routes viewers to the $199/month all-access trial.
