## Campaign Title

**"Season One: The Career Change Diaries"**

---

## Opening

Docu-style content is the strongest format trend in the feed right now. Day-in-the-life formats consistently outperform polished brand spots, mini-documentary series are a breakout genre on YouTube, and "real people, real stakes" storytelling is what audiences reward with watch time. The Career Change Diaries gives Skillharbor its own binge-able franchise: a season of short, honest, well-crafted alumni episodes, cut down into every format each platform favors.

The timing also works in our favor. New-year career-reset content peaks in exactly our launch window, career-pivot storytelling continues to trend across platforms, and episodic weekly drops are the cadence audiences have been trained to follow. A season rhythm lets Skillharbor plug into those currents together rather than betting everything on a single hero asset.

It is also a natural fit for the brand. Skillharbor's best marketing asset has always been its graduates, and a season format turns those individual stories into a sustained presence rather than a one-off testimonial push.

---

## The Tactics

### 1. Alumni Docu-Series ("Season One")
Eight episodes, each following one Skillharbor graduate: where they started, the messy middle, and where they are now. Shot vertical-first, docu-style, unscripted. Episodes drop weekly to build a season rhythm and give audiences a reason to follow the brand between drops.

### 2. Paid Social Distribution
Each episode gets a paid push on Reels, TikTok, and Shorts, with hook-tested openers and episode trailers. Cutdowns are format-native rather than ad-style edits, so the paid flight reads as content, not interruption, and best-performing hooks are re-cut mid-season to keep the flight fresh.

### 3. Podcast & YouTube Clip Cutdowns
Every episode also becomes audio-format clips and a mid-length YouTube cut for the audience that prefers longer storytelling. Alumni with strong audio presence are pitched to career podcasts as guests, extending each story beyond our own channels at no additional media cost.

## Who This Reaches

Prospects in the consideration stage — people who already know Skillharbor exists and are deciding whether it is worth their money. They are reading reviews, lurking in alumni threads, and comparing options, and at this stage a real graduate's story carries more weight than any promise the brand could make. The series gives them the proof they are quietly looking for, one relatable episode at a time.

### 4. LinkedIn Amplification
Alumni post their own episodes to LinkedIn with first-person reflections, amplified with paid support. Career-change stories are LinkedIn's native genre, and each alumni post lands as social proof in the exact feed where consideration-stage prospects do their research.

### 5. Landing Hub
A "Career Change Diaries" hub collects the full season, organizes episodes by starting career and program, and routes viewers to the $199/month all-access trial.

## Why This Works

Almost no edtech brand has committed to a genuine season format, which leaves the strongest content trend on every platform essentially unclaimed in this category. Career Change Diaries turns Skillharbor's happiest customers into its content engine, sustains presence across a full quarter rather than a single burst, and gives consideration-stage prospects the binge-able proof that tips them into the trial. Production is front-loaded, distribution is efficient, and every asset produced continues working for the brand long after the season ends.
