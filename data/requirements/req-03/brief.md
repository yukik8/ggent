# Client Request — Commuter One Launch Campaign

**To:** Meridian & Craft
**From:** Marcus Webb, Chief Marketing Officer, Northtrail Mobility
**Date received:** 2025-03-20
**Subject:** RFP — Northtrail Commuter One launch, summer 2025 riding season

---

Meridian & Craft team,

You know us well after five years together, but this is our biggest product moment yet, so I'm writing this up more formally than usual.

## Company situation

Northtrail has built its name on commuter e-bikes and our battery-swap station network. This May we launch the **Commuter One** — the first bike we've designed from the ground up around the swap system. Lightweight aluminum frame, torque-sensing mid-drive motor, integrated lights, rack, and our quick-release battery. Spec-for-spec it's the best bike we've ever built, and the board wants a launch that treats it that way.

Pricing: **$1,899 upfront, or an $89/month subscription bundle** that includes the bike plus unlimited battery swaps.

## What we want

A big, energetic summer launch campaign. We want the Commuter One everywhere our riders look — video, creators, out-of-home, real-world test rides. Show off the bike, show off the swap ritual, make Northtrail the brand of the summer in our cities. Feature story first: this is the most complete commuter e-bike on the market and people should know it.

One data point from the field, take it or leave it: our dealer partners keep telling us a lot of shoppers love the bike and then walk when they see the upfront price tag. It's why finance pushed for the monthly bundle. Anyway — the campaign should lead with the product; the pricing options are there in the mix.

## Target

Urban commuters aged 25-40 in mid-size cities with decent bike infrastructure. Office workers with a real daily commute who are sick of driving.

## Budget & timeline

- **Budget:** $650,000
- **Window:** 2025-05-01 to 2025-09-30 — the full summer riding season

## Success metric

We are targeting **6,000 Commuter One conversions (outright sales plus subscription bundles) attributed to the campaign by September 30**. Awareness lift matters too, but that unit number is what I've committed upward.

## Deliverables

- Launch strategy and creative platform
- Channel and media plan with budget breakdown
- Experiential/test-ride component — we believe butts-on-seats sells bikes
- Phased timeline and KPI framework

## Process

Given the spend, this is a formal competitive review — four agencies including yourselves. Incumbency counts for something with me but not with our CFO, so the proposal has to win on its own merits. Submissions due in three weeks; finalist presentations the week after.

Ride on,
Marcus
