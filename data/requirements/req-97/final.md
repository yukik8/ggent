## Campaign Title

**"Board Games" — Crisp Thins Belong on Every Snackboard**

---

## Opening: The Problem We Are Actually Solving

Sunbrook's problem is that shoppers who like it cannot recall it. Only 27% of snack buyers aged 18-34 can name a preferred mid-market crisp brand unprompted (ref-01), and private-label share of salty snacks rose from 18% to 23% between 2021 and 2024 (ref-01) — a squeeze that hits hardest with 25-44 crossover buyers who trade between crackers and crisps and will happily take the store brand in either aisle. The result is a loyalty deficit: repeat purchase rate for the average mid-market snack brand is 31%, versus 52% for the top-3 national brands (ref-01).

Crisp Thins, at $4.49 per box, is our answer to that specific problem: a format private label cannot quickly copy, launched inside a cultural context (the snackboard) that makes the Sunbrook name memorable rather than interchangeable. The category supports the bet — US salty snacks retail was valued at $34.2B in 2024, growing 3.1% YoY (ref-01). Every tactic below is selected to move unaided recall and cross-aisle repeat, not just trend impressions.

---

## Target Audience

Primary: snack buyers aged 25-44 who trade between crackers and crisps depending on occasion — the shopper where the private-label substitution risk is highest and where the snackboard occasion activates both aisles at once. They discover food brands in-feed: 71% of Gen-Z respondents discover new brands primarily through short-form video (ref-07), and they are evidence-sensitive — 58% say they distrust ads that make claims without visible evidence or numbers (ref-07). Secondary: existing Sunbrook loyalists curious about the new format.

---

## The Big Idea

**"Board Games"** claims the base-layer position on the snackboard. Because the core problem is recall, the trend is the vehicle, not the strategy: every board built in public attaches the Sunbrook name to a repeatable entertaining occasion, converting one trial box into the habitual cross-aisle purchase where mid-market brands trail 31% to 52% (ref-01).

## The Tactic Stack

The seven tactics survive intact, each now answerable to the recall problem:

1. **#BoardGames hashtag challenge** — because recall is built by participation, not exposure; only 22% respond to discount-led messaging as a first touch while 49% respond to problem-solution storytelling (ref-07).
2. **Snackboard ASMR content** — crunch-forward formats exploit video's attention edge: short-form video ads average 2.4x the completion rate of static feed ads (ref-06).
3. **Micro-influencer board drops** — 64% of Gen-Z respondents have purchased a product recommended by a niche creator with under 100K followers (ref-07).
4. **Duet-bait formats** — participation formats compound reach at creator economics: creator-partnership posts deliver 31% lower cost-per-engagement than brand-owned posts (ref-06).
5. **Trend-jacking calendar** — keeps the name in the feed without over-frequency; frequency above 4 exposures per week correlates with an 18% drop in engagement rate (ref-06).
6. **Retail media surge buys** — grocery retail media campaigns average a ROAS of 3.8x for snack and beverage brands (ref-08), converting the demand social creates.
7. **In-store sampling boards** — in-store sampling lifts trial conversion by 19% on average in grocery channels (ref-01), and serving boards keeps the recall context at shelf.
