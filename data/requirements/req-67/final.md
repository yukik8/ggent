## Campaign Title

**"#BenefitDrop" — Unlock the Learning Budget You Already Have**

---

## Opening: The Problem We Are Actually Solving

There is a funding leak sitting inside Skillharbor's partner companies. Only 38% of employees eligible for employer tuition reimbursement used any of it in 2025 (ref-12) — which means the majority of Skillharbor's most fundable prospects are leaving up to $2,000/year of learning budget untouched. The research is specific about why: non-users most commonly cite unclear approval processes and uncertainty about which programs qualify. This is an administrative problem more than a demand problem, and it compounds a second, well-documented confusion — 66% of career changers aged 25-40 say they cannot identify which skills would actually raise their salary (ref-12).

#BenefitDrop is our answer to that specific leak. The campaign makes the hidden benefit visible where employees actually are — LinkedIn, short-form video, and their own company Slack — and then removes the paperwork barrier entirely with a reimbursement concierge that handles approval on the learner's behalf. Every tactic below exists to close the gap between the 100% of eligible employees and the 38% who currently use the money (ref-12).

---

## Who We're Talking To

Employees at Skillharbor's partner companies with unused tuition benefits — up to $2,000/year already sitting in their compensation package. Most either do not know the benefit exists or assume the paperwork is a nightmare, exactly the two frictions the data identifies (ref-12). They skew 25-45, are active on LinkedIn and short-form video, and pay attention when money is being left on the table. We name the audience here, first, because the entire plan is built around where these specific employees can be reached inside and outside the workplace.

## The Tactics

Because the core problem is an awareness-and-admin leak, each tactic below either surfaces the benefit or removes the paperwork.

### 1. LinkedIn Paid + Display
Because the core problem is that eligible employees do not know the money exists, we announce it in the channel where they think about careers and compensation. New-year career content peaks in January, and sponsored posts, conversation ads, and display retargeting keep "your employer will pay for this" in the feed all quarter.

### 2. Partner HR Email & Internal Comms Kits
The most credible messenger for a benefits message is the employer itself. We arm partner HR teams with ready-made internal announcements — email templates, Slack/Teams drop-in posts, intranet banners, and a printable one-pager. The channel works: lifecycle-triggered emails average a 38% open rate, versus 21% for standard campaign sends (ref-16), and an HR benefits notice is as lifecycle-relevant as email gets.

### 3. Short-Form Video (Reels, TikTok, Shorts)
"Wait, my job will PAY for this?" is problem-solution storytelling in one line — the framing that 49% of younger audiences respond to as a first touch, versus only 22% for discount-led messaging (ref-07). Benefits-hack videos, HR-reaction skits, and 30-second claim walkthroughs run on efficient inventory: Instagram Reels average a $9.40 CPM, TikTok $7.80, and YouTube Shorts $6.90 (ref-06).

### 4. Lunch-and-Learn Webinar Series
A monthly "Use It or Lose It" virtual lunch-and-learn, co-hosted with partner HR teams, walks employees through the reimbursement process live — directly attacking the "unclear approval process" barrier (ref-12) — and ends with a QR code to start enrollment on the spot.

### 5. Landing Page + Reimbursement Concierge Funnel
Because the leak is administrative, the concierge is the conversion unlock, not a nice-to-have. The #BenefitDrop landing page checks eligibility by employer, shows exactly what is covered, and hands users to a concierge flow where Skillharbor handles the approval paperwork end to end.

## Why This Works

This plan attacks a measured leak with a matched fix. The money already exists; only 38% of eligible employees used any of it in 2025 (ref-12), because nobody tells the rest clearly and the paperwork scares them off. #BenefitDrop makes the benefit unmissable through the employer's own channels and the feeds employees actually watch, then deletes the admin barrier with the concierge. Awareness opens the funnel, and paperwork removal closes it — both answerable to the same named problem.
