## Campaign Title

**"The Stack Squad" — the Stackwise Creator Partnership Flight**

---

## Opening

Finance content is one of the fastest-growing corners of the creator economy right now, and Stackwise should be all over it. FinTok and money-Reels creators are pulling serious engagement with everything from budget breakdowns to "loud budgeting" confessionals to no-spend-month vlogs, and the formats keep multiplying. This flight assembles a squad of personal-finance creators around Stackwise and runs a four-month wave of partnership content, paid amplification, and platform-native stunts that put the app inside the money conversation this spring.

The play here is volume and variety: lots of creators, lots of formats, always on trend.

---

## Tactic 1: The Stack Squad Roster

We sign a mixed roster — a couple of anchor names in personal finance plus a wider bench of mid-size and niche money creators — to multi-post deals across the flight. Each creator gets a custom referral code and creative freedom to fit Stackwise into their existing formats: budget-with-me videos, savings challenges, paycheck routines, financial glow-ups.

## Tactic 2: Trend-Jacking Sprints

Every two weeks, we run a trend sprint: whatever money format is spiking (loud budgeting, cash-stuffing aesthetics, "girl math," no-spend January leftovers, whatever February brings), the squad ships Stackwise-flavored takes on it within days. A standing mini-brief process and pre-cleared approval lanes keep the turnaround fast enough to actually catch trends while they're alive.

## Tactic 3: Spark Ads & Whitelisting

The best-performing squad content gets paid legs through Spark Ads on TikTok and whitelisted creator handles on Meta, so the media runs under creator names instead of the brand's. Creator-voiced paid consistently beats brand-voiced paid on engagement, and it lets us scale winners instead of guessing in advance.

## Tactic 4: The Squad Challenge

Mid-flight, the whole roster runs the same one-week savings challenge simultaneously, each documenting it in their own style, all pointing at one hashtag. A synchronized cross-roster moment gives the flight a spike of cultural mass in April, right when tax refunds hit accounts.

## Tactic 5: Referral Leaderboard

Creator referral codes feed a public-facing leaderboard, adding a competitive layer that keeps the roster motivated and gives audiences a rooting interest. Top referrer gets a bonus pool payout and a featured in-app takeover week.

---

## Target Audience

All of this is aimed at followers of personal-finance creators under 35 — people already watching money content, already primed to act on creator recommendations, and already half-convinced they need a system. They skew toward first-job and early-career earners, they're deeply fluent in short-form video, and they can smell an inauthentic brand deal instantly, which is why creative freedom for the roster is non-negotiable.
