## Opening: The Problem We Are Actually Solving

Ledgerline's customers did not buy AP automation to keep coding invoices by hand, yet that is where their hours still go. Finance teams report 62% of invoice processing time is still manual at companies without AP automation (ref-04), and GL coding is the stubborn manual core that survives even after AP tooling arrives — data entry, exception handling, and approval chasing concentrated at month-end. That is the problem this launch sells against: not "AI has arrived," but "your controllers are still losing their close week to coding."

The AI invoice-coding module is the answer to that specific burden. At $9,000 per year against a team's recovered close-week hours, the value case is concrete, and because the audience is existing accounts, we are selling relief to people who already trust the platform. Every tactic below is selected to put a quantified time-back story in front of the controller who is living the problem.

## Channel & Media Plan

Each channel maps back to the manual-coding problem and the proof-driven buyer named above.

### 1. LinkedIn & Account-Based Media
Because this audience is expensive to reach — the LinkedIn B2B benchmark CPM for finance decision-makers is $58, versus $31 for general B2B targeting (ref-04) — we spend impressions only on named existing accounts and intent-flagged roles, never on broad awareness. Creative leads with the manual-time problem, not features.

### 2. Customer Email & Lifecycle Program
Warm-account email is our efficiency engine: lifecycle-triggered emails average a 38% open rate, versus 21% for standard campaign sends (ref-16), so the program is built on behavioral triggers — usage milestones, close-calendar timing — rather than batch blasts. The economics favor this motion structurally: median ROI for retention marketing is $4.60 per dollar spent, versus $2.20 for prospecting (ref-16).

### 3. Webinar & Demo Series
Because the buyer needs to see her own workflow disappear, live sessions code real invoice batches on screen, with customer controllers co-presenting.

### 4. Case Study & Content Production
Proof converts where features do not: case-study-led landing pages convert demo requests at 4.7%, versus 2.1% for feature-led pages (ref-04). Early-adopter stories with named hours-saved and days-cut-from-close numbers are the campaign's core asset, feeding every other channel.

### 5. CSM Enablement & In-App Messaging
CSMs carry the case studies into QBRs, and in-app banners surface the module at the moment of pain — during heavy coding sessions and close week.

### 6. Landing Pages & Creative
The module page is case-study-led, not feature-led, per the 4.7% versus 2.1% conversion gap (ref-04), with demo booking and pricing upfront.
