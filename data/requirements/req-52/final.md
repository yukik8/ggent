## Campaign Title

**"Lunch, Handled" — the Blue Fork Catering Launch**

---

## Opening: The Problem We Are Actually Solving

Blue Fork has a revenue-concentration problem: nearly all of its sales arrive one $12.90 bowl at a time, through individual lunch decisions the brand has to win again every single day. Catering changes the unit of sale — one office booking is dozens of covers at $14 per head, and a satisfied booker repeats — but the buyer is different from anyone Blue Fork currently markets to. The office manager or executive assistant at a 50-500 person office makes a considered, reputation-on-the-line purchase, not an impulse one, and the economics of winning that buyer favor keeping them: median ROI for retention marketing is $4.60 per dollar spent, versus $2.20 for prospecting (ref-16), which is why this plan is built to create repeat accounts, not one-off orders.

The new catering program is our answer to that problem. Build-your-own bowl bars, boxed bowls, and breakfast spreads at a clean $14 per head with online ordering, invoicing, and delivery windows built in — everything offices already love about Blue Fork, scaled to a conference room. Every tactic below is selected for its ability to reach the booking decision-maker and convert them into a repeat account, not merely to make catering look good on camera.

---

## Target Audience

The buyer is the office manager, executive assistant, or team admin at 50-500 person offices — the person who actually places the order and takes the blame or the credit. This is a professional purchase made in work mode, which shapes our media choices: LinkedIn B2B benchmark CPM for finance decision-makers is $58, versus $31 for general B2B targeting (ref-04), so we buy the broader professional targeting tier and let creative do the qualifying. Around the buyer sits an influence ring — employees who tag the office manager in food content, team leads planning offsites, HR running culture budgets — and many buyers already know Blue Fork from their own lunch runs, in a habit set where 74% of diners aged 18-34 check a chain's app or social feed before first visiting a new location (ref-10). That existing familiarity is a warm start most caterers don't get.

---

## The Big Idea

**"Lunch, Handled."** Two words that speak directly to the person stuck organizing lunch. Because the core problem is winning a reputation-conscious buyer and keeping them, the campaign gives that buyer the credit: the bowl bar reveal, the team descending on it, and the "Office Hero" mechanic that rewards whoever booked it.

The stack:

- **The Reveal format** — creators and real offices film the bowl-bar setup moment, timed for the lunchtime scroll.
- **"Office Hero" mechanic** — whoever books the catering gets the credit: a thank-you treat for the booker in every order, plus a repeat-booking reward track that operationalizes the retention economics above (ref-16).
- **Free Bowl-Bar Fridays** — surprise drop-in tastings at target office buildings, filmed for social.
- **Tier-list bait** — we seed "ranking every office catering option" content where Blue Fork wins, obviously.

## Channel & Media Plan

Each channel below maps directly to the decision-maker problem named above.

### 1. Paid Social — LinkedIn + Instagram Reels & TikTok
Because the core problem is reaching a professional buyer, LinkedIn carries the work-mode message — bought efficiently against the benchmark spread of $58 CPM for finance decision-makers versus $31 for general B2B targeting (ref-04) — while Reels and TikTok carry the reveal and unboxing formats at consumer efficiency: Instagram Reels average a $9.40 CPM and TikTok $7.80 (ref-06), and short-form video ads average 2.4x the completion rate of static feed ads (ref-06). Both feed the same booking page.

### 2. Creator Partnerships — WorkTok & Food Creators
Because a considered buyer needs social proof before risking their reputation on a vendor, workplace-comedy and food creators make Blue Fork catering feel pre-approved by the internet. The channel is also efficient: creator-partnership posts deliver 31% lower cost-per-engagement than brand-owned posts (ref-06), and their formats get amplified through paid.

### 3. Office Sampling Drops — "Free Bowl-Bar Fridays"
Because the booking decision is won in person more than in feed, street teams deliver surprise mini bowl-bars to pre-qualified offices in core metros on Fridays through the fall, capturing content and leaving behind booking cards and a first-order discount code. Nothing sells a bowl bar like standing in front of one, and each drop puts us physically in front of the exact buyer this plan targets.

### 4. Email Outreach & Booking Hub
Because a considered purchase needs proof at the point of decision, the booking hub leads with real office case studies rather than menu features — case-study-led landing pages convert demo requests at 4.7%, versus 2.1% for feature-led pages (ref-04). The outreach program nurtures office contacts and Blue Fork's existing app base with triggered flows, where lifecycle-triggered emails average a 38% open rate, versus 21% for standard campaign sends (ref-16).
