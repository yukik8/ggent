## Campaign Title

**"Lunch, Handled" — the Blue Fork Catering Launch**

---

## Opening: Catering Is the Next Frontier

Blue Fork Kitchen is rolling out a full office catering program, and the menu is a genuine flex: build-your-own bowl bars, boxed bowls for meetings, breakfast spreads, all landing at a clean **$14 per head** with online ordering, invoicing, and delivery windows built in. It's everything people already love about Blue Fork, scaled up to feed a conference room.

And the cultural moment is absolutely there. WorkTok is enormous right now — "what my office ordered for lunch" videos, catering unboxings, office-lunch tier rankings, the "corporate girlie" aesthetic, all of it pulls huge numbers. Return-to-office mandates mean more in-person meetings, more team lunches, more "we owe the team a good Friday" moments. Free food is the number one thing employees say makes an office day worth it. Meanwhile the standard office catering options are tired — sad sandwich trays and lukewarm pasta — and a colorful bowl bar photographs like a dream. If Blue Fork shows up in the feeds and the break rooms at the same time, "Lunch, Handled" can make Blue Fork the default answer to "who's getting lunch for the offsite?"

---

## The Big Idea

**"Lunch, Handled."** Two words that speak directly to the person stuck organizing lunch. The campaign turns catering-day into content: the bowl bar reveal, the team descending on it, the empty trays at the end. We lean into every format that's working right now — office-lunch unboxings, tier-list reactions, "POV: you picked the catering and everyone loves you" — and stack them into a constant drumbeat across the flight. Catering becomes the meeting everyone actually shows up for.

The stack:

- **The Reveal format** — creators and real offices film the bowl-bar setup moment, timed for the lunchtime scroll.
- **"Office Hero" mechanic** — whoever books the catering gets the credit: a thank-you treat for the booker in every order, plus a repeat-booking reward track.
- **Free Bowl-Bar Fridays** — surprise drop-in tastings at target office buildings, filmed for social.
- **Tier-list bait** — we seed "ranking every office catering option" content where Blue Fork wins, obviously.

---

## Channel & Media Plan

### 1. Paid Social — LinkedIn + Instagram Reels & TikTok
We'll run LinkedIn ads aimed at office roles alongside Reels and TikTok flights with the reveal and unboxing formats. LinkedIn catches people in work mode; short-form video catches everyone at lunch. Both feed the same booking page.

### 2. Creator Partnerships — WorkTok & Food Creators
A roster of workplace-comedy and food creators produce office-lunch content around Blue Fork catering drops. Creator content is what makes catering feel like a moment instead of a vendor decision, and their formats get amplified through paid.

### 3. Office Sampling Drops — "Free Bowl-Bar Fridays"
Street teams deliver surprise mini bowl-bars to pre-qualified offices in core metros on Fridays through the fall, capturing content and leaving behind booking cards and a first-order discount code. Nothing sells a bowl bar like standing in front of one.

### 4. Email Outreach & Booking Hub
A dedicated catering landing page with menus, instant quotes, and a "book a tasting" CTA, supported by an outreach email program to office contacts and to Blue Fork's existing app base ("your office could use this").

---

## Target Audience

The buyer here is the office manager, executive assistant, or team admin at 50-500 person offices — the person who actually places the order and takes the blame or the credit. They're organized, budget-conscious, and quietly powerful: win one of them and you win a repeat account. Around them sits the broader influence ring — employees who tag the office manager in food content, team leads planning offsites, HR folks running culture budgets. A lot of these buyers already know Blue Fork from their own lunch runs, which gives us a warm start most caterers don't get.
