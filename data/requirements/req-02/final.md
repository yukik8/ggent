## Campaign Title

**"Nothing to Hide" — The Barrier Repair Relaunch**

## The Problem We Are Solving

Velura has a retention problem, not a formula problem. The average DTC skincare brand loses 44% of first-time buyers before a second order (ref-02), and Velura's franchise lives or dies on that second jar, because moisturizer is the highest-frequency skincare repurchase category at 3.4 units per buyer per year (ref-02). A buyer we keep is worth more than three jars a year; a buyer we lose after jar one is a marketing cost we never recover.

The audience tells us exactly how to earn that second order. 62% of skincare buyers under 30 say ingredient transparency is a top-3 purchase driver (ref-02), and 58% of Gen-Z respondents distrust ads that make claims without visible evidence or numbers (ref-07). The reformulated Barrier Repair Moisturizer, with its shorter, cleaner, fragrance-free ingredient list at $32 per 50ml jar, is our answer to that specific problem: proof, shown line by line, that this is a brand worth buying twice. Every tactic below is selected for its ability to convert transparency into trust and trust into repeat purchase, in a clean-beauty segment that reached $11.6B in 2024 and is growing 8.4% year over year while total prestige skincare grew only 2.9% (ref-02).

## Target Audience

Because the core problem is first-order churn among transparency-driven buyers, we focus the plan on skincare buyers under 30 for whom ingredient transparency is a stated purchase driver, the 62% documented above (ref-02). These are researchers, not impulse shoppers, they screenshot labels and verify claims before buying. A secondary audience of clean-beauty-curious shoppers is reached through the same channels at no incremental media cost, but creative, landing experience, and measurement are all built for the label-reader first.

## Big Idea

**"Nothing to Hide."** We put the entire formula in the spotlight, literally. Every creative asset is built around radical openness about what is in the jar and why. The centerpiece is the **Open Formula Series**, a run of short videos in which Velura's formulator walks through the ingredient list line by line, explaining what each ingredient does and, just as importantly, what was removed and why. We pair this with a redesigned ingredient page on the Velura site, transparent packaging call-outs, and creator content that invites skincare enthusiasts to scrutinize the label themselves.

The idea maps directly onto the diagnosis. Only 22% of consumers respond to discount-led messaging as a first touch, while 49% respond to problem-solution storytelling (ref-07). "Nothing to Hide" is problem-solution storytelling with the evidence built in: we are not asking people to trust a claim, we are showing them the receipt.

## Channel & Media Plan

Each channel is retained from the original plan and justified against the retention-through-transparency problem.

1. **Short-form video (TikTok + Instagram Reels)** — the core of the plan, because 71% of Gen-Z respondents discover new brands primarily through short-form video (ref-07), and short-form video ads average 2.4x the completion rate of static feed ads (ref-06). Inventory is efficient here: Instagram Reels averages a $9.40 CPM and TikTok $7.80 (ref-06). We cap delivery below four exposures per user per week, since frequency above 4 exposures per week correlates with an 18% drop in engagement rate (ref-06).
2. **Creator partnerships** — because the core problem is distrust of unevidenced claims (58%, ref-07), third-party scrutiny beats brand assertion. We prioritize credible niche voices: 64% of Gen-Z consumers have purchased a product recommended by a niche creator with under 100K followers (ref-07), and creator-partnership posts deliver 31% lower cost-per-engagement than brand-owned posts (ref-06). Creators receive the full formula documentation and are free to give honest reviews.
3. **Ingredient transparency hub (owned web)** — a rebuilt product and ingredient page that hosts the full formula breakdown, formulator videos, and FAQ, serving as the landing destination for all paid traffic. This is where a 62% transparency-driven audience (ref-02) completes its verification step before purchase.
4. **CRM & email** — the direct lever on the 44% first-order churn number (ref-02): a relaunch announcement flow to the existing list, a formula-comparison edition for past Barrier Repair buyers, and a post-purchase education series engineered to secure the second order.

## KPIs

Each KPI is anchored to the problem and to a benchmark from the evidence base.

- **Second-order rate among relaunch-period first-time buyers**, targeted to beat the category's 44% first-order loss rate (ref-02)
- **Paid social click-through rate**, targeting the 2.6% top-quartile benchmark against a 1.2% cross-vertical average (ref-06)
- **Cost-per-engagement on creator content**, held below brand-owned benchmarks per the 31% creator efficiency advantage (ref-06)
- **Ingredient hub traffic and time-on-page** from paid and creator sources, as the measurable verification step
- **Repurchase velocity among existing customers**, benchmarked against the category's 3.4 units per buyer per year (ref-02)
