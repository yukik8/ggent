## Opening: The Problem We Are Actually Solving

Ledgerline has a shortlist problem, not an integration problem. 73% of mid-market finance software evaluations now start with a shortlist built from peer review sites (ref-04) — which means most finance directors have decided who is even in the running before they ever visit Ledgerline's site or take a sales call. And once an evaluation starts, it drags: the average AP automation deal cycle for 50-500 employee firms is 94 days (ref-04), with integration uncertainty — "will this really work with my ERP?" — the question that stalls deals in security review and IT sign-off. Meanwhile the underlying pain is acute and measurable: finance teams report 62% of invoice processing time is still manual at companies without AP automation (ref-04).

The ERP-integration co-marketing program is our answer to that specific problem. Ledgerline's certified two-way integrations with NetSuite, Sage Intacct, and Microsoft Dynamics 365 Business Central are introduced here not as features but as the shortcut into the buyer's existing circle of trust — the ERP ecosystems and peer research destinations where shortlists actually form. By co-marketing with the ERP brands themselves, at a $24,000 average annual contract value per influenced deal, we position Ledgerline inside the evaluation before it starts and remove the integration objection that stretches the 94-day cycle. Every tactic below is selected to either get Ledgerline onto the shortlist earlier or to shorten the path from shortlist to signature.

---

## Target Audience

Primary: finance directors evaluating ERP-connected AP tools at 50-500 employee companies — the exact segment where evaluations begin on peer review sites (73% of mid-market finance software evaluations start there, ref-04) and where the manual-processing pain is heaviest (62% of invoice processing time still manual without AP automation, ref-04). These buyers live inside their ERP daily, trust peers and proof over promises, and are reachable but expensive on LinkedIn — the B2B benchmark CPM for finance decision-makers is $58, versus $31 for general B2B targeting (ref-04), which is precisely why every impression we buy must carry evidence, not adjectives.

Secondary: controllers and AP managers who influence the evaluation, and ERP consultants and implementation partners who often recommend add-on tools to their clients.

## The Big Idea

**"The Connected Close"** turns the integration itself into the hero. Because the core problem is that shortlists form before Ledgerline is in the conversation, we do not pitch features — we show real month-end closes running through Ledgerline and the ERP together, one connected motion from invoice to ledger, co-signed by the ERP brand the buyer already trusts. The centerpiece is **Connected Close Live**, a co-hosted webinar series where a Ledgerline customer and their ERP partner walk through an actual close, screens and all. This is proof-led by design: case-study-led landing pages convert demo requests at 4.7%, versus 2.1% for feature-led pages (ref-04), and every asset in the program is built to that case-study standard.

## Channel & Media Plan

Each channel below maps directly to the shortlist-and-cycle problem named above.

### 1. LinkedIn Paid & ABM
Because finance decision-makers are a premium audience — the LinkedIn B2B benchmark CPM for finance decision-makers is $58, versus $31 for general B2B targeting (ref-04) — we spend against precision, not volume: sponsored close stories and demo films, plus an ABM layer against named accounts already running our partner ERPs. Every placement drives to case-study-led landing pages, the format that converts demo requests at 4.7% versus 2.1% for feature-led pages (ref-04).

### 2. Co-Hosted Webinar Series ("Connected Close Live")
Because the deal cycle averages 94 days for 50-500 employee firms (ref-04), the webinar series is engineered to compress it: a live customer close answers the integration, security, and workflow questions that normally consume weeks of evaluation, with the ERP partner co-presenting and co-promoting to their installed base. Monthly episodes run March through June, each anchored on a real customer close.

### 3. Peer Review & Case-Study Syndication
Because 73% of mid-market finance software evaluations now start with a shortlist built from peer review sites (ref-04), this channel is our shortlist insurance. We invest in sponsored placement on relevant category pages, refreshed integration-specific reviews, and syndication of co-branded case studies to the research destinations where the evaluation actually begins.

### 4. Email Nurture & Lifecycle Program
Because a 94-day cycle (ref-04) means most leads will not buy this quarter, every campaign touchpoint enters an ERP-specific, behavior-triggered nurture stream. The mechanism matters: lifecycle-triggered emails average a 38% open rate, versus 21% for standard campaign sends (ref-16), so we trigger on webinar attendance, content downloads, and review-site visits rather than blasting a calendar.
