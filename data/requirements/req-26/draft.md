## Campaign Title

**"Own the Cold" — the Northtrail Winter-Ready Push**

---

## Opening: Winter Just Became Northtrail's Best Season

Introducing the Northtrail Winter-Riding Kit, an absolutely gorgeous piece of engineering that turns the Commuter One into a genuine four-season machine. For $249, owners get studded-compatible all-weather tires, full-coverage fenders, a cold-weather battery jacket that keeps the pack warm and happy in freezing temperatures, bar mitts, and a high-output light set built for early winter sunsets. It installs in under an hour and it looks fantastic on the bike.

Alongside the kit, we are pushing the new $99/year Northtrail Service Plan, a wonderfully simple membership that covers a pre-winter tune-up, a spring refresh, priority battery-swap access, and unlimited brake and drivetrain checks. Together, the kit and the plan are the complete winter package, everything an owner needs to keep riding when the snow starts falling.

Winter riding is having a real moment. More and more commuters are discovering that with the right gear, cold-weather cycling is not just possible but genuinely fun, and cities keep improving their bike infrastructure every year. This campaign is Northtrail's chance to show its owner community that the Commuter One was never meant to hibernate.

---

## Target Audience

Our audience is existing Commuter One owners in snow-belt cities, places like Madison, Minneapolis, Buffalo, Denver, and Salt Lake City, where real winter arrives in November and stays. These are practical, thoughtful riders who bought their e-bike as a car alternative, not a toy. They love their bikes and many of them would happily keep riding through winter if someone showed them how.

Owners like this respond to honest, practical communication. They read reviews, they watch long-form breakdowns, they hang out in commuter forums, and they trust other riders far more than they trust ads. They also worry about their battery in the cold, which is exactly the anxiety the kit and the service plan are designed to erase.

---

## The Big Idea

**"Own the Cold"** flips winter from the enemy into the badge. Instead of apologizing for the season, we celebrate the riders who go through it. The centerpiece is the **First Frost Club**, a community program where owners who winterize their bike and log rides through the season earn milestone badges, from First Frost to Coldest Ride to Full Winter Rider. Real owners riding real winters become the campaign's heroes, and the kit plus service plan is simply the equipment that gets you into the club.

## Channel & Media Plan

### 1. Owner CRM — Email & SMS Lifecycle Program
We own the most valuable channel already, the owner list. A warm, well-timed email and SMS sequence will walk every registered Commuter One owner from "winter is coming" awareness through kit purchase and service-plan enrollment. Email is a great channel for existing customers and lets us personalize by city, purchase date, and riding history.

### 2. Paid Social — Short-Form Video (Instagram Reels & YouTube)
Beautiful, atmospheric short-form video of Commuter Ones cutting through fresh snow, breath in the air, city lights at 5pm. Social video is very effective for this kind of emotional, visual storytelling, and we will target our owner lists and lookalikes in snow-belt metros to keep media efficient.

### 3. Creator Partnerships — "A Full Winter, Documented"
We will partner with a handful of commuter and bike-lifestyle creators in snow-belt cities to document an entire winter of riding on a winterized Commuter One, honest weekly updates, cold-morning starts, battery reports, everything. Creator content feels credible to this audience in a way polished ads never will.

### 4. Winter-Ready Weekends — In-Person Install Events
Partnering with Northtrail service hubs and local bike shops, we will run install-and-tune weekends in priority cities through October and November. Owners ride in, get the kit installed, get the pre-winter tune-up from the service plan, and roll out winter-ready the same day. Hot coffee, good energy, and a First Frost Club sticker on the way out.
