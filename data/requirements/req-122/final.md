## Campaign Title

**"Strong Season" — the High-Protein Line Launch**

---

## Opening: The Problem We Are Actually Solving

Blue Fork does not have a menu problem; it has a frequency problem. Loyalty-app members visit fast-casual chains 2.3x more often than non-members and spend 31% more per order (ref-10), yet most Blue Fork guests remain occasional visitors outside that loop. Digital orders average 38% of fast-casual revenue; top-quartile chains exceed 55% (ref-10) — and Blue Fork's digital mix sits below the quartile it wants to join. The category rewards fixing this now: US fast-casual sales reached $209B in 2024, growing 8.2% YoY while full-service restaurants grew 2.4% (ref-10).

The High-Protein Line is our answer to that specific problem. Three $14.50 bowls with 40+ grams of protein and printed macros give the fitness-minded guest a reason to make Blue Fork a weekly routine ordered through the app, not an occasional treat. Every tactic below is selected to move visit frequency and digital mix, not just launch buzz.

---

## Target Audience

Fitness-minded fast-casual diners aged 18-34 — the cohort where routine-driven frequency is winnable. They vet before they visit: 74% of diners aged 18-34 check a chain's app or social feed before first visiting a new location (ref-10). They discover through video: 71% of Gen-Z respondents discover new brands primarily through short-form video (ref-07). And they demand proof: 58% say they distrust ads that make claims without visible evidence or numbers (ref-07) — which is exactly why the printed macro counts lead our creative.

---

## The Big Idea

**"Strong Season"** positions January through April as Blue Fork's season of strength — training moments paired with bowl beauty shots, inviting guests to make Blue Fork part of their routine. Because the core problem is frequency, the routine framing is strategic, not decorative: it converts a launch trial into the weekly app-ordered habit where loyalty economics live (ref-10).

## Channel & Media Plan

Each channel maps back to the frequency-and-digital-mix problem named above.

### 1. Paid Social — Short-Form Video (Reels & TikTok)
Because discovery happens in short-form video for 71% of this cohort (ref-07), it carries awareness. The economics hold: Instagram Reels average a $9.40 CPM and TikTok $7.80 (ref-06), and short-form video ads average 2.4x the completion rate of static feed ads (ref-06). We cap frequency, since above 4 exposures per week engagement drops 18% (ref-06).

### 2. Fitness Creator Partnerships
64% of Gen-Z respondents have purchased a product recommended by a niche creator with under 100K followers (ref-07), so we prioritize niche fitness and food creators, whose posts deliver 31% lower cost-per-engagement than brand-owned posts (ref-06).

### 3. Loyalty App & CRM Promotion
Because frequency is the goal, the app is the campaign's engine. Loyalty members generate 42% of revenue at brands with mature programs while representing 18% of customers (ref-16), and lifecycle-triggered emails average a 38% open rate versus 21% for standard sends (ref-16) — so the "Strong Season" bonus-points streak runs on triggered flows, not batch blasts.

### 4. In-Store & Gym Partnerships
Menu-board takeovers, macro cards, and sampling with boutique gyms and run clubs intercept the target mid-routine — the exact context where a weekly habit forms.
