## Opening: The Problem We Are Actually Solving

Every spring, Hartline fights the one battle it cannot win: a price war. Big-box rivals flood the trade area with doorbuster mulch and $99 grill ads, and a 40-store regional chain will never out-discount them. But price is not what actually moves this market anymore — guidance is. Project-based how-to content drives 2.7x more store visits than price-and-item advertising for regional chains (ref-14), and the customers up for grabs are do-it-yourselfers in a market where US home improvement retail reached $538B in 2025, with DIY purchases accounting for 52% of revenue (ref-14). The suburban homeowner staring at a winter-beaten yard doesn't need the cheapest spreader; she needs to know what to do first, and hesitation is the norm — 69% of first-time homeowners say they feel intimidated starting any project beyond painting (ref-14), and a yard is a project most never got past.

The Spring Yard Shop is our answer to that problem. Each of Hartline's 40 stores becomes a walkable seasonal zone with garden bundles from $99 — curated soil-seed-tool combinations that answer "what do I buy?" in one grab — surrounded by associates who know when to plant tomatoes in Tulsa. Because the core problem is guidance, not price, every tactic below sells the plan for the yard, and lets the bundle be the shopping list.

## Channel Plan

Each channel below maps back to the guidance-not-price problem.

### 1. Paid Social & Short-Form Video
Yard transformations, bundle unboxings, and planting walkthroughs across Reels, TikTok, and YouTube. The inventory is efficient — Instagram Reels average a $9.40 CPM, TikTok $7.80, and YouTube Shorts $6.90 (ref-06) — and the format holds attention: short-form video ads average 2.4x the completion rate of static feed ads (ref-06). We cap frequency, since frequency above 4 exposures per week correlates with a 18% drop in engagement rate (ref-06).

### 2. Content Production — How-To & Inspiration
Because the core problem is "what do I do first," the content series — "Your First Raised Bed," "Weekend Lawn Rescue," "Patio Refresh Under $250" — is the strategic heart of the plan, not decoration. This is precisely the project-based format shown to drive 2.7x more store visits than price-and-item advertising (ref-14), and it fuels the entire paid flight.

### 3. In-Store Weekend Garden Demos
Saturday demos — seed starting, bed prep, container gardening — put guidance physically in the Spring Yard Shop zone with bundles beside the demo table. Hartline's own workshop economics justify the investment: in-store workshop attendees spend 43% more annually than matched non-attendees (ref-14).

### 4. Local Out-of-Home & Radio
Billboards and drive-time radio across the store markets carry the seasonal declaration, not a price point — broad presence that reinforces Hartline as the starting line while big-box outdoor boards shout discounts.

### 5. Email & CRM
A spring countdown series with planting-calendar content localized by region and bundle offers timed to the weather. Timed, weather-triggered sends earn their keep: lifecycle-triggered emails average a 38% open rate, versus 21% for standard campaign sends (ref-16).
