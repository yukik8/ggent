**Prepared by:** Ryo Tanabe, Junior Planner, Meridian & Craft

**Budget:** $490,000

---

## Campaign Title

**"Stronger at Home" — the Kinetiq Strength Kit Launch**

---

## Opening

Introducing the Kinetiq Strength Kit, a $495 add-on that turns every Row One into a full home gym. The kit bundles adjustable dumbbells, a resistance-band system with rower-frame anchor points, and a heart-rate arm band, and it unlocks a new strength class category in the Kinetiq app — guided sessions that program rowing and strength together in one workout.

| Attribute | Detail |
|---|---|
| Price | $495 strength kit add-on |
| Contents | Adjustable dumbbells, anchored band system, HR arm band |
| Software | New strength class category, combined row+strength programs |
| Compatibility | All Row One units, no installation required |
| Availability | Member presale in March, public launch in April |

Strength training is popular and growing, and members have been requesting more variety in the class library. The Strength Kit answers both, expanding what a Kinetiq membership is worth without adding another large machine to the home.

---

## Target Audience

Primary: existing Kinetiq members, who already trust the brand, own the hardware, and can add the kit in one click. Secondary: strength-curious prospects who have hesitated on a rower-only purchase because they want a fuller workout from a single setup. Both groups value efficient, guided training at home.

---

## The Big Idea

**"Stronger at Home."** One corner of your home now covers your whole workout. Creative shows the same member, same corner, same hour of the morning — rowing on Monday, pressing on Tuesday, both in one session on Thursday. The message to members: your rower just got a second act. The message to prospects: this is the complete home gym that fits in two square feet.

---

## Channel & Media Plan

| Channel | Role |
|---|---|
| Member lifecycle — email, push & in-app | Presale announcement, launch offers, upgrade journeys |
| Live strength launch class series | New class category debut with launch-week live events |
| Paid social — short-form video | Prospect-facing reach for the complete-home-gym story |
| Creator partnerships | Members and fitness creators showing real row+strength routines |

### 1. Member Lifecycle — Email, Push & In-App
Members are the fastest revenue path, so the launch opens with a member presale: announcement emails, in-app banners, and push notifications, followed by upgrade journeys for members who click but do not buy.

### 2. Live Strength Launch Class Series
Launch week debuts the strength category with daily live classes and coach-led kit walkthroughs, giving the kit an event moment inside the platform and replay content for the on-demand library.

### 3. Paid Social — Short-Form Video
For prospects, short-form video carries the complete-home-gym story across Reels, TikTok, and Shorts. Social video is effective for demonstrating product, and the two-in-one setup demos well in under 30 seconds.

### 4. Creator Partnerships
Fitness creators and real members show their actual weekly routines with the kit. Authentic routine content tends to outperform polished spots for fitness audiences.

## Why Kinetiq Wins

The Strength Kit grows revenue from the audience Kinetiq already owns while giving hesitant prospects the fuller-workout answer they have been waiting for. A member-first launch keeps early costs low, launch-week classes make the kit feel like an event, and the complete-home-gym story gives paid social something genuinely new to say.
