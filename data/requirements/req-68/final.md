---

## Opening: The Problem We Are Actually Solving

Mid-career professionals are anxious about AI, but anxiety is not what blocks their enrollment — direction is. 66% of career changers aged 25-40 say they cannot identify which skills would actually raise their salary (ref-12), and the AI moment has made that confusion worse: every headline says "reskill," none says into what. The second blocker is a track record of quitting — completion rates average 12% for self-paced online courses versus 68% for cohort-based programs (ref-12) — so a workforce full of abandoned course logins does not believe the next purchase will be different. In a market that reached $13.7B in 2025, growing 11% YoY (ref-12), the provider that resolves direction and completion, not the one that shouts loudest about AI, wins this audience.

The AI-Skills Bootcamp is our answer to that specific problem. A live, practitioner-taught, $3,200 intensive — prompt fluency, workflow automation, AI-assisted analysis, and a capstone portfolio — gives worried professionals a single, concrete skill path instead of a wall of certificates, delivered in the cohort format that finishes at 68% rather than 12% (ref-12). Every tactic below is selected to convert anxious, direction-starved professionals, not merely to reach them.

---

## Target Audience

Mid-career professionals worried about AI displacement — experienced people in their late twenties through forties who read the headlines and feel a knot in their stomach. They spend time on LinkedIn, listen to news and business podcasts, and watch a lot of YouTube. What moves them is specificity: ads featuring a specific salary-outcome statistic drive 2.1x higher free-trial starts than generic skill-building ads (ref-12), so our creative names skills, outcomes, and numbers rather than trading on fear or vague empowerment.

## The Big Idea

**"Ahead of the Curve"** flips the AI conversation from fear to advantage. Because the core problem is direction, the creative does not just show professionals in command of AI — it names exactly which capabilities they gain, what those capabilities are worth, and how twelve weeks with a cohort gets them there. Every asset lands the same feeling: you are not being replaced, you are being upgraded.

## Channel & Media Plan

Each channel below maps directly to the direction-and-completion problem named above.

### 1. Paid Social — Short-Form Video (Reels, TikTok, Shorts)
Because the core problem is that professionals cannot tell which AI skills matter, the video flight answers on screen with named skills and concrete outcomes — the specificity that drives 2.1x higher free-trial starts (ref-12). The inventory is efficient — Instagram Reels average a $9.40 CPM, TikTok $7.80, and YouTube Shorts $6.90 (ref-06) — and short-form video ads average 2.4x the completion rate of static feed ads (ref-06). We hold frequency below the fatigue line, since frequency above 4 exposures per week correlates with an 18% drop in engagement rate (ref-06).

### 2. YouTube & Podcast Sponsorships
Longer formats carry the diagnosis. YouTube pre-roll runs bootcamp trailers built around the skill map, while host-read spots on business and news podcasts meet anxious professionals inside their daily AI headlines with a concrete answer instead of another warning.

### 3. LinkedIn Paid
LinkedIn is where this audience's career anxiety lives. Sponsored content and lead-gen forms carry the problem-first message — which skills, what outcome, what format — and drive directly to the free workshop.

### 4. Free AI Workshop Funnel & Webinars
Because the audience's history is abandoned self-paced courses, the live "Your First AI Workflow" workshop lets them experience the cohort format — the one that completes at 68% versus 12% for self-paced (ref-12) — before the $3,200 decision. Attendees flow into a nurture sequence toward bootcamp enrollment windows.

### 5. Creator Partnerships
Work-and-productivity creators demonstrate the skills in their own voice, making the promise verifiable rather than advertised. The economics also favor it: creator-partnership posts deliver 31% lower cost-per-engagement than brand-owned posts (ref-06).
