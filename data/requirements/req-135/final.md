## Campaign Title

**"Let It Grow"**

---

## Opening: The Problem We Are Actually Solving

Stackwise's challenge with this launch is not rate competitiveness, it is trust. 61% of savers under 30 say they distrust finance apps that lead with investment returns instead of safety (ref-13) — meaning a yield-first pitch for a 4.1% APY account risks reading as exactly the kind of product this audience deletes apps over. And the account only pays off for Stackwise if it deepens engagement in a category where the average consumer fintech app loses 73% of new users within 30 days of download (ref-13).

The High-Yield Savings Account, free with the $8/month premium tier, is our answer to both problems at once: a safety-framed home for idle money that gives existing users a durable, recurring reason to stay. Because trust is the barrier, every execution below leads with "your money stays yours" — no minimums, instant transfers back out, the same dashboard they already use — and lets the 4.1% arrive as the quiet second beat. Every tactic maps back to converting trusted idle balances, not chasing rate shoppers.

---

## Target Audience

Existing Stackwise users holding balances in low-interest checking. They have already crossed the app's trust threshold — the scarcest asset in the category, given that users who link a payroll deposit in their first week retain at 3.2x the rate of those who do not (ref-13). Moving their idle money is a trust transaction first and a rate decision second.

---

## The Big Idea

**"Let It Grow"** shows what idle money could be doing — the same $1,000 sitting still in checking versus quietly growing inside the Stackwise interface users already know. Because the core problem is yield-skepticism, the split-screen creative anchors on familiarity and withdrawal flexibility before the number, inverting the standard fintech rate ad.

---

## Channel Plan

Each channel maps back to the trust-and-retention problem named above.

### In-App & Lifecycle Email
Because the target already lives inside the product, direct idle-balance prompts in the trusted dashboard carry the launch — the one context where a savings suggestion reads as service, not sales.

### Short-Form Video (TikTok & Reels)
For users and lookalikes, short-form video is the efficient reach layer: short-form video app-install campaigns average a $4.10 CPI for finance apps, versus $6.80 on search (ref-13). Creative follows the safety-first script, since 61% of under-30 savers distrust yield-led finance messaging (ref-13).

### Creator Explainers
Finance creators walk through opening the account and their first month of real, modest growth — believable numbers on real screens, the format this skeptical cohort actually trusts.

### Paid Social Retargeting
Converts announcement viewers and site visitors who stalled, re-presenting the safety framing rather than escalating the rate pitch.
