# Cascade Valley Harvest Festival Weekend Push — Proposal

**Prepared by:** Emma Lindqvist, Junior Planner, Meridian & Craft
**Reviewed by:** Daniel Okafor, Senior Planner
**Version:** Final v2

**Client:** Cascade Valley Tourism Board
**Campaign window:** August 15 – October 31, 2028
**Budget:** $250,000

---

## Campaign Title

**"Harvest Days in the Valley"**

---

## Opening

The Cascade Valley Harvest Festival is the valley's signature fall event, and the $39 harvest festival pass makes it easier than ever to experience. The pass unlocks a strong lineup: orchard picking, a cider barn crawl, a farmers' feast market with dozens of local producers, pumpkin fields, hayrides, and a lantern-lit evening market. Autumn is the valley's most photogenic season, and the festival packages that seasonal appeal into one simple, affordable offer.

Our campaign brings the harvest season to families and food-motivated travelers across the drive market and converts that appeal into pass sales.

---

## Target Audience

Drive-market families and food-motivated travelers aged 25-50. These travelers value seasonal traditions and local food experiences, and they plan fall weekends around events they can book simply. A single $39 pass lowers the barrier to committing to the trip.

---

## The Big Idea

**"Harvest Days in the Valley"** positions the festival as the region's signature fall tradition. The creative is warm and abundant — market tables, orchards, cider, the lantern-lit evening market — leading to one clear call to action: grab your $39 pass and taste the season.

---

## Channel & Media Plan

### 1. Paid Social — Short-Form Video
Fall festival content performs reliably well on Reels and TikTok, and industry reporting suggests a majority of regional weekend trips are now inspired by content seen on social platforms. Hero videos carry the awareness push.

### 2. Creator Partnerships
Regional food and family creators experience the festival and share their favorite moments, adding credibility that brand-produced ads alone cannot.

### 3. Drive-Market Out-of-Home
Harvest billboards along key routes into the valley keep the festival visible throughout the season.

### 4. Regional Search & Display
Captures active searches for fall festivals, apple picking, and autumn weekend ideas.

### 5. Email & Landing Page
A pass-purchase page plus a short email series to the board's existing list.

## Why This Will Work

The festival is a proven product, the pass is an easy yes at $39, and fall is the valley's best creative season. With short-form video and creators building the appetite, out-of-home reinforcing it on the drive routes, and search capturing in-market demand, "Harvest Days in the Valley" turns seasonal interest into ticketed visits for the tourism board.
