## Opening: The Problem We Are Actually Solving

Northtrail's problem in this segment is the same one blocking the whole category: price stands between interest and purchase. 68% of urban commuters aged 25-40 say upfront price is the main barrier to buying an e-bike (ref-03), and there is no reason to believe a $1,999 sticker reads easier at 55 than at 35. The market's answer is already visible — subscription and financing models grew to 21% of e-bike transactions in 2024, from 9% in 2022 (ref-03) — which is exactly why the $92/month bundle, not the bike, is the hero of this campaign. The category tailwind is real: US e-bike unit sales hit 1.4M in 2024, a 12% YoY increase (ref-03). Our job is to remove the sticker from the conversation and put a small monthly number in its place.

The step-through comfort bike is the answer to that reframed purchase: an easy, stable, upright ride whose $92/month price compares to a gym membership, not a major purchase. Every tactic below is selected to lead with the monthly math and convert price-hesitant riders in flat-terrain suburbs.

## Target Audience

Primary: active adults aged 55-70 in flat-terrain suburbs — a segment with time, income, and riding history, but facing the same price wall the category data documents (ref-03). Because the core problem is the upfront barrier, all messaging leads with $92/month and positions the bike as an everyday utility, not a luxury. Trusted local voices and hands-on trial carry more weight here than any spec sheet.

## Channel & Media Plan

Each channel below maps back to the price-barrier problem named above.

### 1. Paid Social & Video
Because the barrier is the sticker, video creative is built around the monthly-cost comparison, not features. Facebook and YouTube lifestyle films and testimonial cuts close on the $92/month card. Short-form video ads average 2.4x the completion rate of static feed ads (ref-06), so the comparison story gets seen through to the price reveal.

### 2. Local Print, Radio & Community Sponsorships
Community media puts the monthly framing in trusted local spaces — newspapers, local radio, farmers markets, community rides — where a price-cautious audience hears it from familiar voices rather than a national ad.

### 3. Dealer Test-Ride Weekends
Because a hesitant buyer needs to feel the value before accepting even a monthly commitment, relaxed dealer weekends convert consideration into a first ride, with same-day $92/month signup on site. The product's range comfortably covers this audience's routes: average commute distance in mid-size US cities is 7.8 miles, within a single e-bike battery charge (ref-03) — suburban loops are shorter still.

### 4. Regional Digital Display & Search
Search captures riders already researching comfort and step-through models and routes them to a landing page that leads with the monthly price and the dealer locator, catching in-market demand at the moment the sticker question arises.
