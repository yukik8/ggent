# Sunbrook Mascot Revival Social Content Push — DRAFT

**Prepared by:** Marcus Bell, Junior Planner, Meridian & Craft
**Client:** Sunbrook Foods
**Campaign window:** September 1 – November 30, 2027
**Budget:** $190,000

---

## Campaign Title

**"Sunny's Back" — the Return of the Sunbrook Mascot**

---

## Opening

Introducing the comeback nobody saw coming: Sunny, the Sunbrook Crisps mascot retired in 2009, returns to the feed. Nostalgia-core is one of the strongest currents on social right now — Y2K aesthetics, retro brand lore, "they don't make it like this anymore" edits — and Sunny is a ready-made asset sitting in the brand vault. We bring him back lo-fi, self-aware, and extremely online.

---

## The Tactic Stack

1. **The mysterious return arc** — grainy "sightings" of Sunny posted with no explanation, seeded to meme accounts before any official announcement.
2. **Lo-fi lore drops** — scanned 90s print ads, VHS-filter commercials, and "found footage" from the archive, drip-fed weekly.
3. **Self-aware comeback content** — Sunny reacts to his own retirement, does trend formats badly on purpose, and posts like a millennial rediscovering the internet.
4. **Meme-account partnerships** — paid seeding with nostalgia and regional Midwest meme pages, where this content already lives.
5. **Creator duet bait** — "explain Sunny to Gen Z" formats and reaction content from niche snack creators.
6. **Community moment** — a fan vote on Sunny's new catchphrase, run natively in comments and stories.
7. **Reactive trend desk** — a standing content team that puts Sunny on any trending format within 48 hours.

---

## Target Audience

The core audience is nostalgia-driven snack buyers aged 25-40 on social video — people who grew up with Sunny in the pantry and now share retro content as identity. A secondary audience is younger viewers who enjoy retro lore ironically and amplify it.

## Why This Will Work

Nostalgia is the cheapest attention on the internet, and Sunny is paid-off brand equity nobody else can copy. The mystery arc earns the reveal, the lore rewards the fans, and the trend desk keeps the character alive long after launch week. Give the internet a mascot with a story and it does the media buying for you.
