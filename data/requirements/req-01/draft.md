# Sunbrook Crisps "Bold Batch" Launch Proposal — DRAFT

**Prepared by:** Emma Lindqvist, Junior Planner, Meridian & Craft
**Client:** Sunbrook Foods
**Campaign window:** March 10 – June 30, 2025
**Budget:** $420,000

---

## Campaign Title

**"Turn Up the Heat" — the Bold Batch Launch**

---

## Opening: An Exciting New Chapter for Sunbrook

Introducing Sunbrook Crisps Bold Batch, the boldest thing to come out of the Sunbrook kitchen since the original crisp. Bold Batch is a brand-new spicy flavor line that comes in three escalating heat levels — Ember, Blaze, and Inferno — each built on real chili blends rather than artificial heat powders. The bags feature a striking new matte-black packaging system with heat-level color coding that will absolutely pop on shelf next to the familiar Sunbrook core line.

At $3.99 per bag (a $0.50 premium over the core line), Bold Batch positions Sunbrook as a brand that can play in the premium flavor space while staying true to the quality Midwest shoppers already know. Spicy snacking is having a real moment right now, and young consumers love bold, adventurous flavors. This launch is Sunbrook's chance to bring that energy to grocery aisles across the Midwest and get the brand in front of a whole new generation of snack lovers.

---

## Target Audience

Our primary target is snack buyers aged 18-34 in Midwest grocery drive markets. These are young, social, flavor-curious consumers who snack throughout the day and are always open to trying something new. They spend a lot of time on their phones, they care about authenticity, and they get excited about products that feel fun and shareable. Social media is a huge part of how this group discovers new products, so it will be central to our plan.

Secondary audiences include existing Sunbrook loyalists who may trade up to the new line, and household shoppers picking up snacks for gatherings.

---

## The Big Idea

**"Turn Up the Heat"** is a challenge-driven launch platform. We invite consumers to climb the three heat levels — starting at Ember, surviving Blaze, and daring to attempt Inferno — and to film and share their reactions. The escalating-heat mechanic is naturally social, naturally repeatable, and gives creators an easy format to riff on. Everything in the campaign, from packaging color coding to in-store displays to short-form video, ladders up to one simple invitation: how far can you go?

---

## Channel & Media Plan

### 1. Paid Social — Short-Form Video (Instagram Reels & TikTok)
Short-form video is where our audience lives, and it is a very effective format for food content. We will run a heavy flight of paid Reels and TikTok placements featuring heat-challenge reaction content, product beauty shots, and cut-downs of creator videos. Social video will carry the bulk of awareness building for the campaign.

### 2. Creator Partnerships
We will partner with a roster of food and lifestyle creators to seed the "Turn Up the Heat" challenge. Creator content feels more authentic to young audiences than polished brand ads, and creators give us a steady stream of content we can amplify through paid. We will prioritize creators with genuinely engaged followings over pure reach plays.

### 3. In-Store Sampling & Shopper Activation
Nothing sells a flavor like tasting it. We will run weekend sampling events at high-traffic grocery locations across priority Midwest markets, with branded "heat station" displays where shoppers can try Ember and Blaze (Inferno stays a dare you take home). Sampling teams will push the challenge mechanic and hand out heat-level stickers.

### 4. Grocery Retail Media
We will support the launch with sponsored product placements and banner inventory on our grocery partners' retail media networks, keeping Bold Batch visible at the digital point of purchase and capturing shoppers who saw the social content earlier in the week.

## Why Sunbrook Wins

Bold Batch is a genuinely exciting product with a fun, ownable launch platform. The challenge mechanic gives young consumers a reason to participate rather than just watch, and the combination of social buzz and in-store tasting means we meet shoppers both where they scroll and where they buy.
