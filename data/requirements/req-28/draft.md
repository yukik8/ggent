## Campaign Title

**"Trade Your Traffic" — the Denver Flagship Launch**

---

## Opening: Denver, Meet the Showroom of the Future

The new Northtrail Denver flagship is not a bike shop — it is a destination. Two floors in RiNo, a live battery-swap demo wall, an indoor test-ride loop, a coffee bar, and the full lineup on display: the Commuter One at $1,899 and the cargo model at $2,799. Visitors can watch a battery swap happen in under a minute, configure their bike on a giant touchscreen, and roll out for a supervised street test ride, all in one visit. It is the kind of space people post about, and that is exactly the point.

E-bikes are having a huge cultural moment right now. Bike-commute content is blowing up on TikTok, "car-free" lifestyle creators are everywhere, and cities like Denver keep adding bike lanes. The showroom gives Northtrail a physical stage right in the middle of all that energy, and this campaign is about turning the opening into the talk of the Denver summer.

---

## The Big Idea

**"Trade Your Traffic"** invites Denverites to trade one rush-hour drive for one test ride. Everything hangs on a simple, shareable swap mechanic: bring the frustration of your commute, leave with the feeling of the ride. Billboards troll traffic where traffic is worst, creators film POV "traded my drive" videos, and the showroom becomes the place where the trade actually happens. It is playful, it is very filmable, and it plugs straight into the trends this audience is already watching.

---

## Channel & Media Plan

### 1. Short-Form Paid Social (TikTok & Instagram Reels)
Short-form video is where the momentum is, and commuter-POV content performs really well right now. We will run a summer-long flight of TikTok and Reels placements: test-ride POV footage, battery-swap "oddly satisfying" clips, and showroom walkthroughs. Trending audio and native-style edits throughout — nothing that feels like a traditional ad.

### 2. Denver Creator Partnerships
We will build a roster of Denver-based creators — bike commuters, outdoor lifestyle accounts, "day in my life" creators — and have each one film their own "Trade Your Traffic" episode ending at the flagship. Creator content is far more trusted than brand ads with this crowd, and it gives us a steady content pipeline to boost with paid.

### 3. Traffic-Trolling OOH & Transit
Billboards along I-25 and Colfax that speak directly to people stuck in traffic ("You could be past this by now"), plus bus shelter and light-rail placements pointing to the showroom. OOH is having a comeback because people screenshot clever boards and repost them, so the placements double as social content.

### 4. Pop-Up Test-Ride Tour
A branded trailer takes demo bikes to farmers markets, breweries, and trailhead lots across the metro all summer, funneling riders back to the flagship for the full experience. Everyone who rides gets a follow-up message and an invite to the showroom's opening-month events. Nothing converts like the ride itself.

### 5. Geofenced Mobile & Paid Search
Geofenced mobile ads around downtown parking garages, park-and-rides, and competitor bike shops, plus paid search on e-bike and commuter terms in the Denver metro, capturing the demand the rest of the campaign creates.

---

## Target Audience

Primarily we are talking to Denver metro commuters aged 25-45 who are curious about e-bikes and open to a test ride. Many of them drive to work but wish they didn't, lots of them have seen e-bikes around and wondered, and a good number would probably switch if the moment was right. They are active on social, they like trying new things, and a great in-person experience really matters to them. Secondary audiences include weekend recreation riders and families interested in the cargo model.
