## Campaign Title

**"Book It Like a Haircut" — the Clearbrook Online Booking Push**

---

## Opening: The Problem We Are Actually Solving

Clearbrook's problem is not clinical quality or even awareness — it is that the front door is closed to the way younger patients now choose care. 64% of patients under 40 choose a clinic based on online booking availability before considering reputation (ref-15). Reputation is the tiebreaker; the booking test is the entry filter, and every patient who hits a phone-only step is a patient Clearbrook never sees. The pool of patients making that choice is large and growing: 41% of US adults under 45 have no primary-care physician, up from 33% in 2019 (ref-15), and the traditional alternative is slow — the average wait for a new-patient primary-care appointment in mid-size metros is 26 days (ref-15).

Clearbrook's new online booking flow is our answer to that specific problem: live availability across every location, instant confirmation, booking free, and a $149 flat cash-pay urgent-care visit with no surprises. The transparent price is itself a conversion asset — transparent cash-pay pricing pages increase new-patient bookings by 34% for urgent-care services (ref-15). Every tactic below is selected for its ability to put the booking flow in front of under-40 patients at the exact moment they are choosing a clinic, not just to advertise that it exists.

---

## Target Audience

Patients under 40 in Clearbrook's metro footprint — the cohort where the 64% booking-first selection behavior is measured (ref-15). These are mobile-first people who run their lives from their phones and route episodic care needs to whichever provider passes the booking test first. They are also evidence-sensitive: 58% say they distrust ads that make claims without visible evidence or numbers (ref-07), which is why our creative shows the booking flow working in real time rather than asserting that it is easy.

Secondary audience: parents booking for kids after hours, and past patients who still call.

---

## The Big Idea

**"Book It Like a Haircut."** You book your barber, your oil change, and your dinner reservation online — why is a doctor's appointment the one thing stuck in 1995? Because the core problem is that booking availability is the entry filter for under-40 clinic choice (ref-15), the creative does not dramatize Clearbrook's brand; it dramatizes the booking moment itself. Split-screen spots show a haircut booked in twelve seconds next to a clinic hold line, ending on the real Clearbrook flow confirming a real appointment. Every asset ends the same way: **Clearbrook. Book it like everything else.**

## Channel & Media Plan

Each channel below maps directly to the booking-first selection problem named above.

### 1. Paid Social — Short-Form Video (Reels, TikTok, Shorts)
Because the core problem is reaching under-40 patients before they need care, we build awareness where this cohort discovers new things: 71% of Gen-Z respondents discover new brands primarily through short-form video (ref-07). The format is efficient and attention-holding — Instagram Reels average a $9.40 CPM, TikTok $7.80, and YouTube Shorts $6.90 (ref-06), and short-form video ads average 2.4x the completion rate of static feed ads (ref-06). We cap frequency deliberately, since frequency above 4 exposures per week correlates with a 18% drop in engagement rate (ref-06).

### 2. Paid Search & Google Maps / Local Listings
Because the booking test happens at the moment of need — "urgent care near me" at 9pm — Clearbrook must be the listing where booking is one tap from the map. Always-on search coverage plus fully optimized location listings turn the 64% booking-first behavior (ref-15) directly into appointments. We benchmark against the 1.2% cross-vertical paid average CTR, targeting the 2.6% top quartile (ref-06).

### 3. Out-of-Home — Transit & Billboards
Because a transparent flat price is a proven booking driver — transparent cash-pay pricing pages increase new-patient bookings by 34% for urgent-care services (ref-15) — the boards lead with the number: "$149. Booked by the next stoplight." OOH makes the price and the booking promise unavoidable on commuter routes.

### 4. Landing Page & Booking-Flow Creative
Because every claim in this campaign is really a claim about the flow, the landing page drops visitors directly into it. Demo videos and screenshots feed every other channel, letting the product prove the promise for an audience that distrusts unevidenced claims (ref-07).

### 5. Email & SMS to Existing Patients
Because thousands of past patients still call, a short series shows them the new flow and invites the next booking online. Lifecycle-triggered emails average a 38% open rate, versus 21% for standard campaign sends (ref-16), so the series triggers off recent visits rather than blasting the full list.

## Why Clearbrook Wins

This plan does not advertise a feature; it attacks the measured reason under-40 patients never arrive — a phone-only front door in a market where 64% choose by booking availability before reputation (ref-15). Social builds the habit-level association, search and Maps capture the moment of need, OOH plants the $149 number, and the flow itself closes. Every tactic is answerable to the same named problem.
