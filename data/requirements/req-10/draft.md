# Cascade Valley Spring Festival Visitor Campaign — Proposal Draft

**Prepared by:** Ryo Tanabe, Junior Planner, Meridian & Craft
**Client:** Cascade Valley Tourism Board
**Version:** Draft v1

---

## Campaign Title

**"Bloom Weekend: Spring Starts in Cascade Valley"**

## Opening

The Cascade Valley Spring Festival is the region's signature seasonal event, and this year it is bigger and better than ever. The festival features three weekends of orchard blossom trails, a riverside food and craft market with over 60 local vendors, live music on two stages, guided wildflower walks, and a lantern release evening that has become a fan favorite. The festival weekend package is positioned around $350 per couple and bundles lodging, festival entry, and a dining credit, which makes it a really attractive and easy-to-book offer.

Cascade Valley has so much to offer in spring, and the festival is the perfect showcase for it. Our campaign will bring the color, energy, and charm of the festival to families and couples across the region and get them excited to visit. We are confident that with the right creative and media mix, "Bloom Weekend" can become an event people look forward to every single year.

## Target Audience

Families and couples within drive-market range of Cascade Valley who are planning spring trips. Broadly, this means adults roughly 25 to 55 who enjoy weekend getaways, seasonal events, food and outdoor experiences, and who live close enough to reach the valley by car. Many of these travelers love discovering new destinations, and spring is a time when people are eager to get out after winter, so the timing works strongly in our favour.

## The Big Idea

**"Bloom Weekend" — a spring weekend so photogenic you can't stay home.**

We position the Spring Festival not just as an event but as the official start of spring for the whole region. The creative celebrates blossoms, food, music, and togetherness, with two emotional lanes:

1. **For couples** — a romantic, screen-worthy escape (blossom trails, lantern release, valley dining).
2. **For families** — an easy, packed-with-stuff-to-do weekend the kids will actually remember (craft market, music, wildflower walks).

Everything ladders up to one simple call to action: book your Bloom Weekend package.

## Channel & Media Plan

Social media is very effective for travel inspiration, and short videos perform especially well, so short-form video sits at the center of the plan.

| Channel | Role |
|---|---|
| Paid social (Reels, TikTok, Shorts) | Hero short-form videos of blossoms, food, lanterns; drives inspiration and clicks |
| Creator partnerships | Regional travel and family creators visit the festival and share authentic content, which audiences trust a lot |
| Regional digital display & paid search | Capture people actively searching for spring trips and weekend ideas |
| Drive-market out-of-home | Billboards on key routes into and around the drive market to build awareness |
| Email & landing page | A dedicated Bloom Weekend booking page plus emails to the tourism board's existing list |

Young travelers and families alike spend a lot of time on social platforms, and creator content feels much more genuine than regular ads, so we expect strong engagement across the board. Search and display will pick up in-market demand, and out-of-home keeps the festival top of mind on the roads our visitors actually drive.

## KPIs

- Grow awareness of the Spring Festival across the drive market
- Strong engagement on short-form video and creator content
- Meaningful traffic to the Bloom Weekend booking page
- A healthy number of festival package bookings at the $350 per couple price point
- Positive sentiment and social buzz around the festival weekends

## Why This Will Work

Spring travel demand is strong, and people really love festivals and seasonal events. The festival product itself is fantasic, the package is easy to say yes to, and our creative will make the valley look irresistible. With social, creators, search, and out-of-home working together, we will reach families and couples wherever they are planning there next trip, and turn that inspiration into bookings for the Cascade Valley Tourism Board.
