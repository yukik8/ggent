# Client Request — Spring Festival Visitor Campaign

**To:** Meridian & Craft
**From:** Tom Ashford, Executive Director, Cascade Valley Tourism Board
**Date received:** 2026-01-06
**Subject:** RFP — 2026 Spring Festival visitor campaign

---

Folks,

Happy new year. As you know from the last eight years of doing this together, spring planning starts the minute the holiday decorations come down. Here's what the board has approved for this season.

## The situation

The Spring Festival is our flagship, and 2026 is the biggest edition yet: three weekends of orchard blossom trails, the riverside food and craft market with over 60 local vendors, live music on two stages, guided wildflower walks, and the lantern release evening everybody loves. We've packaged a festival weekend at around $350 per couple — lodging, festival entry, and a dining credit bundled together.

I'll be straight with you on one thing that keeps coming up at board meetings: the festival weekends themselves do fine, but our April occupancy overall has been stuck below half-full for three springs running. The festival sells out its Saturdays and the valley sits empty the rest of the season. Nobody on the board quite agrees on what to do about that, so for now the ask is the festival campaign — but I mention it because it's the cloud this all sits under.

## What we want

A campaign that gets the festival in front of families and couples across our drive market and gets those weekend packages booked. We want the valley looking gorgeous — blossoms, food, lanterns, kids having a ball. Awareness and bookings.

## Audience

Families and couples within drive-market range planning spring trips. You know our visitor better than anyone by now.

## Budget and timeline

- **Budget:** $260,000 total (public funds — full line-item accounting required).
- **Timeline:** In market 2026-02-15 through 2026-05-15, covering the booking window and the festival itself.

## Success measure

The board has set the target: **1,500 festival weekend packages sold at the $350 per couple price point.** That's what I have to report against in the June board meeting.

## Deliverables

1. Campaign strategy and creative concept
2. Media plan with audience rationale
3. Itemized budget (procurement rules apply)
4. Measurement plan

## Process

Because we're public sector, this goes out as an open RFP — four agencies are responding, including you as our longtime partner. The selection committee scores on strategy, evidence, and value. Submissions due January 30; decision expected by the end of February.

Bring us your sharpest thinking. The committee has gotten pickier about numbers since the state audit.

Tom

Tom Ashford
Executive Director, Cascade Valley Tourism Board
