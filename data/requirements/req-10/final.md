## Campaign Title

**"Bloom Weekend: Spring Starts in Cascade Valley"**

## Opening

The Cascade Valley Spring Festival is the region's signature seasonal event, and this year it is bigger and better than ever. The festival features three weekends of orchard blossom trails, a riverside food and craft market with over 60 local vendors, live music on two stages, guided wildflower walks, and a lantern release evening that has become a visitor favorite. The festival weekend package is positioned around $350 per couple and bundles lodging, festival entry, and a dining credit — an attractive, easy-to-book offer.

Cascade Valley has a great deal to offer in spring, and the festival is the perfect showcase for it. Our campaign will bring the color, energy, and charm of the festival to families and couples across the region and inspire them to visit. With the right creative and media mix, "Bloom Weekend" can become an event people look forward to every year.

## Target Audience

Families and couples within drive-market range of Cascade Valley who are planning spring trips. Broadly, this means adults aged roughly 25 to 55 who enjoy weekend getaways, seasonal events, food, and outdoor experiences, and who live close enough to reach the valley by car. Spring is a period when travelers are eager to get out after winter, so the seasonal timing works in our favor.

## The Big Idea

**"Bloom Weekend" — a spring weekend so photogenic you can't stay home.**

We position the Spring Festival not just as an event but as the official start of spring for the whole region. The creative celebrates blossoms, food, music, and togetherness, with two emotional lanes:

1. **For couples** — a romantic, screen-worthy escape (blossom trails, lantern release, valley dining).
2. **For families** — an easy, activity-packed weekend the kids will actually remember (craft market, live music, wildflower walks).

Everything ladders up to one simple call to action: book your Bloom Weekend package.

## Channel & Media Plan

Social media is highly effective for travel inspiration, and short-form video performs especially well with this audience, so it sits at the center of the plan. Industry reporting suggests a majority of regional weekend trips are now inspired by content seen on social platforms.

| Channel | Role |
|---|---|
| Paid social (Reels, TikTok, Shorts) | Hero short-form videos of blossoms, food, and lanterns; drives inspiration and clicks |
| Creator partnerships | Regional travel and family creators visit the festival and share authentic content that audiences trust |
| Regional digital display & paid search | Capture people actively searching for spring trips and weekend ideas |
| Drive-market out-of-home | Billboards on key routes into and around the drive market to build awareness |
| Email & landing page | A dedicated Bloom Weekend booking page plus emails to the tourism board's existing list |

Families and couples alike spend significant time on social platforms, and creator content feels more genuine than standard advertising, so we expect strong engagement. Search and display will capture in-market demand, and out-of-home keeps the festival top of mind on the routes our visitors actually drive.

## KPIs

- Measurable lift in Spring Festival awareness across the drive market (pre/post survey)
- Above-benchmark engagement rate on short-form video and creator content
- 100,000+ visits to the Bloom Weekend booking page over the campaign flight
- 1,500+ festival package bookings at the $350 per couple price point
- Positive sentiment and earned social coverage across the three festival weekends

## Why This Will Work

Spring travel demand is strong, and festivals and seasonal events consistently draw regional visitors. The festival product itself is fantastic, the package is easy to say yes to, and our creative will make the valley look irresistible. With social, creators, search, and out-of-home working together, we will reach families and couples wherever they are planning their next trip, and turn that inspiration into bookings for the Cascade Valley Tourism Board.
